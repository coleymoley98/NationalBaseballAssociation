Wilkinson Fires Shutout in Pawtucket Victory
Thursday, July 10th, 1902
"Good pitching beats good hitting" is an old baseball adage.

Well, good pitching also beats bad hitting.

Max Wilkinson of the Pawtucket Juniors proved the point at Juniors Ballpark today, holding the New Britain Juniors to 3 hits in a complete game shutout victory.

"Wilkinson made us look real bad today," Juniors manager Jason Lafrenière said in his postgame interview. "He was outstanding. He was down in the zone with all his pitches. Everything was sinking."

Wilkinson had 6 strikeouts and gave up 2 walks as the Juniors took the 8-0 win.

On the season Wilkinson has compiled a 10-2 mark with a 2.13 ERA in 14 starts. In pitching 105.2 innings he has 87 strikeouts and 32 walks while limiting the opposition to a .198 batting average.
