Roger McLarty Captures CL Most Valuable Player Award
Monday, September 14th, 1903
Roger McLarty has been around Canadian League enough to know a good hot streak when he sees one -- and he got to see one firsthand all year. For his standout run through the 1903 season, he's taking home the Most Valuable Player Award in the Canadian League.

The New York shortstop put up a batting average of .351 with 18 doubles, 10 triples, 2 home runs and 36 RBIs.

He received 4 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Chongrak LaPorte of the Boston Redcoats finished second in voting, while Muangkan Fawley of the New York Rebels finished third.
