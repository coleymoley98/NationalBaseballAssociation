Team Pitcher Gets Most Valuable Player Award
Friday, March 20th, 1896
When you fashion a 0.00 ERA in Canadian Senior Baseball Tournament, you're going to turn some heads. Add in 1 starts, 7 innings, 3 strikeouts and only 4 walks, with a .269 opponent's batting average and you've got a case for being the league's most valuable player.

That player is Rémi Émond of the Team Quebec, who fashioned a 1-0 record and has been named the 1896 Canadian Senior Baseball Tournament Most Valuable Player Award winner after a stellar campaign.

"Everything just felt right this year," the 29-year-old said. "I had great defense and some good luck too."

He received 5 first place votes out of a possible 6. Thanawat Branch of the Team New York finished second in voting with 1 first place vote, while Jozie Rivière of the Team Michigan finished third.
