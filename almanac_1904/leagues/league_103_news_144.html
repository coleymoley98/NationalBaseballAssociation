Gerard DiPaolo Takes MVP Trophy
Friday, May 6th, 1904
At the age of 18, Gerard DiPaolo of the Minneapolis Juniors could very well have his best years ahead of him.

It's a startling thought when you consider the year he had in 1904 was good enough to win the center fielder the Francophone Junior Circuit Most Valuable Player Award.

"See the ball, hit the ball," he said. "That's my basic approach."

The top hitter put up a .324 batting average this year, as well as a .430 on-base percentage. He played 50 games and racked up 59 hits, 18 doubles, 1 triple, 9 home runs, 41 RBIs and 50 runs scored.

He received 11 first place votes out of a possible 12. Natipong Lybrand of the Coude Juniors finished second in voting, while Dorian Hall of the Coude Juniors finished third.
