Coude-Juneau Brawl, Damrong and Piché Suspended
Wednesday, April 6th, 1904
If there wasn't a history between Saksit Damrong of Coude and Sonny Piché of Juneau, then there is one now. The incident between the two sparked a bench-clearing scrum with those two being the center of attention. Now they will miss 4 and 9 games respectively as a result of their actions. The league office announced their suspensions today.

"We're both really competitive," Damrong said later, "and sometimes things go a little too far because of it."

On the season, Damrong is batting .100 with no home runs and 0 RBIs. He has played in 7 games.

Piché has taken the mound for 13 relief appearances this season, nailing down 2 saves while notching a 1-2 record with a 8.10 ERA. The Juneau reliever has limited opposing batters to a .309 batting average.
