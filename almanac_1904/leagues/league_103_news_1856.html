Best Season In FJC Belongs To Gamache
Thursday, May 6th, 1897
He may have less experience than most of his Francophone Junior Circuit peers, but that didn't slow Antoine Gamache down at all in 1897.

At the age of 18, the Coude third baseman has been named the winner of the Francophone Junior Circuit Most Valuable Player Award.

He had a .360 batting average and a .408 on-base percentage this year. In 48 games he piled up 72 hits, 16 doubles, no triples, 9 home runs, 42 RBIs and 35 runs scored.

"I'm blown away by the season I was able to put together," he said. "I'm looking forward to trying to do it all again next year."

He received 8 first place votes out of a possible 12. David Morrow of the Sainte-Paul Juniors finished second in voting with 4 first place votes, while Sam Hoppe of the Davenport Juniors finished third.
