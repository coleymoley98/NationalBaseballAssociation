Promrak Selected FJC's Premier Player
Monday, May 6th, 1901
He may have less experience than most of his Francophone Junior Circuit peers, but that didn't slow Vilas Promrak down at all in 1901.

At the age of 16, the Juneau shortstop has been named the winner of the Francophone Junior Circuit Most Valuable Player Award.

He had a .376 batting average and a .431 on-base percentage this year. In 49 games he piled up 73 hits, 15 doubles, 2 triples, 10 home runs, 41 RBIs and 38 runs scored.

"I'm blown away by the season I was able to put together," he said. "I'm looking forward to trying to do it all again next year."

He received 9 first place votes out of a possible 12. Timothy Jacobs of the Sherbrooke Juniors finished second in voting with 2 first place votes, while Josh Birdsong of the Sherbrooke Juniors finished third with 1 first place vote.
