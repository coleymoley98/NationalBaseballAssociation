Springfield's Sawangpian Takes 1896 Most Valuable Player Award
Tuesday, August 4th, 1896
Pong Sawangpian of the Springfield Juniors had a fine season this year and today he accepted the Most Valuable Player Award for 1896.

The 18-year-old shortstop put up a .368 average and a .427 on-base percentage, piling up 70 hits, 11 doubles, no triples, 6 home runs, 28 RBIs and 30 runs scored.

He received 12 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Benny Savard of the Canton Juniors finished second in voting, while Remington Fowler of the Pawtucket Juniors finished third.
