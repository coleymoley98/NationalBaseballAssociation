Shaw Big Deal in Quebec: Hits for Cycle
Saturday, July 11th, 1896
Getting 4 hits in a game is no easy feat, but doing it to hit for the cycle is even more rare.

Nathan Shaw of the Quebec Nordiques pulled it off today at Bennett Park, going 4-for-5 against the Detroit Lions. His batting escapades sparked the Nordiques to a 15-10 victory.

Shaw tallied 3 runs and drove in 4 during the game. Hitting for the cycle is particularly rare because of the different skills required - enough power for a home run, enough speed for a triple and enough patience at the plate to see at least four hittable balls.

"It just happened," Shaw said to reporters after the win. "Must have been fate or something, I wasn't expecting it. Heck, I wasn't even really trying for it."

Nathan Shaw doubled in the 3rd, singled in the 5th, hit a two-run triple in the 6th, struck out in the 7th and hit a two-run home run off Kittiphan Mai in the 8th.

Shaw is batting at a .257 pace this season, picking up 57 hits in 222 at-bats with 6 home runs, 39 RBIs and 34 runs scored.
