Tuesday, February 9th, 1904
Nordiques left fielder Patipat Thomassin required medical attention yesterday after severely burning himself while barbecuing at his home. Thomassin suffered first and second degree burns when a flame from the grill he was using suddenly shot up his right arm. "It was a pretty scary scene," said teammate Jukkapant Simard, who was with Thomassin when the accident occurred. "I thought it was going to totally engulf him for a moment." The fire also singed Patipat Thomassin's hair and eyebrows, but luckily failed to ignite the shirt he was wearing at the time. "It honestly could have been much worse," said Simard. The injury will reportedly keep Thomassin out of action for anywhere from 3 weeks up to a month, but doctors expect the burns to fully heal.
