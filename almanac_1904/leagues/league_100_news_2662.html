Jones Selected CL's Top Player
Saturday, September 10th, 1898
At the age of 22, Hunter Jones of the Philadelphia Liberty could very well have his best years ahead of him.

It's a startling thought when you consider the year he had in 1898 was good enough to win the left fielder the Canadian League Most Valuable Player Award.

"See the ball, hit the ball," he said. "That's my basic approach."

The top hitter put up a .386 batting average this year, as well as a .424 on-base percentage. He played 103 games and racked up 162 hits, 18 doubles, 7 triples, 1 home run, 38 RBIs and 63 runs scored.

He received 4 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Rusty Dang of the Philadelphia Liberty finished second in voting, while Rocky Boehm of the Toronto Blue Sox finished third.
