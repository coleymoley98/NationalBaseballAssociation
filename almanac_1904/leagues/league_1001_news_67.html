Marden Selected 1904 CSBT Most Valuable Player Award
Thursday, March 17th, 1904
Denzel Marden took Canadian Senior Baseball Tournament by storm this year, and he did it all at the age of 21.

For his spectacular campaign, the Team Quebec star has been named the 1904 Canadian Senior Baseball Tournament Most Valuable Player Award recipient.

"To be considered amongst the best in the league is overwhelming," said the center fielder.

On the season he compiled a .476 batting average to go with a .522 on-base percentage. The native of Montréal, The United Provinces also added 4 doubles, no triples, no home runs, 3 RBIs and 3 runs scored.

He received 4 first place votes out of a possible 6. Cy Bourbeau of the Team Illinois finished second in voting, while John Pridmore of the Team Ontario finished third.
