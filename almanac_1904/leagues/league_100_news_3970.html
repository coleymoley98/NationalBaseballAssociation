Che-moh Gets No-Hitter Against New York
Sunday, June 30th, 1901
Philadelphia's Hafit Che-moh was right on the mark today at Polo Grounds in a sensational no-hitter against the New York Rebels, shutting them down 4-0.

Che-moh chalked up 5 strikeouts and gave up 2 walks in a game that was instantly one of the highlights of the season. The brash right-hander, who is known for a confidence that sometimes borders on arrogance, told reporters afterward that he "was the best pitcher in the league today."

Philadelphia skipper Anisong Ilic said in his postgame interview that Che-moh "tossed a masterpiece today."

"Hafit likes to talk but it ain't bragging if you can back it up," he said. "Today he certainly did just that."

Che-moh has made 14 starts so far this year and put up a 7-6 won-lost record with a 3.91 ERA. In 124.1 innings he has allowed 135 hits, walked 29 and struck out 45.
