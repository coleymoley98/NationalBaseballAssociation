Eagan Sends Detroit Bats to Deep Freeze
Tuesday, June 19th, 1900
The Detroit Lions manager was philosophical in the clubhouse following a shutout loss to the Montreal Royales. The Lions were overpowered by Montreal starter Pat Eagan, who hurled his club to victory, 1-0.

"It was just one of those games -- shake it off and do better next time," Adrian Miramontes said.

Over 9 innings, Eagan blanked Detroit on 5 hits with 2 walks and 5 strikeouts.

"I kept them off balance. I could tell a lot of them were late on fastballs because they were fouling them off. Once you see that, it sets them up for other pitches," Eagan said.

"I set them up, where if they chased one breaking ball, they were going to chase another one."

This year Eagan has fashioned a 8-6 mark, 2.69 ERA and pitched 130.1 innings. Opposing clubs are batting .242 against him and he has logged 38 strikeouts.
