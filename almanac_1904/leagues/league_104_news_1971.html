Desjardins Touches All the Bases, Hits for Cycle
Thursday, July 1st, 1897
A.J. Desjardins of the Halifax Juniors touched all the bases today at Juniors Ballpark in the 18-13 victory over Springfield, hitting for the cycle.

He teed off on Juniors pitching, collecting 1 single, 1 double, 1 triple and 1 home run. The feat is rare in baseball as it requires a game of no fewer than four hits and the unusual combination of a home run, which requires a batter to hit for some power -- and a triple, which requires a batter to have both speed and a favorable fall of the ball.

"The hardest part was the triple," Desjardins confessed after the game. "A lot has to go right. It's real easy to get greedy trying to stretch a double and get thrown out. It's really hard to get a three-bagger."

He finished the big day with 4 hits in 4 at-bats, 7 runs batted in, 2 runs scored -- and a place in the record books.

A.J. Desjardins hit an RBI double in the 1st, hit a two-run triple in the 2nd, hit an RBI single in the 4th and hit a three-run home run off Suriyakarn Lavallée in the 5th.

This season Desjardins has hit at a .276 clip with 19 RBIs, 3 home runs and 14 runs scored. He has played in 28 games.
