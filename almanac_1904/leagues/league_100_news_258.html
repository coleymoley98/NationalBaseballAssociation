Toronto Southpaw Tosses 2-0 Shutout
Friday, June 17th, 1904
Arthur Joly of the Toronto Blue Sox was on top of his game today.

The 22-year-old tantalized the Ottawa Indians with crisp curves and changeups, mixed in with just enough fastballs to keep the batters honest and came away with a 2-0 shutout.

"He had the changeup working," Ottawa manager Nattachai Kantong said in postgame comments. "You knew it was coming and you still grounded out -- and then he threw it even slower."

The Toronto hurler had 2 strikeouts, gave up 2 walks and surrendered 11 hits in his complete-game effort.

Joly told reporters after the game that "keeping the ball down is the key to being successful in this league."

"If you don't do that, they'll make you pay," he said.

This year Joly has fashioned a 6-5 mark, 2.02 ERA and pitched 102.1 innings. Opposing clubs are batting .263 against him and he has logged 62 strikeouts.
