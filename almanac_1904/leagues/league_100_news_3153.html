Liberty Crowned Champions
Monday, September 4th, 1899
A celebratory mood filled the air as the Philadelphia Liberty closed out the season with a 6-3 victory over the Toronto Blue Sox at Canadian League Park I to capture the Canadian Series, 4-3.

Giving the Blue Sox credit for a hard-fought series, Liberty players said they believed throughout the postseason that their "destiny" was to be crowned Canadian League champions.

"We felt like this was our year all season long," said first baseman Ben Newman, who was named series MVP. "It was our destiny to win it and we did."

It was the 1st in franchise history. During the regular season Philadelphia put up a 56-52 record and finished second.

"They're the champions and they deserve it," said a dejected Toronto manager Alexandre Alexandre. "We played good ball, they just played better than us."
