Kwanjai Selected FJC's Best Player
Saturday, May 6th, 1899
Kritsananon Kwanjai took Francophone Junior Circuit by storm this year, and he did it all at the age of 16.

For his spectacular campaign, the Coude Juniors star has been named the 1899 Francophone Junior Circuit Most Valuable Player Award recipient.

"To be considered amongst the best in the league is overwhelming," said the third baseman.

On the season he compiled a .352 batting average to go with a .394 on-base percentage. The native of Juneau, The United Provinces also added 12 doubles, 7 triples, 5 home runs, 33 RBIs and 40 runs scored.

He received 8 first place votes out of a possible 12. Édouard Pichette of the Quebec City Juniors finished second in voting with 3 first place votes, while Prak U-chareon of the Sherbrooke Juniors finished third with 1 first place vote.
