Berkheimer Batters Drummondville Big Time
Monday, March 14th, 1904
Even as a kid, J.R. Berkheimer said his coaches told him the key to hitting was making contact, and letting everything else flow from there. At Ballpark of Drummondville today, Berkheimer showed how well he learned that lesson.

The 15-year-old right fielder had 5 hits in 6 at-bats for the Minneapolis Juniors, helping his team knock off the Drummondville Juniors by a score of 7-3.

"They always said, just keep your eye on the ball, don't overswing, make contact and you never know what will happen," he said. "That's all I did today."

J.R. Berkheimer singled in the 1st, grounded out in the 3rd, doubled in the 5th, hit an RBI single in the 7th, singled in the 9th and hit a three-run home run off Lukden Giguère in the 10th.

This year Berkheimer is batting .442 with 2 home runs.
