Wyatt Reece Says Farewell to Baseball
Saturday, May 9th, 1903
Wyatt Reece said years of hard work have taken their toll on him physically, and he's decided it's now time to retire.

"I don't know any way other than full throttle, I've always been that way," he said. "Going so hard for so many years might have cost me some time on my career, but I don't regret a thing."

The Montreal Royales catcher is leaving the game behind at age 36.

Reece is batting .224 with 179 career base hits, including 18 doubles, 6 triples and no home runs with 74 RBIs and 81 runs scored. He has played in 265 games, stolen 1 bases and compiled a .286 OBP.
