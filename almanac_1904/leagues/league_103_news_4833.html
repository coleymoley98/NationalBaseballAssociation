Hit Parade: DiPaolo Gets Cycle
Tuesday, April 14th, 1903
In an outing that will go down in the annals of baseball history, Minneapolis left fielder Gerard DiPaolo hit for the cycle today as the Juniors marched to victory over the Juneau Juniors, 13-12.

DiPaolo collected 4 hits in 5 at-bats, including 1 single, 1 double, 1 triple and 1 home run. He added 3 runs batted in and 4 runs scored to help Minneapolis take the Juniors Ballpark contest.

"It was the last thing on my mind, really," DiPaolo said. "I'm out there trying to get a blooper or a walk or whatever might help the team. Even if I had wanted to, hitting for the cycle is probably as much about luck as about skill anyway."

Gerard DiPaolo doubled in the 1st, hit a two-run home run off Nutthawat Ogburn in the 2nd, singled in the 4th, hit an RBI triple in the 6th, walked in the 7th and struck out in the 9th.

Currently DiPaolo is batting .344 with 8 home runs and 24 RBIs.
