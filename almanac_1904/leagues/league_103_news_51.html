Lévy 5-for-6 for Gauthier
Wednesday, March 9th, 1904
Isaac Lévy knows as well as anyone that in baseball, the biggest contracts go to the biggest hitters. At Gauthier Grounds today, he made his case to be considered among that group.

The 17-year-old second baseman hammered 5 hits in 6 chances, though the remarkable effort came even as his Gauthier Juniors fell, 12-8, to the Sherbrooke Juniors.

"We made some bad pitches and he took advantage of it," Juniors manager Lance Vandergriff told BNN sportswriters after the game. "Thankfully, we were able to do enough elsewhere that Lévy wasn't the difference."

Isaac Lévy hit a solo-shot off Alex Turcotte in the 2nd, grounded out in the 4th, singled in the 6th, singled in the 8th, walked in the 10th, singled in the 12th and singled in the 13th.

This year Lévy has socked 9 hits in 17 at-bats for a .529 average with 1 home run. He is getting on base at a .579 pace and his totals also include 2 RBIs and 5 runs scored.
