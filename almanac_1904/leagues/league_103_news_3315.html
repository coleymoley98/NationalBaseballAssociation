Rigsbee Cracks 5 Hits
Friday, March 23rd, 1900
Bill Scot Rigsbee was at the head of a Drummondville Juniors hit parade today at Gauthier Grounds. The 17-year-old went to the plate 6 times and came away with 5 hits, but it was the Gauthier Juniors that marched off with the 9-8 win.

"We didn't have it today," griped Juniors skipper Jake Trudel following the game. "Bill Scot did fine, but as a team, we didn't do it with the bats and we didn't do it pitching."

Rigsbee did more than fine, dominating the Juniors at the plate during his record-making 5-for-6 performance.

"Each time I went to the plate, I was thinking about hitting the ball hard somewhere," the sweet-swinging right fielder told reporters. "When I focus on that, I usually have quality at-bats."

Bill Scot Rigsbee struck out in the 1st, singled in the 3rd, hit an RBI single in the 4th, singled in the 6th, hit an RBI single in the 7th and singled in the 9th.

This year Rigsbee is batting .344 with no home runs. He has scored 4 runs and driven in 4.
