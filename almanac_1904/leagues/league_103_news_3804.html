Nickles and Reynolds Suspended 3 Games for Brawl
Sunday, April 7th, 1901
During a recent Coude-Gallant game at Coude Grounds, Robert Nickles of the Juniors and Vince Reynolds of the Juniors got into an altercation on the field and had to be separated from each other as the benches emptied. Both players knew they would have to face the music and today the league commissioner's office announced their punishments. They have been given 3-game suspensions because of their actions.

For the season, Nickles is hitting at a .317 clip with 7 home runs, 22 RBIs and has scored 26 runs. He has 39 hits, 15 walks and a .397 on-base percentage.

Reynolds has taken the mound for 18 relief appearances this season, nailing down 3 saves while notching a 2-3 record with a 8.10 ERA. The Gallant reliever has limited opposing batters to a .270 batting average.
