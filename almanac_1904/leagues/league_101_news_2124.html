Roberts NL Selection for 1897 Most Valuable Player Award
Saturday, September 11th, 1897
The manager of the Detroit Lions said he thinks Josh Roberts is only going to get better as he gets older.

He may only be 26, but it will be hard to top the left fielder's 1897 campaign, as he has been named the winner of the National League Most Valuable Player Award.

His winning campaign featured a .385 batting average and a .439 on-base percentage this year. In 105 games he piled up 163 hits, 24 doubles, 12 triples, 4 home runs, 80 RBIs and 75 runs scored.

He received 3 first place votes out of a possible 4. Nathan Shaw of the Quebec Nordiques finished second in voting with 1 first place vote, while Eric Scism of the Chicagou Frontenacs finished third.
