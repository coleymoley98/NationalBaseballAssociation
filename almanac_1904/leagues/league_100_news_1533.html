Boston Blanks Liberty, Konchok at His Best
Monday, July 20th, 1896
Piyawat Konchok hurled a fine game for the Boston Redcoats at Exposition Park today, holding the Philadelphia Liberty to 4 hits in a shutout win.

"When he started out, his command wasn't that good, but he's a tough guy," Boston manager Herb Lamaison said. "The more pitches he throws, the better his command is."

The left-hander had 7 strikeouts and 1 walk in the 7-0 victory.

"An outstanding job," Philadelphia manager Alex Bolduc said. "We couldn't get anything going. Every time we got a hit, he got out of the jam. He pitched a terrific game."

On his stats sheet for the year Konchok shows a 13-11 record, 25 starts, 199 innings, 77 strikeouts and a 3.75 ERA.
