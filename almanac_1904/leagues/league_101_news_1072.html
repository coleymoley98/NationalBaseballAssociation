Charles Bergeron Wins 1895 NL Batting Title
Sunday, August 25th, 1895
Charles Bergeron said he doesn't know how many years he has left in the sport, so a batting title is an "incredibly sweet" honor.

The 39-year-old Chicagou Frontenacs second baseman batted .376 to capture the 1895 National League batting championship.

On the year, he had 142 hits, 18 doubles, 4 triples and 2 home runs for the year. In 94 games Bergeron totaled 64 RBIs and scored 77 runs.
