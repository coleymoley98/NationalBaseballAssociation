DiPaolo 5-for-7 Against Juniors
Saturday, April 4th, 1903
It's not something you read about in the sports pages every day -- a 5-for-7 day. For many, it might come around only once in a career -- and for Gerard DiPaolo, it came around today.

The 17-year-old left fielder scattered hits all over Juniors Ballpark to pace the Minneapolis Juniors to a 10-8 victory over the Quebec City Juniors.

Quebec City skipper Leomar Durán told reporters in the press room that "DiPaolo deserves a hat-tip."

"He hit some good pitches against us," he said. "In fact, according to our scouting reports, DiPaolo hit several that he is not supposed to be able to hit."

Gerard DiPaolo singled in the 1st, grounded out in the 2nd, singled in the 4th, singled in the 5th, hit a solo-shot off Shaun Bethard in the 7th, grounded out in the 9th and hit a two-run home run off Byron Brackens in the 11th.

This year DiPaolo has collected a .339 batting average to date with 41 hits in 121 at-bats, 17 RBIs, 5 home runs and scored 35 times. He has played in 31 games.
