Johnson Calls it a Day in Chicagou
Tuesday, May 10th, 1898
The Chicagou Frontenacs will have to find a new left fielder next year as veteran Ty Johnson is set to retire at the end of the 1898 campaign.

Johnson, age 38, is ready for retirement. "Time takes its toll on this old body. I can't do it as well as I want anymore. It's time to hang up the spikes and make way for a younger guy."

Johnson is batting .362 lifetime with 210 hits, 23 doubles, 29 triples, 9 home runs, 121 RBIs to date. He has also scored 150 runs.
