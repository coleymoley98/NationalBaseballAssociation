NL Announces Top Player Award
Wednesday, September 11th, 1901
Montreal's Mark Potter put the wraps on a fine campaign today by winning the 1901 National League Most Valuable Player Award.

The 24-year-old veteran scorched the opposition with a .343 average, picking up 152 hits, 15 doubles, 12 triples, 4 home runs and 44 RBIs, while scoring 75 runs.

He received 3 first place votes out of a possible 4. William Davison of the Chicagou Frontenacs finished second in voting with 1 first place vote, while Rocky Boehm of the Quebec Nordiques finished third.
