Jones Smacks 5 Hits for Liberty
Thursday, July 11th, 1901
Hunter Jones was at the head of a Philadelphia Liberty hit parade today at Exposition Park. The 25-year-old went to the plate 5 times and came away with 5 hits, but it was the Toronto Blue Sox that marched off with the 5-3 win.

"We didn't have it today," griped Liberty skipper Anisong Ilic following the game. "Hunter did fine, but as a team, we didn't do it with the bats and we didn't do it pitching."

Jones did more than fine, dominating the Blue Sox at the plate during his record-making 5-for-5 performance.

"Each time I went to the plate, I was thinking about hitting the ball hard somewhere," the sweet-swinging left fielder told reporters. "When I focus on that, I usually have quality at-bats."

Hunter Jones hit an RBI single in the 1st, singled in the 3rd, singled in the 5th, singled in the 7th and doubled in the 9th.

This season Jones is batting .367 with 62 hits, 1 home run, 26 RBIs, .398 OBP and has scored 22 runs.
