One Run Enough for New York
Sunday, July 31st, 1904
Jozie Rivière was in a mood to talk following the New York Rebels contest against the Boston Redcoats, expounding to BNN reporters on his pitching philosophy. He had good reason to be excitable, having tossed a complete game shutout of the Redcoats, 1-0.

"There are three important elements in pitching success: location, location, and location," Rivière said. "I was hitting my spots time and time again."

The right-hander blanked Boston on a 4-hitter, allowing 1 walk and racking up 6 strikeouts.

On the season Rivière has a 15-5 record with a 2.12 ERA.
