McKinnie Electric in 12-0 Pawtucket Win
Friday, June 17th, 1904
The Pawtucket Juniors shut out the Quincy Juniors 12-0 today behind the strong pitching of Arthur McKinnie. McKinnie surrendered only 6 hits in recording the complete-game victory, and struck out 10.

Following the last out, McKinnie appeared to celebrate a little too boisterously for Quincy manager Ethan Bax, who could be seen scolding the famously outspoken left-hander as the clubs left the field. McKinnie's own manager then intervened with some words for his counterpart.

The Juniors skipper would not comment on the exchange, but at the Juniors press conference, McKinnie confirmed that it was regarding the celebration.

"He basically told me I was being a lousy winner," McKinnie said.

"And I told him he was being a lousy loser," Pawtucket manager Jetrin Charron added.

Checking out his stats for the season, McKinnie has racked up 3 wins and 1 loss in 4 starts, 3.99 ERA and struck out 33 batters in 38.1 innings.
