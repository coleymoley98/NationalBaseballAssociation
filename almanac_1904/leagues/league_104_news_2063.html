Thursday, August 5th, 1897
Remington Fowler took Anglo Junior Circuit by storm this year, and he did it all at the age of 20.

For his spectacular campaign, the Pawtucket Juniors star has been named the 1897 Anglo Junior Circuit Most Valuable Player Award recipient.

"To be considered amongst the best in the league is overwhelming," said the center fielder.

On the season he compiled a .342 batting average to go with a .423 on-base percentage. The native of Boston, The United Provinces also added 12 doubles, 2 triples, 12 home runs, 40 RBIs and 59 runs scored.

He received 11 first place votes out of a possible 12. Wes Malone of the Pawtucket Juniors finished second in voting with 1 first place vote, while Philippe Duquesne of the Pawtucket Juniors finished third.
