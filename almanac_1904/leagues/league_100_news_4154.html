McLarty Selected CL's Top Player
Thursday, September 12th, 1901
He may have less experience than most of his Canadian League peers, but that didn't slow Roger McLarty down at all in 1901.

At the age of 23, the New York shortstop has been named the winner of the Canadian League Most Valuable Player Award.

He had a .317 batting average and a .348 on-base percentage this year. In 104 games he piled up 132 hits, 18 doubles, 11 triples, no home runs, 57 RBIs and 68 runs scored.

"I'm blown away by the season I was able to put together," he said. "I'm looking forward to trying to do it all again next year."

He received 4 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Nick Jones of the Toronto Blue Sox finished second in voting, while Elliott Boyland of the Toronto Blue Sox finished third.
