Royer at His Best, Whips Juniors
Tuesday, June 23rd, 1896
Zachary Royer bent a little, but he didn't break against the Pawtucket Juniors. The Toronto Juniors pitcher hurled a 1-hit 5-0 shutout in a fine performance, surrendering no walks and tallying 16 strikeouts.

"Zachary is good at getting out of jams," Juniors manager Samuel Sénécal told reporters after the game. "He's almost more effective when he gets guys on base or gets behind in the count, because he uses your aggressiveness against you."

In 8 starts this season Royer has notched a 5-1 record and a 1.25 ERA.
