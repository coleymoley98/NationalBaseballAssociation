NL Player of the Week Trophy Presented
Monday, July 11th, 1898
Jack Brown doesn't have the arm of a youngster, but he's still capable of putting together quality stretches of baseball. Last week was one such instance as Brown cruised to 2 wins and no losses for the Chicagou Frontenacs and took the National League Player of the Week Award.

The 35-year-old was at the top of his game last week, eating up 18 innings while posting a 0.50 ERA. He struck out 2 and walked 2.

So far this year Brown has made 22 starts, tossed 170.1 innings, struck out 27 batters and walked 61. His record is 10-9 with a 4.07 ERA and he has limited opposing teams to a .292 batting average.
