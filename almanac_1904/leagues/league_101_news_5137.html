Brasseurs Listless in 6-0 Loss to Lions
Wednesday, August 5th, 1903
Magic was in the air and on the mound at Bennett Park today as Theodore Paradis held the Juneau Brasseurs spellbound in his 6-0 shutout victory. The Detroit Lions pitcher went the distance, rang up 6 strikeouts, walked 5 and held the Brasseurs to 8 hits to garner the win.

"He just had us in his thrall," Juneau manager Owen Turcotte said later. "We couldn't put anything together against him."

This year Paradis has posted a record of 5 wins and 1 loss with a 2.35 ERA. He has appeared in 8 games and struck out 29 in 65 innings.
