Juniors Spellbound, Strong Hurls No-Hitter
Wednesday, June 19th, 1901
Milton Strong really did a number on the Providence Juniors at Juniors Ballpark today -- and that number was zero.

The Toronto Juniors hurler had what he later called "one of the best moments of my life," tossing a no-hitter and leading the Juniors to victory, 2-0.

"This is something special," Strong said. "I knew from the fifth inning that I had a no-hitter working. I tried to get it out of my mind, but you just can't. They have too many scoreboards in the park. They're everywhere you look."

The right-hander faced only 29 batters in the no-hit gem, struck out 9 and walked 2.

"We simply couldn't get any wood on the ball," Providence manager Luke Cairns said when asked about the epic performance. "Strong was brilliant out there. He certainly had our number."

Thus far this season, Strong has tossed 33 innings in 5 starts, fanned 35 batters, walked 10 and put up a 3.82 ERA with 1 win and 3 losses.
