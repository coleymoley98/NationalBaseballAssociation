Nordiques No Match for Paradis, Lions in 9-0 Thumping
Wednesday, June 11th, 1902
Theodore Paradis was in a mood to talk following the Detroit Lions contest against the Quebec Nordiques, expounding to BNN reporters on his pitching philosophy. He had good reason to be excitable, having tossed a complete game shutout of the Nordiques, 9-0.

"There are three important elements in pitching success: location, location, and location," Paradis said. "I was hitting my spots time and time again."

The right-hander blanked Quebec on a 3-hitter, allowing no walks and racking up 1 strikeout.

In 10 starts this season Paradis has crafted a 5-4 record with a 2.41 ERA.
