National League Selects Week's Best Player
Monday, August 4th, 1902
Reuban Seals seems to put youthful exuberance into every pitch he throws and last week he threw up numbers that even the oldest of veterans would look at with envy. That's why the 24-year-old hurler was chosen the National League Player of the Week Award.

Seals notched 2 wins for the Montreal Royales. He finished the week with 3 strikeouts and an ERA of 0.79 while holding the opposition to a .167 average.

This season Seals has worked 178.2 innings with 66 strikeouts en route to a 16-5 record and a 2.32 ERA.
