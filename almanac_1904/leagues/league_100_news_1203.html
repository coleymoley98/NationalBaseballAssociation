Downing Suffers Heatstroke
Tuesday, January 21st, 1896
Jake Downing of the Philadelphia Liberty was hospitalized earlier today after suffering what doctors described as mild heatstroke.

Downing had reportedly been working outdoors at his offseason home where the temperature was over 100 degrees Fahrenheit. According to Liberty manager Alex Bolduc, who spoke with Downing by phone, the third baseman was in good condition and will probably be released from the hospital tomorrow.

"He just sort of overdid it a little bit," said Bolduc. "He's already feeling a lot better."

Team doctor Ronson Tyler says it's extremely unlikely that Downing will suffer any lingering effects and should be fully recovered in time for spring training.
