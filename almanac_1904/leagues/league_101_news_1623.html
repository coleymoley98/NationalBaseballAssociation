Bergeron 1896 NL Batting Champ
Tuesday, August 25th, 1896
At an age when many of his peers are slowing down or even retiring, Charles Bergeron of the Chicagou Frontenacs is perhaps at his best.

The 40-year-old wore out opposing pitchers for a .390 average this season, to capture the 1896 National League batting championship.

The batting star collected 136 hits, 19 doubles, 3 triples and 3 home runs for the year. In 88 games Bergeron totaled 53 RBIs and scored 67 runs.
