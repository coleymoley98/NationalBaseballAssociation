Seals Sharp in 7-0 Shutout
Sunday, June 24th, 1900
"Good pitching beats good hitting" is an old baseball adage.

Well, good pitching also beats bad hitting.

Reuban Seals of the Montreal Royales proved the point at West Side Grounds today, holding the Chicagou Frontenacs to 8 hits in a complete game shutout victory.

"Seals made us look real bad today," Frontenacs manager Mustafa Campeau said in his postgame interview. "He was outstanding. He was down in the zone with all his pitches. Everything was sinking."

Seals had 2 strikeouts and gave up no walks as the Royales took the 7-0 win.

This year Seals has compiled a 13-3 record with a 2.97 ERA.
