Sanders Goes 5-for-5 Against Quebec
Wednesday, August 28th, 1901
After a 5-hit effort to lead Chicagou's 13-1 victory over the Quebec Nordiques, first baseman Aaron Sanders told BNN beat writers it had been "a special day."

"It really doesn't matter whether it's sandlot ball, high school or college, two hits in a game is nice," he said. "Three or four is better, but five or more is out of sight. And to do that at the National League-level is really something special."

The 25-year-old player earned his accolades today at West Side Grounds with a spectacular 5-for-5 game against the Nordiques.

"His swing is getting there," said Frontenacs skipper Mustafa Campeau, who spoke glowingly of Sanders after the game. "We want him to be quick with his swing, short and compact. We want him to be patient and go the other way when he can. Aaron did that to perfection today."

Aaron Sanders singled in the 1st, singled in the 3rd, tripled in the 5th, singled in the 6th and singled in the 8th.

So far this season Sanders is batting .295 with 2 home runs.
