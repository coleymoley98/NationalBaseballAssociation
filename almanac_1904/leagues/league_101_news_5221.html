Superb Year for Frey, Wins Most Valuable Player Award
Sunday, September 13th, 1903
At the age of 23, Ara Frey of the Detroit Lions could very well have his best years ahead of him.

It's a startling thought when you consider the year he had in 1903 was good enough to win the first baseman the National League Most Valuable Player Award.

"See the ball, hit the ball," he said. "That's my basic approach."

The top hitter put up a .338 batting average this year, as well as a .377 on-base percentage. He played 106 games and racked up 148 hits, 35 doubles, 3 triples, 11 home runs, 75 RBIs and 83 runs scored.

He received 5 first place votes out of a possible 6. Garrett Foreman of the Montreal Royales finished second in voting with 1 first place vote, while Stephen Huseman of the Montreal Royales finished third.
