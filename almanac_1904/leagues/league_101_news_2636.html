Foreman of Montreal Wins Pitching Triple Crown
Thursday, August 25th, 1898
Garrett Foreman of the Montreal Royales could be headed for the National League Pitcher of the Year Award after an unforgettable 1898 season. The Royales pitching star won the league's Triple Crown, winning 22 games, striking out 157 and logging a 2.07 ERA -- all league bests.

"It's an honor," Foreman told BNN, "Not many pitchers can say they've won the Triple Crown. Usually the batting Triple Crown gets all the attention."

In his sensational year he registered 22 wins and 10 losses. An opposing manager said, "I can see how he won 22 games. What I don't understand is how he lost 10."
