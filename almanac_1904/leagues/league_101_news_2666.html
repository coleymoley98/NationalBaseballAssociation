National League Names Top Player
Wednesday, September 14th, 1898
James Mulligan, the talented Quebec first baseman, certainly made his name known among National League hurlers this season. He hammered them for 140 hits, 23 doubles, 2 triples, 10 home runs, 77 RBIs and scored 67 times. That kind of output made him the choice for National League Most Valuable Player Award for 1898.

He received 4 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Zachary Price of the Montreal Royales finished second in voting, while Prasarn Heemkhao of the Montreal Royales finished third.
