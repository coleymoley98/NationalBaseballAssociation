Best Player Named by Canadian League
Saturday, September 13th, 1902
James Papp took Canadian League by storm this year, and he did it all at the age of 25.

For his spectacular campaign, the Boston Redcoats star has been named the 1902 Canadian League Most Valuable Player Award recipient.

"To be considered amongst the best in the league is overwhelming," said the first baseman.

On the season he compiled a .339 batting average to go with a .374 on-base percentage. The native of Pittsburgh, The United Provinces also added 14 doubles, 3 triples, 4 home runs, 48 RBIs and 44 runs scored.

He received 3 first place votes out of a possible 4. Chongrak LaPorte of the Boston Redcoats finished second in voting with 1 first place vote, while Nick Jones of the Toronto Blue Sox finished third.
