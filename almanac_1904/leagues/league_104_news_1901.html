Paré and Stenner Fight, Suspended 8 Games
Saturday, June 5th, 1897
When fights happen, suspensions are not far behind. That axiom was held to be true today when New Britain's Justin Paré and Buffalo's Prakorb Stenner were given a temporary vacation from the game, courtesy of the league commissioner's office.

They both were suspended for 8 games for their actions in a recent ruckus between the two teams. Both benches cleared when Paré charged Stenner.

Both players have accepted their fate and will begin serving their suspensions immediately. Neither would talk to reporters about the confrontation.

Paré has a .294 average and a .400 on-base percentage for the year. He has picked up 5 hits, 2 home runs and driven in 8 runs.
