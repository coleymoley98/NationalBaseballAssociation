AJC Names Most Valuable Player Award Winner
Tuesday, August 4th, 1903
The future looks extremely bright for Rungarun Inglis. At the age of 19, many players are still trying to find their way in Anglo Junior Circuit.

Inglis has not only found his way, he's already established himself as one of the game's brightest stars, today being named the winner of the Anglo Junior Circuit Most Valuable Player Award.

"I didn't expect to hit .362 this year, that's for sure," the third baseman said.

The Quincy Juniors star also put up a .424 on-base percentage this year, with 68 hits, 11 doubles, 3 triples, 10 home runs, 32 RBIs and 47 runs scored.

He received 7 first place votes out of a possible 12. Scott Thériault of the Quincy Juniors finished second in voting with 5 first place votes, while Apichart Rousselle of the Fort Wayne Juniors finished third.
