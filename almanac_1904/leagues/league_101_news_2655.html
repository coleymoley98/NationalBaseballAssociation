Nordiques Triumph in 1898 Série nationale
Sunday, September 4th, 1898
A celebratory mood filled the air as the Quebec Nordiques closed out the season with a 6-2 victory over the Montreal Royales at Montreal Grounds to capture the Série nationale, 4-3.

Giving the Royales credit for a hard-fought series, Nordiques players said they believed throughout the postseason that their "destiny" was to be crowned National League champions.

"We felt like this was our year all season long," said left fielder Joseph Lynch, who was named series MVP. "It was our destiny to win it and we did."

It was the 3rd in franchise history. During the regular season Quebec put up a 57-51 record and finished second.

"They're the champions and they deserve it," said a dejected Montreal manager Charles Read. "We played good ball, they just played better than us."
