Hits Blitz for Juniors Bouchard
Friday, June 12th, 1896
It was a special day for Stéphan Bouchard at Juniors Ballpark -- the Fort Wayne Juniors left fielder hit for the cycle, getting every type of hit possible.

The 18-year-old Bouchard racked up 2 singles, 1 double, 1 triple and 1 home run to get his name in the history books. It was all for naught, however, as the Juniors didn't win the ballgame, dropping the decision to the Quincy Juniors, 15-13.

"He hit everything we threw," said Quincy manager Ethan Bax. "There was just no getting him out today."

For the game, he was 5-for-6 with 2 RBI and 5 runs scored.

"I'm glad for Stéphan," Fort Wayne manager Ace Dugandzic said. "It's a once-in-a-lifetime thing and a nice feather in his cap. Would've been better if we could have won the game for him, too."

Speaking to the press after the game, Bouchard said the key for his record-setting day was "just getting some good pitches to hit."

Stéphan Bouchard singled in the 1st, hit an RBI triple in the 2nd, singled in the 4th, hit a solo-shot off Rattana Thethinee in the 5th, flied out in the 7th and doubled in the 9th.

Bouchard is batting .340 this year with 1 home run, 6 RBIs and 12 runs scored. He has 18 hits in 53 at-bats.
