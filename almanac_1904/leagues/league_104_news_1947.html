Tuesday, June 22nd, 1897
For once, it was the Quincy Juniors picking up the slack for Xavier Forget.

The club's "offensive catalyst," as Quincy manager Ethan Bax has frequently called him, went uncharacteristically silent today and saw his 21-game hitting streak snapped by the Springfield Juniors. Forget was bailed out by the rest of the Juniors, however, who stopped the Juniors, 4-1.

The 21-year-old first baseman finished the day 0-for-4, and told reporters at the postgame press conference that "everybody did their job but me."

"Not that it was a huge problem -- we still won the game," he said with a grin. "The streak was a good run though. I'll try to start another one tomorrow."
