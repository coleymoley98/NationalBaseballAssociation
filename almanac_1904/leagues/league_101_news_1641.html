Série nationale to Detroit
Thursday, September 3rd, 1896
Sometimes the winner is the one who got the lucky bounces and the lucky calls, and sometimes the winner is just the one who played better.

It was the latter case in 1896, as the Detroit Lions topped the Quebec Nordiques 11-8 tonight at Ballpark of Quebec to win the Série nationale. It was the 1st title in franchise history.

"When you play for championships, you have to play better than the team in the other dugout. We did that in this series, and all year. Now we're the best team in baseball," said Detroit center fielder Bhumibol Bernard, who was named series MVP.

The Lions took the series 4-2. The new titleholders completed the regular season with a second-place finish in the and a 61-47 record.

Detroit manager Enzo Kelly grinned and nodded when asked to comment on Bernard's words.

"Bhumibol said it best -- we played better than the other team," he said.

Hometown officials announced they were already planning a "grand celebration" for the winners in the days to come.
