1905 National League Hall of Fame Inductees to be announced next week
Wednesday, November 2nd, 1904
It's likely to be a quiet year for the National League Hall of Fame. The 1905 voting results will be released soon, but it's not expected anyone will garner enough votes to gain enshrinement.

The top vote-getter is expected to be 1894 Most Valuable Player Award winner Charles Bergeron. He put together a 17-year career and made his mark as a member of the Chicagou Frontenacs. He retired in 1898 at the age of 42.

Voters are reminded that ballots may be cast up until the votes are announced on November 9th, 1904.

Bergeron's career batting average is .387. In 340 games, he has gotten 532 hits, 9 home runs, 245 RBIs and scored 282 runs.
