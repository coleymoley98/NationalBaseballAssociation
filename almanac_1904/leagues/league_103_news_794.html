FJC Announces #1 Player Award
Monday, May 6th, 1895
He may have less experience than most of his Francophone Junior Circuit peers, but that didn't slow Gordon Robillard down at all in 1895.

At the age of 16, the Gallant center fielder has been named the winner of the Francophone Junior Circuit Most Valuable Player Award.

He had a .406 batting average and a .461 on-base percentage this year. In 50 games he piled up 84 hits, 15 doubles, 3 triples, 6 home runs, 48 RBIs and 50 runs scored.

"I'm blown away by the season I was able to put together," he said. "I'm looking forward to trying to do it all again next year."

He received 9 first place votes out of a possible 12. Vince Watkins of the Sainte-Paul Juniors finished second in voting with 3 first place votes, while Ronny Corkery of the Quebec City Juniors finished third.
