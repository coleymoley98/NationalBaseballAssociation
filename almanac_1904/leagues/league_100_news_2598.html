Watson Sets New Single-Season Strikeout Record
Saturday, August 13th, 1898
Toronto hurler Adam Watson put his name in the league record book today at Canadian League Park I when he struck out his 120th batter of the year, thus erasing the previous single-season record set by Piyawat Konchok. The historic performance received a loud, standing ovation from the fans and both ball teams. At the press conference after the game was asked the secret of his strikeout success. Always good with an answer, Watson glibly replied, "I just rare back and throw it!" Strikeout victim number 120 was proud of his achievement, too. He told reporters, "Watson gave me no chance. He just blew it by me. But it's an honor. I'll have another paragraph in all the baseball books."

So far this year Watson has made 32 starts, tossed 276 innings, struck out 120 batters and walked 76. His record is 19-10 with a 2.35 ERA and he has limited opposing teams to a .249 batting average.
