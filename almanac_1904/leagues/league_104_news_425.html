Inglis Nabs AJC Most Valuable Player Award
Thursday, August 4th, 1904
The future looks extremely bright for Rungarun Inglis. At the age of 20, many players are still trying to find their way in Anglo Junior Circuit.

Inglis has not only found his way, he's already established himself as one of the game's brightest stars, today being named the winner of the Anglo Junior Circuit Most Valuable Player Award.

"I didn't expect to hit .399 this year, that's for sure," the third baseman said.

The Quincy Juniors star also put up a .528 on-base percentage this year, with 65 hits, 15 doubles, no triples, 8 home runs, 36 RBIs and 43 runs scored.

He received 10 first place votes out of a possible 12. Tulimak Raun of the New Britain Juniors finished second in voting, while Justin Fecteau of the New Britain Juniors finished third with 2 first place votes.
