Redcoats Win 1901 Canadian Series
Monday, September 2nd, 1901
It was a hard-fought battle for both teams to make it this far. But as the saying goes, "to the victors go the spoils." For the victorious Boston Redcoats, that meant the Canadian League Canadian Series trophy.

The Redcoats took a 10-7 victory over the New York Rebels at Polo Grounds, winning their 6th championship after taking the series 4-1.

"We've got grit," said Redcoats first baseman James Papp, who was named MVP of the series. "We know how to win when we have to."

The new titleholders completed the regular season with a first-place finish in the and a 61-47 record.
