Great Stroke: Graybeal Gets 5 Hits
Saturday, July 20th, 1895
Today at Ballpark of Quebec, Kantapol Graybeal put on an impressive display.

The Montreal Royales center fielder tagged Quebec pitching for 5 hits in 5 at-bats, but his teammates were unable to capitalize on the spree and lost the game to the Quebec Nordiques by the score of 7-5.

"The loss takes some of the joy out of it, but I'll take it," Graybeal said. "5-for-5 ain't bad in any league."

Despite the terrific performance, the Royales manager was unwilling to offer his center fielder much praise, saying he holds his "whole roster" accountable for losses.

"Kantapol told me 'Coach, I had 5 hits' and I said 'Well that's great, but it looks like we needed one more from you,'" manager Charles Read said.

Kantapol Graybeal singled in the 1st, singled in the 3rd, hit an RBI double in the 5th, hit an RBI triple in the 7th and singled in the 9th.

This season Graybeal is swinging the bat at a .311 pace. He has hit 3 home runs and scored 42 times, while driving in 24. His on-base percentage is .357 and he has played in 50 games.
