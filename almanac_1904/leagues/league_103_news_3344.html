Red-Hot Vézina Scorches Juniors
Friday, April 20th, 1900
After a 5-hit effort to lead Sainte-Paul's 16-4 victory over the Montreal Juniors, second baseman Newin Vézina told BNN beat writers it had been "a special day."

"It really doesn't matter whether it's sandlot ball, high school or college, two hits in a game is nice," he said. "Three or four is better, but five or more is out of sight. And to do that at the Francophone Junior Circuit-level is really something special."

The 17-year-old player earned his accolades today at Ballpark of Montreal with a spectacular 5-for-6 game against the Juniors.

"His swing is getting there," said Juniors skipper J.J. Aubut, who spoke glowingly of Vézina after the game. "We want him to be quick with his swing, short and compact. We want him to be patient and go the other way when he can. Newin did that to perfection today."

Newin Vézina singled in the 1st, tripled in the 3rd, singled in the 5th, hit a two-run home run off Haji Dubuc in the 7th, struck out in the 8th and singled in the 9th.

Currently Vézina is hitting .379 and has collected 7 home runs, 31 RBIs and scored 35 times, while playing in 36 games.
