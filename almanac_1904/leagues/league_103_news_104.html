Moore Finally Fails, Streak Ends at 28
Friday, April 15th, 1904
For once, it was the Coude Juniors picking up the slack for Bowie Moore.

The club's "offensive catalyst," as Coude manager Cole Siller has frequently called him, went uncharacteristically silent today and saw his 28-game hitting streak snapped by the Gallant Juniors. Moore was bailed out by the rest of the Juniors, however, who stopped the Juniors, 4-2.

The 17-year-old second baseman finished the day 0-for-4, and told reporters at the postgame press conference that "everybody did their job but me."

"Not that it was a huge problem -- we still won the game," he said with a grin. "The streak was a good run though. I'll try to start another one tomorrow."

Presently Moore is batting .404 with no home runs and 35 RBIs. He has played in 40 games and scored 37 times.
