Liles Goes Cycling
Saturday, June 12th, 1897
Daquein Liles did everything he could for the Canton Juniors today -- literally.

The 21-year-old right fielder hit a single, a double, a triple and a home run for the Juniors, going for the cycle and helping them get past the Toronto Juniors, 15-7, at Juniors Ballpark.

"Yeah, I was some kind of hitter in this game," Liles reflected later. "It was like all I had to do was close my eyes and swing the bat."

In a game headed for the record books, Liles banged out 5 hits in 5 at-bats and totaled 2 RBI and 4 runs scored. Speaking with the press about the feat, he attributed it to "a lot of luck."

"A triple is so hard to hit," he said. "To just decide to get one in a game when you need it for a cycle, it's impossible. Usually something weird has to happen, the ball kicking past an outfielder or somebody falling down. There's a ton of luck in hitting for a cycle."

Daquein Liles singled in the 1st, hit an RBI triple in the 3rd, singled in the 5th, doubled in the 7th and hit a solo-shot off Micah Farnborough in the 8th.

So far this season, Liles has recorded a .400 batting average with 20 hits, 1 home run, 11 RBIs, 14 runs scored and a .436 OBP.
