St-Onge Selected AJC's Top Player
Monday, August 4th, 1902
Sarawut St-Onge took Anglo Junior Circuit by storm this year, and he did it all at the age of 19.

For his spectacular campaign, the Pawtucket Juniors star has been named the 1902 Anglo Junior Circuit Most Valuable Player Award recipient.

"To be considered amongst the best in the league is overwhelming," said the right fielder.

On the season he compiled a .391 batting average to go with a .481 on-base percentage. The native of Pittsburgh, The United Provinces also added 11 doubles, no triples, 15 home runs, 57 RBIs and 40 runs scored.

He received 12 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Emrick Dagenais of the Fort Wayne Juniors finished second in voting, while T.J. Gent of the Pawtucket Juniors finished third.
