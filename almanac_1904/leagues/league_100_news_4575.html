Foreman Claims #1 Spot in Record Book
Monday, July 28th, 1902
The pursuit is over. Lee Foreman of the Boston Redcoats has broken the career home record of Daniel Verreault by hitting number 24. Even as pressure mounted and he inched closer to the hallowed record, Foreman tried to remain even-keel.

"I took it one home run at a time," he said. "Records have a way of taking care of themselves."

Lee Foreman singled in the 2nd and homered off Prayad Summers in the 4th.

This season Foreman has hit at a .238 clip with 20 RBIs, 3 home runs and 20 runs scored.
