Though he's a bit older than your typical rookie, 33-year-old Piroj Neal, a starter for Quebec, has proven that he can really pitch in the National League with this past month. For his strong effort, he was given the Rookie of the Month Award today.

In June Neal rang up a fine 4-6 record. He made 10 starts, logged 17 strikeouts in 80.1 innings, 3.25 ERA and only allowed opposing hitters to hit .242 against him.

Pretty fair pitching for the old rookie.

To date this season, Neal has registered 19 starts, 9-9 won-lost mark, 3.20 ERA, 157.2 IP, 35 strikeouts and 41 walks. Opposing teams have hit .244 against him.
