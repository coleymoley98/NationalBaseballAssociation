Great Day for Kitamoto -- 5 Hits
Tuesday, August 4th, 1903
The Montreal Royales stopped the Quebec Nordiques by a score of 7-6 at Ballpark of Quebec today, with the lion's share of the credit going to second baseman Edward Kitamoto. The 21-year-old had a career day, collecting 5 hits.

"Every batter wants to have a quality at-bat every time he steps up to the plate," Kitamoto said later. "He wants to hit the ball hard."

Kitamoto did just that in his 6 at-bats, much to the chagrin of the Nordiques.

"Not much that I can say," offered Quebec manager Frédéric Lévesque. "He just hammered the heck out of us."

Edward Kitamoto singled in the 1st, singled in the 2nd, singled in the 4th, flied out in the 6th, singled in the 9th and doubled in the 11th.

So far this year Kitamoto is batting .250 with no home runs.
