Tanner AJC's Top Player for 1898
Thursday, August 4th, 1898
At the age of 16, Perry Tanner of the Fort Wayne Juniors could very well have his best years ahead of him.

It's a startling thought when you consider the year he had in 1898 was good enough to win the right fielder the Anglo Junior Circuit Most Valuable Player Award.

"See the ball, hit the ball," he said. "That's my basic approach."

The top hitter put up a .440 batting average this year, as well as a .483 on-base percentage. He played 49 games and racked up 95 hits, 31 doubles, 3 triples, 7 home runs, 55 RBIs and 62 runs scored.

He received 8 first place votes out of a possible 12. Suriyakarn Bossicar of the Buffalo Juniors finished second in voting with 4 first place votes, while Von Rivera of the Canton Juniors finished third.
