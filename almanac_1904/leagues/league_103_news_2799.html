Pichette Hits in 20 Consecutive Games
Wednesday, March 22nd, 1899
The Quebec City Juniors may have proven to be mere mortals today as they lost 14-8 to the Gauthier Juniors, but Édouard Pichette continued to hit like a god.

The first baseman collected 3 hits in 5 at-bats in the loss and extended his hitting streak to 20 games.

"Pichette is playing great and is on fire right now," Juniors manager Leomar Durán said. "Hopefully his play can spark the rest of our team and help us pick up some wins."

Pichette was apparently not happy with the game and when pressed by reporters in the locker room only said "nothing else matters but the loss."
Édouard Pichette hit an RBI single in the 1st, grounded out in the 2nd, grounded out in the 5th, singled in the 7th and hit a two-run home run off Clap Youngblood in the 8th.

Pichette is batting at a .380 pace this season, picking up 35 hits in 92 at-bats with 4 home runs, 16 RBIs and 21 runs scored.
