Boston Blanked by Johnston
Wednesday, July 6th, 1898
The Boston Redcoats struggled at the plate today against Liberty hurler Jonathan Johnston, who shackled them on 2 hits while hurling a complete game 2-0 shutout.

"What can you say? We didn't hit well," said Redcoats skipper Herb Lamaison in his postgame comments. "Hopefully we shake this off and bounce back tomorrow."

Johnston finished the game with 4 strikeouts and 1 walk in an excellent mound performance.

"He pitched real well," Philadelphia manager Alex Bolduc said. "Jonathan made pitches when he had to. He hit his spots and did a great job of mixing in his changeup and using his fastball effectively."

On the season Johnston has compiled a 10-4 mark with a 1.70 ERA in 14 starts. In pitching 122 innings he has 45 strikeouts and 13 walks while limiting the opposition to a .229 batting average.
