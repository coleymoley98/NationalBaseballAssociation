Allard 'Lights Out' in Shutout Win
Thursday, August 21st, 1902
The Detroit Lions shut out the Montreal Royales 1-0 today behind the strong pitching of Komkrit Allard. Allard surrendered only 5 hits in recording the complete-game victory, and struck out 2.

Following the last out, Allard appeared to celebrate a little too boisterously for Montreal manager Charles Read, who could be seen scolding the famously outspoken right-hander as the clubs left the field. Allard's own manager then intervened with some words for his counterpart.

The Royales skipper would not comment on the exchange, but at the Lions press conference, Allard confirmed that it was regarding the celebration.

"He basically told me I was being a lousy winner," Allard said.

"And I told him he was being a lousy loser," Detroit manager Adrian Miramontes added.

This year Allard has worked 239 innings with 86 strikeouts en route to a 9-20 record and 3.54 ERA.
