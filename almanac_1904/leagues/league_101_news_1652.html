Josh Roberts Picked NL's Best Player
Sunday, September 13th, 1896
The manager of the Detroit Lions said he thinks Josh Roberts is only going to get better as he gets older.

He may only be 25, but it will be hard to top the left fielder's 1896 campaign, as he has been named the winner of the National League Most Valuable Player Award.

His winning campaign featured a .383 batting average and a .427 on-base percentage this year. In 97 games he piled up 153 hits, 19 doubles, 4 triples, 10 home runs, 61 RBIs and 78 runs scored.

He received 4 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Charles Bergeron of the Chicagou Frontenacs finished second in voting, while Sirisak Winter of the Detroit Lions finished third.
