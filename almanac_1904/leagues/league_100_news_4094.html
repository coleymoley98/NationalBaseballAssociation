Fontaine Sizzles in No-Hitter
Monday, August 5th, 1901
The no-hitter is one of baseball's truly egalitarian achievements. It's done by the great and the not-so-great alike, and for nearly all of them it represents the pinnacle of a career. Boston Redcoats pitcher Jocelyn Fontaine had his moment today at Exposition Park, holding the Philadelphia Liberty hitless and beating them, 9-2.

"Everything was working for him," Liberty manager Anisong Ilic said. "He had a lot of late movement that seemed to cause the guys trouble, especially from the fifth inning on. He was in command."

Fontaine rung up 3 strikeouts, issued 5 walks and faced only 36 hitters in his historic outing. After the game, he spoke to BNN reporters in the Boston locker room.

"I really got nervous in the last inning," Fontaine recalled. "I knew I had a chance at a no-hitter but it was hard to stay calm and concentrate. Thank goodness for my catcher. He told me he would do all the thinking and I could just concentrate on hitting his glove."

For the year Fontaine has a 10-4 record with a 2.22 ERA in 15 starts.
