Great Day for O'Dell, Hits for Cycle
It was a special day for Bobby O'Dell at Comiskey Park 1910 -- the New York Rebels left fielder hit for the cycle, getting every type of hit possible.

The 24-year-old O'Dell racked up 1 single, 1 double, 1 triple and 1 home run to get his name in the history books. It was all for naught, however, as the Rebels didn't win the ballgame, dropping the decision to the Chicagou White Sox, 6-4.

"He hit everything we threw," said Chicagou manager Tommy Dupuis. "There was just no getting him out today."

For the game, he was 4-for-5 with 2 runs batted in and 3 runs scored.

"I'm glad for Bobby," New York manager William Davison said. "It's a once-in-a-lifetime thing and a nice feather in his cap. Would've been better if we could have won the game for him, too."

Speaking to the press after the game, O'Dell said the key for his record-setting day was "just getting some good pitches to hit."

Bobby O'Dell tripled in the 1st, doubled in the 3rd, singled in the 6th, hit a two-run home run off Weerasak Morillon in the 8th and grounded out in the 9th.

This season O'Dell is hitting at a .277 pace with 3 home runs, 13 RBIs and has scored 23 times.
