Division Series Goes the Distance, Montreal Prevails
Members of the Montreal Royales couldn't hold back their emotions after beating the Quebec Nordiques in the final game of the Division Series.

"It's just been such a roller coaster," said Royales starting pitcher Larry Millette after the club's 2-0 win. "You play all season and then everything comes down to this one game. We know how easily we could have been on the other side of it."

The other side was a subdued Nordiques clubhouse, where players packed up their belongings and headed home for the year, with a somber mood in the air.

For the series, Millette was named MVP, as he had a 1.13 ERA over 16 innings pitched. He added 8 strikeouts and picked up 2 wins.

The Montreal Royales will play against the Chicagou Frontenacs in the next round. Chicagou won their series against the St. Louis Cardinaux 3 games to 1. The League Chamionship Series schedule will be announced after the Division Series finishes.
