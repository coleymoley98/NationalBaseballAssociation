Rainiers End Kitano's Streak
Tuesday, June 16th, 1925
It was the best of both worlds for the Seattle Rainiers today at Ballpark of Seattle, as they defeated the Edmonton Eskimos 14-6 and also snapped Akihiro Kitano's 21-game hitting streak. The 23-year-old Edmonton center fielder failed to get a hit in 5 at-bats.

"Nothing personal against Kitano, we just wanted to win the game," said Rainiers skipper Sebastian Woodhall. "We did a great job keeping him off-balance and guessing."

When asked by reporters about his streak coming to an end, Kitano frowned and said, "I won't lie and say that I'm not disappointed about it, but I'd feel much better if we had won."

This year Kitano is hitting .424, produced 9 RBIs and 1 home runs, scored 15 times and gotten on base at a .472 rate.
