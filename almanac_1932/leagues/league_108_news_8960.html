Boudreau Rips Calgary for 5 Hits
Tuesday, August 16th, 1927
The Saskatoon Coyotes stopped the Calgary Wranglers by a score of 4-3 at Coyotes Ballpark today, with the lion's share of the credit going to third baseman Arnaud Boudreau. The 19-year-old had a career day, collecting 5 hits.

"Every batter wants to have a quality at-bat every time he steps up to the plate," Boudreau said later. "He wants to hit the ball hard."

Boudreau did just that in his 5 at-bats, much to the chagrin of the Wranglers.

"Not much that I can say," offered Calgary manager Eric Fitzsimmons. "He just hammered the heck out of us."

Arnaud Boudreau tripled in the 1st, hit a solo-shot off Cédric Duchesneau in the 4th, singled in the 6th, walked in the 7th, singled in the 10th and singled in the 12th.

For the season Boudreau has hit .383 with 9 home runs and 68 RBIs. He has played in 67 games and scored 53 times.
