Lobdell Selected ABL's Best Player
Wednesday, August 20th, 1924
The manager of the Montreal Jr. Royales said he thinks Beau Lobdell is only going to get better as he gets older.

He may only be 17, but it will be hard to top the second baseman's 1924 campaign, as he has been named the winner of the Atlantic Baseball League ABL Player of the Year.

His winning campaign featured a .347 batting average and a .442 on-base percentage this year. In 44 games he piled up 58 hits, 14 doubles, 4 triples, 9 home runs, 31 RBIs and 41 runs scored.

He received 11 first place votes out of a possible 14. Surapong St-Onge of the Quebec City Jr. Nordiques finished second in voting with 2 first place votes, while Thaiwat Jinta of the Quebec City Jr. Nordiques finished third with 1 first place vote.
