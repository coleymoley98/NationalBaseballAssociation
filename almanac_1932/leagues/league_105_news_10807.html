Ratanaveroj Struts Stuff in Win Over Juneau
Friday, June 7th, 1929
All went well for Giovanni Ratanaveroj of the Quebec Nordiques as he pitched his team to a 2-0 shutout victory over the Juneau Brasseurs, limiting them to 7 hits. In the postgame interview, both managers had glowing things to say about Ratanaveroj on the day.

"Giovanni's got a good selection of pitches he can throw at a guy," said Nordiques manager Donatien Auger. "He changes speed, gives different looks to different hitters. When he's on his game, he's quite a handful."

In the contest, the Quebec right-hander rang up 2 and surrendered 2 walks.

"He didn't give us any second chances," said Brasseurs manager Joshua Barry. "It's a guessing game with him and today we were always guessing wrong."

To date this season Ratanaveroj sports a 2-4 mark with a 4.82 ERA in 7 starting assignments. He has held the opposition to a .322 batting average and has 14 strikeouts in 52.1 innings.
