Morales Sets Season Mark with Hit #169
Friday, September 12th, 1924
An amazing season for Carmen Morales reached its climax today, as the Boston Redcoats's shortstop set the league record for most hits in a single season with 169, passing the great Christophe Perras. Fans at Fenway Park 1912 gave him a 5-minute standing ovation after the historic hit.

Carmen Morales walked in the 1st, grounded out in the 3rd, flied out in the 5th and singled in the 7th.

Morales is hitting .400 for the season with 5 home runs, 69 RBIs and 81 runs scored. He has 169 hits in 423 at-bats and played in 108 games.
