Cardin Goes on Rampage Against Giants
Thursday, July 12th, 1928
It's not something you read about in the sports pages every day -- a 5-for-5 day. For many, it might come around only once in a career -- and for James Cardin, it came around today.

The 22-year-old left fielder scattered hits all over Oracle Park to pace the Winnipeg Thunder to a 15-6 victory over the Vancouver Giants.

Vancouver skipper Tony Green told reporters in the press room that "Cardin deserves a hat-tip."

"He hit some good pitches against us," he said. "In fact, according to our scouting reports, Cardin hit several that he is not supposed to be able to hit."

James Cardin hit a sac fly in the 1st, hit an RBI double in the 3rd, hit an RBI single in the 4th, hit an RBI double in the 6th, singled in the 7th and hit an RBI single in the 8th.

Presently Cardin is batting .300 with 45 hits and 5 home runs. He has knocked in 25 runs and scored 25 times.
