Injury Forces Lambert Out of Baseball
Wednesday, May 8th, 1929
The day has come for Rudy Lambert to call it a career. The Boise starting pitcher, because of a partially torn ulnar collateral ligament, has been out of action for a while. Now, he has decided to retire from baseball. He made it official in a brief appearance to reporters. "I was told I've got an outside shot at playing again," he said. "But I've decided to pursue other options at this time." Best of luck to Lambert. The team will surely miss him.
