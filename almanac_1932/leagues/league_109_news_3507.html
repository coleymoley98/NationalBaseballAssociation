King of the Hill Announced by Eastern League
Sunday, September 18th, 1932
Mel Berman is the latest Eastern League Pitcher of the Year Award winner. The Kingston starter really shone on the hill time and again and finished 1932 with a sparkling 12-3 record.

In 17 starts, he fanned 78, walked 48 in 149.1 innings and posted a 3.92 ERA. Berman held opposing hitters to a .284 batting average.

He received 7 first place votes out of a possible 10. Joe Paquin of the Trois-Rivieres Royales finished second in voting with 3 first place votes, while Kittichai Kejmanee of the Providence Redcoats finished third.
