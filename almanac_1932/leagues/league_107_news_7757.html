Best Season In WBL Belongs To Byrd
Sunday, August 15th, 1926
At the age of 18, William Byrd of the Boise Broncos could very well have his best years ahead of him.

It's a startling thought when you consider the year he had in 1926 was good enough to win the right fielder the Western Baseball League WBL Player of the Year.

"See the ball, hit the ball," he said. "That's my basic approach."

The top hitter put up a .337 batting average this year, as well as a .447 on-base percentage. He played 46 games and racked up 56 hits, 12 doubles, 2 triples, 6 home runs, 38 RBIs and 44 runs scored.

He received 5 first place votes out of a possible 8. J.J. Chevallier of the Boise Broncos finished second in voting with 3 first place votes, while Russ Gunning of the Boise Broncos finished third.
