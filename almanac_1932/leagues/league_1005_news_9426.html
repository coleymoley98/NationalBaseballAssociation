Team Ace Takes Best Pitcher
Friday, March 30th, 1928
Tony Doolin had a marvelous 1928 season. The Team mound mainstay garnered the most votes and has been named the CJBC Best Pitcher honoree.

The Ontario star dominated his opposition, finishing with 3 wins, no losses and a 1.66 ERA. In 4 starts he worked a total of 21.2 innings, striking out 15, while walking 3. Other clubs managed to hit only .286 against him.

He received 16 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Stephen St. Hill of the Team Pennsylvania finished second in voting, while Jon Craft of the Team Montana finished third.
