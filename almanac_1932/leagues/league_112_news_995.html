ABL's Top Pitcher is Carrier
Thursday, August 21st, 1930
He's in the early phase of his career, but this past season William Carrier performed like a veteran on the hill for the Pointe-Sainte-Anne Acadiens and walked away with the Atlantic Baseball League ABL Pitcher of the Year in the process.

The 17-year-old Acadiens star fashioned a 7-1 won-loss record in 1930 with a 1.48 ERA. In 73 innings of work and 10 starts, Carrier struck out 95 while giving up 53 hits and 21 walks. He held opposing batters to a .198 average.

He received 14 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Steve Lowenstein of the Quebec City Jr. Nordiques finished second in voting, while Val Ransom of the Quebec City Jr. Nordiques finished third.
