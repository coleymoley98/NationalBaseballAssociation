League Chamionship Series Goes the Distance, Montreal Prevails
Tuesday, September 23rd, 1930
The joy and relief on the faces of the Montreal Royales players was obvious as they celebrated their League Chamionship Series win over the Chicagou Frontenacs.

"In a series like this, the smallest thing can make the difference in how it turns out," said Montreal right fielder Orber Teran, shortly after the 3-2 win that clinched the series 4-3. "We're happy we did what it takes to get it done."

The team is now off to the Riel Cup.

Teran won series MVP honors on the strength of his .464 batting average during the series. He also had a .500 on-base percentage and 1 home runs, drove in 5 RBI and scored 5 runs.

The Montreal Royales will play against the Wolfeburg Celtics in the next round. Wolfeburg won their series against the Boston Redcoats 4 games to 3. The Riel Cup schedule will be announced after the League Chamionship Series finishes.
