Bruins Win Tight Atlantic Race
Saturday, July 26th, 1930
For the 9th time in franchise history, the Providence Bruins are champions of the Atlantic.

But it was no easy task, as the Bruins battled until the season's final day in order to capture the title.

"It makes it worth all the effort to pull it out this late," said starting pitcher Sidney Gaudet. "It's hard to describe the feeling, but needless to say we're all feeling pretty good."

The team is tops in the division with a 27-24 record and the club will be looking for the 6th Atlantic Baseball League championship in franchise history.
