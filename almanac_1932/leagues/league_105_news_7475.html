Saksit Hill Wins Number 150
Friday, July 9th, 1926
Saksit Hill of the Philadelphia Liberty will go down as one of the league's legendary pitchers after his 5-2 win today against the Boston Redcoats.

"It felt like it was a postseason game, one out at a time," said his manager. "It was something special to see."

The 35-year-old Hill picked up his 150th career victory at Baker Bowl 1910. He hurled 9 innings, gave up 9 hits and registered 1 strikeout along with 3 walks.

"I've worked a long time for this," Hill said. "I can't believe it's really here. It feels great!"

Through the years Hill has fashioned a 150-132 mark, 3.35 ERA and thrown 2575 innings. Opposing clubs are batting .268 lifetime against him and he has logged 882 career strikeouts.
