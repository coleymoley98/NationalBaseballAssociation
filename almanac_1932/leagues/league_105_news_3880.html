Surabordee Yodyingyong Bans Poker from Clubhouse
Sunday, June 17th, 1923
Surabordee Yodyingyong said he has banned poker games from taking place in the St. Louis Cardinaux clubhouse, as he feels it's too big a distraction for his team.

"We have guys talking about straights and flushes when they should be preparing for baseball," he said. "They can play cards on their own time, but when they are in the clubhouse they are working for us."

The news wasn't overly popular with some of the team's more outspoken players.

"We need some downtime and it's a great way to bond as a team," said Michael Hopkins. "I think this is a mistake."

Hawk Traynor added that he does agree that preparation for games is important, but he didn't see a big deal with an occasional card game.

"Banning something the guys enjoy isn't going to go over too well," he said.

Yodyingyong said any players caught playing poker will be issued fines.
