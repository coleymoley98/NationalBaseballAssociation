Injury to Séguin A Career-Ender
Monday, June 29th, 1925
This is not how Wasan Séguin wanted his career to end -- with an injury. He wanted to go out on his terms. Instead, a torn ulnar collateral ligament has forced him to retire from baseball. The Sherbrooke starting pitcher plans to meet with the media later this afternoon. In the meantime, he released a brief statement, in which he acknowledged the support of "the organization, the training staff, my teammates, and all the fans."

To date Séguin is 3-0 in 7 starts with a 2.24 ERA and has 55 strikeouts in 52.1 innings.
