Guzmán Socks it to Celtics, Cracks 5 Hits
Wednesday, June 3rd, 1931
Rivaldo Guzmán put on a hitting clinic today as the Philadelphia Liberty dumped the Wolfeburg Celtics 15-5 at Baker Bowl 1910.

The Philadelphia shortstop made Wolfeburg defenders earn their pay, lashing Wolfeburg pitching for 5 hits in 5 at-bats in the game. He told BNN reporters after the game that his strategy was "just hit it where they pitched it."

"Too often I try too hard to pull the ball," Guzmán said in his postgame press conference. "Today, I just went with the pitch."

Rivaldo Guzmán singled in the 2nd, hit a two-run double in the 3rd, singled in the 4th, hit an RBI single in the 6th and hit a two-run home run off Ambroise Thériault in the 8th.

Guzmán is currently hitting at a .344 pace with 2 home runs, 17 RBIs and has scored 19 times.
