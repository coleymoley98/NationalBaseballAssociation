Canadiens Come up Short of Playoffs
Sunday, July 20th, 1930
The bid by the Sherbrooke Canadiens for a postseason berth came to a close today. The club has been mathematically eliminated from the 1930 playoffs, and team manager C.J. Chambers said players on the team "are taking it hard."

The Canadiens have missed the playoffs 35 times in their 36 years as a Atlantic Baseball League franchise. This playoff miss is the club's 8th in a row, with their last postseason appearance coming in 1921. In the last 10 seasons, the Canadiens have missed the playoffs 9 times. Sherbrooke has won the Laurier Cup 2 times in their history.
