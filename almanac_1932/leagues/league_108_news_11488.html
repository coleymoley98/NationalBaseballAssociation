Seattle Triumphs in World Series
Tuesday, September 10th, 1929
A celebratory mood filled the air as the Seattle Rainiers closed out the season with a 4-3 victory over the Saskatoon Coyotes at Ballpark of Seattle to capture the World Series, 4-3.

Giving the Coyotes credit for a hard-fought series, Rainiers players said they believed throughout the postseason that their "destiny" was to be crowned Western League champions.

"We felt like this was our year all season long," said left fielder Lou Lepore, who was named series MVP. "It was our destiny to win it and we did."

It was the 1st in franchise history. During the regular season Seattle put up a 50-32 record and finished first.

"They're the champions and they deserve it," said a dejected Saskatoon manager Maurice Wigglesworth. "We played good ball, they just played better than us."
