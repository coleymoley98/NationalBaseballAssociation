Week's Top Player: Montreal's Lachance
Monday, June 30th, 1924
It can be difficult for a young player to step out of the shadows and assert himself in the National League, but last week Adam Lachance of the Montreal Royales reminded everyone just why he's here as he threw up numbers that made him an easy selection for Player of the Week. Lachance rocked opposing pitching for a .464 average (13-for-28), 3 home runs and 12 RBIs to come away with the award.

This season Lachance is swinging the bat at a .363 rate with 62 hits in 171 at-bats. To date he has hit 6 home runs, scored 33 runs and driven in 26.
