Welsh Suffers Heatstroke
Sunday, February 24th, 1924
Lee Welsh of the Minnesota Saints was hospitalized earlier today after suffering what doctors described as mild heatstroke.

Welsh had reportedly been working outdoors at his offseason home where the temperature was over 100 degrees Fahrenheit. According to Saints manager A.J. Provost, who spoke with Welsh by phone, the right fielder was in good condition and will probably be released from the hospital tomorrow.

"He just sort of overdid it a little bit," said Provost. "He's already feeling a lot better."

Team doctor Phiphatpon Lyon says it's extremely unlikely that Welsh will suffer any lingering effects and should be fully recovered in time for spring training.
