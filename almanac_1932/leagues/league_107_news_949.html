WBL Names WBL Player of the Year Winner
Tuesday, August 12th, 1930
The manager of the Vancouver Giants said he thinks Mike Cordaro is only going to get better as he gets older.

He may only be 18, but it will be hard to top the right fielder's 1930 campaign, as he has been named the winner of the Western Baseball League WBL Player of the Year.

His winning campaign featured a .406 batting average and a .438 on-base percentage this year. In 48 games he piled up 80 hits, 13 doubles, 5 triples, 8 home runs, 42 RBIs and 41 runs scored.

He received 8 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Leandro González of the Abbotsford Alpines finished second in voting, while Burt O'Neill of the Vancouver Giants finished third.
