Drouin Pitches Cardinaux to Shutout Victory
Monday, August 11th, 1930
Dominic Drouin was in a mood to talk following the St. Louis Cardinaux contest against the Minnesota Saints, expounding to BNN reporters on his pitching philosophy. He had good reason to be excitable, having tossed a complete game shutout of the Saints, 2-0.

"There are three important elements in pitching success: location, location, and location," Drouin said. "I was hitting my spots time and time again."

The right-hander blanked Minnesota on a 2-hitter, allowing 1 walk and racking up 2 strikeouts.

Thus far this season, Drouin has worked 149.2 innings with 64 strikeouts en route to a 10-3 record and a 3.31 ERA.
