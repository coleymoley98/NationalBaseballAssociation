Taradin Boulay Wins NL Batting Title
Monday, September 12th, 1927
At an age where many of his peers are still trying to establish themselves in National Baseball Association, Taradin Boulay of the Minnesota Saints stepped up to win a National League batting title, with a .350 mark.

On the season, he compiled 135 hits, 27 doubles, 1 triple and 14 home runs. In 101 games Boulay totaled 75 RBIs and scored 65 runs.
