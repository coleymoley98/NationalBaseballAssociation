Minney and Ribeiro Suspended 5 Games
Monday, June 22nd, 1931
You would have thought you were at a hockey game yesterday at Eskimos Ballpark. A free-for-all broke out when Phillip Minney of Spokane and Abraham Ribeiro of Abbotsford rushed one another on the field and had to be restrained by umpires and teammates. Few blows landed, but it was enough for them to be ejected from the game.

Today both teams received notification from the league office that the two players would be suspended for 5 games.

So far this season Minney is batting .286 with 2 home runs, 10 RBIs and 11 runs scored. He has 20 hits in 70 at-bats, 12 walks and a .425 OBP. Minney has played in 19 games.
