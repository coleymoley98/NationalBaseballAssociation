#1 Player Named by Atlantic Baseball League
Friday, August 19th, 1932
Jeremy Cabana took Atlantic Baseball League by storm this year, and he did it all at the age of 17.

For his spectacular campaign, the Hartford Whalers star has been named the 1932 Atlantic Baseball League ABL Player of the Year recipient.

"To be considered amongst the best in the league is overwhelming," said the third baseman.

On the season he compiled a .360 batting average to go with a .442 on-base percentage. The native of Montréal, The United Provinces also added 16 doubles, no triples, 16 home runs, 51 RBIs and 48 runs scored.

He received 11 first place votes out of a possible 14. Nat Falardeau of the Providence Bruins finished second in voting with 3 first place votes, while Anthony Michaud of the Pointe-Sainte-Anne Acadiens finished third.
