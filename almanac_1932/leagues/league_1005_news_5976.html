Boulay Selected CJBC's #1 Player
Monday, March 30th, 1925
At the age of 19, Taradin Boulay of the Team Columbia could very well have his best years ahead of him.

It's a startling thought when you consider the year he had in 1925 was good enough to win the first baseman the Canadian Junior Baseball Championship Most Valuable Player Award.

"See the ball, hit the ball," he said. "That's my basic approach."

The top hitter put up a .528 batting average this year, as well as a .622 on-base percentage. He played 17 games and racked up 38 hits, 9 doubles, no triples, 1 home run, 24 RBIs and 31 runs scored.

He received 10 first place votes out of a possible 16. Basil Betts of the Team Columbia finished second in voting with 5 first place votes, while Somjit Fanchaiwang of the Team Columbia finished third.
