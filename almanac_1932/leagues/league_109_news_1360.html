Blue Sox Boudreau Stops Braves
Monday, June 1st, 1931
The Tecumseh Braves struggled at the plate today against Blue Sox hurler Roméo Boudreau, who shackled them on 2 hits while hurling a complete game 5-0 shutout.

"What can you say? We didn't hit well," said Braves skipper Calvert DeShon in his postgame comments. "Hopefully we shake this off and bounce back tomorrow."

Boudreau finished the game with 5 strikeouts and 4 walks in an excellent mound performance.

"He pitched real well," Brampton manager Nelson Méthot said. "Roméo made pitches when he had to. He hit his spots and did a great job of mixing in his changeup and using his fastball effectively."
