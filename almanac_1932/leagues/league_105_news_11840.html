Fortier Gets Award for Relief Work
Monday, October 13th, 1930
At the young age of only 24, Logan Fortier has been named the National League Reliever of the Year Award winner for 1930.

The future appears bright for the Juneau Brasseurs relief ace, who captured the award with 4 saves in 30 relief appearances, 24 strikeouts in 37.2 innings and a record of 3-6 to go with his 3.11 ERA.

He received 19 first place votes out of a possible 20. Hafit Duangsawang of the Detroit Lions finished second in voting with 1 first place vote, while Taylor Ratel of the St. Louis Cardinaux finished third.
