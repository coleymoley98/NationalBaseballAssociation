Juneau Narrowly Wins West
Wednesday, July 24th, 1929
The Juneau Jr. Brasseurs have captured the West for the 9th time.

In what was a tight race into the season's final days, the Jr. Brasseurs have come out on top and will be heading to the postseason full of confidence.

"We feel good. The West is full of great teams and we were pushed all season long," said starting pitcher Nathan Lin. "People can argue who they think is going to win the Adams Cup, but there's a lot of belief in this room."

A championship would be the 4th in franchise history.
