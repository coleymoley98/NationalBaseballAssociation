Laporte Caps Memorable Season with EL Most Valuable Player Award Honor
Saturday, September 22nd, 1923
He's only 21 years old, but Ray Laporte of the Pittsburgh Liberty has clearly established himself as a star of Eastern League.

His status grew today as the ace pitcher was named the winner of the 1923 Eastern League Most Valuable Player Award.

"I just want to pitch well and win games," he said. "The awards are nice though."

Laporte's award-winning season saw him put up a 13-4 record through 20 starts, throwing 142.2 innings. He had 117 strikeouts with 33 walks and a 2.59 ERA. Opponents hit only .213 against him during the season.

He received 4 first place votes out of a possible 8. Victor Yoshida of the Pittsburgh Liberty finished second in voting with 1 first place vote, while Jason Pelletier of the Providence Redcoats finished third with 2 first place votes.
