Voyageurs Clinch Wild Card
Tuesday, July 26th, 1932
The Coude Voyageurs may not go on to win a Laurier Cup, but the team celebrated like they had won a championship tonight after securing a wild card spot.

"We were definitely feeling the pressure. To go so late into the season and not know whether you're going to make the playoffs or not, it's intense. That's why you're seeing such an excited reaction," said starting pitcher Abe Nye. "All our guys I think are just relieved that we made it after all that."

It did indeed take a tough battle and with their record at 29-22, the Voyageurs will now look to bring the 6th Laurier Cup championship to Coude.
