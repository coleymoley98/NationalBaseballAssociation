NL Awards Player of the Week Trophy
Monday, August 17th, 1931
Renato Joly drew on his youthful energy and pitching skill to win National League Player of the Week honors last week.

The St. Louis Cardinaux pitcher registered 2 wins and no losses, 0.00 ERA and 9 strikeouts in 18 innings to sew up the award. The 23-year-old also held opposing hitters to 4 hits and a .074 batting average.

Joly has made 20 starts, tossed 142.2 innings, struck out 86 batters and walked 39. His record is 8-6 with a 3.79 ERA and he has limited other teams to a .269 batting average.
