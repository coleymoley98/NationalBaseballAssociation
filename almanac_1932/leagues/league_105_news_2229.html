Monday, September 21st, 1931
It's a rare event when a player hits three homers in a game and rarer still when he does it and loses.

It happened at League Park today, as Sidney Boyer of the Boston Redcoats unloaded three times against the Wolfeburg Celtics but was still bumped off 14-13 by the Celtics.

"We were lucky to win today," said Wolfeburg skipper Chaichan Phonsa. "You can't give up that many home runs and expect to win on a regular basis."

For the game, the Boston first baseman had 3 hits in 3 at-bats, scored 3 times, and drove in 5.

Boyer spoke to the writers in the postgame press room with mixed feelings, saying he was sad to lose but happy to be seeing the ball well.

"I was just trying to swing early and often to give myself a chance," he said. "I let the pitcher supply all the power and try to figure out how to get the bat on the ball."
