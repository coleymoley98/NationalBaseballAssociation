Lalancette Big Deal in Pointe-Sainte-Anne: Hits for Cycle
Thursday, June 20th, 1929
Somporn Lalancette was jubilant as the Pointe-Sainte-Anne Acadiens left the field with a 7-3 win over the Quincy Gunners today. He had every right to be.

The 18-year-old first baseman hit for the cycle at Juniors Ballpark, had a 5-for-5 game and chalked up 2 runs batted in and 2 runs to boot.

"It's too bad I can't do that every day," Lalancette said with a smile to reporters after the game. "It would do wonders for my batting average."

Pointe-Sainte-Anne manager Rudy Seguro said he was "proud" of his first baseman's feat, but was not expecting it to happen.

"Cycles are tough because triples are tough," he said. "As soon as the ball left the bat on that triple, guys were jumping off the bench for him. Honestly, I wasn't sure he was going to be able to leg it out."

Somporn Lalancette hit a solo-shot off Finley Page in the 1st, singled in the 3rd, doubled in the 5th, hit an RBI triple in the 6th and singled in the 8th.

Lalancette is batting .416 this year with 1 home run, 14 RBIs and 11 runs scored. He has 32 hits in 77 at-bats.
