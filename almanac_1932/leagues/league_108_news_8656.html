3 Boomers for Tremblay
Saturday, July 9th, 1927
Watcharapol Tremblay was sending souvenirs to the sun gods all game long in the 26-4 Calgary Wranglers triumph at Oracle Park today.

The 19-year-old put on a show for fans, launching not one, not two, but three home runs into the stands and powering the Wranglers past the Vancouver Giants.

"One homer's good, two's great, three's out of sight," Tremblay told BNN reporters after the game. "I can't wait until next game. I really feel good at the plate."

The left fielder went 3-for-5, rung up 6 RBIs and scored 4 runs on his record-setting day.

Watcharapol Tremblay struck out in the 1st, hit a two-run home run off Dean Pool in the 3rd, hit a solo-shot off Nigel Adler in the 3rd, walked in the 4th, walked in the 6th, hit a three-run home run off Guilhermino Anzaldua in the 7th and struck out in the 9th.

Posting a .339 batting average to date, Tremblay has 6 home runs and 15 RBIs.
