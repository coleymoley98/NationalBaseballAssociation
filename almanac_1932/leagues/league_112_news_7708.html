Whalers Prevail, as Division Finals Goes to the Wire
Wednesday, August 4th, 1926
The joy and relief on the faces of the Hartford Whalers players was obvious as they celebrated their Division Finals win over the Quebec City Jr. Nordiques.

"In a series like this, the smallest thing can make the difference in how it turns out," said Hartford left fielder Ryan Shin, shortly after the 9-2 win that clinched the series 4-3. "We're happy we did what it takes to get it done."

The team is now off to the Laurier Cup.

Shin won series MVP honors on the strength of his .500 batting average during the series. He also had a .548 on-base percentage and 4 home runs, drove in 8 RBI and scored 9 runs.

The Hartford Whalers will play against the Coude Voyageurs in the next round. Coude won their series against the St. John's Mariners 4 games to 2. The Laurier Cup schedule will be announced after the Division Finals finishes.
