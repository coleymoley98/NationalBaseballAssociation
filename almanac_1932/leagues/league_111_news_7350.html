Black Sox Plugged by Berger, Jr. Brasseurs, 3-0
Friday, June 25th, 1926
The Juneau Jr. Brasseurs shut out the Gallant Black Sox 3-0 today behind the strong pitching of Vince Berger. Berger surrendered only 3 hits in recording the complete-game victory, and struck out 12.

Following the last out, Berger appeared to celebrate a little too boisterously for Gallant manager Sutee Leblanc, who could be seen scolding the famously outspoken left-hander as the clubs left the field. Berger's own manager then intervened with some words for his counterpart.

The Black Sox skipper would not comment on the exchange, but at the Jr. Brasseurs press conference, Berger confirmed that it was regarding the celebration.

"He basically told me I was being a lousy winner," Berger said.

"And I told him he was being a lousy loser," Juneau manager Von Rivera added.

This year Berger has fashioned a 2-3 mark, 4.33 ERA and pitched 35.1 innings. Opposing clubs are batting .248 against him and he has logged 42 strikeouts.
