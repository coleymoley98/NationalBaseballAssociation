Atlantic Baseball League Pitcher of the Year Named
Saturday, August 18th, 1928
The Atlantic Baseball League made the official announcement today. The 1928 Atlantic Baseball League ABL Pitcher of the Year has gone to Steve Lowenstein of Quebec City.

He had a superlative season, chalking up a 11-4 won-lost mark with an impressive 1.84 ERA.

Lowenstein logged 17 starts, threw 132.1 innings, yielded 108 hits, struck out 143 and held the opposition to a .214 batting average.

He received 14 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Cédric Bruneau of the Pointe-Sainte-Anne Acadiens finished second in voting, while Ashley Matcham of the Montreal Jr. Royales finished third.
