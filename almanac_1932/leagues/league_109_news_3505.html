EL Best Skipper, Payne of Columbus
Saturday, September 17th, 1932
Manager Tyler Payne was speaking before a Columbus fan luncheon when word came down that he'd been awarded the Eastern League Manager of the Year. Payne's face turned to surprise as the club's owner made the hasty announcement.

"Wow... how lucky can a guy be? Manage a great bunch of guys... have great fans... have a great season... and now this... life don't get any better than this."

Columbus fans applauded, recognizing how fortunate they were to have Payne at the helm. Both fans and players in attendance toasted their skipper as they truly enjoyed the day.

1932 was an extra-special season for Payne as he piloted the Columbus Mets to a 46-36 record and a second place spot in the standings. A record good enough to win the 1932 Manager of the Year Award.
