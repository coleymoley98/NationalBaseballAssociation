CJBC Most Valuable Player Award Goes to Doolin
Friday, March 30th, 1928
Not only has Tony Doolin been named winner of the 1928 Canadian Junior Baseball Championship Most Valuable Player Award, he's done it at an age when many of his peers are still trying to establish themselves in Canadian Junior Baseball Championship.

"I feel fortunate to win this award at age 21," he said. "I feel like I still have my best years ahead of me. Hopefully I can put my name on this trophy a few more times."

The Team Ontario ace had a 3-0 record over 4 starts and 21.2 innings. He struck out 15, issued 3 walks and had an impressive 1.66 ERA.

Batters hit only .286 against him during the season.

He received 12 first place votes out of a possible 16. Stephen St. Hill of the Team Pennsylvania finished second in voting, while Dean Ashcroft of the Team Erie finished third with 4 first place votes.
