Towers Hits 3 Homers Against Trois-Rivieres
Saturday, July 23rd, 1927
Carlton Towers brutalized the Trois-Rivieres Royales pitching staff, launching three home runs in today's 9-1 victory at Rebels Stadium. The Albany Rebels first baseman was red-hot, the ball just exploding off his bat all day.

"I don't try to hit homers," he said later. "I try to go out there and put a good swing on the ball, and sometimes it is going to go out of the park. I didn't try to hit the first one or the second one or the third one."

For the game, he went 3-for-4 with 6 RBIs and 3 runs scored.

"Too much Carlton Towers today," Royales manager Jeremy Anglin said. "That's my assessment of the game. That's what our problems boiled down to."

Carlton Towers struck out in the 1st, hit a three-run home run off Elih Rivas in the 3rd, hit a solo-shot off Elih Rivas in the 5th and hit a two-run home run off Monty Walden in the 6th.

This season in 47 games Towers is batting .319 with 12 home runs, 37 RBIs and has scored 36 times.
