Liberty Top Royales
Friday, September 9th, 1927
It was a hard-fought battle for both teams to make it this far. But as the saying goes, "to the victors go the spoils." For the victorious Pittsburgh Liberty, that meant the Eastern League World Series trophy.

The Liberty took a 7-4 victory over the Trois-Rivieres Royales at Liberty Ballpark, winning their 4th championship after taking the series 4-2.

"We've got grit," said Liberty catcher Shane Haro, who was named MVP of the series. "We know how to win when we have to."

The new titleholders completed the regular season with a first-place finish in the and a 49-33 record.
