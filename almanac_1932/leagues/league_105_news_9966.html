Big Day with Bat: Hubbard 5-for-5
Wednesday, July 25th, 1928
Today at League Park, Jamad Hubbard put on an impressive display.

The Wolfeburg Celtics shortstop tagged Toronto pitching for 5 hits in 5 at-bats, but his teammates were unable to capitalize on the spree and lost the game to the Toronto Blue Sox by the score of 12-9.

"The loss takes some of the joy out of it, but I'll take it," Hubbard said. "5-for-5 ain't bad in any league."

Despite the terrific performance, the Celtics manager was unwilling to offer his shortstop much praise, saying he holds his "whole roster" accountable for losses.

"Jamad told me 'Coach, I had 5 hits' and I said 'Well that's great, but it looks like we needed one more from you,'" manager A.J. Desjardins said.

Jamad Hubbard singled in the 1st, hit an RBI double in the 3rd, tripled in the 4th, hit an RBI single in the 6th and hit a two-run single in the 8th.

Presently Hubbard is hitting .545 with no home runs and 4 RBIs. He has played in 5 games and scored 2 times.
