Top Pitcher Trophy Won by Jr. Cardinaux Perkins
Wednesday, August 17th, 1932
The votes are in for the 1932 Central Baseball League Pitcher of the Year Award. It has been bestowed on Ned Perkins of the St. Louis Jr. Cardinaux.

The 16-year-old Jr. Cardinaux ace finished the year with a 12-2 mark, logged 15 starts, threw 115.1 innings, yielded 85 hits, struck out 129 and held the opposition to a .201 batting average.

He received 6 first place votes out of a possible 8. Naret Perron of the St. Louis Jr. Cardinaux finished second in voting with 1 first place vote, while Gene Robichaud of the Lincoln Pioneers finished third with 1 first place vote.
