Best CL Reliever Named
Thursday, October 14th, 1926
Mathieu Gagné of the New York Rebels has been named the winner of the 1926 Canadian League Reliever of the Year Award.

The award comes at an opportune time too, as the 32-year-old is an impending free agent.

"You always hope to have this type of year every year, but it's pretty special to have it before I go to free agency, if that happens," he said.

On the year, the relief ace had a 2.79 ERA over 61.1 innings of work. He made 29 relief appearances and had 8 saves, 21 strikeouts, 21 walks and a record of 10-3. Opponent batters hit only .289 against him.

He received 16 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Hervé Brunet of the Toronto Blue Sox finished second in voting, while Alex Smalls of the Chicagou White Sox finished third.
