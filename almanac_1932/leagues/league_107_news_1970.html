Broncos Beat Silvertips in Bennett Cup
Wednesday, August 5th, 1931
Sometimes the winner is the one who got the lucky bounces and the lucky calls, and sometimes the winner is just the one who played better.

It was the latter case in 1931, as the Boise Broncos topped the Everett Silvertips 2-1 tonight at Broncos Ballpark to win the Bennett Cup. It was the 3rd title in franchise history.

"When you play for championships, you have to play better than the team in the other dugout. We did that in this series, and all year. Now we're the best team in baseball," said Boise right fielder Isaak Pounds, who was named series MVP.

The Broncos took the series 3-2. The new titleholders completed the regular season with a first-place finish in the and a 36-15 record.

Boise manager Rudy Seguro grinned and nodded when asked to comment on Pounds's words.

"Isaak said it best -- we played better than the other team," he said.

Hometown officials announced they were already planning a "grand celebration" for the winners in the days to come.
