New York's Gunning Reaps CL's Rookie Award
Wednesday, October 15th, 1930
1930 was a good year for Russ Gunning. In his debut season the New York rookie rapped out a .383 batting average with 143 hits in 373 at-bats, 10 home runs and 73 RBIs. He walked 37 times and had a .438 OBP. Those numbers got him the Canadian League Rookie of the Year Award.

He received 20 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Walter Faulk of the Hamilton Steelers finished second in voting, while Roman Poe of the Hamilton Steelers finished third.
