Jr. Brasseurs Star Receives EBL Pitcher of the Year
Thursday, August 15th, 1929
It was a glorious year for Nathan Lin of the Juneau Jr. Brasseurs.

He finished the season with 5 victories and only 1 loss, with an impressive 1.66 ERA. The talented Jr. Brasseurs pitcher started 9 games and struck out 81 batters in 65 innings and won the Eastern Baseball League EBL Pitcher of the Year.

"It means a lot," Lin said. "I hadn't really had a chance to sit down and think about it yet. It feels good right now. I can't really put it into words, but I'm sure later on tonight when I have a chance to sit down with my family and think about it, I'll have some words for it. But right now, I'm just happy that it happened this year."

He received 3 first place votes out of a possible 10. Kian Shearer of the Hamilton Jr. Steelers finished second in voting with 4 first place votes, while Victor Powe of the Juneau Jr. Brasseurs finished third with 1 first place vote.
