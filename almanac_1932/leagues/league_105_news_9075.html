Gilliard 5-for-6 Against Robins
Thursday, September 8th, 1927
No matter what the Baltimore Robins threw at him today, Samuel Gilliard hit it. He easily handled Robins pitching, going 5 for 6 and helping the Redcoats to a 18-6 triumph at Sportsmans Park 1911.

"The way I was swinging today, I think I could have closed my eyes and hit the ball," he told BNN reporters in the postgame interview.

Told of Gilliard's quip, Robins skipper Eldin Gabriel said he "would take him up on that next time."

Samuel Gilliard singled in the 1st, flied out in the 3rd, hit an RBI single in the 4th, hit an RBI triple in the 6th, hit a two-run triple in the 7th, walked in the 8th and singled in the 9th.

This season Gilliard is hitting .325 with 1 home run.
