Wintringham Wins Best Bat Award in June
Friday, July 1st, 1932
David Wintringham battered Canadian League pitchers all June and wound up taking home the Batter of the Month Award.

"I've been around National Baseball Association long enough to know you really have to work hard day in and day out to be able to put up this kind of consistency," the 26-year-old Wintringham told reporters. "I put in a lot of work, even on the days I might not take the field, and it's always nice when everything comes together for me like it did this last month."

In 26 games, Wintringham hit .387 with 10 home runs, 32 RBIs and scored 26 runs.

In his 1932 season totals, Wintringham has 24 home runs, .406 BA, .425 OBP, 65 RBIs and 55 runs scored.
