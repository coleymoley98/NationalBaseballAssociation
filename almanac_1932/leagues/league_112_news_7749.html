Skipper Wilkins Banished from Dugout by Acadiens
Thursday, August 12th, 1926
The Pointe-Sainte-Anne Acadiens have sent manager Timothy Wilkins packing, and there are plans to announce a replacement soon.

The Pointe-Sainte-Anne general manager was appreciative of his efforts this past year, but said the team did not make the progress necessary to make them a contender.

"We might promote within the organization, or we might just go out and get the best skipper available," GM Joe Unknown said. "No decision has been made on that, but we will make it soon."

The departing manager told reporters that "management and owners want instant improvement and instant gratification."

"Building a contender takes time and good players," Wilkins said. "We made some progress, but it wasn't enough."
