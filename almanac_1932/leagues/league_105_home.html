Veteran Ratté Signs 3-Year Deal
Monday, December 19th, 1932
In a move expected to bring depth to the Indians, Ottawa brass announced the signing today of starting pitcher Sommai Ratté.

Ratté will be pocketing $18,333 per season, an amount some BNN analysts think might be too high.

His career record is 149 victories and 163 defeats. Ratté also has struck out 723 batters and compiled a 4.11 ERA.
Veteran Corriher, New York Complete Deal
Monday, December 19th, 1932
New York Rebels fans welcomed Hackett Corriher into the fold, as the veteran announced he'd reached an agreement with New York brass.

Some were critical of the move, insisting that you shouldn't invest $32,800 in a 40-year-old man, no matter how good he might be on the field. The team believes he will deepen their roster and ultimately win a championship.

Corriher has appeared in 491 games in his career, picked up 25 saves, tossed 2846.1 innings and posted a 152-157 record. He has a lifetime ERA of 3.43.
