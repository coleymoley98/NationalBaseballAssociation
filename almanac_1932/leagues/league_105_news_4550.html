Quebec Sweeps Rebels
Wednesday, September 26th, 1923
It's party time in Quebec. The confetti is flying and residents are smiling.

The Quebec Nordiques have reached the mountaintop, capturing the 1923 Riel Cup today. They defeated the New York Rebels 10-3 at Polo Grounds to officially wrap up the series 4-0.

Asked how it feels to be champion, third baseman Sheehan Thompson was to the point.

"It feels great, like a million bucks. I'm so happy right now, not just for me but for all the guys," he said. "We put a lot of work into this and deserve to be champions."

The team is planning a victory parade and fan appreciation day later in the week. Quebec finished the regular season 66-46 and claimed first place in the National League.
