Cardin Selected WL's Best Player
Wednesday, September 25th, 1929
At the age of 23, James Cardin of the Winnipeg Thunder could very well have his best years ahead of him.

It's a startling thought when you consider the year he had in 1929 was good enough to win the left fielder the Western League Most Valuable Player Award.

"See the ball, hit the ball," he said. "That's my basic approach."

The top hitter put up a .390 batting average this year, as well as a .448 on-base percentage. He played 75 games and racked up 108 hits, 15 doubles, 2 triples, 5 home runs, 43 RBIs and 47 runs scored.

He received 2 first place votes out of a possible 8. Ansley LaVallee of the Seattle Rainiers finished second in voting with 1 first place vote, while Somkiat Westbrooks of the Saskatoon Coyotes finished third with 2 first place votes.
