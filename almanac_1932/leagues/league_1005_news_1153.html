Hollomon Raps Team for 6 Hits
Monday, March 23rd, 1931
No matter what the Team Columbia threw at him today, Pattara Hollomon hit it. He easily handled Columbia pitching, going 6 for 7 and helping the Erie to a 15-1 triumph at New York Ballpark.

"The way I was swinging today, I think I could have closed my eyes and hit the ball," he told BNN reporters in the postgame interview.

Told of Hollomon's quip, Columbia skipper Dustan Polk said he "would take him up on that next time."

Pattara Hollomon struck out in the 1st, singled in the 3rd, hit a two-run double in the 4th, hit a two-run single in the 5th, singled in the 7th, singled in the 8th and hit an RBI single in the 9th.

This season Hollomon is swinging the bat at a .434 rate with 23 hits in 53 at-bats. To date he has hit 1 home run, scored 15 runs and driven in 22.
