5-for-6 Game for Welter
Thursday, June 4th, 1931
It's not something you read about in the sports pages every day -- a 5-for-6 day. For many, it might come around only once in a career -- and for Cole Welter, it came around today.

The 27-year-old third baseman scattered hits all over Edmonton Grounds to pace the Victoria Admirals to a 10-7 victory over the Edmonton Eskimos.

Edmonton skipper Nattaporn Lamontagne told reporters in the press room that "Welter deserves a hat-tip."

"He hit some good pitches against us," he said. "In fact, according to our scouting reports, Welter hit several that he is not supposed to be able to hit."

Cole Welter tripled in the 1st, grounded out in the 2nd, singled in the 5th, singled in the 6th, singled in the 8th and hit a two-run single in the 9th.
