Great Year Gets Wintringham CL Most Valuable Player Award
Saturday, October 22nd, 1932
What a way to finish off the year -- being honored with the 1932 Most Valuable Player Award in the Canadian League. It had already been a very special season for Ottawa's David Wintringham, but today's announcement really put the icing on his cake.

All season long David Wintringham gave opposing pitchers fits by hitting .419 as he collected 185 hits, 29 doubles, 7 triples, 45 home runs and 134 RBIs.

He received 20 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Jake Stimson of the Philadelphia Liberty finished second in voting, while Orestes González of the Toronto Blue Sox finished third.
