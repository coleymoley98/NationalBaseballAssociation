Wintringham Raps 3 HR Against Blue Sox
Sunday, August 5th, 1928
David Wintringham went yard three times in a Ottawa Indians victory over the Toronto Blue Sox at Lincoln Park today.

"I was in a groove," he said. "Every pitch they tossed up there looked like a grapefruit."

For the day, the Ottawa left fielder went 5-for-5 against Toronto with 6 RBIs and 4 runs scored in a brilliant display of power hitting that led the Indians to the 13-10 win.

"I tell you what. I'm shocked because I didn't think that last ball had enough to get out," he told BNN reporters in the clubhouse following the game. "I thought it was just going to be a long out."

David Wintringham hit an RBI double in the 1st, hit a two-run home run off Kyle Gandy in the 3rd, singled in the 4th, hit a two-run home run off Vihaan Nuregesan in the 6th and hit a solo-shot off Vihaan Nuregesan in the 7th.

This year Wintringham is hitting .318 with 13 home runs and 54 RBIs to his credit. He has gotten on base at a .348 clip and scored 49 runs.
