Hall of Fame Selection announcement coming next week
Wednesday, December 31st, 1930
The 1931 National Baseball Association Hall of Fame class will soon be welcomed into baseball history.

The leading candidate for enshrinement is 1912 Pitcher of the Year Award winner Stephan Signoret, who put up an impressive 16-year career.

Signoret retired in 1923 at the age of 35, and said if elected he plans to go in as a member of the St. Louis Cardinaux.

Voters are reminded that ballots may be cast up until the votes are announced on January 7th, 1931.

During his career the 42-year-old Signoret has piled up 2927.1 innings. Over that time, he has fanned 1475 batters, walked 919 and put up a 2.89 ERA. His lifetime won-lost mark is 153-153 and opposing clubs have hit .255 against him.
