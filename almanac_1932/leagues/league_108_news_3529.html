Western League Names League's Best
Friday, September 23rd, 1932
He may have less experience than most of his Western League peers, but that didn't slow Cookie Rodríguez down at all in 1932.

At the age of 23, the Portland left fielder has been named the winner of the Western League Most Valuable Player Award.

He had a .409 batting average and a .448 on-base percentage this year. In 59 games he piled up 99 hits, 18 doubles, 11 triples, 15 home runs, 59 RBIs and 62 runs scored.

"I'm blown away by the season I was able to put together," he said. "I'm looking forward to trying to do it all again next year."

He received 8 first place votes out of a possible 10. William Wood of the Edmonton Eskimos finished second in voting, while Amporn Wooten of the Winnipeg Thunder finished third.
