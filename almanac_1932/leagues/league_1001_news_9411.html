1928 Top Player Selected In CSBT
Monday, March 26th, 1928
The manager of the Team Ontario said he thinks Lewis Maday is only going to get better as he gets older.

He may only be 20, but it will be hard to top the left fielder's 1928 campaign, as he has been named the winner of the Canadian Senior Baseball Tournament Most Valuable Player Award.

His winning campaign featured a .632 batting average and a .667 on-base percentage this year. In 5 games he piled up 12 hits, 3 doubles, 1 triple, no home runs, 6 RBIs and 7 runs scored.

He received 8 first place votes out of a possible 14. David Wintringham of the Team Saskatchewan finished second in voting with 2 first place votes, while Sombat Dominguez of the Team Michigan finished third with 4 first place votes.
