Hopson Honored, 1925 CBL Most Valuable Player Award
Sunday, August 16th, 1925
Apisit Hopson took Central Baseball League by storm this year, and he did it all at the age of 18.

For his spectacular campaign, the Adams Mounties star has been named the 1925 Central Baseball League Most Valuable Player Award recipient.

"To be considered amongst the best in the league is overwhelming," said the center fielder.

On the season he compiled a .415 batting average to go with a .473 on-base percentage. The native of Rapid City, The United Provinces also added 9 doubles, 1 triple, 11 home runs, 25 RBIs and 31 runs scored.

He received 6 first place votes out of a possible 8. Grosvenor Gesell of the St. Louis Jr. Cardinaux finished second in voting, while Walter Faulk of the Omaha Jr. Falcons finished third.
