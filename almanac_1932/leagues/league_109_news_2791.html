5-Hit Blitz by Kehagias
Wednesday, June 15th, 1932
After his 5-hit effort at Ballpark of Hamilton was tarnished by Brampton's 9-7 loss to the Harrisburg Robins, third baseman Kaeo Kehagias told BNN beat writers it was "a missed opportunity for a special day."

"It really doesn't matter whether it's sandlot ball, high school or college, two hits in a game is nice," he said. "I would have been happy to stop there if it meant we got a win."

He didn't get the win, but the 28-year-old had a spectacular 5-for-6 game against the Robins.

"His swing is getting there," said Blue Sox skipper Nelson Méthot after the game. "We want him to be quick with his swing, short and compact. We want him to be patient and go the other way when he can. Kaeo did that to perfection today and we wasted it."

Kaeo Kehagias doubled in the 1st, flied out in the 3rd, singled in the 5th, singled in the 7th, singled in the 9th, walked in the 11th and hit an RBI double in the 13th.

So far this year Kehagias has hit .298 with no home runs, 3 RBIs and 9 runs scored.
