Pum Beasley Takes MVP Trophy
Tuesday, April 9th, 1929
The manager of the Team Saskatchewan said he thinks Pum Beasley is only going to get better as he gets older.

He may only be 21, but it will be hard to top the left fielder's 1929 campaign, as he has been named the winner of the Canadian Senior Baseball Tournament Most Valuable Player Award.

His winning campaign featured a .549 batting average and a .556 on-base percentage this year. In 13 games he piled up 28 hits, 4 doubles, no triples, 6 home runs, 21 RBIs and 15 runs scored.

He received 11 first place votes out of a possible 18. Phuwanart McInnis of the Team Montana finished second in voting with 3 first place votes, while Kitti Lei of the Team Illinois finished third.
