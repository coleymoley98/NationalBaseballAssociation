Jr. Royales Take It on Chin, Lose, 12-0, to Providence
Saturday, June 20th, 1931
The Montreal Jr. Royales manager was philosophical in the clubhouse following a shutout loss to the Providence Bruins. The Jr. Royales were overpowered by Providence starter Azwan Philips, who hurled his club to victory, 12-0.

"It was just one of those games -- shake it off and do better next time," Calvin Pitts said.

Over 9 innings, Philips blanked Montreal on 3 hits with 1 walk and 14 strikeouts.

"I kept them off balance. I could tell a lot of them were late on fastballs because they were fouling them off. Once you see that, it sets them up for other pitches," Philips said.

"I set them up, where if they chased one breaking ball, they were going to chase another one."

This season Philips has compiled a 4-1 mark, 1.50 ERA and made 6 starts.
