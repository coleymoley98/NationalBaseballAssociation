Minnesota Narrowly Wins National League
Sunday, September 11th, 1927
The manager of the Minnesota Saints said "a lot of hard work, talent and luck" are the reasons for his team's National League championship.

"This club believes in themselves and goes out and gets it done," said A.J. Provost. "You can't ask for much more than that as a manager."

It's the club's 4th division crown and gives them an opportunity to win the franchise's 2nd Riel Cup.

"We want that title badly," said reliever Prayad Adeleye as he took a break from celebrating with teammates. "This is just a start."
