McClinton Torches Dakota for 5 Hits
Friday, March 16th, 1928
Team right fielder Randell McClinton had a day to remember today, 5-for-6 against the Team Dakota at Los Angeles Grounds as the New England picked up a 11-7 victory.

"He was patient up there today," Team skipper Owen Turcotte told the press after the game. "I like the way he waited for his pitch and wasn't overly aggressive. McClinton has a tendency to chase bad balls sometimes."

Randell McClinton singled in the 1st, flied out in the 3rd, singled in the 5th, hit an RBI single in the 6th, singled in the 8th and hit a three-run triple in the 9th.

McClinton is hitting .360 in 1928 with 8 RBIs and 1 home run.
