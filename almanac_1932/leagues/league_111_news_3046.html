League Punishes Pair for Fighting
Thursday, July 14th, 1932
If there wasn't a history between Jean-Michel Poulin of Juneau and Lincoln Boswell of Toronto, then there is one now. The incident between the two sparked a bench-clearing scrum with those two being the center of attention. Now they will miss 5 and 3 games respectively as a result of their actions. The league office announced their suspensions today.

"We're both really competitive," Poulin said later, "and sometimes things go a little too far because of it."

Poulin is currently batting .248 with 6 home runs and 20 RBIs.

Boswell has taken the mound for 14 relief appearances this season, nailing down 1 save while notching a 2-4 record with a 9.88 ERA. The Toronto reliever has limited opposing batters to a .366 batting average.
