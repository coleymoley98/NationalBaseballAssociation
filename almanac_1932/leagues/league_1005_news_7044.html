3-HR Binge for Betts Against Team
Monday, March 15th, 1926
Team Erie right fielder Basil Betts put on a dazzling display of power today at Toronto Memorial Fields against the Team Dakota. In the 15-0 Erie victory Betts penned his name into the record books, slamming a trio of home runs in one of baseball's finest single-day efforts.

"He can hit them out anywhere, right, center, left, it doesn't matter," Team manager Lloyd Ducharme said. "He's super strong and stays on the ball so well."

Along with his three home runs, the 21-year-old had 3 hits in 3 at-bats, 6 RBIs and 4 runs scored.

"Life will always throw you curves, just keep fouling them off," Betts said after the game, when asked about his hitting philosophy. "The right pitch will come, and when it does, be prepared to run the bases. Or trot around them, like I did today."

Basil Betts hit a solo-shot off D.J. Hope in the 2nd, hit a three-run home run off D.J. Hope in the 4th, hit a two-run home run off D.J. Hope in the 6th, walked in the 7th and walked in the 8th.

Thus far this season in 6 games Betts is batting .600 with 3 home runs, 11 RBIs and 14 runs scored. He has 12 hits in 20 at-bats.
