Jones Electric in 2-0 Win
Monday, August 13th, 1923
The Trois-Rivieres Royales did little to support starter Charles Jones against the Providence Redcoats, but it turned out he didn't need the help.

The left-hander spun a complete game shutout against the Redcoats, striking out 8 and surrendering 2 hits. The performance from Jones allowed Trois-Rivieres to escape with the 2-0 win despite an anemic offense.

"Nobody came to me and said 'Charles, we're gonna need you to win this one for us today,' but I could feel it," Jones told BNN reporters following the win. "I'm the guy that will step up for you when you need it."

This year Jones has compiled a 4-1 mark with a 2.51 ERA in 7 starts.
