Top Pitcher Award Announced by CBL
Saturday, August 18th, 1923
Tony Gonzalez walked away with the top pitching honors in the Central Baseball League this year. The Omaha Jr. Falcons starter was named the Pitcher of the Year Award winner for 1923 earlier today.

This outstanding 15-year-old hurler fashioned a fine 8-4 won-lost record in 14 starts. He threw 101 innings, giving up 60 hits and 18 walks and holding opponents to a .169 average. Gonzalez struck out 103 and posted a 1.51 ERA.

He received 4 first place votes out of a possible 8. Cyril Cork of the Adams Mounties finished second in voting with 3 first place votes, while Weerasak Morillon of the Winnipeg Jr. Thunder finished third with 1 first place vote.
