Petdon Selected CSBT's Best Player
Wednesday, April 9th, 1930
He may have less experience than most of his Canadian Senior Baseball Tournament peers, but that didn't slow Brian Petdon down at all in 1930.

At the age of 22, the Team left fielder has been named the winner of the Canadian Senior Baseball Tournament Most Valuable Player Award.

He had a .435 batting average and a .526 on-base percentage this year. In 17 games he piled up 27 hits, 6 doubles, 1 triple, 4 home runs, 15 RBIs and 13 runs scored.

"I'm blown away by the season I was able to put together," he said. "I'm looking forward to trying to do it all again next year."

He received 13 first place votes out of a possible 18. Liam Sérafin of the Team New England finished second in voting with 1 first place vote, while Valéry Jetté of the Team Columbia finished third.
