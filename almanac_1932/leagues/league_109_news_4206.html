Hoy Bombs 3 HR Against Providence
Monday, July 23rd, 1923
Brian Hoy brutalized the Providence Redcoats pitching staff, launching three home runs in today's 11-0 victory at Harrisburg Grounds. The Harrisburg Robins right fielder was red-hot, the ball just exploding off his bat all day.

"I don't try to hit homers," he said later. "I try to go out there and put a good swing on the ball, and sometimes it is going to go out of the park. I didn't try to hit the first one or the second one or the third one."

For the game, he went 5-for-5 with 5 RBIs and 3 runs scored.

"Too much Brian Hoy today," Redcoats manager Grayson St-Louis said. "That's my assessment of the game. That's what our problems boiled down to."

Brian Hoy singled in the 1st, hit a solo-shot off E.J. Eskow in the 3rd, hit a two-run home run off E.J. Eskow in the 4th, doubled in the 7th and hit a two-run home run off Andy Carper in the 8th.

This season Hoy has hit 7 home runs and posted a .339 batting average.
