Central Baseball League Names Top Player
Friday, August 20th, 1926
Woody Choe of the Winnipeg Jr. Thunder proved that you can step out of the shadows and onto the stage if you've got the talent and the drive to hone your skills. Hard work, long hours and a tireless resolve paid off for him today in the form of the Central Baseball League Most Valuable Player Award.

Choe hit for a .337 average while collecting 66 hits, 17 doubles, no triples, 16 home runs, 49 RBIs and scoring 42 times.

He received 4 first place votes out of a possible 8. Renato Joly of the Davenport Rivermen finished second in voting with 2 first place votes, while Laurent Ruel of the Winnipeg Jr. Thunder finished third with 1 first place vote.
