5-Hit Blitz by Hubbard
Saturday, June 4th, 1932
A jubilant Langley Hubbard joined the postgame press conference at Sportsmans Park 1911 today to offer some unusual words of encouragement -- for the Philadelphia Liberty pitching staff he had just vanquished.

"It may not have looked like it," said the Baltimore Robins shortstop, "but I saw some awful good pitches today. If luck hadn't been on my side it might have turned out a lot different."

That would not have been the conclusion of anyone who saw his dominant 5-for-8 hitting performance, which led the Robins to a 31-9 win.

"He made it look easy," said Kasemchai Simoyan, manager of the Liberty. "We never figured out what to do with him. Obviously."

Langley Hubbard grounded out in the 1st, hit an RBI single in the 2nd, flied out in the 3rd, singled in the 4th, singled in the 5th, hit an RBI single in the 5th, grounded out in the 6th and hit an RBI double in the 8th.

This season Hubbard is hitting .328 with no home runs.
