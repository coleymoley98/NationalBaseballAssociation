Wintringham Caps Amazing Year with .419 BA
Wednesday, September 14th, 1932
He may be young compared to many National Baseball Association stars, but at the age of 26, David Wintringham has already proven himself as an elite performer.

A .419 batting average on the season has put the Ottawa Indians star into rarefied air.

"He's only going to get better, that's the scary thing," the Ottawa manager told reporters.

It will be hard to improve on a campaign that saw the left fielder stroke 185 hits, with 29 doubles, 7 triples, 45 home runs, 134 RBIs and 104 runs scored.

"I definitely never expected to hit .419 this year, that's for sure," Wintringham said.
