Dostie Batters St. John's for 5 Hits
Wednesday, June 27th, 1923
It was a high-five day for Derek Dostie, at least for a while.

The Drummondville first baseman got plenty of them from his teammates when he reached the five-hit milestone during today's game against the St. John's Mariners, but they were in short supply at the end, as St. John's took a 21-12 win. Dostie finished with an impressive 5-for-5 line, but the Voltigeurs couldn't hold off the Mariners.

"Derek was hitting everything," said Voltigeurs manager Tai Grigsby. "It's too bad the other guys couldn't rise to the occasion."

Derek Dostie hit an RBI single in the 1st, singled in the 3rd, tripled in the 5th, singled in the 7th and hit a three-run home run off Amnart Lavigne in the 8th.

In 25 games this season Dostie has batted at a .390 pace with 39 hits and 9 home runs.
