Jetté Produces Record-Breaking Hit
Saturday, March 22nd, 1924
Baseball has a new all-time single-season hit leader. In a game at San Francisco Memorial Fields Valéry Jetté broke the record set previously by Johnny Hubner. The crowd went wild when he banged out hit number 18. Asked by reporters, if it was tough to break the record, Jetté replied, "Yeah, getting hits is tough, but there's no pressure build-up. I figured I would get this hit. This wasn't pressure. Pressure is the playoffs. Breaking this record was just fun... just another day at the ballpark."

Valéry Jetté singled in the 1st.

Thus far this season Jetté has hit .486 with no home runs, 5 RBIs and scored 8 times.
