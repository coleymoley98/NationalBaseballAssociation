Rhoden Grabs EL Reliever of the Year Award
Monday, September 20th, 1926
D.J. Rhoden was a calming influence out of the Sherbrooke Nordiques bullpen this season.

His teammates and manager said he had a poised demeanor about him that was typical of someone much older and more experienced. His poise has paid off, as the 23-year-old turned in a sparkling season and has been named the winner of the Eastern League Reliever of the Year Award for 1926.

The Montréal, The United Provinces native won the award with 11 saves in 34 relief appearances, 51 strikeouts in 61.1 innings and a record of 3-1 to go with his 2.35 ERA.

He received 8 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Andy Carper of the Providence Redcoats finished second in voting, while Billy Wall of the Providence Redcoats finished third.
