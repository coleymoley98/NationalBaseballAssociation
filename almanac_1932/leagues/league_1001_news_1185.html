Premier Player Named by Canadian Senior Baseball Tournament
Saturday, April 11th, 1931
Justin Lapierre took Canadian Senior Baseball Tournament by storm this year, and he did it all at the age of 20.

For his spectacular campaign, the Team Iowa star has been named the 1931 Canadian Senior Baseball Tournament Most Valuable Player Award recipient.

"To be considered amongst the best in the league is overwhelming," said the left fielder.

On the season he compiled a .500 batting average to go with a .519 on-base percentage. The native of St. Louis, The United Provinces also added 5 doubles, 3 triples, no home runs, 7 RBIs and 17 runs scored.

He received 12 first place votes out of a possible 18. Gautam Mijangos of the Team Illinois finished second in voting, while Pum Beasley of the Team Saskatchewan finished third.
