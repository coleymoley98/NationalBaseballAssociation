Jimmy Michell Latest NBA HOF Inductee
Wednesday, January 4th, 1928
At his Hall of Fame induction legendary pitcher, Jimmy Michell reminisced about his baseball career: "My how time flies. It is only a mere moment from your first spring training game to your first oldtimer's game. But it a magnificent journey."

Today Michell was installed into the National Baseball Association Hall of Fame after his selection by the Baseball Writers Association.

Baseball's newest HOF inductee was one fine hurler. When Michell retired at age 37, he had registered 157 wins and 125 losses, struck out 1122 hitters, pitched in 321 games and compiled a 2.83 ERA.

Among the many people he mentioned in his induction speech was his first manager. "He gave me my first opportunity when he could have kept other people. I was lucky -- he was a Jimmy Michell fan. You can't get in the Hall of Fame without your first chance."
