Cincinnati's Alvarez Takes Home NL Batting Honors
Tuesday, September 11th, 1923
He may only be 23 years old, but Teeratat Alvarez has already established himself as an elite National Baseball Association player.

The Cincinnati Reds star posted a .374 batting average this year, which won him the National League batting crown.

"I can't even believe it, to be honest," he told Baseball News Network.

His fantastic campaign consisted of 164 hits, 18 doubles, 8 triples and 4 home runs for the year. In 110 games Alvarez added 45 RBIs and scored 62 runs.
