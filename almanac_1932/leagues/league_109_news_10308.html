The manager of the Albany Rebels said he thinks Derek Dostie is only going to get better as he gets older.

He may only be 21, but it will be hard to top the first baseman's 1928 campaign, as he has been named the winner of the Eastern League Most Valuable Player Award.

His winning campaign featured a .327 batting average and a .396 on-base percentage this year. In 81 games he piled up 105 hits, 20 doubles, 2 triples, 23 home runs, 71 RBIs and 59 runs scored.

He received 3 first place votes out of a possible 8. Dominic Grigg of the Trois-Rivieres Royales finished second in voting with 4 first place votes, while Clifton Blacksher of the Trois-Rivieres Royales finished third with 1 first place vote.
