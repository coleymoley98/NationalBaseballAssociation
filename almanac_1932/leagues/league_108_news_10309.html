Jordane Garnier Nabs Best Player Trophy
Tuesday, September 25th, 1928
Jordane Garnier took Western League by storm this year, and he did it all at the age of 21.

For his spectacular campaign, the Edmonton Eskimos star has been named the 1928 Western League Most Valuable Player Award recipient.

"To be considered amongst the best in the league is overwhelming," said the third baseman.

On the season he compiled a .368 batting average to go with a .430 on-base percentage. The native of Boston, The United Provinces also added 30 doubles, 2 triples, 11 home runs, 77 RBIs and 67 runs scored.

He received 5 first place votes out of a possible 8. Jesse Rojas of the Edmonton Eskimos finished second in voting with 1 first place vote, while Martino Gutierrez of the Omaha Falcons finished third with 2 first place votes.
