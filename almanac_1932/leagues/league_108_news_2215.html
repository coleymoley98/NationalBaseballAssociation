Portland Hurler Wins WL Most Valuable Player Award
Friday, September 18th, 1931
He's only 24 years old, but Isaac Dupont of the Portland Thorns has clearly established himself as a star of Western League.

His status grew today as the ace pitcher was named the winner of the 1931 Western League Most Valuable Player Award.

"I just want to pitch well and win games," he said. "The awards are nice though."

Dupont's award-winning season saw him put up a 15-2 record through 17 starts, throwing 149 innings. He had 51 strikeouts with 39 walks and a 1.87 ERA. Opponents hit only .248 against him during the season.

He received 7 first place votes out of a possible 10. Oliver Legault of the Calgary Wranglers finished second in voting with 3 first place votes, while Greg Eston of the Saskatoon Coyotes finished third.
