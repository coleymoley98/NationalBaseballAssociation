WL Reliever of the Year Award Winner Named
Monday, September 21st, 1925
Wutthisak Pohji was a calming influence out of the Portland Thorns bullpen this season.

His teammates and manager said he had a poised demeanor about him that was typical of someone much older and more experienced. His poise has paid off, as the 23-year-old turned in a sparkling season and has been named the winner of the Western League Reliever of the Year Award for 1925.

The Toronto, The United Provinces native won the award with 6 saves in 31 relief appearances, 36 strikeouts in 45.2 innings and a record of 4-0 to go with his 6.31 ERA.

He received 6 first place votes out of a possible 8. Tida Tremblay of the Vancouver Giants finished second in voting with 2 first place votes, while Oliver Settle of the Seattle Rainiers finished third.
