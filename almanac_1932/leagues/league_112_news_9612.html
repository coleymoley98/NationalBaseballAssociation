Chinma Hits for the Cycle
Sunday, June 10th, 1928
At Hartford Grounds today, Tewesh Chinma led Pawtucket to a 8-7 victory over the Hartford Whalers by hitting for the cycle, one of baseball's more difficult feats.

"Usually the triple is the hardest one to get, but I was most worried about the homer today because the ball wasn't carrying," Chinma said later. "When I took it deep, I was expecting the ball to die on the warning track, so it was sweet seeing the ump give me the signal to circle the bases."

In addition to going 5-for-5, Chinma chalked up 3 runs batted in and scored 3 runs in an exceptional performance.

Tewesh Chinma doubled in the 2nd, singled in the 3rd, hit an RBI double in the 5th, tripled in the 7th and hit a two-run home run off Boontawee Indrakul in the 9th.

This year Chinma is batting .395 with 15 hits, 2 home runs, 8 RBIs, 5 walks, .465 OBP and has scored 7 runs.
