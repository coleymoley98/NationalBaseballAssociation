Jr. Coyotes Plugged by Petersen, Rivermen, 5-0
Saturday, June 18th, 1932
"Good pitching beats good hitting" is an old baseball adage.

Well, good pitching also beats bad hitting.

Rick Petersen of the Davenport Rivermen proved the point at Jr. Coyotes Ballpark today, holding the Saskatoon Jr. Coyotes to 5 hits in a complete game shutout victory.

"Petersen made us look real bad today," Jr. Coyotes manager Daquein Liles said in his postgame interview. "He was outstanding. He was down in the zone with all his pitches. Everything was sinking."

Petersen had 12 strikeouts and gave up 1 walk as the Rivermen took the 5-0 win.

In 5 starts this season Petersen has compiled a 3-2 record and a 1.20 ERA.
