Hits Blitz for Erie Paulson
Saturday, March 20th, 1926
It was a quite a day for Paul Paulson at Toronto Memorial Fields.

He hit for the cycle, belting out every kind of hit there is -- a single, double, triple and home run. The Team second baseman accomplished the rare feat during his team's 11-10 victory over the Team New York.

"I never even thought about hitting for the cycle and what do you know, I did it," he told BNN reporters at the postgame press conference. "The cycle really doesn't matter to me, it's the win that's important."

Paulson went 4-for-6 in the game with 4 runs batted in and 3 runs scored.

Paul Paulson tripled in the 1st, hit a two-run home run off Stephen McCord in the 3rd, hit a two-run double in the 4th, grounded out in the 6th, grounded out in the 7th and singled in the 9th.

This season Paulson is batting .463 with 1 home run. His totals include 14 RBIs and 19 runs scored.
