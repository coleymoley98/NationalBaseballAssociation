Valéry Jetté Chosen CSBT's #1 Player
Saturday, March 29th, 1924
The future looks extremely bright for Valéry Jetté. At the age of 20, many players are still trying to find their way in Canadian Senior Baseball Tournament.

Jetté has not only found his way, he's already established himself as one of the game's brightest stars, today being named the winner of the Canadian Senior Baseball Tournament Most Valuable Player Award.

"I didn't expect to hit .482 this year, that's for sure," the third baseman said.

The Team Columbia star also put up a .525 on-base percentage this year, with 27 hits, 5 doubles, no triples, no home runs, 8 RBIs and 17 runs scored.

He received 6 first place votes out of a possible 14. Simon Cowan of the Team Columbia finished second in voting with 3 first place votes, while Elliot Provost of the Team Quebec finished third with 3 first place votes.
