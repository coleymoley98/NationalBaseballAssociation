Willardson Named Top Reliever for CBL
Friday, August 14th, 1931
At the age of 15, Sebastian Willardson says he still has a lot to learn about life in Central Baseball League.

But he certainly seems to be a quick study, as the Adams Mounties reliever was better than all others this year and has captured the 1931 Central Baseball League Reliever of the Year Award.

"I hope to win this a few more times before all is said and done in my career," the Bouchard, The United Provinces native said.

For the season, Willardson had 4 saves in 13 relief appearances, which totaled 19. He fanned 15 betters, while walking 5 and put up a record of 0-1 to go with his 2.84 ERA.

He received 3 first place votes out of a possible 8. Thanakarn Buangam of the Adams Mounties finished second in voting with 4 first place votes, while Wilbur Simmons of the Minneapolis Jr. Saints finished third with 1 first place vote.
