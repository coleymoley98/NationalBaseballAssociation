Bédard Belts 3 HRs
Thursday, June 16th, 1932
Canton Legends first baseman Surat Bédard almost did it all today. He sent three shots out of the ballpark, scored 3 times and drove in 4 runs and had 3 hits in 4 at-bats.

The one thing he could not do, however, was lead the Legends to victory. The Buffalo Rapids escaped with the 6-5 win by the Buffalo despite being on the receiving end of the barrage from Bédard.

"I'd be lying if I said I didn't like hitting three homers, but losing takes a little glitter off the game," he admitted to BNN writers after the game.

Surat Bédard hit a two-run home run off Jean-Luc Labrie in the 1st, hit a solo-shot off Jean-Luc Labrie in the 4th, grounded out in the 6th and hit a solo-shot off Jean-Luc Labrie in the 8th.

"When someone hits three home runs, you put yourself in a tough position to win," said Canton manager Von Rivera. "We were lucky to win this one. I don't think it matters how hard you throw, especially in the Eastern Baseball League. If you leave a ball out over the plate to good Eastern Baseball League hitters, if they get a bead on you and you leave it over the middle, they're probably going to take a good hack at it."

On the year Bédard has hit .491 with 8 home runs, 18 RBIs and scored 14 runs.
