Bruins Philips, ABL Pitcher of the Year Winner
Wednesday, August 19th, 1931
What does it take to win the ABL Pitcher of the Year?

Just ask Azwan Philips of the Providence Bruins -- the latest Atlantic Baseball League ABL Pitcher of the Year winner.

"My dad always told me to dream big and dream always," he said. "It's not like I shoot for the ABL Pitcher of the Year," he said. "I have big aspirations for what I want to do in this game and what I want to achieve in a long career."

The Bruins pitching ace made some of his dreams come true this year, winning 11 games and losing only 1. In 14 starts, Philips registered a 0.91 ERA and limited other clubs to a .179 batting average. He fanned 120 in 109 innings and issued 15 walks.

He received 14 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Steve Lowenstein of the Quebec City Jr. Nordiques finished second in voting, while Tyler Nimmo of the Coude Voyageurs finished third.
