Atlantic Baseball League Names Smyth Best Player
Sunday, August 26th, 1923
At the age of 17, Leroy Smyth of the Pawtucket Red Sox could very well have his best years ahead of him.

It's a startling thought when you consider the year he had in 1923 was good enough to win the left fielder the Atlantic Baseball League ABL Player of the Year.

"See the ball, hit the ball," he said. "That's my basic approach."

The top hitter put up a .421 batting average this year, as well as a .483 on-base percentage. He played 48 games and racked up 77 hits, 8 doubles, 1 triple, 17 home runs, 49 RBIs and 45 runs scored.

He received 14 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Max Boucher of the Coude Voyageurs finished second in voting, while Edge Rufolo of the Quincy Gunners finished third.
