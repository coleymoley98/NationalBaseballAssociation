Boston's Samuel Gilliard Injured
Wednesday, August 17th, 1932
The Boston Redcoats learned that Samuel Gilliard is likely to be out for 4 weeks after he injured himself making a defensive play in today's game against the Toronto Blue Sox. A round of tests by the team doctor revealed the injury to be a herniated disc. Upon hearing the diagnosis, the Boston manager told the press, "As you all know, we can't control injuries -- they are a part of the game. We just have to do the best we can until Samuel is back in action."

This year Gilliard has hit at a .296 clip with 49 RBIs, 4 home runs and 44 runs scored.
