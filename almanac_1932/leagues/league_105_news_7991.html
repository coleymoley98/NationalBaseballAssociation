Best NL Reliever Named
Thursday, October 14th, 1926
The Cincinnati Reds got more than they expected out of Wachirawit Therrien in 1926.

At the age of 34, there were questions about whether or not Therrien could still compete at a high level, but those questions were answered emphatically as he has won the National League Reliever of the Year Award.

On the season, the South Bend, The United Provinces native recorded 14 saves in 48 relief appearances and threw 80 innings. He recorded 53 strikeouts and 35 walks, while posting an opponent's batting average of .232.

He received 16 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Taylor Ratel of the Detroit Lions finished second in voting, while Jack Huba of the Montreal Royales finished third.
