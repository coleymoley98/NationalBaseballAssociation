Jetté Selected NL's #1 Player
Monday, October 18th, 1926
Valéry Jetté took National Baseball Association by storm this year, and he did it all at the age of 22.

For his spectacular campaign, the Montreal Royales star has been named the 1926 National League Most Valuable Player Award recipient.

"To be considered amongst the best in the league is overwhelming," said the third baseman.

On the season he compiled a .310 batting average to go with a .406 on-base percentage. The native of White Rock, The United Provinces also added 15 doubles, 14 triples, 9 home runs, 48 RBIs and 80 runs scored.

He received 9 first place votes out of a possible 16. Giovanni Ratanaveroj of the Quebec Nordiques finished second in voting with 5 first place votes, while Daniel Ibarra of the Quebec Nordiques finished third with 2 first place votes.
