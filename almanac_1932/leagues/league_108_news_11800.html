1930 #1 Player Chosen By WL
Saturday, September 20th, 1930
Justin Lapierre took Western League by storm this year, and he did it all at the age of 19.

For his spectacular campaign, the Calgary Wranglers star has been named the 1930 Western League Most Valuable Player Award recipient.

"To be considered amongst the best in the league is overwhelming," said the left fielder.

On the season he compiled a .407 batting average to go with a .462 on-base percentage. The native of St. Louis, The United Provinces also added 26 doubles, 8 triples, 8 home runs, 69 RBIs and 85 runs scored.

He received 4 first place votes out of a possible 10. Jerold Palmisano of the Calgary Wranglers finished second in voting with 2 first place votes, while Jacob McGowan of the Omaha Falcons finished third with 4 first place votes.
