Nordiques Flame Out Against Saibara, Redcoats
Saturday, July 16th, 1932
The Providence Redcoats shut out the Sherbrooke Nordiques 2-0 today behind the strong pitching of Keith Saibara. Saibara surrendered only 6 hits in recording the complete-game victory, and struck out 4.

Following the last out, Saibara appeared to celebrate a little too boisterously for Sherbrooke manager Michael Halliday, who could be seen scolding the famously outspoken right-hander as the clubs left the field. Saibara's own manager then intervened with some words for his counterpart.

The Nordiques skipper would not comment on the exchange, but at the Redcoats press conference, Saibara confirmed that it was regarding the celebration.

"He basically told me I was being a lousy winner," Saibara said.

"And I told him he was being a lousy loser," Providence manager Freddie Mallin added.

In 9 starts this year Saibara has recorded a 4-4 record and a 4.35 ERA.
