Redcoats Listless in 6-0 Loss to Rebels
Saturday, June 21st, 1930
The Boston Redcoats struggled at the plate today against Rebels hurler Ernest Robertson, who shackled them on 3 hits while hurling a complete game 6-0 shutout.

"What can you say? We didn't hit well," said Redcoats skipper Brian Ahn in his postgame comments. "Hopefully we shake this off and bounce back tomorrow."

Robertson finished the game with 4 strikeouts and 4 walks in an excellent mound performance.

"He pitched real well," New York manager William Davison said. "Ernest made pitches when he had to. He hit his spots and did a great job of mixing in his changeup and using his fastball effectively."

So far this season Robertson has a record of 5-1 with 31 strikeouts and 27 walks in 53.1 innings and a 5.74 ERA.
