Devin Gallegos Wins MVP Trophy
Wednesday, August 15th, 1928
He may have less experience than most of his Central Baseball League peers, but that didn't slow Devin Gallegos down at all in 1928.

At the age of 16, the Winnipeg right fielder has been named the winner of the Central Baseball League Most Valuable Player Award.

He had a .368 batting average and a .442 on-base percentage this year. In 48 games he piled up 64 hits, 8 doubles, 3 triples, 9 home runs, 22 RBIs and 38 runs scored.

"I'm blown away by the season I was able to put together," he said. "I'm looking forward to trying to do it all again next year."

He received 5 first place votes out of a possible 8. Fingers Ordoñez of the Winnipeg Jr. Thunder finished second in voting with 3 first place votes, while Ray Saenz of the Davenport Rivermen finished third.
