3-Homer Hat Trick for McNeely
Thursday, June 5th, 1930
Fans seated in the bleacher section at Oracle Park were taking cover all day long as Jakkapan McNeely zeroed in on them, rattling three long blasts off the seats in a display of Herculean power.

The big game from the Vancouver third baseman helped the Giants take the victory, 12-4, over the Seattle Rainiers.

"I told myself not to press on the last one," he told reporters in the postgame press conference. "I knew I couldn't force it -- it has to be a nice easy swing."

For the game, he collected 4 hits in 5 at-bats, drove in 5 and scored 3 runs.

Jakkapan McNeely flied out in the 1st, hit a solo-shot off Nigel Adler in the 4th, hit a two-run home run off Matty Braun in the 4th, hit a solo-shot off Matty Braun in the 6th and hit an RBI double in the 8th.

For the year McNeely is hitting .333 with 4 home runs. His stats include 8 runs batted in and 4 runs scored.
