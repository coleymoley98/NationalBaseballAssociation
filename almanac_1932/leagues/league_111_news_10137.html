St-Onge is Best In EBL
Sunday, August 19th, 1928
Wyatt St-Onge took Eastern Baseball League by storm this year, and he did it all at the age of 18.

For his spectacular campaign, the Hamilton Jr. Steelers star has been named the 1928 Eastern Baseball League EBL Player of the Year recipient.

"To be considered amongst the best in the league is overwhelming," said the second baseman.

On the season he compiled a .419 batting average to go with a .471 on-base percentage. The native of Toronto, The United Provinces also added 8 doubles, 3 triples, 4 home runs, 37 RBIs and 36 runs scored.

He received 9 first place votes out of a possible 10. Tawil O'Brien of the Gallant Black Sox finished second in voting with 1 first place vote, while Renaud Ratté of the Fort Wayne Pioneers finished third.
