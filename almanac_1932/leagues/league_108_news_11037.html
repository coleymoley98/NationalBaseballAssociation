Omaha Punchless in 9-0 Loss to Coyotes
Saturday, July 6th, 1929
The Omaha Falcons manager was philosophical in the clubhouse following a shutout loss to the Saskatoon Coyotes. The Falcons were overpowered by Saskatoon starter Alberto Badeau, who hurled his club to victory, 9-0.

"It was just one of those games -- shake it off and do better next time," Kiatisuk Sanders said.

Over 9 innings, Badeau blanked Omaha on 7 hits with 3 walks and 8 strikeouts.

"I kept them off balance. I could tell a lot of them were late on fastballs because they were fouling them off. Once you see that, it sets them up for other pitches," Badeau said.

"I set them up, where if they chased one breaking ball, they were going to chase another one."

To this point in the season Badeau has 4 wins and 4 losses and a 2.62 ERA.
