Albany Shut Out by Mayes
Friday, June 26th, 1931
The Columbus Mets shut out the Albany Rebels 7-0 today behind the strong pitching of Andrew Mayes. Mayes surrendered only 5 hits in recording the complete-game victory, and struck out 7.

Following the last out, Mayes appeared to celebrate a little too boisterously for Albany manager Daniel Harris, who could be seen scolding the famously outspoken right-hander as the clubs left the field. Mayes's own manager then intervened with some words for his counterpart.

The Rebels skipper would not comment on the exchange, but at the Mets press conference, Mayes confirmed that it was regarding the celebration.

"He basically told me I was being a lousy winner," Mayes said.

"And I told him he was being a lousy loser," Columbus manager Tyler Payne added.

This year in 6 starts, Mayes has recorded 3 wins and absorbed 2 losses. He has pitched 36.1 innings, allowed 28 hits, struck out 29 and has a 2.48 ERA.
