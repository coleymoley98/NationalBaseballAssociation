Fort Wayne Moves on to Adams Cup
Tuesday, July 31st, 1928
The Division Finals didn't live up to the billing from those who thought it would be an all-out battle to the last game.

But the Fort Wayne Pioneers are no doubt pleased by the results, as they have knocked out the Buffalo Rapids 3-1 to advance to the Adams Cup.

"It doesn't matter how you win it, as long as you win it and get yourself moving forward," said Pioneers second baseman Xavier Langlais. "I know a lot of people thought this was going to be a really tight series the whole way through, but we're glad it didn't go right down to the wire."

Fort Wayne won the final game by a score of 9-5 at Ballpark of Buffalo.

Langlais was named series MVP after hitting .421 with a .476 on-base percentage. He also had 1 home runs, drove in 5 RBI and scored 5 runs.

The Fort Wayne Pioneers don't know who they will face in the next round. They will play the winner of the Gallant Black Sox - Hamilton Jr. Steelers series. That series is tied at 2 games apiece. The Adams Cup schedule will be announced after the Division Finals finishes.
