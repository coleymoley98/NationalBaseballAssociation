Semple Wins Number 100
Wednesday, May 29th, 1929
His goal at twenty was to win twenty games one year, pitch until he was thirty, win the league pennant and to make some money. Well, Hakim Semple did something even better -- the 31-year-old Minnesota Saints star has now won 100 games in his career. That put him in a very select group of pitchers.

He joined that exclusive club today at Ballpark of Quebec by whipping the Quebec Nordiques 5-1, holding them to 9 hits in 9 innings while chalking up 6 strikeouts and 2 walks.

In the postgame interview, the modest Semple said, "It's special to win 100 games. You don't do it by yourself. You do it with good stuff and a good bullpen. You are only as good as the guys behind you. Years from now I might look back on it and give myself a little pat on the back."

Reviewing his career stats, Semple has worked 1623 innings with 778 strikeouts en route to a 100-71 record. The 31-year-old also has a 3.54 ERA.
