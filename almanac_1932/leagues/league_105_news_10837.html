Blue Sox End Toliver's Streak
Monday, June 10th, 1929
The 20-game hitting streak of Wolfeburg Celtics first baseman Van Toliver came to an end today at Griffith Stadium 1911. The Toronto Blue Sox held him hitless in 3 at-bats and also sent the Celtics down to defeat, 6-5.

Later, Toliver acknowledged to BNN reporters that the hit streak meant something to him.

"It was special," he said, "but I think other people make a bigger deal out of it than I do, or the players do. It was what we're all supposed to do; go out and hit the ball and try to score runs. It was a cool deal, but it's over now. Let's try to start a new one."

In his season totals Toliver has 2 home runs and a .398 batting average.
