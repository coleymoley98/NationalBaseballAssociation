Frontenacs Heading to Riel Cup
Saturday, October 1st, 1932
Chicagou third baseman Luke O'Neill couldn't hide his reaction when asked about being named MVP of the League Chamionship Series.

"It's a nice honor," he said, failing to suppress a wide smile. The trophy was presented to O'Neill moments after his club eliminated the Montreal Royales to advance to the Riel Cup.

Chicagou won the final game by a score of 8-6 at Montreal Grounds and took the series 4-2.

"We won the series and that's the main thing," O'Neill said. "They were a tough team and we didn't want to take anything for granted."

For the series, O'Neill hit .458 with a .500 on-base percentage. He had 0 home runs, drove in 9 RBI and scored 5 runs.

"He was tough to stop and that's a big reason they're there and we're here," said a dejected Montreal manager Hervé Morel.

The Chicagou Frontenacs will play against the Chicagou White Sox in the next round. Chicagou won their series against the Ottawa Indians 4 games to 2. The Riel Cup schedule will be announced after the League Chamionship Series finishes.
