Wale on a Tear, Hits for Cycle
Sunday, June 19th, 1932
Kitsakorn Wale hit for the cycle and paced the Albany Rebels to a 16-1 victory over the Providence Redcoats at Providence Grounds today.

The Rebels right fielder led the way for his club with 5 hits in 6 at-bats -- 2 singles, 1 double, 1 triple and 1 home run -- along with 5 RBI and 5 runs.

"Now that I know what Kitsakorn is capable of, I'm going to expect it more often," Albany manager Bret Kerridge joked with reporters after the game.

Kitsakorn Wale tripled in the 2nd, hit a two-run home run off Ellis Eastman in the 3rd, hit an RBI single in the 4th, flied out in the 6th, singled in the 8th and hit a two-run double in the 9th.

This year Wale has hit at a .379 clip with 19 RBIs, 5 home runs and 24 runs scored.
