Sinclair Selected EBL's Premier Player
Monday, August 15th, 1927
At the age of 18, Eli Sinclair of the North Bay Battalion could very well have his best years ahead of him.

It's a startling thought when you consider the year he had in 1927 was good enough to win the third baseman the Eastern Baseball League EBL Player of the Year.

"See the ball, hit the ball," he said. "That's my basic approach."

The top hitter put up a .342 batting average this year, as well as a .411 on-base percentage. He played 50 games and racked up 64 hits, 14 doubles, 1 triple, 7 home runs, 31 RBIs and 28 runs scored.

He received 4 first place votes out of a possible 10. Mustafa Lachapelle of the Canton Legends finished second in voting with 4 first place votes, while Eldrick Moore of the Fort Wayne Pioneers finished third with 2 first place votes.
