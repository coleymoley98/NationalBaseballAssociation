New York Rebels Win Riel Cup
Friday, October 3rd, 1924
It's party time in New York. The confetti is flying and residents are smiling.

The New York Rebels have reached the mountaintop, capturing the 1924 Riel Cup today. They defeated the Detroit Lions 10-2 at Polo Grounds to officially wrap up the series 4-2.

Asked how it feels to be champion, second baseman Jimmy Gance was to the point.

"It feels great, like a million bucks. I'm so happy right now, not just for me but for all the guys," he said. "We put a lot of work into this and deserve to be champions."

The team is planning a victory parade and fan appreciation day later in the week. New York finished the regular season 69-44 and claimed first place in the Canadian League.
