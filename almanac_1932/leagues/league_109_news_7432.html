Welter Raps 3 HR Against Robins
Saturday, July 3rd, 1926
Fans are still buzzing after Trois-Rivieres whipped the Harrisburg Robins by the score of 22-2 today at Trois-Rivieres Grounds.

Cole Welter smashed three home runs in the contest, a feat that would be a career highlight for most players and few fans ever see. In a tremendous performance, the third baseman bashed out 3 hits in 4 at-bats, had 5 RBIs and scored 4 times.

At the postgame press conference, Welter said it was an "unbelievable" feeling to hit three home runs in a game.

"Getting one homer is special, and two is great," Welter said. "Three is just... wow."

Cole Welter hit a solo-shot off Windsor Helm in the 1st, grounded out in the 3rd, hit a three-run home run off Windsor Helm in the 4th, walked in the 5th, walked in the 6th and hit a solo-shot off Eduardo Medina in the 8th.

This year Welter has 10 home runs and a .238 batting average.
