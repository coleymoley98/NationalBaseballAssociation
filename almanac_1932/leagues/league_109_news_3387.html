5-Hit Game for Gaddis
Friday, August 26th, 1932
It was a sensational day at the plate for Kingston Indians catcher Calvy Gaddis, but a tough one for the team, as the Indians dropped the contest 30-21 to the Brampton Blue Sox.

Even with the loss, Gaddis was pure misery for the Brampton pitching staff. He left them shell-shocked after going 5-for-5 in an explosive display of hitting.

"It's always good to get a bunch of hits in a game, but you also want to win," said Gaddis, who is the 28-year old catcher .

"Gaddis really hit the ball hard and when you do that, you've got a good chance to do some damage," Blue Sox manager Nelson Méthot told BNN reporters after the game. "I feel like we threw him some good pitches, but he just put some good swings on the ball."

Calvy Gaddis singled in the 3rd, hit a two-run single in the 4th, hit a two-run triple in the 6th, hit an RBI single in the 7th and hit an RBI single in the 8th.

To date, Gaddis has collected 53 hits for a .239 average. He has posted 4 home runs, scored 28 runs and driven in 31 runs.
