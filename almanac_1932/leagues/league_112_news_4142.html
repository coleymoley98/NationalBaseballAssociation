Division Remains Up for Grabs, but St. John's in Postseason
Thursday, July 19th, 1923
Celebrating a playoff clinch never gets old, according to Joe Eckes of the St. John's Mariners.

"I could do this each and every year," the starting pitcher said as he celebrated his team officially having clinched a playoff spot. "It's why we play the game - to get to the playoffs and have a chance to become Laurier Cup champions."

The Atlantic club has a record of 31-14 and will be seeking the franchise's 1st Atlantic Baseball League championship.
