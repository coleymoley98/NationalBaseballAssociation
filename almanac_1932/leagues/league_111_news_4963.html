Lepage Collects Cycle
Monday, June 2nd, 1924
It was a special day for A.J. Lepage at Ballpark of Buffalo today. Not only did Lepage's Buffalo Rapids beat the Morgantown Mountaineers 11-9, but the first baseman hit for the cycle along the way.

The 18-year-old Lepage racked up 2 singles, 1 double, 1 triple and 1 home run to get his name in the record books. For the game, he went 5-for-5 with 3 runs batted in and 3 runs scored.

"He hit everything we threw," said Mountaineers manager Logan Hevey. "There was just no getting him out today."

Speaking to the press after the game, Lepage said his goal for the day had been to look for "first pitch fastballs."

"I got some good looks today," he said.

A.J. Lepage tripled in the 1st, hit an RBI single in the 2nd, hit an RBI single in the 4th, doubled in the 6th and hit a solo-shot off Nattaporn Piller in the 8th.
