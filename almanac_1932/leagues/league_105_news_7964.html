New York Wins it All
Sunday, October 3rd, 1926
Sometimes the winner is the one who got the lucky bounces and the lucky calls, and sometimes the winner is just the one who played better.

It was the latter case in 1926, as the New York Rebels topped the Quebec Nordiques 6-2 tonight at Yankee Stadium 1923 to win the Riel Cup. It was the 5th title in franchise history.

"When you play for championships, you have to play better than the team in the other dugout. We did that in this series, and all year. Now we're the best team in baseball," said New York catcher Tommy Ngui, who was named series MVP.

The Rebels took the series 4-3. The new titleholders completed the regular season with a first-place finish in the Canadian League and a 67-45 record.

New York manager Alexis Blouin grinned and nodded when asked to comment on Ngui's words.

"Tommy said it best -- we played better than the other team," he said.

Hometown officials announced they were already planning a "grand celebration" for the winners in the days to come.
