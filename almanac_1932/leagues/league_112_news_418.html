Sparks Big Deal in Montreal: Hits for Cycle
Wednesday, June 11th, 1930
It was a special day for Sir'Anthony Sparks at Ballpark of Montreal today. Not only did Sparks's Montreal Jr. Royales beat the Drummondville Voltigeurs 19-15, but the second baseman hit for the cycle along the way.

The 14-year-old Sparks racked up 1 single, 1 double, 1 triple and 1 home run to get his name in the record books. For the game, he went 4-for-4 with 4 runs batted in and 3 runs scored.

"He hit everything we threw," said Voltigeurs manager Tai Grigsby. "There was just no getting him out today."

Speaking to the press after the game, Sparks said his goal for the day had been to look for "first pitch fastballs."

"I got some good looks today," he said.

Sir'Anthony Sparks hit an RBI triple in the 1st, hit a solo-shot off Mario Gonzalez in the 3rd, hit an RBI single in the 5th and hit an RBI double in the 6th.

For the year Sparks has hit at a .380 clip with 9 RBIs, 1 home run and 10 runs scored.
