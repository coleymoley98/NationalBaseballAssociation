Provost Tops All, Wins WL Most Valuable Player Award
Monday, September 22nd, 1924
If he wasn't already a household name in Western League, Elliot Provost of the Omaha Falcons certainly is now.

He has a bright future ahead of him after today being named the winner of the 1924 Western League Most Valuable Player Award, at the age of only 26.

"I feel like I'm getting better all the time, so I'm looking forward to the years to come," said the ace, who this year put up a 10-4 record.

Overall, Provost made 15 starts and pitched 129.1 innings, racking up 74 strikeouts with 39 walks and a 2.23 ERA.

He received 4 first place votes out of a possible 8. Calvin Boisvert of the Calgary Wranglers finished second in voting with 2 first place votes, while Tommy Audette of the Saskatoon Coyotes finished third with 2 first place votes.
