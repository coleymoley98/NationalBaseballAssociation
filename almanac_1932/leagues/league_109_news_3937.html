Ruckus Reaps 4-Game Suspensions
Sunday, June 24th, 1923
There were a few hotheads at the Sherbrooke-Hamilton game at Nordiques Ballpark yesterday, but the cooler heads prevailed and kept fisticuffs to a minimum. It all started when Washington Golson of the Nordiques and Edgardo Connolly of the Blue Sox apparently exchanged some verbal unpleasantries and rushed at one another and began wrestling on the ground. Both benches emptied, but didn't get involved other than a little shoving. Order was quickly restored, the two instigators were ejected by the umpires and the game continued.

The league office took quick action and today announced that both players would be suspended 4 games for inappropriate on-field behavior.

Golson is hitting .339 this year with 3 home runs and 11 RBIs. He also has an OBP of .431, 8 walks and scored 12 times.

This season Connolly has no wins and no losses and a 4.76 ERA.
