Royales Sweep Boston
Thursday, October 8th, 1931
It was a hard-fought battle for both teams to make it this far. But as the saying goes, "to the victors go the spoils." For the victorious Montreal Royales, that meant the National Baseball Association Riel Cup trophy.

The Royales took a 12-9 victory over the Boston Redcoats at Fenway Park 1912, winning their 9th championship after taking the series 4-0.

"We've got grit," said Royales shortstop Arlo Cunningham, who was named MVP of the series. "We know how to win when we have to."

The new titleholders completed the regular season with a first-place finish in the National League East and a 73-39 record.
