Levasseur Crushes 4 Home Runs
Sunday, July 15th, 1928
Praphas Levasseur of the Hamilton Jr. Steelers had a history-making game today against the North Bay Battalion, clobbering 4 home runs in an awesome display of power in the 10-5 Jr. Steelers victory.

The Hamilton first baseman went 4-for-5, had 7 RBIs and scored 4 times in the game.

"There have been a few times when I consciously tried to hit one out," said Levasseur later. "It doesn't often work, but it did today for the last one. I guess this day was just meant to be."

Praphas Levasseur hit a three-run home run off Paisarn Seangkaew in the 1st, grounded out in the 3rd, hit a solo-shot off Paisarn Seangkaew in the 6th, hit a solo-shot off Chalermpong Amison in the 8th and hit a two-run home run off Chalermpong Amison in the 9th.

A look at his season totals shows Levasseur with 8 home runs, .372 BA, .430 OBP, 26 RBIs and 21 runs scored.
