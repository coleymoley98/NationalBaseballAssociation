Baluch Shuts Out Redcoats, 1-0
Tuesday, September 7th, 1926
Liam Baluch hurled a fine game for the Baltimore Robins at Fenway Park 1912 today, holding the Boston Redcoats to 7 hits in a shutout win.

"When he started out, his command wasn't that good, but he's a tough guy," Baltimore manager Eldin Gabriel said. "The more pitches he throws, the better his command is."

The right-hander had 3 strikeouts and 1 walk in the 1-0 victory.

"An outstanding job," Boston manager Nonthawat Blanchard said. "We couldn't get anything going. Every time we got a hit, he got out of the jam. He pitched a terrific game."

This year Baluch has worked 214.1 innings with 82 strikeouts en route to a 12-13 record and a 4.03 ERA.
