No Easy Feat: Pitcher Wins EL Most Valuable Player Award
Sunday, September 21st, 1924
Not only has Bastien Rioux been named winner of the 1924 Eastern League Most Valuable Player Award, he's done it at an age when many of his peers are still trying to establish themselves in Eastern League.

"I feel fortunate to win this award at age 20," he said. "I feel like I still have my best years ahead of me. Hopefully I can put my name on this trophy a few more times."

The Pittsburgh Liberty ace had a 12-4 record over 21 starts and 130.2 innings. He struck out 134, issued 36 walks and had an impressive 2.27 ERA.

Batters hit only .234 against him during the season.

He received 5 first place votes out of a possible 8. Watcharin Reske of the Pittsburgh Liberty finished second in voting, while Reynold DeShay of the Sherbrooke Nordiques finished third.
