Winnipeg Shuts Out Wranglers, Vega Winner
Tuesday, July 20th, 1926
Kyle Vega of the Winnipeg Thunder was on top of his game today.

The 31-year-old tantalized the Calgary Wranglers with crisp curves and changeups, mixed in with just enough fastballs to keep the batters honest and came away with a 6-0 shutout.

"He had the changeup working," Calgary manager Eric Fitzsimmons said in postgame comments. "You knew it was coming and you still grounded out -- and then he threw it even slower."

The Winnipeg hurler had 7 strikeouts, gave up 1 walk and surrendered 2 hits in his complete-game effort.

Vega told reporters after the game that "keeping the ball down is the key to being successful in this league."

"If you don't do that, they'll make you pay," he said.

To date this season Vega has compiled a 5-3 record with a 3.15 ERA in 8 starts.
