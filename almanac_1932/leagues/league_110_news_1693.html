Davenport Beats Lincoln 7-1, Leclerc Ks 16
Monday, July 6th, 1931
Led by an outstanding performance from starter Oliver Leclerc, the Davenport Rivermen topped the Lincoln Pioneers today by a score of 7-1 at Ballpark of Davenport. The 18-year-old Leclerc brought his top game to the mound, striking out 16 batters while allowing 3 hits and giving up 4 walks.

"He made a lot of good pitches out there," Pioneers manager Arran Laplante said. "He made some of our guys look bad. We didn't help matters, but we really have to give him credit -- he didn't make many mistakes."

In 9 starts this season Leclerc has notched a 6-2 record and a 1.28 ERA.
