Tonkanyaa Goes 5-for-5
Thursday, August 30th, 1928
There aren't many who could be pro ballplayers and there are even fewer who could put up 5 hits in a day.

Atchawut Tonkanyaa is one of those rare few, as he went 5-for-5 against the Seattle Rainiers at Coyotes Ballpark today. The monster effort from the 19-year-old third baseman helped the Saskatoon Coyotes take the win, 16-3.

"We made some bad pitches and he took advantage of it," said Rainiers manager Sebastian Woodhall. "That's the game, though. Don't make mistakes and you'll win ballgames. Make mistakes and, well, you saw what happened today."

Atchawut Tonkanyaa singled in the 1st, walked in the 3rd, singled in the 4th, hit a two-run double in the 6th, singled in the 7th and singled in the 8th.

In his stats this season, Tonkanyaa has posted a batting average of .322 with 49 hits in 152 at-bats, no home runs and 27 RBIs. He has also scored 25 runs, walked 12 times and compiled a .361 OBP.
