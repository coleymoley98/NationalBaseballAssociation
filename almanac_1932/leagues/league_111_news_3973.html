Chambers Rips 3 Homers Against Falcons
Thursday, June 28th, 1923
Toronto Jr. Blue Sox first baseman Calvin Chambers put on a dazzling display of power today at Juniors Ballpark against the Springfield Falcons. In the 14-7 Jr. Blue Sox victory Chambers penned his name into the record books, slamming a trio of home runs in one of baseball's finest single-day efforts.

"He can hit them out anywhere, right, center, left, it doesn't matter," Springfield manager Cal Mentink said. "He's super strong and stays on the ball so well."

Along with his three home runs, the 17-year-old had 3 hits in 5 at-bats, 6 RBIs and 3 runs scored.

"Life will always throw you curves, just keep fouling them off," Chambers said after the game, when asked about his hitting philosophy. "The right pitch will come, and when it does, be prepared to run the bases. Or trot around them, like I did today."

Calvin Chambers grounded out in the 1st, flied out in the 2nd, hit a two-run home run off Matthew Moreland in the 4th, hit a solo-shot off Matthew Moreland in the 6th and hit a two-run home run off Chaiwut Blackburn in the 8th.

Currently Chambers is batting .297 with 30 hits and 5 home runs. He has knocked in 18 runs and scored 17 times.
