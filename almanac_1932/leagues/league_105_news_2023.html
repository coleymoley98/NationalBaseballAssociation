Quakers Take It on Chin, Lose, 10-0, to Philadelphia
Thursday, August 13th, 1931
Eric Chabot of the Philadelphia Liberty was on top of his game today.

The 22-year-old tantalized the Pittsburgh Quakers with crisp curves and changeups, mixed in with just enough fastballs to keep the batters honest and came away with a 10-0 shutout.

"He had the changeup working," Pittsburgh manager Pierre Boutin said in postgame comments. "You knew it was coming and you still grounded out -- and then he threw it even slower."

The Philadelphia hurler had 9 strikeouts, gave up 2 walks and surrendered 7 hits in his complete-game effort.

Chabot told reporters after the game that "keeping the ball down is the key to being successful in this league."

"If you don't do that, they'll make you pay," he said.

Presently Chabot has a 1-3 mark in 4 starts with a 3.86 ERA. He has given up 35 hits in 30.1 innings, struck out 13 and issued 11 walks. Opposing clubs have compiled a .294 batting average against him.
