Star SP White Signs for 7 years
Tuesday, April 29th, 1930
Fans hailed the signing of Davian White today as the Chicagou White Sox starting pitcher came to terms with the club.

Set to make $160,200 over the upcoming 7 years, White signed primarily out of loyalty to the organization, thrilling the most diehard supporters who think there isn't much loyalty in a market driven by free agency.

During his career the 29-year-old White has piled up 879.1 innings. Over that time, he has fanned 252 batters, walked 280 and put up a 3.69 ERA. His lifetime won-lost mark is 54-37 and opposing clubs have hit .266 against him.
