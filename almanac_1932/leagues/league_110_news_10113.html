Winnipeg's Ordoñez Gets Pitcher of the Year Award
Tuesday, August 14th, 1928
This season's Central Baseball League Pitcher of the Year Award trophy went to Fingers Ordoñez, the ace of the Winnipeg pitching staff.

He dominated all season, finishing with 6 wins and 1 loss and a 1.73 ERA. In 10 starts he worked a total of 67.2 innings, striking out 71, while walking 18. Other clubs managed to hit only .170 against him.

He received 7 first place votes out of a possible 8. Ray Saenz of the Davenport Rivermen finished second in voting with 1 first place vote, while Phat Watitapun of the Winnipeg Jr. Thunder finished third.
