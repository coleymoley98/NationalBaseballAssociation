League Suspends Moore and Borish for Fisticuffs
Saturday, June 18th, 1932
Tom Moore of the Pointe-Sainte-Anne Acadiens and Whistler Borish of the Hartford Whalers received notification from the league commissioner's office that they had been suspended for 4 and 8 games respectively for their part in the on-field ruckus in a recent game at Field of the Acadiens.

For the year Moore is batting .176 with 12 hits and no home runs. He has scored 6 times and driven in 5.

Borish has taken the mound for 7 relief appearances this season, nailing down no saves while notching a 1-0 record with a 9.00 ERA. The Hartford reliever has limited opposing batters to a .342 batting average.
