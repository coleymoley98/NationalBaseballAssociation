Liberty Suffer 11-0 Drubbing from Rebels
Thursday, August 23rd, 1923
In a remarkable performance, New York Rebels starter Louis Mongeau beat the Philadelphia Liberty 11-0 in an impressive 4-hit shutout.

"My arm felt good, mechanics were good and my stuff was working," he said later. "It doesn't get any better than this."

The Rebels hurler finished with 6 strikeouts and no walks. He showed pinpoint control and a sharp fastball.

"It was a rough one for my guys," Philadelphia manager Jeremy Janrath said following the game. "We weren't at our best and their guy was."

So far this year Mongeau has made 22 starts, tossed 198.2 innings, struck out 58 batters and walked 48. His record is 16-6 with a 2.76 ERA and he has limited opposing teams to a .254 batting average.
