Jr. Nordiques, Honeywell Stonewall Pointe-Sainte-Anne, 6-0
Friday, July 10th, 1925
Mason Honeywell of the Quebec City Jr. Nordiques spun his magic on the Pointe-Sainte-Anne Acadiens today. He blanked them 6-0 on 4 hits, registering 8 strikeouts and no walks.

"The bottom line was I just kept the ball down," Honeywell said. "My breaking stuff was down. Everything was down."

Talking to reporters in the postgame interview, Quebec City skipper Xavier Dupont said the team "got solid pitching, good defense and took great team at-bats in a lot of different situations."

"Mason pitched really well but this was a solid performance by everyone," he said.

This year Honeywell has a 8-1 mark in 10 starts with a 1.01 ERA. He has given up 51 hits in 80.1 innings, struck out 89 and issued 19 walks. Opposing clubs have compiled a .183 batting average against him.
