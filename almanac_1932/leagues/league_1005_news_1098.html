Haynes Bashes 5 Base Hits
Wednesday, March 4th, 1931
After a 5-hit effort to lead Team's 13-2 victory over the Team Quebec, center fielder Clement Haynes told BNN beat writers it had been "a special day."

"It really doesn't matter whether it's sandlot ball, high school or college, two hits in a game is nice," he said. "Three or four is better, but five or more is out of sight. And to do that at the Canadian Junior Baseball Championship-level is really something special."

The 20-year-old player earned his accolades today at New York Ballpark with a spectacular 5-for-7 game against the Quebec.

"His swing is getting there," said New England skipper Ozzie Tremblay, who spoke glowingly of Haynes after the game. "We want him to be quick with his swing, short and compact. We want him to be patient and go the other way when he can. Clement did that to perfection today."

Clement Haynes singled in the 1st, flied out in the 2nd, hit an RBI single in the 4th, singled in the 5th, singled in the 7th, singled in the 9th and flied out in the 9th.
