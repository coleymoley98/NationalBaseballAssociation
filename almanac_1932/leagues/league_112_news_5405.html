Providence Sweeps into the Laurier Cup
Wednesday, July 30th, 1924
It was a sweet sweep for the Providence Bruins as they eased past the Drummondville Voltigeurs to advance to the Laurier Cup.

Providence won the final game by a 18-9 count and the series 4-0.

"We're just fortunate to get through it quickly and be able to rest up for the next series," said Charles Poulin, who was named series MVP.

For the series, Poulin hit .467 with a .529 on-base percentage. He had 4 home runs, drove in 9 RBI and scored 6 runs.

The Providence Bruins will play against the Montreal Jr. Royales in the next round. Montreal won their series against the Quebec City Jr. Nordiques 4 games to 0. The Laurier Cup schedule will be announced after the Division Finals finishes.
