Montreal Takes Division Finals in Deciding Game
Thursday, August 4th, 1927
The Montreal Jr. Royales are heading to the Laurier Cup, having narrowly defeated the Halifax Highlanders.

Montreal edged Halifax in the series 4-3, with a win at Ballpark of Montreal in the clinching game by a 5-1 margin.

Thurston Armfield turned in a strong offensive performance for the Jr. Royales and got series MVP for his efforts.

"I'll never turn down another trophy for my mantle, another accomplishment to remember once my career is over," he said. "What I would really remember, though, is a Laurier Cup win."

It remains to be seen how the playoffs will conclude, but if Armfield continues to swing a hot bat, his team will no doubt have a chance.

For the series, he hit .357 with a .438 on-base percentage. He had 2 home runs, 6 RBI and scored 7 runs.

The Montreal Jr. Royales will play against the Quincy Gunners in the next round. Quincy won their series against the Hartford Whalers 4 games to 2. The Laurier Cup schedule will be announced after the Division Finals finishes.
