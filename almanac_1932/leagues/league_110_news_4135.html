Jr. Cardinaux Shut Out Jr. Falcons, Zwick Winner
Monday, July 16th, 1923
The St. Louis Jr. Cardinaux shut out the Omaha Jr. Falcons 2-0 today behind the strong pitching of Totchtawan Zwick. Zwick surrendered only 6 hits in recording the complete-game victory, and struck out 6.

Following the last out, Zwick appeared to celebrate a little too boisterously for Omaha manager Jesse O'Byrne, who could be seen scolding the famously outspoken right-hander as the clubs left the field. Zwick's own manager then intervened with some words for his counterpart.

The Jr. Falcons skipper would not comment on the exchange, but at the Jr. Cardinaux press conference, Zwick confirmed that it was regarding the celebration.

"He basically told me I was being a lousy winner," Zwick said.

"And I told him he was being a lousy loser," St. Louis manager Khalil Norrell added.

Checking out his stats for the season, Zwick has registered 4 wins and 3 losses in 11 starts, 2.82 ERA and struck out 74 batters in 73.1 innings.
