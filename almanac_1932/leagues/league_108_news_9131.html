Omaha Pitcher Gets Most Valuable Player Award
Thursday, September 22nd, 1927
Martino Gutierrez said he heard the chants of "MVP! MVP!" during his starts towards the latter half of the season, but he never anticipated he'd actually win the Most Valuable Player Award.

The Omaha Falcons star has in fact won the honor, capturing the 1927 Western League award.

"You always just assume it's going to go to a position player," said the 28-year-old.

But Gutierrez's season demanded recognition, as he was 11-1, with a 2.82 ERA in 13 starts.

He threw 111.2 innings and had 91 strikeouts with 53. Western League hitters struggled all year against the ace, hitting to an average of only .197.

He received 6 first place votes out of a possible 8. Troy McFarland of the Vancouver Giants finished second in voting with 2 first place votes, while Guillaume Beaulieu of the Omaha Falcons finished third.
