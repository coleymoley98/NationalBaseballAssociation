Russ Gunning Captures Prestigious Trophy
Wednesday, September 25th, 1929
He may have less experience than most of his Eastern League peers, but that didn't slow Russ Gunning down at all in 1929.

At the age of 19, the Albany left fielder has been named the winner of the Eastern League Most Valuable Player Award.

He had a .375 batting average and a .429 on-base percentage this year. In 81 games he piled up 118 hits, 25 doubles, 7 triples, 11 home runs, 57 RBIs and 58 runs scored.

"I'm blown away by the season I was able to put together," he said. "I'm looking forward to trying to do it all again next year."

He received 7 first place votes out of a possible 8. Walt Campisano of the Hamilton Blue Sox finished second in voting with 1 first place vote, while Kyle Gandy of the Hamilton Blue Sox finished third.
