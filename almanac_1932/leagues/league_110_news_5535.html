Wintringham Terrific, Takes CBL Most Valuable Player Award
Tuesday, August 19th, 1924
The manager of the Saskatoon Jr. Coyotes said he thinks David Wintringham is only going to get better as he gets older.

He may only be 18, but it will be hard to top the left fielder's 1924 campaign, as he has been named the winner of the Central Baseball League Most Valuable Player Award.

His winning campaign featured a .427 batting average and a .452 on-base percentage this year. In 49 games he piled up 79 hits, 7 doubles, no triples, 31 home runs, 67 RBIs and 44 runs scored.

He received 8 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Logan Fortier of the Adams Mounties finished second in voting, while Landon Gustin of the Saskatoon Jr. Coyotes finished third.
