EL Player of the Week, Ratté of Harrisburg
Monday, July 7th, 1930
It can be difficult for a young player to step out of the shadows and assert himself in the Eastern League, but last week Renaud Ratté of the Harrisburg Robins reminded everyone just why he's here as he threw up numbers that made him an easy selection for Player of the Week. Ratté rocked opposing pitching for a .485 average (16-for-33), 4 home runs and 13 RBIs to come away with the award.

This season Ratté is swinging the bat at a .342 pace. He has hit 6 home runs and scored 30 times, while driving in 27. His on-base percentage is .369 and he has played in 34 games.
