1923 WBL Best Player, Ranier's Girard
Monday, August 13th, 1923
The manager of the Ranier Seahawks said he thinks Remi Girard is only going to get better as he gets older.

He may only be 18, but it will be hard to top the right fielder's 1923 campaign, as he has been named the winner of the Western Baseball League WBL Player of the Year.

His winning campaign featured a .422 batting average and a .513 on-base percentage this year. In 44 games he piled up 70 hits, 20 doubles, no triples, 12 home runs, 40 RBIs and 44 runs scored.

He received 8 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Regis Delorme of the Kelowna Apple Sox finished second in voting, while Barry Leboeuf of the Spokane Eagles finished third.
