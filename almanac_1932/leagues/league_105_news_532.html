Paulson on Fire: 5-for-5 for Detroit
Tuesday, June 24th, 1930
Detroit second baseman Paul Paulson added his name to the record books with a 5-hit spree at Bennett Park today, but found his special moment spoiled when the Montreal Royales took a shootout win over the Lions.

"I would have much preferred getting only a couple of hits and winning the game," he confessed to reporters afterward. "It's hard to go back into the clubhouse and celebrate a 5-hit game when you also lost."

Lions skipper Jake Downing called Paulson's game "a once-in-a-lifetime moment."

"It's a shame the guy can't really enjoy it," he said. "It's also a shame what happened with our pitching today, but at least that we can try to fix."

Paul Paulson singled in the 1st, walked in the 2nd, singled in the 3rd, doubled in the 5th, tripled in the 7th and singled in the 9th.

Thus far this year Paulson has hit .431 with no home runs and 17 RBIs.
