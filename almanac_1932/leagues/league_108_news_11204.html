Giants Chill Cardin's Hot Streak
Thursday, July 25th, 1929
It was the best of both worlds for the Vancouver Giants today at Oracle Park, as they defeated the Winnipeg Thunder 6-1 and also snapped James Cardin's 23-game hitting streak. The 23-year-old Winnipeg left fielder failed to get a hit in 3 at-bats.

"Nothing personal against Cardin, we just wanted to win the game," said Giants skipper Russell Staley. "We did a great job keeping him off-balance and guessing."

When asked by reporters about his streak coming to an end, Cardin frowned and said, "I won't lie and say that I'm not disappointed about it, but I'd feel much better if we had won."

For the year Cardin has hit at a .428 clip with 32 RBIs, 4 home runs and 25 runs scored.
