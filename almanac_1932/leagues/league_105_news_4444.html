Theriot Beats Brasseurs for 100th Win
Thursday, August 30th, 1923
What would you do after you had just won your 100th game? Run around Bennett Park doing cartwheels, jumping for joy and waving to the crowd? Well, maybe you and I would do that, but not Joseph Theriot -- that's not his way.

After he notched number 100, the 35-year-old Detroit hurler spoke to the press like he always has -- in a soft, quiet voice. He wasn't doing any cartwheels or even anything resembling a jump for joy. "Obviously it feels very good," Theriot commented. "To win this many games, you've got to have a lot of help."

Theriot got his milestone victory by beating the Juneau Brasseurs 8-6. He held them to 9 hits in 8 innings and rung up 1 strikeout with 3 walks.

After sharing his thoughts with the press, he went to the clubhouse. There he was greeted by the guys who had helped him get #100. His teammates drenched him and then gave him a rousing round of applause. That got to him -- for the first time today, Theriot's face broke into a big smile.

Since his debut, Theriot has marked up 100 wins and 81 losses and fashioned an ERA of 2.85. He has fanned 858 batters while pitching 1714.2 innings.
