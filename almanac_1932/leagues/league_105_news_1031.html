Hall of Fame Inducts Signoret
Wednesday, January 7th, 1931
There is a new statue in the National Baseball Association Hall of Fame.

It belongs Stephan Signoret. The star pitcher was inducted this past weekend. All it took to get there was 153 wins.

"I always dreamed of being a National Baseball Association player, but I never dreamed of being in the Hall of Fame," Signoret said, "I could have never envisioned myself standing next to my childhood idols and yet here I am. And to the Hall of Famers sitting behind me, I am truly humbled and in awe of standing here in front of you."

In his career, two-time Pitcher of the Year Award winner Stephan Signoret posted a record of 153-153 and fanned 1475 batters in 2927.1 innings with a 2.89 ERA.

At times Stephan Signoret fought back tears as he thanked his family and close friends for sticking by him when he had to overcome some difficult personal problems.

Summing up his years on the diamond, Signoret offered a hopeful message to the large induction crowd.

"I care for this game with my heart and soul. I dedicated my life to being the best pitcher I could be. You leave me humbled and grateful for this honor. I'd like to leave an offering of a message of hope. That is, with the grace of God, you can change your life, whoever you are."
