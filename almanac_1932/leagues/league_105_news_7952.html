Rebels Win League Championship Series
Wednesday, September 22nd, 1926
The New York Rebels clubhouse was filled with glee and celebration tonight, while the Philadelphia Liberty room was filled with despair and sadness after the League Championship Series concluded.

"Someone's got to win and someone's got to lose, we're just glad we're on the good end of it tonight and can enjoy this," said Trent Norrell, who was named MVP for a strong series of offensive performance.

New York won the final game by a 6-5 count at Yankee Stadium 1923 and the series 4-2.

Philadelphia manager Hervé Morel was simple and to-the-point in his assessment of the series.

"They were the better team and deserve to move on," he said.

For the series, Norrell hit .409 with a .458 on-base percentage. He had 2 home runs, drove in 6 RBI and scored 5 runs.

The New York Rebels don't know who they will face in the next round. They will play the winner of the Quebec Nordiques - Detroit Lions series. Quebec leads the series 3 games to 2. The Riel Cup schedule will be announced after the League Championship Series finishes.
