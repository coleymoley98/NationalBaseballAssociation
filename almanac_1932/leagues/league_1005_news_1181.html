Rogers Selected 1931 CJBT Most Valuable Player Award
Sunday, April 5th, 1931
The manager of the Team Iowa said he thinks Robert Rogers is only going to get better as he gets older.

He may only be 20, but it will be hard to top the first baseman's 1931 campaign, as he has been named the winner of the Canadian Junior Baseball Championship Most Valuable Player Award.

His winning campaign featured a .549 batting average and a .606 on-base percentage this year. In 19 games he piled up 45 hits, 9 doubles, 4 triples, no home runs, 21 RBIs and 27 runs scored.

He received 6 first place votes out of a possible 18. Xavier Langlais of the Team Michigan finished second in voting with 6 first place votes, while Felix Nadeau of the Team Erie finished third.
