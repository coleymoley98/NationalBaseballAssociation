Rainiers Win Tight Race
Friday, August 30th, 1929
The manager of the Seattle Rainiers said "a lot of hard work, talent and luck" are the reasons for his team's championship.

"This club believes in themselves and goes out and gets it done," said Sebastian Woodhall. "You can't ask for much more than that as a manager."

It's the club's 2nd division crown and gives them an opportunity to win the franchise's 1st World Series.

"We want that title badly," said starting pitcher Ansley LaVallee as he took a break from celebrating with teammates. "This is just a start."
