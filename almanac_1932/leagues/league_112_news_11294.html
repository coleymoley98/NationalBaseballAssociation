Halifax Takes Division Finals in Deciding Game
Monday, August 5th, 1929
The Halifax Highlanders and Champlain Coureurs held nothing back in an entertaining Division Finals that went the distance.

In the end, it will be Halifax advancing to the Laurier Cup, after a 4-3 triumph in the final game at Juniors Ballpark, to wrap up the series 4-3.

The Highlanders players celebrated jubilantly in their clubhouse after the game, while the scene was one of dismay on the opposing side for the Coureurs.

Ruell Blanchette was named series MVP for Halifax. For the series, Blanchette hit .519 with a .545 on-base percentage. He had 0 home runs, drove in 6 RBI and scored 3 runs.

The Halifax Highlanders will play against the Drummondville Voltigeurs in the next round. Drummondville won their series against the Pawtucket Red Sox 4 games to 1. The Laurier Cup schedule will be announced after the Division Finals finishes.
