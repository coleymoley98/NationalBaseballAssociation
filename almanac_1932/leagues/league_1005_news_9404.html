Great Day for Levy -- 6 Hits
Wednesday, March 21st, 1928
After a 6-hit effort to lead Team's 26-16 victory over the Team Cordillera, right fielder Ratchapak Levy told BNN beat writers it had been "a special day."

"It really doesn't matter whether it's sandlot ball, high school or college, two hits in a game is nice," he said. "Three or four is better, but five or more is out of sight. And to do that at the Canadian Junior Baseball Championship-level is really something special."

The 22-year-old player earned his accolades today at Los Angeles Grounds with a spectacular 6-for-7 game against the Cordillera.

"His swing is getting there," said Illinois skipper Luke Kallstrom, who spoke glowingly of Levy after the game. "We want him to be quick with his swing, short and compact. We want him to be patient and go the other way when he can. Ratchapak did that to perfection today."

Ratchapak Levy grounded out in the 1st, singled in the 4th, singled in the 5th, hit an RBI single in the 5th, singled in the 7th, hit a three-run double in the 7th and hit a two-run single in the 9th.
