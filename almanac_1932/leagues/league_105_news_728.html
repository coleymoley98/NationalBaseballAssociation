5-for-6 Day for Failai
Monday, July 14th, 1930
After a 5-hit effort to lead Minnesota's 14-5 victory over the St. Louis Cardinaux, second baseman Issara Failai told BNN beat writers it had been "a special day."

"It really doesn't matter whether it's sandlot ball, high school or college, two hits in a game is nice," he said. "Three or four is better, but five or more is out of sight. And to do that at the National Baseball Association-level is really something special."

The 23-year-old player earned his accolades today at St. Louis Grounds with a spectacular 5-for-6 game against the Cardinaux.

"His swing is getting there," said Saints skipper A.J. Provost, who spoke glowingly of Failai after the game. "We want him to be quick with his swing, short and compact. We want him to be patient and go the other way when he can. Issara did that to perfection today."

Issara Failai hit an RBI single in the 1st, hit a two-run triple in the 2nd, grounded out in the 4th, hit a two-run home run off Tony Doolin in the 6th, hit an RBI double in the 7th and singled in the 9th.

Presently Failai is batting .262 and has collected 1 home run, 33 RBIs, .318 OBP and scored 31 runs.
