Ridin' High: Kitano Hits for Cycle
Wednesday, August 17th, 1927
It was a special day for Akihiro Kitano at Oracle Park today. Not only did Kitano's Edmonton Eskimos beat the Vancouver Giants 8-7, but the first baseman hit for the cycle along the way.

The 25-year-old Kitano racked up 1 single, 1 double, 1 triple and 1 home run to get his name in the record books. For the game, he went 4-for-6 with 2 runs batted in and 3 runs scored.

"He hit everything we threw," said Giants manager Tony Green. "There was just no getting him out today."

Speaking to the press after the game, Kitano said his goal for the day had been to look for "first pitch fastballs."

"I got some good looks today," he said.

Akihiro Kitano doubled in the 1st, struck out in the 2nd, hit a solo-shot off Lee Colato in the 4th, grounded out in the 6th, hit an RBI triple in the 8th and singled in the 9th.

This season Kitano has 3 home runs and a .332 batting average.
