Tuesday, July 10th, 1928
It was not a good day at the plate for the St. Louis Cardinaux. They came up empty when it mattered against Detroit Lions right-hander Tadanori Kamai, who scattered 7 hits across 9 innings and held the Cardinaux scoreless.

"We had 7 hits so I know he wasn't completely untouchable, but he was close," St. Louis manager Miguel Roque said later.

Kamai chalked up 4 strikeouts and 1 walk in the 1-0 Lions win.

In 11 starts this season Kamai has chalked up a 3-7 record and a 3.54 ERA.
