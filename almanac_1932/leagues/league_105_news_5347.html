Champagne Dominates in 11-5 Win over Canadian League All-Stars
Tuesday, July 22nd, 1924
Thanit Champagne said the annual all-star game is mostly for the fans to enjoy, but the players have a lot of fun too.

"It's a great time to get that much talent on one ball diamond," he said. "You feel fortunate to even be part of it."

Champagne wasn't just a part of the game, he was named the game MVP, as his National League All-Stars won 11-5 over the Canadian League All-Stars.

Champagne went 2 for 2 in the game to earn the nod as the game's best.

Champagne has a career .324 batting average with a .402 OBP. He has hit 8 homers, driven in 84 runs and scored 112 runs since he came into the league.
