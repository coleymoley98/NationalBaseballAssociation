Lions Listless in 9-0 Loss to Cardinaux
Thursday, May 26th, 1932
Jacob McGowan of the St. Louis Cardinaux spun his magic on the Detroit Lions today. He blanked them 9-0 on 4 hits, registering 5 strikeouts and no walks.

"The bottom line was I just kept the ball down," McGowan said. "My breaking stuff was down. Everything was down."

Talking to reporters in the postgame interview, St. Louis skipper Virgil McLain said the team "got solid pitching, good defense and took great team at-bats in a lot of different situations."

"Jacob pitched really well but this was a solid performance by everyone," he said.

Up to now McGowan has recorded a record of 4-1. In 5 starts he has fanned 19 batters in 41.1 innings with a 2.61 ERA.
