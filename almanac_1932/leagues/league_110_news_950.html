Lacroix Grabs CBL Reliever of the Year Award
Tuesday, August 12th, 1930
At the age of 17, Anuwat Lacroix says he still has a lot to learn about life in Central Baseball League.

But he certainly seems to be a quick study, as the Adams Mounties reliever was better than all others this year and has captured the 1930 Central Baseball League Reliever of the Year Award.

"I hope to win this a few more times before all is said and done in my career," the Big Beaver House, The United Provinces native said.

For the season, Lacroix had 3 saves in 15 relief appearances, which totaled 29. He fanned 27 betters, while walking 9 and put up a record of 5-0 to go with his 1.55 ERA.

He received 8 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. François Fortin of the Adams Mounties finished second in voting, while Bilal Horner of the Omaha Jr. Falcons finished third.
