Shearer Whitewashes Canton
Tuesday, June 28th, 1927
Kian Shearer of the Hamilton Jr. Steelers was on top of his game today.

The 16-year-old tantalized the Canton Legends with crisp curves and changeups, mixed in with just enough fastballs to keep the batters honest and came away with a 2-0 shutout.

"He had the changeup working," Canton manager Sean Wilkinson said in postgame comments. "You knew it was coming and you still grounded out -- and then he threw it even slower."

The Hamilton hurler had 8 strikeouts, gave up 1 walk and surrendered 5 hits in his complete-game effort.

Shearer told reporters after the game that "keeping the ball down is the key to being successful in this league."

"If you don't do that, they'll make you pay," he said.

Shearer has made 6 starts so far this year and put up a 1-4 won-lost record with a 2.01 ERA. In 49.1 innings he has allowed 44 hits, walked 8 and struck out 50.
