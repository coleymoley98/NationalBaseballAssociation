Powers Twirls No-Hitter Against Rivermen
Sunday, July 10th, 1927
The Adams Mounties were just hoping for a solid outing from Millard Powers. Instead, they got something special.

The 18-year-old hurler tossed a no-hitter against the Davenport Rivermen, helping the Mounties take the 4-0 win.

"I felt good throughout the game," he said after his spectacular outing for the Mounties. "As I was going into the ninth inning, I wasn't nervous. I knew I had a chance to do something special."

Powers's repertoire has a cutter, changeup, and slider, and he used it all to full advantage, keeping the Davenport hitters off balance all game long. The Adams hurler fanned 7 and walked 0 in his history-making performance.

"His cutter looked about the size of a watermelon seed and it hissed at you as it passed," Davenport manager Hudson Cranford joked after the game.

Currently Powers has a record of 4-1 with 60 strikeouts and 11 walks in 63 innings and a 2.00 ERA.
