Jr. Falcons Spank Jr. Coyotes, 10-0
Sunday, June 29th, 1924
The Omaha Jr. Falcons shut out the Saskatoon Jr. Coyotes 10-0 today behind the strong pitching of Tony Gonzalez. Gonzalez surrendered only 4 hits in recording the complete-game victory, and struck out 7.

Following the last out, Gonzalez appeared to celebrate a little too boisterously for Saskatoon manager Kerry Pope, who could be seen scolding the famously outspoken left-hander as the clubs left the field. Gonzalez's own manager then intervened with some words for his counterpart.

The Jr. Coyotes skipper would not comment on the exchange, but at the Jr. Falcons press conference, Gonzalez confirmed that it was regarding the celebration.

"He basically told me I was being a lousy winner," Gonzalez said.

"And I told him he was being a lousy loser," Omaha manager Jesse O'Byrne added.

On his stats sheet this season Gonzalez shows a 4-2 record, 7 starts, 51.2 innings, 51 strikeouts and a 2.96 ERA.
