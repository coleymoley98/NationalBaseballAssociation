Boston Moves on to Riel Cup
Sunday, September 16th, 1928
The Boston Redcoats are riding high after winning the League Championship Series and eliminating the Toronto Blue Sox by a series score of 4-1.

The final blow came tonight at Griffith Stadium 1911, when Boston secured its spot in the Riel Cup with a 7-5 victory.

"It feels good to put them away because we really didn't want to let them crawl back into this series," said first baseman Phuwanart McInnis, who was named series MVP. "A team like that, you need to bury them when you get the chance."

McInnis certainly helped his team's cause, as he hit .350 with a .350 on-base percentage in the series. He also chipped in 3 home runs, drove in 12 RBI and scored 5 runs.

The Boston Redcoats will play against the Montreal Royales in the next round. Montreal won their series against the Chicagou Frontenacs 4 games to 0. The Riel Cup schedule will be announced after the League Championship Series finishes.
