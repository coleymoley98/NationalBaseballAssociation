Pitcher of the Year Award Goes to Quebec Hurler
Sunday, October 17th, 1926
The votes have been tabulated for the 1926 National League Pitcher of the Year Award. It has been given to Giovanni Ratanaveroj of the Quebec Nordiques, who piled up 15 wins this season with an impressive 2.42 ERA.

In 18 starts Ratanaveroj had a 15-4 mark with 38 strikeouts in 156 innings.

He received 12 first place votes out of a possible 16. Hakim Semple of the Minnesota Saints finished second in voting with 3 first place votes, while Daniel Ibarra of the Quebec Nordiques finished third.
