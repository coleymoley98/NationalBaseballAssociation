Redcoats Win Tight Canadian League Race
Monday, September 10th, 1928
The Boston Redcoats celebrated their Canadian League championship tonight, but relief was more readily visible than jubilation for the players.

"The whole season is such a long battle, so to take the division in the final day is really remarkable," said left fielder Seamus Sexauer. "It honestly just feels like a huge weight is off all our shoulders. Now we can relax and focus on the playoffs."

But of course, the playoffs won't be without a fair bit of stress and anxiety too. But team manager Brian Ahn said he believes his team is good under pressure and will rise to the occasion.

"These guys are battle tested," he said.

This was the 11th Canadian League title in team history.
