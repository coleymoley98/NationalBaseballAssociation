Saskatoon Wins it All
Monday, September 10th, 1928
The Saskatoon Coyotes were good enough to get past the Edmonton Eskimos at Edmonton Grounds today and secure the 2nd championship in franchise history.

"When you wear Coyotes on your shirt, you're good at something," Saskatoon skipper Maurice Wigglesworth told the press.

Coyotes center fielder Shea Rowlinson was named MVP of the series after he went .400 with 2 home runs and 12 runs batted in.

"It feels totally incredible," Rowlinson said. "I could live to 1,000 years old and I'd remember this moment."

The new champions capped off a great season with a 5-2 victory over the Eskimos to win the World Series 4-3.
