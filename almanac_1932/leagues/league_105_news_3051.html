David Wintringham Wins MVP in National League Victory over Canadian League
Friday, July 15th, 1932
Despite his best efforts, game MVP David Wintringham's Canadian League All-Stars couldn't overcome the powerful National League squad in the annual All-Star Game.

"We tried our best, but did you see the talent in that lineup?" Wintringham was quoted as saying after the game. "I'm honored to be named MVP of the game. To stand among the greatest stars of the game and be declared the best among them is truly an honor, even if we didn't end up winning the game. I had fun, and I think the fans did as well."

The National League All-Stars triumphed in the game by a final score of 5-4 in this year's game at Sportsmans Park 1911.

Lifetime Wintringham has played in 559 games, batted .367 with 162 home runs and 850 base hits.
