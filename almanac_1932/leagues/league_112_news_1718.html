Homen Befuddles Providence in 3-0 New Britain Win
Sunday, July 12th, 1931
Before the game, Jiradej Homen said he liked his chances. His instincts proved to be right. In a superb outing at Juniors Ballpark, the Yankees pitcher whitewashed the Providence Bruins 3-0.

"I just had a feeling it was going to be my day," Homen said in the clubhouse afterward. "My control was awesome. I could throw my curveball even when I was behind in the count."

The New Britain starter held Providence scoreless, giving up 6 hits while registering 6 strikeouts and issuing 1 walk.

"We didn't have very many good at-bats," Bruins manager Marshall Kirkpatrick said in his postgame interview. "When that happens, you usually lose."

This season Homen has compiled a 3-5 record with a 3.39 ERA.
