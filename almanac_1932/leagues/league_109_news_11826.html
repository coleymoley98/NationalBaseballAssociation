Smyser Canned as Manager by Tecumseh
Saturday, October 4th, 1930
A "difference of opinion" has cost Ross Smyser his job as manager of the Tecumseh Braves.

Following a postgame meeting with front office officials and ownership, Smyser left the ballpark without addressing reporters. It was then revealed by the team's general manager that Smyser had been dismissed from the club. Few details are available on the firing at this time.

"Basically, it just came down to a difference of philosophy between Ross and management," general manager Joe Unknown said.

When reached for comment at his Tecumseh home, Smyser refused to comment on the firing other than to say, "We just mutually parted ways. That's it."

The club will begin a search for Smyser's successor immediately. Several interviews have already been set up, but team officials declined to say who is on their list of possible replacements.
