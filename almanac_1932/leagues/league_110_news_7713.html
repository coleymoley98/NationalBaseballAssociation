Jr. Thunder Win Dumont Cup Crown
Thursday, August 5th, 1926
Sometimes the winner is the one who got the lucky bounces and the lucky calls, and sometimes the winner is just the one who played better.

It was the latter case in 1926, as the Winnipeg Jr. Thunder topped the Saskatoon Jr. Coyotes 4-3 tonight at Jr. Thunder Ballpark to win the Dumont Cup. It was the 1st title in franchise history.

"When you play for championships, you have to play better than the team in the other dugout. We did that in this series, and all year. Now we're the best team in baseball," said Winnipeg third baseman Jordan Diomede, who was named series MVP.

The Jr. Thunder took the series 4-3. The new titleholders completed the regular season with a first-place finish in the and a 31-20 record.

Winnipeg manager Rafaël Bérubé grinned and nodded when asked to comment on Diomede's words.

"Jordan said it best -- we played better than the other team," he said.

Hometown officials announced they were already planning a "grand celebration" for the winners in the days to come.
