Super Season, Michaud Wins ABL Player of the Year
Thursday, August 20th, 1931
Anthony Michaud of the Pointe-Sainte-Anne Acadiens had a fine season this year and today he accepted the ABL Player of the Year for 1931.

The 17-year-old left fielder put up a .431 average and a .468 on-base percentage, piling up 84 hits, 12 doubles, 4 triples, 10 home runs, 46 RBIs and 49 runs scored.

He received 14 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Eli Leven of the Providence Bruins finished second in voting, while Worthep Leduc of the Pointe-Sainte-Anne Acadiens finished third.
