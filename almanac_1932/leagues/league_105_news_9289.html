Tuptimtong Honored by First Ballot Selection to NBA Hall of Fame
Wednesday, January 4th, 1928
During his fantastic pitching career Ittipol Tuptimtong was hard-nosed competitor. Prior to an important game, he once told reporters, "If we lose today, it will be over my dead body. They'll have to leave me face down on the mound."

It was this attitude that helped to inspire Tuptimtong to such an outstanding career. Today he was rewarded with his admission into the National Baseball Association Hall of Fame.

Tuptimtong retired from baseball at age 35 and fashioned a 147-99 won-lost record in his career with a 2.10 ERA. The gifted hurler appeared in 313 games, fanned 1106 hitters and piled up 2360 innings pitched.

One of his managers at the induction ceremony observed, "Ittipol was a bulldog on the mound. If a man put a gun to my head and said I'm going to pull the trigger, if you lose this game, I'd want him to pitch that game."
