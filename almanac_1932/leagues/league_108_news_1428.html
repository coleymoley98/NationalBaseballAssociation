Big Day with Bat: Vilain 5-for-7
Wednesday, June 10th, 1931
After a 5-hit effort to lead Saskatoon's 12-9 victory over the Seattle Rainiers, center fielder Etienne Vilain told BNN beat writers it had been "a special day."

"It really doesn't matter whether it's sandlot ball, high school or college, two hits in a game is nice," he said. "Three or four is better, but five or more is out of sight. And to do that at the Western League-level is really something special."

The 21-year-old player earned his accolades today at Ballpark of Seattle with a spectacular 5-for-7 game against the Rainiers.

"His swing is getting there," said Coyotes skipper Peerapong Owens, who spoke glowingly of Vilain after the game. "We want him to be quick with his swing, short and compact. We want him to be patient and go the other way when he can. Etienne did that to perfection today."

Etienne Vilain singled in the 1st, grounded out in the 2nd, hit a two-run home run off Mark Dukes in the 4th, singled in the 6th, singled in the 8th, hit an RBI triple in the 9th and flied out in the 10th.

This season Vilain is batting .333 with 2 home runs. His totals include 8 RBIs and 5 runs scored.
