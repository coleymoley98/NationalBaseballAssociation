Wilder Goes 5-for-7 Against Ottawa
Wednesday, May 6th, 1931
Harry Wilder knows as well as anyone that in baseball, the biggest contracts go to the biggest hitters. At Yankee Stadium 1923 today, he made his case to be considered among that group.

The 25-year-old center fielder hammered 5 hits in 7 chances, though the remarkable effort came even as his New York Rebels fell, 22-20, to the Ottawa Indians.

"We made some bad pitches and he took advantage of it," Indians manager Frank Boon told BNN sportswriters after the game. "Thankfully, we were able to do enough elsewhere that Wilder wasn't the difference."

Harry Wilder singled in the 1st, hit an RBI double in the 2nd, hit an RBI single in the 2nd, hit a three-run home run off Samuel Léveillé in the 4th, flied out in the 5th, flied out in the 7th and singled in the 9th.
