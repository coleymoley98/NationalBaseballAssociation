Monday, October 14th, 1929
The manager of the Montreal Royales said he thinks Valéry Jetté is only going to get better as he gets older.

He may only be 25, but it will be hard to top the third baseman's 1929 campaign, as he has been named the winner of the National League Most Valuable Player Award.

His winning campaign featured a .326 batting average and a .400 on-base percentage this year. In 109 games he piled up 133 hits, 16 doubles, 9 triples, 10 home runs, 72 RBIs and 75 runs scored.

He received 4 first place votes out of a possible 16. Watcharapol Tremblay of the Chicagou Frontenacs finished second in voting with 5 first place votes, while Taradin Boulay of the Minnesota Saints finished third with 1 first place vote.
