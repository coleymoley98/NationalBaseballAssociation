Gaumer Torches Rebels for 5 Hits
Sunday, August 31st, 1930
It was a good day at Lincoln Park. The Ottawa Indians whipped the New York Rebels 16-15 and third baseman Zach Gaumer showed off a hot bat, going 5-for-6 at the plate.

"Some days, lady luck is on your side," Gaumer told BNN reporters after the game.

Indians manager Frank Boon told the beat writers otherwise, calling the 23-year-old "focused and patient."

"It's not luck," he said. "He has a solid approach to hitting. Zach hits to all fields. The more solid at-bats you put together, the more success you're going to have."

Zach Gaumer hit an RBI double in the 1st, hit a two-run single in the 2nd, grounded out in the 4th, hit a solo-shot off Kittisak Wiles in the 7th, singled in the 8th, singled in the 9th and walked in the 11th.

Presently Gaumer is batting .330 with 7 home runs and 76 RBIs. He has played in 94 games and scored 58 times.
