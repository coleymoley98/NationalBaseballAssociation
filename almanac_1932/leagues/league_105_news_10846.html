Chicagou's Le Roy Goes Ballistic with 5 Hits
Tuesday, June 11th, 1929
There aren't many who could be pro ballplayers and there are even fewer who could put up 5 hits in a day.

Marc Le Roy is one of those rare few, as he went 5-for-5 against the Cincinnati Reds at Great American Ball Park today. The monster effort from the 26-year-old second baseman helped the Chicagou Frontenacs take the win, 7-3.

"We made some bad pitches and he took advantage of it," said Reds manager Hoot Sinkus. "That's the game, though. Don't make mistakes and you'll win ballgames. Make mistakes and, well, you saw what happened today."

Marc Le Roy singled in the 1st, singled in the 3rd, singled in the 5th, doubled in the 7th and hit an RBI triple in the 9th.

So far on the season Le Roy is hitting .304 with no home runs, 12 RBIs and 18 runs scored. He has 35 hits and played in 27 games.
