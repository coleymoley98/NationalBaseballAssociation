T.J. Andrews Nabs Prestigious Pitcher of the Year Award
Wednesday, October 14th, 1925
The 1925 National League Pitcher of the Year Award has gone to Juneau Brasseurs ace T.J. Andrews after a stellar 16-11 season.

In 25 starts he piled up 222.1 innings, 88 strikeouts, 47 walks and a 3.04 ERA. Andrews held opposing hitters to a .268 batting average.

He received 8 first place votes out of a possible 16. Caleb Boisvert of the Chicagou Frontenacs finished second in voting with 7 first place votes, while Kitsada Richardson of the Minnesota Saints finished third with 1 first place vote.
