Santo Vivas Wins 1926 CL Batting Crown
Tuesday, September 14th, 1926
The numbers speak for themselves: a .395 batting average in 87 games, 126 hits, 22 doubles, no triples, 13 home runs, 67 RBIs, 66 runs scored and 52 walks.

Those numbers added up to a batting title for Santo Vivas of the Toronto Blue Sox.

The 26-year-old left fielder received the 1926 Canadian League batting trophy today. At the award ceremony, Vivas was definitely proud of his achievement.

"I always knew that I had the talent to win a batting title," Vivas commented to reporters, "but I've had trouble at times with consistency. Everything came together this season. I hope this is not the last time. It will be a challenge, but I hope to win it again."
