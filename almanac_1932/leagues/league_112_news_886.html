Bruins Advance to Laurier Cup
Saturday, August 2nd, 1930
The Division Finals may not have lived up to the expectation that it would be a dogfight that would go the distance. The Providence Bruins, however, are not complaining.

The Bruins were plenty happy to have wrapped up the series early and to have knocked out the Pointe-Sainte-Anne Acadiens to advance to the Laurier Cup.

Providence won the final game by a 16-12 count at Field of the Acadiens and the series 4-1.

Bruins catcher Warayu Collier was named series MVP, as he hit .478 with a .478 on-base percentage. He added 0 home runs, drove in 15 RBI and scored 6 runs.

"It's not going to be the last time Pointe-Sainte-Anne makes it to the playoffs, so it's sure a nice feeling to put them out this year," Collier said. "We just need to keep this going and take it day by day."

The Providence Bruins will play against the St. John's Mariners in the next round. St. John's won their series against the Quebec City Jr. Nordiques 4 games to 0. The Laurier Cup schedule will be announced after the Division Finals finishes.
