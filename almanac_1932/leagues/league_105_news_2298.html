National League Names Top Player
Friday, October 23rd, 1931
Wherever Arlo Cunningham signs on next year, the shortstop will be hauling home a good paycheck after the impressive season he just wrapped up. Cunningham received word of his National League Most Valuable Player Award selection earlier today. That should make him a valuable commodity for next year.

In 1931 he boasted a .322 average and a .371 on-base percentage as he piled up 148 hits, 28 doubles, 19 triples, 1 home run, 60 RBIs and 87 runs scored.

He received 15 first place votes out of a possible 20. Panuwat Sumonrat of the Detroit Lions finished second in voting with 4 first place votes, while Taradin Boulay of the Minnesota Saints finished third with 1 first place vote.
