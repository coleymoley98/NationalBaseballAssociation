Reliever of the Year Award Goes to Guglielmo
Monday, October 19th, 1931
They say you can't teach an old dog new tricks, but in the case of Zach Guglielmo of the Chicagou White Sox, you don't have to - his old tricks worked just fine in 1931.

The 35-year-old veteran turned in a sparkling performance and has won the Canadian League Reliever of the Year Award.

He captured the award with 9 saves in 37 relief appearances, 11 strikeouts and 17 walks in 59.2 innings. He put up a record of 6-3 to go with his 2.72 ERA.

He received 20 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Zayden Lange of the Wolfeburg Celtics finished second in voting, while Crawford Jones of the New York Rebels finished third.
