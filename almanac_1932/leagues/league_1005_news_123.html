Wharton 5-for-5 for New England
Saturday, March 22nd, 1930
There aren't many who could be pro ballplayers and there are even fewer who could put up 5 hits in a day.

Jaxon Wharton is one of those rare few, as he went 5-for-5 against the Team Oregon at Tokyo Memorial Coliseum today. The monster effort from the 20-year-old shortstop helped the Team New England take the win, 14-5.

"We made some bad pitches and he took advantage of it," said Oregon manager Ethan Doyon. "That's the game, though. Don't make mistakes and you'll win ballgames. Make mistakes and, well, you saw what happened today."

Jaxon Wharton doubled in the 1st, singled in the 3rd, hit a two-run double in the 4th, hit an RBI double in the 5th, singled in the 8th and walked in the 9th.

So far this season Wharton is batting .361 with 2 home runs, 13 RBIs and 19 runs scored. He has 22 hits in 61 at-bats, 12 walks and a .480 OBP. Wharton has played in 14 games.
