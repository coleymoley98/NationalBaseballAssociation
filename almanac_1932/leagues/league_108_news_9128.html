WL Pitcher of the Year Award Announced
Wednesday, September 21st, 1927
A sparkling 11-1 mark for the year just about says it all -- that's what Martino Gutierrez of the Omaha Falcons put up this season. That's why the Western League rated him the #1 hurler in the league and named him the 1927 Pitcher of the Year Award winner.

He held opposing clubs to a .197 batting average and compiled a 2.82 ERA. Gutierrez stood out with 91 strikeouts while giving up 79 hits and 53 walks in 111.2 innings.

He received 7 first place votes out of a possible 8. Charles-Olivier King of the Calgary Wranglers finished second in voting with 1 first place vote, while Guillaume Beaulieu of the Omaha Falcons finished third.
