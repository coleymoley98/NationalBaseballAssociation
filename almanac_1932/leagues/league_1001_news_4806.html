Jetté Eclipses Chamberland with Hit
Saturday, March 22nd, 1924
Records are made to be broken. Case in point: the career hits record.

Once owned by Julien Chamberland, the Canadian Senior Baseball Tournament record for total base hits in a career is now held by third baseman Valéry Jetté of the Team Columbia. Jetté established a new record today, recording hit number 19.

"It's humbling to even be mentioned in the same breath as Julien Chamberland," said Jetté. "I consider him a hero -- one of the all-time greats."

Valéry Jetté singled in the 1st, flied out in the 2nd and singled in the 5th.

In 9 games this season Jetté has batted at a .487 pace with 19 hits and no home runs.
