Jr. Steelers Prevail, as Division Finals Goes to the Wire
Thursday, August 2nd, 1928
The Hamilton Jr. Steelers and Gallant Black Sox didn't hold back anything in the Division Finals, with each team pulling out all the stops in an effort to advance to the Adams Cup.

Hamilton won the final game by a score of 3-2 at Jr. Steelers Ballpark and that concluded the series, 3-2.

"Relief is the biggest thing," Hamilton first baseman Praphas Levasseur said when asked how he felt. "It's a grind both mentally and physically to have a long series like this, but our guys stuck with it and Gallant deserves credit for the hard fight."

Levasseur paced the Hamilton offence and earned MVP honors. For the series, he hit .409 with a .417 on-base percentage, 2 home runs, 11 RBI and scored 8 runs.

The Hamilton Jr. Steelers will play against the Fort Wayne Pioneers in the next round. Fort Wayne won their series against the Buffalo Rapids 3 games to 1. The Adams Cup schedule will be announced after the Division Finals finishes.
