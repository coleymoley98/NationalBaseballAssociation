O'Neill Holds White Sox Scoreless
Monday, July 8th, 1929
Andy O'Neill hurled a fine game for the Philadelphia Liberty at Baker Bowl 1910 today, holding the Chicagou White Sox to 11 hits in a shutout win.

"When he started out, his command wasn't that good, but he's a tough guy," Philadelphia manager Kendrick Markowski said. "The more pitches he throws, the better his command is."

The right-hander had 1 strikeout and 1 walk in the 9-0 victory.

"An outstanding job," Chicagou manager Eric Scism said. "We couldn't get anything going. Every time we got a hit, he got out of the jam. He pitched a terrific game."

So far this season O'Neill has a record of 5-8 with 16 strikeouts and 17 walks in 108.1 innings and a 4.57 ERA.
