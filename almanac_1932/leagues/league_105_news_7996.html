Woodmore Wins Canadian League Pitching Crown
Sunday, October 17th, 1926
The Philadelphia Liberty have a rising superstar or maybe even a budding Hall-of-Famer in the making.
It's Eric Woodmore, this year's Canadian League Pitcher of the Year Award selection. This blossoming hurler wowed fans, peers and sportswriters with a fabulous performance.

Woodmore compiled a 13-12 record in 25 starts, tallied 57 strikeouts in 231 innings and recorded a 3.12 ERA. Other clubs managed only a .260 batting average against him.

He received 12 first place votes out of a possible 16. Aindriú Hoban of the Boston Redcoats finished second in voting with 4 first place votes, while Nicola Patterson of the Chicagou White Sox finished third.
