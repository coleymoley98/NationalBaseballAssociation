Cothern Goes Cycling
Wednesday, August 17th, 1932
Getting 4 hits in a game is no easy feat, but doing it to hit for the cycle is even more rare.

Seamus Cothern of the Cincinnati Reds pulled it off today at Ballpark of Quebec, going 4-for-9 against the Quebec Nordiques. His batting escapades sparked the Reds to a 6-4 victory.

Cothern tallied 2 runs and drove in 1 during the game. Hitting for the cycle is particularly rare because of the different skills required - enough power for a home run, enough speed for a triple and enough patience at the plate to see at least four hittable balls.

"It just happened," Cothern said to reporters after the win. "Must have been fate or something, I wasn't expecting it. Heck, I wasn't even really trying for it."

Seamus Cothern tripled in the 2nd, flied out in the 3rd, grounded out in the 6th, flied out in the 9th, singled in the 11th, struck out in the 13th, flied out in the 16th, doubled in the 18th and hit a solo-shot off Kameron Antonelli in the 19th.

So far this season Cothern is hitting .284 with 2 home runs, 22 RBIs and 12 runs scored. He has 42 hits in 148 at-bats and a .325 OBP.
