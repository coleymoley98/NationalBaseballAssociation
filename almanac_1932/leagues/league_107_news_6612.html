Western Baseball League Top Hurler Announced
Saturday, August 15th, 1925
The spotlight today is on Albus Brushett of the Victoria Admirals. He was named the top pitcher in the Western Baseball League and will be honored with the 1925 WBL Pitcher of the Year.

Brushett compiled an impressive 8-3 won-lost mark to capture this year's honor. In 123.1 innings of work and 16 starts, the Admirals star struck out 120, while giving up 92 hits and 25 walks. He limited opposing batters to a .202 average.

He received 7 first place votes out of a possible 8. Chi Broadus of the Vancouver Giants finished second in voting with 1 first place vote, while Kane Eichhorn of the Kelowna Apple Sox finished third.
