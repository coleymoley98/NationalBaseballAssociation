Western League Chooses Best Manager
Wednesday, September 19th, 1923
Just about any objective fan of the Omaha Falcons would have to admit that Emeril Martineau is worthy of the Western League Manager of the Year Award. But it wasn't that way at first.

"In the beginning it was rocky," noted Martineau, "The fans really gave it to me. Somewhere along the line we just began putting things together, picking up a win here and there, and morphing into a team--not just a bunch of guys doing their own thing."

Martineau paced the Falcons to a first place finish in the . Omaha wound up the season with a 58-24 record.

As for next year, Martineau said, "We're always tweaking things, trying to improve the club. You don't rest after winning awards. I'm here to make the Falcons a perennial powerhouse."
