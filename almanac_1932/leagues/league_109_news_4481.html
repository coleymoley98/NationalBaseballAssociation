Redcoats Torpedoed by Horowitz in 1-0 Loss to Pittsburgh
Monday, September 3rd, 1923
Ezra Horowitz of the Pittsburgh Liberty humiliated the Providence Redcoats at Liberty Ballpark today, spinning a complete game shutout and holding the visitors to 2 hits.

"He stayed aggressive and his ball was really moving," said Providence manager Grayson St-Louis, after the game.

Horowitz whiffed 10 and walked 0 in the 1-0 win.

"If I make good pitches, I'm going to get some outs," Horowitz said. "I was making good pitches the entire game."

On the season Horowitz has compiled a 7-6 mark with a 3.05 ERA in 17 starts. In pitching 112 innings he has 106 strikeouts and 26 walks while limiting the opposition to a .233 batting average.
