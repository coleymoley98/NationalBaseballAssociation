Mets Show No Mercy in 11-0 Thrashing of Robins
Sunday, July 17th, 1932
The Columbus Mets shut out the Harrisburg Robins 11-0 today behind the strong pitching of Ted Hamelin. Hamelin surrendered only 10 hits in recording the complete-game victory, and struck out 6.

Following the last out, Hamelin appeared to celebrate a little too boisterously for Harrisburg manager Cedric Chéron, who could be seen scolding the famously outspoken right-hander as the clubs left the field. Hamelin's own manager then intervened with some words for his counterpart.

The Robins skipper would not comment on the exchange, but at the Mets press conference, Hamelin confirmed that it was regarding the celebration.

"He basically told me I was being a lousy winner," Hamelin said.

"And I told him he was being a lousy loser," Columbus manager Tyler Payne added.

This year in 10 starts, Hamelin has recorded 5 wins and absorbed 4 losses. He has pitched 74.1 innings, allowed 95 hits, struck out 40 and has a 5.81 ERA.
