Townsell Named 1931 WBL WBL Player of the Year Winner
Sunday, August 16th, 1931
He may have less experience than most of his Western Baseball League peers, but that didn't slow Caleb Townsell down at all in 1931.

At the age of 15, the Everett catcher has been named the winner of the Western Baseball League WBL Player of the Year.

He had a .238 batting average and a .381 on-base percentage this year. In 38 games he piled up 31 hits, 6 doubles, no triples, 8 home runs, 32 RBIs and 26 runs scored.

"I'm blown away by the season I was able to put together," he said. "I'm looking forward to trying to do it all again next year."

He received 7 first place votes out of a possible 8. Ollie Fortier of the Everett Silvertips finished second in voting with 1 first place vote, while A.J. Valcourt of the Boise Broncos finished third.
