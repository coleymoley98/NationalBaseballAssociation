Manager Frye Leaves, Dismissed by Omaha
Sunday, August 3rd, 1930
The winds of change have hit Omaha.

Many saw the news coming, but it still came as a bit of a surprise to the sports world when Kittithat Frye was fired earlier today.

The team cited "intellectual differences" as contributing to the decision but refused to go into details. The only thing that is clear right now is that the hunt is on for a new manager.

"Kittithat was a top-notch manager, he just wasn't the right guy for our team and our situation right now," said a team official speaking on condition of anonymity.

The Jr. Falcons spokesman indicated that Frye had not been meeting goals set out for him by the team owner.

"What's done is done," Frye told reporters. "I'm just looking at my situation going forward, not backward. There's nothing I can change about the situation in Omaha. I just have to focus on what's to come next in my future."
