Pitcher of the Year Award Presented to Minneapolis Star
Monday, August 17th, 1931
The 1931 Central Baseball League Pitcher of the Year Award winner is Elvis Guégan of the Minneapolis Jr. Saints.

He started 11 games and compiled a 9-1 record with an ERA of 0.95 in 94.2 innings with 123 strikeouts.

He received 8 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Oliver Leclerc of the Davenport Rivermen finished second in voting, while Wittaya Brookover of the Minneapolis Jr. Saints finished third.
