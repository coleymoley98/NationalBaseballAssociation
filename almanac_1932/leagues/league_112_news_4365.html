Gunners Crowned Champions
Wednesday, August 15th, 1923
It's party time in Quincy. The confetti is flying and residents are smiling.

The Quincy Gunners have reached the mountaintop, capturing the 1923 Laurier Cup today. They defeated the Providence Bruins 11-8 at Juniors Ballpark to officially wrap up the series 4-3.

Asked how it feels to be champion, first baseman Josh Mathews was to the point.

"It feels great, like a million bucks. I'm so happy right now, not just for me but for all the guys," he said. "We put a lot of work into this and deserve to be champions."

The team is planning a victory parade and fan appreciation day later in the week. Quincy finished the regular season 32-18 and claimed third place in the Atlantic.
