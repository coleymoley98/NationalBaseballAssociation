CBL Selects Top Manager, Mounties Wilkins Wins
Thursday, August 14th, 1930
When Timothy Wilkins was hired to lead the Adams Mounties, fans were expecting improvement over the last manager. Today, flanked by team officials, Wilkins accepted the Central Baseball League Manager of the Year Award, noting the level of passion for the Adams Mounties.

"The biggest challenge was the fans. I don't know how many times I was asked, if I thought the club was good enough. It was as if they expected failure. It was much easier to win over the ballplayers. Slowly but surely the fans began to believe in us."

Wilkins led Adams to a first place finish in the . The Mounties ended up with a 40-10 record.

Wilkins is looking forward to the offseason. "We're reviewing our options. You never stand still in baseball. You either get better or worse, 'cause everyone else is out to get your free agents or deal for your prospects. It's going to be a challenge." But the skipper has more than proven he is equal to the task.
