St. John's Narrowly Wins Atlantic
Thursday, July 26th, 1923
It was far from a foregone conclusion, but the St. John's Mariners are now champions of the Atlantic.

"We battled, day in and day out," said starting pitcher Joe Eckes. "This is one to savor, as it was close right down to the end and that puts a lot of pressure on a team."

With a record of 34-17, the Mariners have officially won their 1st division crown.

Manager Arran Laplante said there's "a lot of pride and excitement" involved in coming out on top of the Atlantic.

The club will now be looking to win the 1st Atlantic Baseball League championship in franchise history.
