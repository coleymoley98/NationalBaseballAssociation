Quebec Advances to Riel Cup
Thursday, September 23rd, 1926
The Quebec Nordiques took time to celebrate with the Quebec fans who stuck around after the game, following the team's League Championship Series triumph over the Detroit Lions.

Members of the Nordiques hung around chatting, high-fiving and taking pictures with the Quebec fans in attendance at Bennett Park, long after the last pitch had been thrown in their 7-4 win to seal the series 4-2.

"We've got great fan support," said right fielder Orber Teran. "Whether we're at home or on the road, the fans support us. So we wanted to show them how important that is to us. Some Detroit fans that were there even congratulated us on the win."

For the series, Teran hit .455 with a .520 on-base percentage. He had 1 home runs, 7 RBI and scored 6 runs.

Things weren't so cordial on the Lions side of things. Their clubhouse was closed to media after the game and all players and coaches quickly dressed and left the stadium after the end to their season.

The Quebec Nordiques will play against the New York Rebels in the next round. New York won their series against the Philadelphia Liberty 4 games to 2. The Riel Cup schedule will be announced after the League Championship Series finishes.
