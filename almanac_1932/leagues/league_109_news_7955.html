Pittsburgh Ace Named EL Most Valuable Player Award
Friday, September 24th, 1926
He's only 21 years old, but Jacob McGowan of the Pittsburgh Liberty has clearly established himself as a star of Eastern League.

His status grew today as the ace pitcher was named the winner of the 1926 Eastern League Most Valuable Player Award.

"I just want to pitch well and win games," he said. "The awards are nice though."

McGowan's award-winning season saw him put up a 7-4 record through 14 starts, throwing 104 innings. He had 115 strikeouts with 31 walks and a 1.99 ERA. Opponents hit only .196 against him during the season.

He received 5 first place votes out of a possible 8. Bill Dougherty of the Providence Redcoats finished second in voting with 3 first place votes, while Randell McClinton of the Providence Redcoats finished third.
