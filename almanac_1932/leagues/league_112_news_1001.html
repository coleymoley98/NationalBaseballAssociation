Leven Named 1930 ABL ABL Player of the Year Winner
Friday, August 22nd, 1930
The future looks extremely bright for Eli Leven. At the age of 15, many players are still trying to find their way in Atlantic Baseball League.

Leven has not only found his way, he's already established himself as one of the game's brightest stars, today being named the winner of the Atlantic Baseball League ABL Player of the Year.

"I didn't expect to hit .429 this year, that's for sure," the first baseman said.

The Providence Bruins star also put up a .489 on-base percentage this year, with 72 hits, 20 doubles, 1 triple, 8 home runs, 37 RBIs and 43 runs scored.

He received 13 first place votes out of a possible 14. Nuttawut Hinton of the Providence Bruins finished second in voting with 1 first place vote, while Watcharin Sankla of the St. John's Mariners finished third.
