CL Announces Outstanding Player Award
Monday, October 8th, 1928
What a way to finish off the year -- being honored with the 1928 Most Valuable Player Award in the Canadian League. It had already been a very special season for New York's Trent Norrell, but today's announcement really put the icing on his cake.

All season long Trent Norrell gave opposing pitchers fits by hitting .416 as he collected 153 hits, 22 doubles, 5 triples, 11 home runs and 74 RBIs.

He received 9 first place votes out of a possible 16. Seamus Sexauer of the Boston Redcoats finished second in voting with 7 first place votes, while Phuwanart McInnis of the Boston Redcoats finished third.
