Maday 5-for-5 Against Buffalo
Thursday, July 3rd, 1924
After a 5-hit effort to lead Toronto's 13-7 victory over the Buffalo Rapids, right fielder Lewis Maday told BNN beat writers it had been "a special day."

"It really doesn't matter whether it's sandlot ball, high school or college, two hits in a game is nice," he said. "Three or four is better, but five or more is out of sight. And to do that at the Eastern Baseball League-level is really something special."

The 17-year-old player earned his accolades today at Juniors Ballpark with a spectacular 5-for-5 game against the Rapids.

"His swing is getting there," said Jr. Blue Sox skipper Pongsak Strong, who spoke glowingly of Maday after the game. "We want him to be quick with his swing, short and compact. We want him to be patient and go the other way when he can. Lewis did that to perfection today."

Lewis Maday hit an RBI double in the 1st, hit a two-run double in the 2nd, hit an RBI single in the 4th, hit an RBI single in the 6th and hit a two-run single in the 7th.
