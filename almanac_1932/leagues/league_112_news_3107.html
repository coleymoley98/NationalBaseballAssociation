Yes, It's a No-No by Banks
Thursday, July 21st, 1932
The no-hitter is one of baseball's truly egalitarian achievements. It's done by the great and the not-so-great alike, and for nearly all of them it represents the pinnacle of a career. Sherbrooke Canadiens pitcher David Banks had his moment today at Ballpark of Drummondville, holding the Drummondville Voltigeurs hitless and beating them, 4-0.

"Everything was working for him," Voltigeurs manager Tai Grigsby said. "He had a lot of late movement that seemed to cause the guys trouble, especially from the fifth inning on. He was in command."

Banks rung up 8 strikeouts, issued 3 walks and faced only 30 hitters in his historic outing. After the game, he spoke to BNN reporters in the Sherbrooke locker room.

"I really got nervous in the last inning," Banks recalled. "I knew I had a chance at a no-hitter but it was hard to stay calm and concentrate. Thank goodness for my catcher. He told me he would do all the thinking and I could just concentrate on hitting his glove."

Banks has made 10 starts so far this year and put up a 4-1 won-lost record with a 1.92 ERA. In 61 innings he has allowed 38 hits, walked 25 and struck out 53.
