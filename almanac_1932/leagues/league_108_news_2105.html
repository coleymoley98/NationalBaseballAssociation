Hit Parade: Hernández Pops 5 Hits
Wednesday, August 26th, 1931
Speaking with reporters after a dominating hitting performance, Rafael Hernández said his club's tilt with the Vancouver Giants "felt almost like batting practice." The 23-year-old shortstop went a remarkable 5-for-7 at the plate.

"I saw the ball well and could just about hit it anywhere I wanted," he recalled later.

However, his sensational day wasn't enough for the Winnipeg Thunder, who were topped by the Giants, 9-8.

"It's a good lesson for him, I guess," Thunder manager Kendrick Markowski said. "Sometimes Rafael is going to lose. But I would rather him go 5-for-7 and lose than go 0-for-7 and lose."

Rafael Hernández singled in the 1st, hit an RBI triple in the 3rd, flied out in the 5th, doubled in the 7th, grounded out in the 9th, singled in the 11th and singled in the 13th.

This year Hernández is batting .500 with 26 hits, 1 home run, 7 RBIs, 4 walks, .552 OBP and has scored 13 runs.
