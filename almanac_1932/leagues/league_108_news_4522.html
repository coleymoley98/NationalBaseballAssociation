Pohji Gets Award for Relief Work
Monday, September 17th, 1923
He may only be 21, but Wutthisak Pohji of the Winnipeg Thunder has already established himself as one of the elite relievers in the Western League.

The reliever was today named the WL Reliever of the Year Award winner for 1923.

He won the award with a strong year that saw him put up 5 saves in 29 relief appearances and 36 strikeouts in 40.1 innings. He had a record of 5-4 to go with his 3.57 ERA.

He received 8 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Samuel Léveillé of the Portland Thorns finished second in voting, while Jir Thiffault of the Saskatoon Coyotes finished third.
