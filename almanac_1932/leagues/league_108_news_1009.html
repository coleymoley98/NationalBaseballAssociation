Thunder Blank Portland, Wong Winner
Saturday, August 23rd, 1930
The Winnipeg Thunder shut out the Portland Thorns 8-0 today behind the strong pitching of Drew Wong. Wong surrendered only 6 hits in recording the complete-game victory, and struck out 0.

Following the last out, Wong appeared to celebrate a little too boisterously for Portland manager Charlie Fleury, who could be seen scolding the famously outspoken right-hander as the clubs left the field. Wong's own manager then intervened with some words for his counterpart.

The Thorns skipper would not comment on the exchange, but at the Thunder press conference, Wong confirmed that it was regarding the celebration.

"He basically told me I was being a lousy winner," Wong said.

"And I told him he was being a lousy loser," Winnipeg manager Kendrick Markowski added.

Currently Wong has a 2-1 won-lost record in 1 starts with a 5.48 ERA. He also has 12 strikeouts in 47.2 innings.
