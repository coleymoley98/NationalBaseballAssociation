Blanchette Selected ABL's Premier Player
Monday, August 26th, 1929
The future looks extremely bright for Ruell Blanchette. At the age of 18, many players are still trying to find their way in Atlantic Baseball League.

Blanchette has not only found his way, he's already established himself as one of the game's brightest stars, today being named the winner of the Atlantic Baseball League ABL Player of the Year.

"I didn't expect to hit .427 this year, that's for sure," the right fielder said.

The Halifax Highlanders star also put up a .500 on-base percentage this year, with 79 hits, 10 doubles, 3 triples, 5 home runs, 44 RBIs and 45 runs scored.

He received 14 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Sol Cunningham of the Drummondville Voltigeurs finished second in voting, while Tui Maier of the Halifax Highlanders finished third.
