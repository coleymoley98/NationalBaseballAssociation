St. John's Win Division Finals
Thursday, August 2nd, 1928
After eliminating the Pointe-Sainte-Anne Acadiens 6-0 in the Division Finals at Field of the Acadiens, the victorious St. John's Mariners will now move on to the next round: the Laurier Cup.

St. John's beat Pointe-Sainte-Anne 4-2 in the series. The MVP trophy was awarded to Mariners right fielder Walt Turnquist, who knocked in 4 runs, hit .400 and scored 5 times.

"The guys were all chanting 'M-V-P' when I went into the clubhouse," Turnquist said later, with a smile. "It was kind of embarrassing but it feels great to be here."

The St. John's Mariners will play against the Quebec City Jr. Nordiques in the next round. Quebec City won their series against the New Britain Yankees 4 games to 2. The Laurier Cup schedule will be announced after the Division Finals finishes.
