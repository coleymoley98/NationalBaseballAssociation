Semple, Saints Shut Out Frontenacs, 5-0
Thursday, May 9th, 1929
The Minnesota Saints shut out the Chicagou Frontenacs 5-0 today behind the strong pitching of Hakim Semple. Semple surrendered only 5 hits in recording the complete-game victory, and struck out 4.

Following the last out, Semple appeared to celebrate a little too boisterously for Chicagou manager Archie Cooper, who could be seen scolding the famously outspoken left-hander as the clubs left the field. Semple's own manager then intervened with some words for his counterpart.

The Frontenacs skipper would not comment on the exchange, but at the Saints press conference, Semple confirmed that it was regarding the celebration.

"He basically told me I was being a lousy winner," Semple said.

"And I told him he was being a lousy loser," Minnesota manager A.J. Provost added.
