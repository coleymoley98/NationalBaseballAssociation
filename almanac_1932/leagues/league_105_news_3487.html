Wintringham Sets Season Mark with Hit
Monday, September 12th, 1932
Baseball has a new all-time single-season hit leader. In a game at Lincoln Park David Wintringham broke the record set previously by Trent Norrell. The crowd went wild when he banged out hit number 185. Asked by reporters, if it was tough to break the record, Wintringham replied, "Yeah, getting hits is tough, but there's no pressure build-up. I figured I would get this hit. This wasn't pressure. Pressure is the playoffs. Breaking this record was just fun... just another day at the ballpark."

David Wintringham struck out in the 1st, grounded out in the 3rd and homered off Davian White in the 4th.

This year Wintringham has hit at a .420 clip with 185 hits, 45 home runs, 131 RBIs and 103 runs scored.
