Jr. Nordiques Prevail, as Division Finals Goes to the Wire
Wednesday, August 5th, 1925
The Quebec City Jr. Nordiques are off to the Laurier Cup after an exciting Division Finals triumph.

Quebec City downed the Montreal Jr. Royales, but needed the entire series to do so, with a 7-3 win tonight at Juniors Ballpark wrapping up the series 4-3.

"It's every guy in this room that made this happen. Without us all pulling in the same direction, we don't win this series," said Thaiwat Jinta, who earned MVP honors for a standout offensive series.

Jinta put up a .444 with a .500 on-base percentage in the series. He also had 0 home runs, drove in 6 runs and scored 7 runs himself.

The Quebec City Jr. Nordiques will play against the Pointe-Sainte-Anne Acadiens in the next round. Pointe-Sainte-Anne won their series against the Providence Bruins 4 games to 2. The Laurier Cup schedule will be announced after the Division Finals finishes.
