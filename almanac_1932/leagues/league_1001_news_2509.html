1932 Top Player Honored By CSBT
Thursday, April 7th, 1932
What a way to finish off the year -- being honored with the 1932 Most Valuable Player Award in the Canadian Senior Baseball Tournament. It had already been a very special season for Team's Boyce Marrou, but today's announcement really put the icing on his cake.

All season long Boyce Marrou gave opposing pitchers fits by hitting .509 as he collected 27 hits, 3 doubles, no triples, 3 home runs and 8 RBIs.

He received 8 first place votes out of a possible 18. Pierre Léveillé of the Team Saskatchewan finished second in voting with 2 first place votes, while Bentley Lundeen of the Team Pallister finished third with 4 first place votes.
