Western Baseball League Best Pitcher Chosen
Friday, August 17th, 1928
Azman Eason of the Vancouver Giants had a fine 9-2 season in 1928 and today claimed the Western Baseball League WBL Pitcher of the Year.

He started 14 times, threw 110.2 innings while collecting 120 strikeouts and fashioned a 1.55 ERA. Eason allowed only 82 hits, 25 walks and a .202 opponents' batting average.

He received 8 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Mathis Séverine of the Boise Broncos finished second in voting, while K.J. Harries of the Vancouver Giants finished third.
