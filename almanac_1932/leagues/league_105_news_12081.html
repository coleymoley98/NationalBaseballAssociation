Delannoy Picks Up NL Rookie of the Year Award
Thursday, October 20th, 1932
The National League Rookie of the Year Award has been given to Panuwat Delannoy of the St. Louis Cardinaux.

In the 1932 season the 20-year-old left fielder played in 64 games, recording a .330 batting average with 7 home runs, 39 RBIs and 39 runs scored.

He received 15 first place votes out of a possible 20. John O'Geary of the Philadelphia Mets finished second in voting with 4 first place votes, while Steve Lowenstein of the Toronto Braves finished third with 1 first place vote.
