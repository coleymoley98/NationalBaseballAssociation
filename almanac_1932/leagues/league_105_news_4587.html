NL Announces Outstanding Player Award
Thursday, October 11th, 1923
Regardless of where Sheehan Thompson signs on next year, the 27-year-old will be considered a marquee pickup after his award-winning 1923 season with Quebec. Thompson received word today that he has won the National League Most Valuable Player Award.

The third baseman pounded out 142 hits, 24 doubles, 6 triples, and 7 home runs while hitting .351 and driving in 70 runs this season.

He received 8 first place votes out of a possible 16. Teeratat Alvarez of the Cincinnati Reds finished second in voting with 8 first place votes, while Sandy Sánchez of the Quebec Nordiques finished third.
