Rhoden Named Top Reliever for CL
Monday, October 13th, 1930
The Reliever of the Year Award goes to the Canadian League reliever who put forward the strongest season.

This season, that player was D.J. Rhoden of the Boston Redcoats, who was above all his peers in 1930.

"I just feel like the ball was doing what I wanted it to this year and I was in command most nights out there," he said. "Some days you don't have your best stuff, but I didn't have too many of those days this year."

On the season, Rhoden pitched 63.2 innings, with 15 strikeouts and an ERA of 3.53. He also had 10 saves in 35 relief appearances.

He received 20 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Andy Carper of the Hamilton Steelers finished second in voting, while Crawford Jones of the New York Rebels finished third.
