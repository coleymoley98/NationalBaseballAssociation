Sumonrat Tames Mets, 4-0
Tuesday, June 3rd, 1930
Goose eggs were the order of the day for the Philadelphia Mets as starter Panuwat Sumonrat shut out the Philadelphia Mets 4-0 at Bennett Park. The Detroit pitcher struck out 4 and walked 2 while allowing 5 hits.

"Sumonrat was good but he wasn't flawless," Philadelphia manager William Dunning said later. "We had a few opportunities to make something happen and we didn't do it. Not to take anything away from Detroit, but this is on us."
