Legault's 20-Game Hitting Streak Ends
Thursday, July 15th, 1926
For once, it was the Morgantown Mountaineers picking up the slack for Oliver Legault.

The club's "offensive catalyst," as Morgantown manager James Ferro has frequently called him, went uncharacteristically silent today and saw his 20-game hitting streak snapped by the Buffalo Rapids. Legault was bailed out by the rest of the Mountaineers, however, who stopped the Rapids, 3-1.

The 18-year-old first baseman finished the day 0-for-2, and told reporters at the postgame press conference that "everybody did their job but me."

"Not that it was a huge problem -- we still won the game," he said with a grin. "The streak was a good run though. I'll try to start another one tomorrow."

So far this year Legault is hitting .314 with 5 home runs.
