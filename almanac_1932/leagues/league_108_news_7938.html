Wigglesworth of Saskatoon Named WL Best Skipper
Sunday, September 19th, 1926
Saskatoon Coyotes field manager Maurice Wigglesworth has brought a winning attitude to the team and the city. His no-nonsense approach, work ethic and baseball experience have paved the way for success.

This season the Coyotes parlayed his managerial skills and some talented ballplayers into a first place spot in the standings. Saskatoon finished the year with a 52-30 record.

To everybody's delight Wigglesworth was voted the 1926 Western League Manager of the Year by the baseball writers.

"Mr. Wigglesworth doesn't take any nonsense. If you aren't doing your job, he's not afraid to call you on it," noted one of his ballplayers. "But the team wouldn't want to play for anyone else. He's tough, demanding, but he treats everyone fairly and doesn't play favorites. Nobody gets pampered on his ballclub."
