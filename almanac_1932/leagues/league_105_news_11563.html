Wintringham Named 1929 CL Most Valuable Player Award Winner
Monday, October 14th, 1929
David Wintringham took National Baseball Association by storm this year, and he did it all at the age of 23.

For his spectacular campaign, the Ottawa Indians star has been named the 1929 Canadian League Most Valuable Player Award recipient.

"To be considered amongst the best in the league is overwhelming," said the left fielder.

On the season he compiled a .379 batting average to go with a .412 on-base percentage. The native of Rosetown, The United Provinces also added 32 doubles, 9 triples, 35 home runs, 107 RBIs and 94 runs scored.

He received 16 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Edge Rufolo of the Baltimore Robins finished second in voting, while Jonathan Steward of the Baltimore Robins finished third.
