WL Gives Outstanding Pitcher Award
Friday, September 19th, 1930
He's in the early phase of his career, but this past season Jacob McGowan performed like a veteran on the hill for the Omaha Falcons and walked away with the Western League Pitcher of the Year Award in the process.

The 25-year-old Falcons star fashioned a 11-3 won-loss record in 1930 with a 2.29 ERA. In 125.2 innings of work and 14 starts, McGowan struck out 60 while giving up 97 hits and 42 walks. He held opposing batters to a .214 average.

He received 10 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Ambroise Thériault of the Portland Thorns finished second in voting, while Chaiya Junjira of the Calgary Wranglers finished third.
