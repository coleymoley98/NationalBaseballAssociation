Blue Sox Show No Mercy in 12-0 Thrashing of Redcoats
Sunday, September 11th, 1932
Kyle Gandy bent a little, but he didn't break against the Boston Redcoats. The Toronto Blue Sox pitcher hurled a 2-hit 12-0 shutout in a fine performance, surrendering 1 walk and tallying 5 strikeouts.

"Kyle is good at getting out of jams," Blue Sox manager Jabari Perrault told reporters after the game. "He's almost more effective when he gets guys on base or gets behind in the count, because he uses your aggressiveness against you."

Gandy has made 23 starts this season, posting 9 wins and 11 losses. Opposing clubs are batting .271 against him and he has 58 strikeouts in 173.1 innings with a 4.31 ERA.
