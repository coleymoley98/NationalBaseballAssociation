Postseason Bid Ends for Cincinnati
Thursday, September 3rd, 1931
It's the better-luck-next-time point of the season for the Cincinnati Reds. The club was officially eliminated from playoff contention today, and will turn their attention to next year's campaign.

"You know, the average player doesn't get that many seasons, so every time you're eliminated, it hurts," said Reds right fielder Timothy Rankin.

The Reds have missed the playoffs 20 times in their 21 years as a National Baseball Association franchise. This playoff miss is the club's 9th in a row, with their last postseason appearance coming in 1921.
