Behind Ormond, Albany Blanks Sherbrooke, 5-0
Thursday, August 5th, 1926
There was no spell, no black magic -- just good pitching.

Wit Ormond of the Albany Rebels blanked the Sherbrooke Nordiques, 5-0, at Rebels Stadium today, and he did it the old-fashioned way. After finishing the 5-hit, complete-game shutout, he was all smiles.

"I threw a lot of my pitches for strikes in this game," Ormond said. "I mixed it up and tried keeping these guys off balance."

Ormond walked 1 and struck out 3 in the victory.

"He was throwing his breaking ball for strikes and we never capitalized on any of his mistakes," Nordiques manager Malik Hill said later.

This year Ormond has gone 2-3 with an ERA of 4.28 and has 63 strikeouts in 73.2 innings.
