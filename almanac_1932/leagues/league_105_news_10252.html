Boulay Hits Way to NL Triple Crown
Monday, September 10th, 1928
Taradin Boulay is sitting on top of the world tonight. He got himself a Triple Crown this year -- the 6th player in NBA history to ever accomplish this feat.

The Minnesota Saints first baseman outpaced the rest of the National League offensively this year. Boulay, age 22, hit .349, collected 15 home runs, and chipped in 74 RBIs for the Saints. He led in all three categories to capture the Triple Crown, one of the rarer feats in baseball.

"It's all about hard work," noted one club official. "Taradin arrives early, takes extra BP and really make a science of hitting. He religiously studies the pitchers. Keeps a journal on all of 'em. He's very thorough."
