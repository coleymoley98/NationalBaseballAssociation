Royales KO Celtics
Thursday, October 2nd, 1930
It was a hard-fought battle for both teams to make it this far. But as the saying goes, "to the victors go the spoils." For the victorious Montreal Royales, that meant the National Baseball Association Riel Cup trophy.

The Royales took a 6-1 victory over the Wolfeburg Celtics at Montreal Grounds, winning their 8th championship after taking the series 4-2.

"We've got grit," said Royales third baseman Clifton Blacksher, who was named MVP of the series. "We know how to win when we have to."

The new titleholders completed the regular season with a first-place finish in the National League East and a 77-35 record.
