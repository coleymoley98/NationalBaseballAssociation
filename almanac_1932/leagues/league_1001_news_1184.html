Manitoba Drouin, Best Pitcher Winner
Saturday, April 11th, 1931
With speculation about his retirement gaining steam last offseason, Manitoba ace Dominic Drouin had something to prove. He proved it in award-winning style as he accepted the Canadian Senior Baseball Tournament Best Pitcher today.

The veteran Team hurler posted a record of 3 wins and no losses this year with a 1.37 ERA. In 3 starts he struck out 6 and walked 4 in 26.1 innings.

He received 14 first place votes out of a possible 18. Glenden Croat of the Team New England finished second in voting with 3 first place votes, while Kritsana Ta-ngam of the Team Ontario finished third with 1 first place vote.
