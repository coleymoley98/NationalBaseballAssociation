Wintringham Belts Bunch of Base Hits
Sunday, August 12th, 1928
It takes more than one player to win a baseball game - as proven by David Wintringham of the Ottawa Indians.

The Ottawa left fielder gave it his all and dominated Philadelphia Liberty pitching today at Baker Bowl 1910, finishing with a great 5-for-5 line. However, when the game ended, the scoreboard showed the Liberty going home the winner, 10-6.

"There's no point gloating about a 5-hit day in a loss," Wintringham said later. "A loss just means you should have done more. Maybe I should have hit 5 home runs instead."

David Wintringham hit an RBI triple in the 1st, singled in the 3rd, singled in the 5th, singled in the 7th and singled in the 9th.

Wintringham is hitting .329 in 1928 with 61 RBIs and 15 home runs.
