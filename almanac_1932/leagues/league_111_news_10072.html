Ratté Rocks Jr. Steelers for 3 HRs
Tuesday, August 7th, 1928
Renaud Ratté went on an explosive hitting spree against the Hamilton Jr. Steelers at Juniors Ballpark today, launching a trio of blasts to lead the Fort Wayne Pioneers to the 15-9 win.

The first baseman finished 4-for-4 with 4 runs scored and 8 runs batted in.

"It was just one of those days," Ratté said in a postgame interview. "I don't know how to explain it. I'm just putting a good swing on the ball."

Hamilton skipper Fabrice Hardy said Ratté was "locked-in."

"We made some pretty good pitches," he said. "Those first two homers were exactly where the pitcher wanted to put them. He's just hot right now -- you've just got to tip your hat to him."

Renaud Ratté hit a two-run single in the 1st, hit a solo-shot off Kian Shearer in the 3rd, hit a three-run home run off Yoddanai Barichat in the 4th, hit a two-run home run off Yoddanai Barichat in the 6th and walked in the 8th.

Ratté is hitting .385 in 1928 with 38 RBIs and 7 home runs.
