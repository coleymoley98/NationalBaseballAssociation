Toronto Sweeps into the Adams Cup
Friday, July 31st, 1931
It's a sweet sweep for the Toronto Jr. Blue Sox and they have won the Division Finals. The Fort Wayne Pioneers were not able to keep pace with the Jr. Blue Sox and dropped the series in short order, losing 7-3 in the deciding game.

"This wasn't really the effort we hoped for," said Fort Wayne manager Jesse O'Byrne.

It's now on to the Adams Cup for Toronto after their 3-0 series win.

Leading the way for the winners was center fielder Pierre-Thomas Caron, who was named MVP. For the series, Caron hit .538 with a .571 on-base percentage. He had 1 home runs, drove in 3 RBI and scored 3 runs.

The Toronto Jr. Blue Sox don't know who they will face in the next round. They will play the winner of the Hamilton Jr. Steelers - Canton Legends series. Canton leads the series 2 game to 1. The Adams Cup schedule will be announced after the Division Finals finishes.
