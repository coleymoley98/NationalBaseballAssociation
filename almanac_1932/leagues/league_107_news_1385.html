Weets Brilliant -- Fires No-Hitter
Saturday, June 6th, 1931
The Everett Silvertips were just hoping for a solid outing from Richard Weets. Instead, they got something special.

The 18-year-old hurler tossed a no-hitter against the Vancouver Giants, helping the Silvertips take the 8-0 win.

"I felt good throughout the game," he said after his spectacular outing for the Silvertips. "As I was going into the ninth inning, I wasn't nervous. I knew I had a chance to do something special."

Weets's repertoire has a curveball, changeup, slider, and fastball, and he used it all to full advantage, keeping the Vancouver hitters off balance all game long. The Everett hurler fanned 13 and walked 2 in his history-making performance.

"His curveball looked about the size of a watermelon seed and it hissed at you as it passed," Vancouver manager Danupong Chan-inkaew joked after the game.
