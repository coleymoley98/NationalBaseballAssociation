Martínez Goes Cycling
Monday, August 6th, 1923
P.J. Martínez accomplished one of the rarer feats in baseball today when he hit for the cycle against the Portland Thorns in the 20-5 Vancouver Giants win at Oracle Park.

Afterward, Martínez acknowledged that his accomplishment was special, but said he was more interested in helping the Giants win.

"Maybe one day I'll look back on this with more meaning, but right now I just want to go out tomorrow and put some good swings on the ball and help my club win another game," Martínez said.

On his historic day, he went 4-for-6 with 4 runs batted in and 2 runs scored -- and also got a spot in the history books.

P.J. Martínez flied out in the 1st, hit a two-run triple in the 2nd, hit a solo-shot off Jarod Lemon in the 3rd, grounded out in the 4th, singled in the 6th and hit an RBI double in the 8th.

This season Martínez is batting .260 with 59 hits, 6 home runs, 45 RBIs, .367 OBP and has scored 48 runs.
