Canadiens Demay No-Hits Jr. Royales
Thursday, July 12th, 1928
In a dominant outing, Gabriel Demay of the Sherbrooke Canadiens totally silenced the bats of the Montreal Jr. Royales, finishing with a no-hitter.

In the Canadiens' 2-1 victory, Demay faced only 32 batters, surrendered no hits, had 6 strikeouts and 3 walks.

"Honestly, I didn't even know the situation was what it was until the seventh inning," Sherbrooke manager C.J. Chambers said. "I happened to look up there to see how many hits we had gotten, and I happened to notice that they hadn't gotten any."

Commenting on the defeat, losing manager Calvin Pitts told reporters in the clubhouse that he had a "simple" analysis of the game.

"The Canadiens had all the hits and all the runs, so they won," he snapped. "But yeah, Demay pitched a heck of a ballgame."

This year Demay has worked 58.2 innings with 51 strikeouts en route to a 5-3 record and a 3.53 ERA.
