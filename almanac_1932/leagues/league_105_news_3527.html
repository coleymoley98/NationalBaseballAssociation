Nordiques Get Zilch in 2-0 Loss to Royales, Millette
Thursday, September 22nd, 1932
The Quebec Nordiques had no answer today for Montreal Royales pitcher Larry Millette. They failed to score a single run against him, losing 2-0. Quebec managed only 1 hit off Millette, who had 3 strikeouts.

The extraordinarily popular Millette got a standing ovation from the hometown crowd as he left the field, the applause continuing until he returned from the dugout to tip his cap to the crowd.

"Royales fans are the best fans, in my opinion," he said. "It's a great feeling to throw a shutout under any circumstances but to do it with the crowd behind you like that is just incredible."

This year Millette has made 22 starts, thrown 172.1 innings, fanned 71 batters and walked 53. His won-lost record is 16-1 with a 1.98 ERA.
