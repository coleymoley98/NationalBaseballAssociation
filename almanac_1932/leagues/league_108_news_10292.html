Western League Top Pitcher Selected
Monday, September 24th, 1928
This year there was no one better on the mound than Martino Gutierrez. Posting 9 wins and a 1.65 ERA was only part of the story for the Omaha superstar. For his outstanding performance in 1928 he was chosen as the Western League Pitcher of the Year Award winner.

From start to finish Gutierrez never let up. In 14 starts he recorded 9 wins and 5 losses, 120.1 innings, 82 strikeouts, 58 walks, yielded 89 hits, and permitted opposing batters to hit .204 against him.

He received 8 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Logan L'Heureux of the Calgary Wranglers finished second in voting, while Alberto Badeau of the Saskatoon Coyotes finished third.
