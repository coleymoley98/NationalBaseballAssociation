Hamilton Manager Méthot Named Top Skipper in EL
Monday, September 23rd, 1929
Hamilton manager Nelson Méthot was selected as the 1929 Eastern League Manager of the Year today.

He was somewhat of a dark horse next to some of the more flashy managers up for the award. Méthot, who prefers that everyone call him "Cap" or "Skip," tries to shy away from the limelight in a media-driven world.

"It's really unfair to the guys that busted their humps all year long to put all the focus on me now. They showed up early, put in extra time in the cage, worked on their fielding, consulted with our coaching staff and really made 1929 a highlight in my career."

In fairness to this year's Manager of the Year, Méthot certainly played an important part in the Blue Sox success as he guided them to a first place finish in the and a 52-31 record.
