McGowan Holds Minnesota Hitless
Thursday, August 15th, 1929
Jacob McGowan relied on excellent pitch selection and pinpoint control to hold Minnesota hitless in a 4-0 win at Field of the Saints today.

"Nobody goes out to pitch a no-hitter," he said later. "We just go out and try to get a win. A 10-9 win is better than a 1-0 loss. My pitching buddies might not be too happy giving up nine runs but a win is a win. The key to it today was I mixed my pitches well. I kept the Saints off balance and guessing the whole game."

The 24-year-old starter rung up 3 strikeouts and 3 walks in whipping the Saints.

"When a guy is throwing strikes the way he was and is locating well, it is going to be a long day for any club," Minnesota skipper A.J. Provost said.

On his stats sheet this season McGowan shows a 3-4 record, 9 starts, 64.2 innings, 22 strikeouts and a 5.01 ERA.
