NL Hands Out Reliever of the Year Award
Wednesday, October 12th, 1927
The manager of the Chicagou Frontenacs said he always had confidence handing the ball to Alan Tonnessen out of the bullpen.

The 29-year-old was a dominant performer all year for Chicagou and his efforts have been rewarded with the National League Reliever of the Year Award for 1927.

The bullpen ace had an impressive 1.08 ERA this season, with 11 saves in 43 relief appearances. He fanned 35 batters in 66.2 innings and sported a record of 8 wins and 2 losses.

He received 16 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. T.J. Andrews of the Juneau Brasseurs finished second in voting, while Pierce Ramirez of the Minnesota Saints finished third.
