Thursday, October 15th, 1925
The future looks extremely bright for Valéry Jetté. At the age of 21, many players are still trying to find their way in National Baseball Association.

Jetté has not only found his way, he's already established himself as one of the game's brightest stars, today being named the winner of the National League Most Valuable Player Award.

"I didn't expect to hit .315 this year, that's for sure," the third baseman said.

The Montreal Royales star also put up a .398 on-base percentage this year, with 135 hits, 20 doubles, 22 triples, 11 home runs, 65 RBIs and 75 runs scored.

He received 9 first place votes out of a possible 16. Taradin Boulay of the Minnesota Saints finished second in voting with 4 first place votes, while Jarel Gibbs of the Cincinnati Reds finished third.
