Blacksher Rips 3 Homers Against Yankees
Saturday, July 10th, 1926
Anyone wearing a glove and sitting in the bleachers for today's Coureurs-Yankees game had a good chance at snagging a home run ball.

Champlain first baseman Clifton Blacksher thrice sent eager fans scurrying to catch a souvenir, helping lead the Champlain to a 14-3 win over New Britain.

"Hey, the three homers were great, but I'm just trying to help my ballclub," Blacksher said later. "I'm not going out there thinking I need another home run."

Blacksher was 4-for-5 on the day with 6 RBI.

Clifton Blacksher hit a solo-shot off Owen Hellman in the 1st, hit a two-run double in the 3rd, flied out in the 4th, hit a solo-shot off Erwin Paxson in the 7th and hit a two-run home run off B.J. Siteti in the 8th.

A look at his current stats shows Blacksher with a .324 average, 7 home runs, 25 RBIs and 27 runs scored. He has played in 36 games.
