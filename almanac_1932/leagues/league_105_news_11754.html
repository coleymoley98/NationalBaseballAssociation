Rare Feat: Norrell Bats .420
Friday, September 5th, 1930
The five-time All-Star Trent Norrell of the New York Rebels is the latest member of baseball's .400 club -- that elite group of players who hit over .400 in a single season.

The 27-year-old Norrell reeled off hit after hit all year long, totaling 184 with 35 doubles, 4 triples and 22 home runs. He also collected 132 RBIs and 97 runs in his epic season.

The Rebels left fielder confessed to the sportswriters, "It's really hard to hit .400, but with the press coverage, it makes it a lot harder. You just try to put it out of your mind during the game. Patience is the key word, patience at the plate and patience with the press and the public. It was a hectic time. I think I'll enjoy it now that it's over."
