Top CBL Reliever Honored
Monday, August 16th, 1926
At the age of 15, Jacob Perreault says he still has a lot to learn about life in Central Baseball League.

But he certainly seems to be a quick study, as the Saskatoon Jr. Coyotes reliever was better than all others this year and has captured the 1926 Central Baseball League Reliever of the Year Award.

"I hope to win this a few more times before all is said and done in my career," the Saskatoon, The United Provinces native said.

For the season, Perreault had 4 saves in 23 relief appearances, which totaled 37. He fanned 43 betters, while walking 9 and put up a record of 7-2 to go with his 1.70 ERA.

He received 8 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Rachan Lévesque of the St. Louis Jr. Cardinaux finished second in voting, while Turner McClendon of the St. Louis Jr. Cardinaux finished third.
