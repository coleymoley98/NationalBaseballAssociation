Player of the Week: Montreal's Jetté
Monday, August 23rd, 1926
Valéry Jetté demonstrated his potential to be a star in the game last week and today he took home the National League Player of the Week Award.

The Montreal third baseman wreaked havoc on opposing pitchers last week, pounding them for a .542 average (13-for-24) with no home runs and 3 RBIs on his way to claiming the honor.

Jetté is hitting .317 this year with 9 home runs, 46 RBIs and 69 runs scored. He has 111 hits in 350 at-bats, 54 walks and a .409 on-base percentage. Jetté has played in 92 games.
