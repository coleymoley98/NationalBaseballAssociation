Tremblay Is NL's Best Rookie
Saturday, October 12th, 1929
All the games have been played, all the votes have been counted and the recipient of the prestigious National League Rookie of the Year Award is Watcharapol Tremblay of the Chicagou Frontenacs.

This talented first-year performer hit .400 and put up a .475 OBP. In 75 games this year, the left fielder collected 104 hits in 260 at-bats, 10 home runs, 55 RBIs and scored 46 times.

He received 16 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Prayad Adeleye of the Minnesota Saints finished second in voting, while Chutipol Fabbri of the Cincinnati Reds finished third.
