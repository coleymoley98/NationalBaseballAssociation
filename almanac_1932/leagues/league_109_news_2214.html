Narcisse Gets Award for Relief Work
Friday, September 18th, 1931
He may only be 24, but Jame Narcisse of the Trois-Rivieres Royales has already established himself as one of the elite relievers in the Eastern League.

The reliever was today named the EL Reliever of the Year Award winner for 1931.

He won the award with a strong year that saw him put up 15 saves in 37 relief appearances and 94 strikeouts in 61 innings. He had a record of 11-2 to go with his 3.39 ERA.

He received 10 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Grandy Rucker of the Providence Redcoats finished second in voting, while Sergio Halloway of the Brampton Blue Sox finished third.
