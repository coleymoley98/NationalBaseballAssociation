Another Setback for Mets Nagata, Forced to Retire
Tuesday, October 4th, 1932
Columbus starting pitcher Yoshitaro Nagata has been diagnosed with a torn meniscus, an injury so severe that will end his baseball career. "It's very disappointing," Nagata told reporters today. "It was totally unexpected, which makes it a little bit harder to deal with." Doctors originally thought he would have a full recovery. The 34-year-old Nagata has been in treatment and reported he was making great progress, when things suddenly went sour. He told BNN, "It went from good to bad ... and then finally, to the worst case -- doctors said my career is over."

In his career, Nagata has crafted a 48-44 mark with a 4.91 ERA and tossed 842.2 innings. Opposing clubs are batting .271 against him and he has 701 strikeouts.
