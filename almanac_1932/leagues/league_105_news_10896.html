Royales O'Gorman Claims 150th Victory
Monday, June 17th, 1929
It was a special day at Bennett Park for Montreal's Ray O'Gorman. The Royales ace logged his 150th career win against the Lions. In the 14-2 decision O'Gorman tossed 8.2 innings, surrendered 7 hits with 3 walks and 4 strikeouts.

"I'm real fortunate that I had the opportunity to play with such good players during my career," said O'Gorman. "I just couldn't have drawn it up any better. I'm glad I did it in this uniform, too. Montreal is a great organization to play for."

During his career the 34-year-old O'Gorman has piled up 2617.1 innings. Over that time, he has fanned 894 batters, walked 785 and put up a 3.68 ERA. His lifetime won-lost mark is 150-128 and opposing clubs have hit .279 against him.
