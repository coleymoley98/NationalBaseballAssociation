McInnis Batters Toronto Big Time
Thursday, July 17th, 1930
It was a sensational day at the plate for Boston Redcoats first baseman Phuwanart McInnis, but a tough one for the team, as the Redcoats dropped the contest 11-10 to the Toronto Blue Sox.

Even with the loss, McInnis was pure misery for the Toronto pitching staff. He left them shell-shocked after going 5-for-5 in an explosive display of hitting.

"It's always good to get a bunch of hits in a game, but you also want to win," said McInnis, who is the four-time All-Star .

"McInnis really hit the ball hard and when you do that, you've got a good chance to do some damage," Blue Sox manager Jabari Perrault told BNN reporters after the game. "I feel like we threw him some good pitches, but he just put some good swings on the ball."

Phuwanart McInnis singled in the 2nd, hit an RBI single in the 4th, singled in the 6th, hit an RBI double in the 8th and singled in the 9th.

So far this year in 54 games McInnis is hitting .370 with 6 home runs, 45 RBIs and 46 runs scored.
