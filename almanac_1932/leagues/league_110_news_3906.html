Wintringham Strokes 3 HR Against Omaha
Monday, June 18th, 1923
The "hardest feat in sports" debate is a never-ending one with no correct answer, but hitting three home runs is certainly in the conversation. That's exactly what David Wintringham did today at Jr. Coyotes Ballpark, going deep three times and carrying his team to victory, 9-8.

"It all came together. I was seeing the ball well and I got some good pitches to hit," Wintringham said. "Good things happen, when that happens."

The Saskatoon Jr. Coyotes left fielder blistered the Omaha Jr. Falcons for 4 hits, batted in 4 runs and scored 3 times.

"It ain't like we weren't trying," Omaha manager Jesse O'Byrne said. "He was hot and really torched my boys today."

David Wintringham doubled in the 1st, hit a two-run home run off Evan Sellers in the 3rd, hit a solo-shot off Evan Sellers in the 5th and hit a solo-shot off Philip Lightford in the 7th.

Thus far this season in 17 games Wintringham is batting .297 with 5 home runs, 11 RBIs and 11 runs scored. He has 19 hits in 64 at-bats.
