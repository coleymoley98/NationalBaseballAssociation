Cordillera Pitching Ravaged by Copperman
Sunday, March 11th, 1923
Manny Copperman had an answer for everything the Team Cordillera threw at him today at Atlanta Memorial Stadium. The Team first baseman went 7-for-7 in the game to put his name in the record book and put the Montana on top, 22-1.

Summing up his batting philosophy to BNN writers after the game, Copperman said, "I just see the ball and I hit it."

Manny Copperman singled in the 1st, doubled in the 2nd, hit an RBI single in the 2nd, singled in the 4th, hit a two-run home run off Jeremy Gladney in the 6th, singled in the 7th and hit an RBI double in the 8th.

This season Copperman is swinging the bat at a .444 pace. He has hit 1 home run and scored 8 times, while driving in 6. His on-base percentage is .500 and he has played in 6 games.
