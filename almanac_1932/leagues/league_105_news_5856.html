Hall of Fame Selection announcement coming next week
Wednesday, December 31st, 1924
The 1925 National Baseball Association Hall of Fame voting results will be revealed next week, but it may simply be a formality.

There's not likely to be an inductee this year, with no players standing out as being a shoo-in for enshrinement.

The most likely candidate to get votes is 1910 Pitcher of the Year Award winner Dermot Pilkington. He had a 12-year career and is mostly remembered for his time with the Boston Redcoats. He retired in 1919 at the age of 36.

Voters are reminded that ballots may be cast up until the votes are announced on January 7th, 1925.

In career numbers Pilkington has registered a 117-94 record with a 2.91 ERA.
