Sexauer, Siegel Suspended for Fracas
Thursday, July 2nd, 1931
Seamus Sexauer of the Boston Redcoats and Lindsey Siegel of the Baltimore Robins will be on the sidelines for a few days. They have been suspended for 5 and 3 games respectively by the league office for causing the bench-emptying free-for-all at the recent Redcoats-Robins game at Fenway Park 1912.

Neither player had any comment about the suspensions. Both clubs told BNN Sexauer and Siegel will begin serving them soon.

Thus far this season Sexauer is hitting .323 with 5 home runs, 30 RBIs and 19 runs scored. He has 40 hits in 124 at-bats and has played in 31 games.

Siegel has taken the mound for 11 relief appearances this season, nailing down no saves while notching a 4-2 record with a 3.48 ERA. The Baltimore reliever has limited opposing batters to a .296 batting average.
