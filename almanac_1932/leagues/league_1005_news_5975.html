Best Hurler Award Presented by CJBC
Monday, March 30th, 1925
This year there was no one better on the mound than D.J. Rhoden. Posting 3 wins and a 0.77 ERA was only part of the story for the Team superstar. For his outstanding performance in 1925 he was chosen as the Canadian Junior Baseball Championship Best Pitcher winner.

From start to finish Rhoden never let up. In 4 starts he recorded 3 wins and 1 loss, 23.1 innings, 13 strikeouts, 4 walks, yielded 22 hits, and permitted opposing batters to hit .253 against him.

He received 14 first place votes out of a possible 16. Alberto Badeau of the Team Montana finished second in voting with 2 first place votes, while Warwick Van Allen of the Team Quebec finished third.
