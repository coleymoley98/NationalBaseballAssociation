Blue Sox Swett Smacks 5 Hits
Saturday, July 9th, 1932
It's not something you read about in the sports pages every day -- a 5-for-6 day. For many, it might come around only once in a career -- and for Supakit Swett, it came around today.

The 24-year-old second baseman scattered hits all over SunTrust Park to pace the Brampton Blue Sox to a 16-5 victory over the Tecumseh Braves.

Tecumseh skipper Girvan Greene told reporters in the press room that "Swett deserves a hat-tip."

"He hit some good pitches against us," he said. "In fact, according to our scouting reports, Swett hit several that he is not supposed to be able to hit."

Supakit Swett singled in the 1st, grounded out in the 2nd, hit an RBI single in the 4th, hit a two-run double in the 5th, hit a two-run triple in the 6th, walked in the 8th and singled in the 9th.
