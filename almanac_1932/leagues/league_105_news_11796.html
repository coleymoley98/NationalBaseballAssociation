5-for-6 Day for Pelletier
Thursday, September 18th, 1930
After his 5-hit effort at League Park was tarnished by Boston's 12-11 loss to the Wolfeburg Celtics, right fielder Jason Pelletier told BNN beat writers it was "a missed opportunity for a special day."

"It really doesn't matter whether it's sandlot ball, high school or college, two hits in a game is nice," he said. "I would have been happy to stop there if it meant we got a win."

He didn't get the win, but the 26-year-old had a spectacular 5-for-6 game against the Celtics.

"His swing is getting there," said Redcoats skipper Brian Ahn after the game. "We want him to be quick with his swing, short and compact. We want him to be patient and go the other way when he can. Jason did that to perfection today and we wasted it."

Jason Pelletier grounded out in the 1st, doubled in the 4th, singled in the 6th, hit an RBI single in the 7th, hit an RBI single in the 8th and hit an RBI single in the 10th.

This season Pelletier is batting .364 with 4 home runs.
