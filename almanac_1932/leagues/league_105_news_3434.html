Millette Stands Out in Montreal Shutout
Saturday, September 3rd, 1932
All went well for Larry Millette of the Montreal Royales as he pitched his team to a 6-0 shutout victory over the Detroit Lions, limiting them to 6 hits. In the postgame interview, both managers had glowing things to say about Millette on the day.

"Larry's got a good selection of pitches he can throw at a guy," said Royales manager Hervé Morel. "He changes speed, gives different looks to different hitters. When he's on his game, he's quite a handful."

In the contest, the Montreal right-hander rang up 1 and surrendered 1 walk.

"He didn't give us any second chances," said Lions manager Jake Downing. "It's a guessing game with him and today we were always guessing wrong."

This year Millette has made 21 starts, thrown 163.1 innings, fanned 68 batters and walked 52. His won-lost record is 15-1 with a 1.93 ERA.
