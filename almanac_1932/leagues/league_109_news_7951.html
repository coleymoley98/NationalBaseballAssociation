Jacobs Captures EL Award for Best Manager
Wednesday, September 22nd, 1926
The Providence Redcoats had an outstanding season, much of it due to their astute and gifted skipper. For his managerial excellence, Timothy Jacobs reaped the reward of being voted Eastern League Manager of the Year.

"A manager is only as good as the players he's managing," Jacobs told reporters. "If I didn't have the talent to work with, making all the right moves wouldn't have helped a bit."

Under Jacobs the Redcoats finished first in the with a record of 52-30.
