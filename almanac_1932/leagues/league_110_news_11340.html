CBL Pitcher of the Year Award Goes to Collard
Thursday, August 15th, 1929
The Central Baseball League announced today that Manus Collard has been chosen the Pitcher of the Year Award winner for 1929. The Adams starter had everything working for him this past season as he soared to a 8-3 record.

In his exceptional season Collard chalked up a 1.65 ERA in 93 innings with 98 strikeouts and 32 walks. Enemy teams hit .205 against him in his 12 starts.

He received 8 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Ardell Satterwhite of the Adams Mounties finished second in voting, while Mel Berman of the Adams Mounties finished third.
