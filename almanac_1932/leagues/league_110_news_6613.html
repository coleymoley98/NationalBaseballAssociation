Big Year for Zwick, Wins CBL Pitcher of the Year Award
Saturday, August 15th, 1925
Totchtawan Zwick had a terrific 1925 season in the Central Baseball League. The St. Louis pitching stalwart received the most votes and has been proclaimed this year's Pitcher of the Year Award winner.

The Jr. Cardinaux ace starred with an impressive 6-1 record. In 13 starts, he gave up 62 base hits and struck out 109 in 90.1 innings with a 1.69 ERA. Opposing teams hit .193 against him.

He received 5 first place votes out of a possible 8. Jerome Whitaker of the Adams Mounties finished second in voting with 1 first place vote, while Charles-Olivier King of the Saskatoon Jr. Coyotes finished third with 2 first place votes.
