Bethea Grabs EL Reliever of the Year Award
Monday, September 15th, 1930
Winford Bethea has won the 1930 Eastern League Reliever of the Year Award, which at age 35 is a "milestone achievement" for the Harrisburg Robins reliever.

"I feel accomplished to win this when there are other younger, stronger, harder-throwing guys out there," he said.

The Toronto, The United Provinces native's numbers speak for themselves, as he captured the award with 7 saves in 26 relief appearances, compiling 67 innings. He had 84 strikeouts and 27 walks, a record of 5-1 and an ERA of 2.42.

He received 10 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Gary Ha of the Harrisburg Robins finished second in voting, while Kent Lanser of the Kingston Indians finished third.
