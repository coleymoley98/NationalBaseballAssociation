Carmen Morales Captures Prestigious Trophy
Saturday, October 18th, 1924
The manager of the Boston Redcoats said he thinks Carmen Morales is only going to get better as he gets older.

He may only be 26, but it will be hard to top the shortstop's 1924 campaign, as he has been named the winner of the Canadian League Most Valuable Player Award.

His winning campaign featured a .395 batting average and a .436 on-base percentage this year. In 111 games he piled up 173 hits, 31 doubles, 12 triples, 6 home runs, 72 RBIs and 83 runs scored.

He received 16 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Erick Dour of the Boston Redcoats finished second in voting, while Joshua Millward of the Baltimore Robins finished third.
