Bélanger Big Deal in Boston: Hits for Cycle
Saturday, September 12th, 1925
Nicolas Bélanger was jubilant as the Boston Redcoats left the field with a 15-5 win over the Toronto Blue Sox today. He had every right to be.

The 26-year-old shortstop hit for the cycle at Griffith Stadium 1911, had a 4-for-6 game and chalked up 4 runs batted in and 3 runs to boot.

"It's too bad I can't do that every day," Bélanger said with a smile to reporters after the game. "It would do wonders for my batting average."

Boston manager Apirak MacDonald said he was "proud" of his shortstop's feat, but was not expecting it to happen.

"Cycles are tough because triples are tough," he said. "As soon as the ball left the bat on that triple, guys were jumping off the bench for him. Honestly, I wasn't sure he was going to be able to leg it out."

Nicolas Bélanger hit an RBI single in the 1st, hit a solo-shot off Tadanori Kamai in the 3rd, hit a two-run double in the 3rd, grounded out in the 4th, tripled in the 7th and grounded out in the 9th.

Bélanger is batting .334 with 5 home runs and 60 RBIs this season.
