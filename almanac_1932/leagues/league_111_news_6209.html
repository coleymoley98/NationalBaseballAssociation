One Run Enough for Morgantown
Saturday, June 20th, 1925
Elliot Dostie was in a mood to talk following the Morgantown Mountaineers contest against the Toronto Jr. Blue Sox, expounding to BNN reporters on his pitching philosophy. He had good reason to be excitable, having tossed a complete game shutout of the Jr. Blue Sox, 1-0.

"There are three important elements in pitching success: location, location, and location," Dostie said. "I was hitting my spots time and time again."

The right-hander blanked Toronto on a 6-hitter, allowing no walks and racking up 3 strikeouts.
