Michigan End Lapierre's Streak
Friday, March 18th, 1932
The 0-game hitting streak of Team New York third baseman José Lapierre came to an end today at Villanueva Fields. The Team Michigan held him hitless in 5 at-bats and also sent the New York down to defeat, 19-6.

Later, Lapierre acknowledged to BNN reporters that the hit streak meant something to him.

"It was special," he said, "but I think other people make a bigger deal out of it than I do, or the players do. It was what we're all supposed to do; go out and hit the ball and try to score runs. It was a cool deal, but it's over now. Let's try to start a new one."

So far this season Lapierre is hitting .465 with 3 home runs.
