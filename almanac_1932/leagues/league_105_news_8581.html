Paquette on a Tear, Hits for Cycle
Thursday, June 30th, 1927
Sripatana Paquette truly did it all at League Park today.

The Ottawa Indians second baseman hit for the cycle -- collecting 1 single, 1 double, 1 triple and 1 home run -- as he paced his club to victory, 10-5 over the Wolfeburg Celtics.

"You know that sweet sound you hear when you hit the ball just right? I heard that every time today," Paquette said later. "It was a heck of a feeling."

It was a game good enough for him to make a little history. He bombed Wolfeburg pitching for 4 hits in 4 at-bats and his name will now go into the record books. Paquette also racked up 4 RBI and 3 runs scored in the game.

Sripatana Paquette hit a two-run home run off Dominic Drouin in the 1st, walked in the 3rd, hit an RBI double in the 5th, singled in the 7th and hit an RBI triple in the 8th.

So far this season Paquette is hitting .333 with 2 home runs, 30 RBIs and 27 runs scored. He has 43 hits in 129 at-bats and a .427 OBP.
