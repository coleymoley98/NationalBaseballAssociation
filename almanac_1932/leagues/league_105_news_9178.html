No Easy Feat: Pitcher Wins NL Most Valuable Player Award
Sunday, October 16th, 1927
Eric Woodmore is the winner of the 1927 National League Most Valuable Player Award.

The Chicagou Frontenacs ace was a step above the competition this year, winning the award despite it typically not going to a pitcher.

"I'm honored for sure. To be considered the best in the NBA is remarkable," he said.

On the season, Woodmore had a 2.85 ERA, with 79 strikeouts and 42 walks over 211.2 innings. He made 24 starts and fashioned a 17-7 record, with opponents hitting only .245 against him.

He received 8 first place votes out of a possible 16. Valéry Jetté of the Montreal Royales finished second in voting with 3 first place votes, while Taradin Boulay of the Minnesota Saints finished third with 3 first place votes.
