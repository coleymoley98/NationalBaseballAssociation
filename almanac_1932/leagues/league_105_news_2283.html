NL Hands Out Reliever of the Year Award
Monday, October 19th, 1931
The National League Reliever of the Year Award for 1931 has been bestowed on Montreal Royales relief specialist Dwight Lynch.

The 33-year-old bullpen ace, out of Trenton, The United Provinces, nailed down the award with 14 saves in 37 relief appearances, 28 strikeouts in 49 innings and a record of 7 wins and 2 losses to go with his 1.65 ERA.

He received 20 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Luke Reppenhagen of the Cincinnati Reds finished second in voting, while Jerome Whitaker of the Chicagou Frontenacs finished third.
