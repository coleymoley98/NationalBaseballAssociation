Ng Beats Saints for 100th Win
Wednesday, June 2nd, 1926
His goal at twenty was to win twenty games one year, pitch until he was thirty, win the league pennant and to make some money. Well, Jude Ng did something even better -- the 31-year-old Cincinnati Reds star has now won 100 games in his career. That put him in a very select group of pitchers.

He joined that exclusive club today at Great American Ball Park by whipping the Minnesota Saints 6-2, holding them to 8 hits in 9 innings while chalking up 2 strikeouts and no walks.

In the postgame interview, the modest Ng said, "It's special to win 100 games. You don't do it by yourself. You do it with good stuff and a good bullpen. You are only as good as the guys behind you. Years from now I might look back on it and give myself a little pat on the back."

In his career the 31-year-old Ng has accumulated a 100-103 won-lost mark and opposing clubs have hit .283 against him.
