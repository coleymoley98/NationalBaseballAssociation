1928 Premier Player Picked By NL
Monday, October 8th, 1928
Taradin Boulay took National Baseball Association by storm this year, and he did it all at the age of 22.

For his spectacular campaign, the Minnesota Saints star has been named the 1928 National League Most Valuable Player Award recipient.

"To be considered amongst the best in the league is overwhelming," said the first baseman.

On the season he compiled a .349 batting average to go with a .418 on-base percentage. The native of Kelowna, The United Provinces also added 28 doubles, 3 triples, 15 home runs, 74 RBIs and 69 runs scored.

He received 13 first place votes out of a possible 16. Eric Woodmore of the Chicagou Frontenacs finished second in voting with 2 first place votes, while Kitti Lei of the Montreal Royales finished third.
