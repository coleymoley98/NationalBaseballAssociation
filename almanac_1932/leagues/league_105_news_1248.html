Blue Sox Chill Cordier's Hot Streak
Saturday, May 9th, 1931
Ottawa shortstop Noël Cordier came into today's game against the Toronto Blue Sox on a hot streak. He left on a cold one.

The Blue Sox did their jobs well, beating the Ottawa Indians 13-2 and holding Cordier hitless to snap his 31-game hitting streak. The 27-year-old shortstop had a few words to say after finishing 0-for-3, telling reporters only that he "came up short."

"What Noël did was exciting and quite an achievement," said Indians manager Frank Boon. "It's tough enough to get one hit in this league, much less go 31 games in a row. I'm even more proud of the way he handled the press coverage. He never let the frenzy get to him or affect his play."

Currently Cordier is batting .310 with 1 home run, 3 RBIs and 6 runs scored.
