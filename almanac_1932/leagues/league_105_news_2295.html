Toronto Hurler Wins CL Pitcher of the Year Award
Thursday, October 22nd, 1931
The Toronto Blue Sox have a rising superstar or maybe even a budding Hall-of-Famer in the making.
It's Nicholas Chollet, this year's Canadian League Pitcher of the Year Award selection. This blossoming hurler wowed fans, peers and sportswriters with a fabulous performance.

Chollet compiled a 16-6 record in 25 starts, tallied 56 strikeouts in 206 innings and recorded a 2.93 ERA. Other clubs managed only a .262 batting average against him.

He received 20 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Totchtawan Zwick of the Toronto Blue Sox finished second in voting, while Weerasak Morillon of the Chicagou White Sox finished third.
