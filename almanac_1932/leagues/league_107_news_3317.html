Songprawati is Best In WBL
Tuesday, August 16th, 1932
The manager of the Everett Silvertips said he thinks Jared Songprawati is only going to get better as he gets older.

He may only be 16, but it will be hard to top the second baseman's 1932 campaign, as he has been named the winner of the Western Baseball League WBL Player of the Year.

His winning campaign featured a .316 batting average and a .359 on-base percentage this year. In 46 games he piled up 61 hits, 9 doubles, 4 triples, 14 home runs, 39 RBIs and 40 runs scored.

He received 6 first place votes out of a possible 8. Arthur Rouson of the Spokane Eagles finished second in voting with 2 first place votes, while Igor Pelletier of the Everett Silvertips finished third.
