Canadian League Names Vivas Best Player
Monday, October 18th, 1926
The manager of the Toronto Blue Sox said he thinks Santo Vivas is only going to get better as he gets older.

He may only be 26, but it will be hard to top the left fielder's 1926 campaign, as he has been named the winner of the Canadian League Most Valuable Player Award.

His winning campaign featured a .395 batting average and a .494 on-base percentage this year. In 87 games he piled up 126 hits, 22 doubles, no triples, 13 home runs, 67 RBIs and 66 runs scored.

He received 15 first place votes out of a possible 16. Charles Trottier of the Wolfeburg Celtics finished second in voting with 1 first place vote, while Methanon Moore of the Toronto Blue Sox finished third.
