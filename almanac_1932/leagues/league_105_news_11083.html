League Gives Harris 2-Game Suspension
Friday, July 12th, 1929
Kevin Harris didn't like the strike call, turned around and had some angry words with the home plate umpire. Apparently he went too far. The result is a 2-game suspension by the league office. The league cited his failure to leave the dugout after the ejection was the reason for the suspension.

The Chicagou right fielder was ejected in a game at Comiskey Park 1910 against the New York Rebels following a controversial third-strike call.

Harris has collected 72 hits for the White Sox this year, 3 of them home runs. He has also netted 35 RBIs and scored 47 runs while batting .326.
