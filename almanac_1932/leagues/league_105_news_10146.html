Injury Worsens, Return Delayed For Blue Sox Wise
Monday, August 20th, 1928
Blue Sox starting pitcher Jamison Wise has suffered a setback in his rehabilitation for a strained oblique. He was expected to finish treatment soon and rejoin the team slightly ahead of schedule. Doctors reevaluated him after he felt severe pain in a recent workout. The preliminary examination revealed more damage and is worse than the first injury. Anonymous sources in Toronto told BNN Jamison Wise would be absent from the roster quite a bit longer than originally diagnosed. He would now have to miss at least 4 days.

This season to date Wise has a record of 9 wins and 8 losses with a 4.13 ERA, has appeared in 23 games and struck out 52 in 157 innings.
