Hits Galore: Michaud Drills 5 Hits
Monday, July 7th, 1930
Eliot Michaud was on quite a roll today as he knocked the ball all over Field of the Juniors, collecting 5 hits. His day of glory wasn't able to win the game, however. The New Britain Yankees got the 4-2 victory over the Sherbrooke Canadiens.

"I never realized that a few hits could cause so much commotion," the second baseman said in the clubhouse after the game, crowded by reporters. "Headlines and highlights are great, but it takes a lot of luster off it that we lost."

After his first couple of hits, all eyes in the ballpark were on him each time Michaud stepped into the batter's box. Even some opposing fans were pulling for him, knowing they were watching a little slice of baseball history unfold.

When asked what the secret of hitting so well is, he smiled and said "relax, concentrate, and hit the ball where they ain't."

That's exactly what he did today, as Michaud finished the game with 5 hits in 5 at-bats and a line in the record book. Eliot Michaud hit a two-run double in the 1st, singled in the 3rd, singled in the 5th, singled in the 7th and singled in the 9th.

This season Michaud is batting .371 with 21 RBIs, 15 runs scored and 1 home run.
