David Wintringham Captures CL Most Valuable Player Award
Friday, October 17th, 1930
The manager of the Ottawa Indians said he thinks David Wintringham is only going to get better as he gets older.

He may only be 24, but it will be hard to top the left fielder's 1930 campaign, as he has been named the winner of the Canadian League Most Valuable Player Award.

His winning campaign featured a .361 batting average and a .400 on-base percentage this year. In 108 games he piled up 167 hits, 15 doubles, 6 triples, 46 home runs, 140 RBIs and 106 runs scored.

He received 13 first place votes out of a possible 20. Trent Norrell of the New York Rebels finished second in voting with 7 first place votes, while Dustin Cheng of the Toronto Blue Sox finished third.
