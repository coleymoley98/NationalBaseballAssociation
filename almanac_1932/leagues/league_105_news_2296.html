Detroit's Sumonrat Snags Pitcher of the Year Award
Thursday, October 22nd, 1931
Panuwat Sumonrat of the Detroit Lions was in command and couldn't do much wrong when he was on the mound this season. His outstanding 11-7 won-lost mark and 2.46 ERA were big factors in his winning the 1931 National League Pitcher of the Year Award.

In 182.2 innings of work and 25 starts, Sumonrat struck out 94 while giving up 197 hits and 37 walks. He held opposing batters to a .278 average.

He received 18 first place votes out of a possible 20. Logan L'Heureux of the Chicagou Frontenacs finished second in voting with 2 first place votes, while André Tremblay of the Cincinnati Reds finished third.
