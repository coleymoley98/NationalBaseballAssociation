Week's Top Player: St. John's's Walsh
Monday, July 11th, 1927
It can be difficult for a young player to step out of the shadows and assert himself in the Atlantic Baseball League, but last week Jake Walsh of the St. John's Mariners reminded everyone just why he's here as he threw up numbers that made him an easy selection for Player of the Week. Walsh rocked opposing pitching for a .440 average (11-for-25), 3 home runs and 8 RBIs to come away with the award.

This season Walsh has delivered 36 hits in 138 at-bats for a .261 average with 5 home runs. He is getting on base at a .331 clip and his totals also include 29 RBIs and 20 runs scored. This year Walsh has played in 36 games.
