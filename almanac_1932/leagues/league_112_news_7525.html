Red Sox End Blanchette's Streak
Wednesday, July 14th, 1926
The Pawtucket Red Sox got more than the 9-7 victory at Juniors Ballpark today -- they also put an end to the 27-game hitting streak of Halifax Highlanders right fielder Ruell Blanchette by holding him without a hit in 2 at-bats.

After the game, Blanchette seemed more concerned with his team taking the loss than with the demise of his streak.

"I had some great looks at some pitches that I should have hit," he said. "I missed some that I shouldn't have swung at. All in all, I wasn't much help with the bat today."

Presently Blanchette is hitting .370 with 6 home runs and 24 RBIs. He has played in 40 games and scored 35 times.
