Skipper O'Byrne Put out to Pasture in Pointe-Sainte-Anne
Tuesday, August 16th, 1927
The future of the Pointe-Sainte-Anne Acadiens will not include Jesse O'Byrne.

Much-criticized for his in-game managing by local scribes of late, O'Byrne has been fired as manager of the Acadiens.

The decision is believed to have been made primarily by the team's owner, who was always quick to publicly criticize O'Byrne's decisions on the field and in the clubhouse.

When reached at his home in Pointe-Sainte-Anne, owner The owner of the Pointe-Sainte-Anne Acadiens said the decision was made "in the best interest of the team," but he refused to elaborate on the specific reasons which led to the firing.

O'Byrne told BNN he plans to explore his options for another job in baseball.

"I'll miss Pointe-Sainte-Anne and I'll miss the Acadiens, but I won't miss having everything I do second-guessed," he said.
