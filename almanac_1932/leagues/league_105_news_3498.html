Wintringham Hits Way to CL Triple Crown
Wednesday, September 14th, 1932
This season, David Wintringham exhibited a rare combination of hitting both for average and power. Indeed, the Ottawa Indians star claimed baseball's Triple Crown, leading the Canadian League in batting average, home runs and RBIs.

"I've always taken pride in my ability to hit the ball," Wintringham said. "But honestly I never thought I'd put up numbers like that."

He led the Canadian League with a .419 BA, 45 home runs and 134 RBIs. The 26-year-old Indians left fielder is the 8th player in National Baseball Association history to do this rare feat. Quite a year for quite a player.
