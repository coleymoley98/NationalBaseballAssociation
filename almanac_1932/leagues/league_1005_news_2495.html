Côté Has Hot Hand, Bangs 5 hits
Wednesday, March 30th, 1932
There aren't many who could be pro ballplayers and there are even fewer who could put up 5 hits in a day.

Donnie Côté is one of those rare few, as he went 5-for-7 against the Team Michigan at Villanueva Fields today. The monster effort from the 19-year-old catcher helped the Team Columbia take the win, 24-17.

"We made some bad pitches and he took advantage of it," said Michigan manager Jozie Rivière. "That's the game, though. Don't make mistakes and you'll win ballgames. Make mistakes and, well, you saw what happened today."

Donnie Côté hit a solo-shot off Will Simard in the 2nd, singled in the 3rd, hit an RBI single in the 4th, singled in the 6th, flied out in the 7th, singled in the 8th and flied out in the 9th.

This year Côté has socked 18 hits in 52 at-bats for a .346 average with 1 home run. He is getting on base at a .358 pace and his totals also include 10 RBIs and 11 runs scored.
