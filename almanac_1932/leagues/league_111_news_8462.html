Ruckus Reaps 3-Game Suspensions
Wednesday, June 15th, 1927
In an announcement today, the league informed Jean-Louis Lussier of Canton and John Yeremyan of Juneau that they both had been suspended 3 games for fighting in a recent game.

The league president told them, "This type of aggressive behavior will not be tolerated in this league."

Lussier wasn't happy with the length of his suspension. He thought he got the worst deal and told the league commissioner, "In any fight, whoever throws the first punch should get a more severe penalty. If I threw the first punch, I'd expect to get the most punishment. Whoever took the blow should get a lesser punishment."

His manager agreed with him and said something needs to be done about it. "Lussier is right. The league needs to look at its policy and make sure the punishment fits the crime."

Looking at his present stats, Lussier has compiled a .132 batting average in 13 games with a .214 on-base percentage, while hitting no home runs and driving in 3 runs.

Yeremyan has taken the mound for 6 relief appearances this season, nailing down 2 saves while notching a 0-1 record with a 4.15 ERA. The Juneau reliever has limited opposing batters to a .212 batting average.
