Reliever of the Year Award Goes to Ratel
Sunday, October 11th, 1925
He may only be 25, but Taylor Ratel of the Detroit Lions has already established himself as one of the elite relievers in the National League.

The reliever was today named the NL Reliever of the Year Award winner for 1925.

He won the award with a strong year that saw him put up 10 saves in 34 relief appearances and 15 strikeouts in 41.2 innings. He had a record of 4-3 to go with his 2.38 ERA.

He received 16 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Jack Huba of the Montreal Royales finished second in voting, while Billy Wall of the Montreal Royales finished third.
