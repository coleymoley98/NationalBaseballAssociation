1932 Top Player Selected In EBL
Thursday, August 18th, 1932
He may have less experience than most of his Eastern Baseball League peers, but that didn't slow Jaxon Soucy down at all in 1932.

At the age of 16, the Buffalo right fielder has been named the winner of the Eastern Baseball League EBL Player of the Year.

He had a .339 batting average and a .415 on-base percentage this year. In 49 games he piled up 62 hits, 17 doubles, 1 triple, 16 home runs, 40 RBIs and 44 runs scored.

"I'm blown away by the season I was able to put together," he said. "I'm looking forward to trying to do it all again next year."

He received 9 first place votes out of a possible 10. Surat Bédard of the Canton Legends finished second in voting with 1 first place vote, while Mark Madgewick of the Morgantown Mountaineers finished third.
