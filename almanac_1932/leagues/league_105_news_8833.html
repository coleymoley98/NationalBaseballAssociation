Sexauer Slaps 5 Hits
Saturday, July 30th, 1927
Seamus Sexauer knows as well as anyone that in baseball, the biggest contracts go to the biggest hitters. At Griffith Stadium 1911 today, he made his case to be considered among that group.

The 24-year-old left fielder hammered 5 hits in 5 chances, though the remarkable effort came even as his Boston Redcoats fell, 6-4, to the Toronto Blue Sox.

"We made some bad pitches and he took advantage of it," Blue Sox manager Jabari Perrault told BNN sportswriters after the game. "Thankfully, we were able to do enough elsewhere that Sexauer wasn't the difference."

Seamus Sexauer singled in the 1st, singled in the 3rd, singled in the 5th, hit an RBI single in the 7th and singled in the 9th.

This year Sexauer is hitting at a .372 pace with 4 home runs and 40 RBIs. He has played in 65 games and scored 45 times.
