Matthis Coderre Chosen CJBT's #1 Player
Friday, April 4th, 1930
At the age of 20, Matthis Coderre of the Team Pennsylvania could very well have his best years ahead of him.

It's a startling thought when you consider the year he had in 1930 was good enough to win the catcher the Canadian Junior Baseball Championship Most Valuable Player Award.

"See the ball, hit the ball," he said. "That's my basic approach."

The top hitter put up a .453 batting average this year, as well as a .584 on-base percentage. He played 22 games and racked up 39 hits, 8 doubles, no triples, 5 home runs, 19 RBIs and 30 runs scored.

He received 6 first place votes out of a possible 18. Max Carr of the Team Oregon finished second in voting with 5 first place votes, while Jirawat Wagers of the Team New York finished third with 4 first place votes.
