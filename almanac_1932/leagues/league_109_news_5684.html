Braddock Gets Award for Relief Work
Wednesday, September 17th, 1924
They say you can't teach an old dog new tricks, but in the case of Mike Braddock of the Providence Redcoats, you don't have to - his old tricks worked just fine in 1924.

The 37-year-old veteran turned in a sparkling performance and has won the Eastern League Reliever of the Year Award.

He captured the award with 6 saves in 27 relief appearances, 55 strikeouts and 21 walks in 75.1 innings. He put up a record of 7-4 to go with his 2.87 ERA.

He received 8 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Zach Guglielmo of the Sherbrooke Nordiques finished second in voting, while Joe Paquin of the Albany Rebels finished third.
