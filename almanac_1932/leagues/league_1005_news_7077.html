Hanprab Caps Memorable Season with CJBC Most Valuable Player Award Honor
Saturday, April 3rd, 1926
If he wasn't already a household name in Canadian Junior Baseball Championship, Watcharapong Hanprab of the Team Erie certainly is now.

He has a bright future ahead of him after today being named the winner of the 1926 Canadian Junior Baseball Championship Most Valuable Player Award, at the age of only 20.

"I feel like I'm getting better all the time, so I'm looking forward to the years to come," said the ace, who this year put up a 4-0 record.

Overall, Hanprab made 5 starts and pitched 29.2 innings, racking up 8 strikeouts with 5 walks and a 1.21 ERA.

He received 6 first place votes out of a possible 16. Basil Betts of the Team Erie finished second in voting with 7 first place votes, while Randy Kau of the Team Columbia finished third with 2 first place votes.
