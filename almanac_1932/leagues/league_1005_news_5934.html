Labelle Socks it to Michigan, Cracks 5 Hits
Saturday, March 7th, 1925
There aren't many who could be pro ballplayers and there are even fewer who could put up 5 hits in a day.

Jean-Christophe Labelle is one of those rare few, as he went 5-for-5 against the Team Michigan at Yokohama Memorial Grounds today. The monster effort from the 21-year-old left fielder helped the Team Erie take the win, 19-1.

"We made some bad pitches and he took advantage of it," said Michigan manager Warut Myers. "That's the game, though. Don't make mistakes and you'll win ballgames. Make mistakes and, well, you saw what happened today."

Jean-Christophe Labelle hit an RBI single in the 1st, singled in the 2nd, walked in a run in the 3rd, hit an RBI double in the 4th, singled in the 6th and singled in the 8th.

This year Labelle has batted at a .706 pace with 12 hits, 1 home run, 8 RBIs and scored 9 times.
