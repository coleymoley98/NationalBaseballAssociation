Strained PCL Shelves Whisler for Next 3 Weeks
Friday, August 28th, 1925
What was originally thought to be a minor injury has turned into a major headache for Corbin Whisler and the Chicagou. Whisler suffered the injury while out for a morning run. "All I felt at the time was a little pull and some soreness," said the Chicagou starting pitcher. "Then it gradually became more painful." An MRI revealed that Whisler has a strained posterior cruciate ligament, which will keep him out of action for about 3 weeks. "We'll just have to make do without him and hope he's back with the team soon," said manager Phi Fini. Asked if the team is exploring replacement options for Whisler, Fini said, "We'll re-evaluate the situation in 3 weeks, but I really doubt we'll need to worry past that point."
