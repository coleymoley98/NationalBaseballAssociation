Best WL Reliever Named
Sunday, September 18th, 1927
The Winnipeg Thunder got more than they expected out of Tida Tremblay in 1927.

At the age of 35, there were questions about whether or not Tremblay could still compete at a high level, but those questions were answered emphatically as he has won the Western League Reliever of the Year Award.

On the season, the Youngstown, The United Provinces native recorded no saves in 14 relief appearances and threw 84.2 innings. He recorded 42 strikeouts and 36 walks, while posting an opponent's batting average of .300.

He received 8 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Négro Silvas of the Seattle Rainiers finished second in voting, while Nigel Adler of the Vancouver Giants finished third.
