Sunday, June 15th, 1924
The "hardest feat in sports" debate is a never-ending one with no correct answer, but hitting three home runs is certainly in the conversation. That's exactly what Josh Banks did today at Jr. Steelers Ballpark, going deep three times and carrying his team to victory, 15-6.

"It all came together. I was seeing the ball well and I got some good pitches to hit," Banks said. "Good things happen, when that happens."

The Toronto Jr. Blue Sox first baseman blistered the Hamilton Jr. Steelers for 4 hits, batted in 6 runs and scored 3 times.

"It ain't like we weren't trying," Hamilton manager Prasarn Heemkhao said. "He was hot and really torched my boys today."
