Brunet Grabs NL Reliever of the Year Award
Tuesday, October 14th, 1924
Hervé Brunet appears headed for a big payday.

The impending free agent turned in a dominant season with the St. Louis Cardinaux in 1924 and has won the National League Reliever of the Year Award.

With his contract situation unclear for next season, it appears his dominant performance will have him poised to command big money.

For the year, the 30-year-old native of Toronto, The United Provinces put up a 2.49 ERA, over 72.1 innings of work. He made 52 relief appearances and had 11 saves, 46 strikeouts, 29 walks and a record of 10-6.

He received 11 first place votes out of a possible 16. Alan Tonnessen of the Chicagou Frontenacs finished second in voting with 5 first place votes, while Rudy Robitaille of the Detroit Lions finished third.
