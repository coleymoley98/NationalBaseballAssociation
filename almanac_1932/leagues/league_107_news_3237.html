Everett Wins Bennett Cup!
Friday, August 5th, 1932
Sometimes the winner is the one who got the lucky bounces and the lucky calls, and sometimes the winner is just the one who played better.

It was the latter case in 1932, as the Everett Silvertips topped the Spokane Eagles 3-1 tonight at Silvertips Ballpark to win the Bennett Cup. It was the 2nd title in franchise history.

"When you play for championships, you have to play better than the team in the other dugout. We did that in this series, and all year. Now we're the best team in baseball," said Everett center fielder Igor Pelletier, who was named series MVP.

The Silvertips took the series 3-2. The new titleholders completed the regular season with a first-place finish in the and a 32-18 record.

Everett manager Kerry Pope grinned and nodded when asked to comment on Pelletier's words.

"Igor said it best -- we played better than the other team," he said.

Hometown officials announced they were already planning a "grand celebration" for the winners in the days to come.
