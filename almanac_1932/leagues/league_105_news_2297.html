David Wintringham Nabs Best Player Trophy
Friday, October 23rd, 1931
At the age of 25, David Wintringham of the Ottawa Indians could very well have his best years ahead of him.

It's a startling thought when you consider the year he had in 1931 was good enough to win the left fielder the Canadian League Most Valuable Player Award.

"See the ball, hit the ball," he said. "That's my basic approach."

The top hitter put up a .397 batting average this year, as well as a .436 on-base percentage. He played 108 games and racked up 182 hits, 30 doubles, 14 triples, 28 home runs, 119 RBIs and 117 runs scored.

He received 20 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Beau Lobdell of the Ottawa Indians finished second in voting, while Harry Wilder of the New York Rebels finished third.
