WL Star of the Week Honored
Monday, June 27th, 1932
It's always a thrill for fans of the team and students of the game alike when a superstar in the making shows what he's got. William Wood, the Edmonton Eskimos 22-year-old first baseman, put himself in the spotlight last week making him the clear-cut choice for Western League Player of the Week honors.

He ripped 14 hits in 27 at-bats, compiling a .519 average with 2 home runs and 11 RBIs.

This season Wood is batting .464 with 25 RBIs, 26 runs scored and 7 home runs.
