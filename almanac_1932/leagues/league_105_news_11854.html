Best Hurler Award Presented by NL
Thursday, October 16th, 1930
With speculation about his retirement gaining steam last offseason, Royales ace Ray O'Gorman had something to prove. He proved it in award-winning style as he accepted the National League Pitcher of the Year Award today.

The veteran Montreal hurler posted a record of 14 wins and 4 losses this year with a 2.53 ERA. In 21 starts he struck out 84 and walked 37 in 174 innings.

He received 14 first place votes out of a possible 20. Larry Millette of the Montreal Royales finished second in voting with 6 first place votes, while Charles-Olivier King of the Chicagou Frontenacs finished third.
