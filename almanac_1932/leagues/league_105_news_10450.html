Boonsom Honored by First Ballot Selection to NBA Hall of Fame
Wednesday, January 2nd, 1929
At the Hall of Fame induction ceremonies today, the longtime friend and manager of Naithim Boonsom told the audience, "The worst thing as a manager is the day you realize you want to win more than the players do. I never had that problem with Naithim. Nobody wanted to win more than this gifted pitcher."

For his strong and consistent pitching over the years, Boonsom was voted into the National Baseball Association Hall of Fame by the Baseball Writers Association.

When he ended his career at age 35, Boonsom had a career won-loss record of 164 wins and 119 losses with 1231 strikeouts in 2780.2 innings and a 2.44 ERA.
