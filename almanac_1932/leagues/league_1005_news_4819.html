CJBC Awards Most Valuable Player Award, Brochard Gets Prize
Friday, March 28th, 1924
Supakit Brochard of the Team Columbia had a fine season this year and today he accepted the Most Valuable Player Award for 1924.

The 20-year-old first baseman put up a .494 average and a .528 on-base percentage, piling up 39 hits, 14 doubles, 1 triple, 3 home runs, 30 RBIs and 26 runs scored.

He received 8 first place votes out of a possible 16. Supphakorn Tienphosuwan of the Team Ontario finished second in voting with 6 first place votes, while Jules Caouette of the Team Erie finished third.
