Grandy Rucker said his performance in 1928 was a "statement" to the entire league.

"As an impending free agent you feel like you've got something to prove, something to work for to ensure that next contract," said the Philadelphia Liberty pitcher, who is seemingly heading for free agency.

The bullpen ace certainly proved himself this year, and he has won the Canadian League Reliever of the Year Award.

The Trois-Rivières, The United Provinces native had a 2.85 ERA this season over 66.1 innings of work. He made 35 relief appearances and had 3 saves, 12 strikeouts, 23 walks and a record of 9-3. Opponent batters hit only .290 against him.

He received 16 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Mathieu Gagné of the New York Rebels finished second in voting, while Logan Freund of the Chicagou White Sox finished third.
