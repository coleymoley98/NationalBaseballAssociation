Bloom Hits for the Cycle
Saturday, June 11th, 1927
Glenn Bloom did everything he could for the Everett Silvertips today -- literally.

The 15-year-old left fielder hit a single, a double, a triple and a home run for the Silvertips, going for the cycle and helping them get past the Abbotsford Alpines, 4-1, at Alpines Ballpark.

"Yeah, I was some kind of hitter in this game," Bloom reflected later. "It was like all I had to do was close my eyes and swing the bat."

In a game headed for the record books, Bloom banged out 4 hits in 5 at-bats and totaled 2 RBI and 2 runs scored. Speaking with the press about the feat, he attributed it to "a lot of luck."

"A triple is so hard to hit," he said. "To just decide to get one in a game when you need it for a cycle, it's impossible. Usually something weird has to happen, the ball kicking past an outfielder or somebody falling down. There's a ton of luck in hitting for a cycle."

Glenn Bloom singled in the 1st, tripled in the 3rd, hit an RBI double in the 5th, grounded out in the 7th and hit a solo-shot off Rickie Daniels in the 9th.

Currently Bloom has 3 home runs and a .341 batting average.
