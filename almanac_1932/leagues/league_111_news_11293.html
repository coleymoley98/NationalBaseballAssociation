Hamilton Sweeps Jr. Blue Sox
Monday, August 5th, 1929
The Hamilton Jr. Steelers made it look easy today as they collected the 1st Adams Cup championship in club history.

They swept the Toronto Jr. Blue Sox in straight games, winning the series 3-0. The Jr. Steelers disposed of Toronto 8-2 in the series finale at Juniors Ballpark.

"They were the better team this year, no doubt about it," said Jr. Blue Sox manager Pongsak Strong.

During the regular season, Hamilton put up a 30-20 record and finished first in the East.
