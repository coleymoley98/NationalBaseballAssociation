Order in the Court - Arbitration Hearings Arrive
Friday, October 24th, 1930
Few fans have the patience to keep up with the ins and outs of the NBA's collective bargaining agreement. For many fans, arbitration day is simply one day closer to spring, and the beginning of a new National Baseball Association season.

For those that do follow NBA details, arbitration day is an important milestone. Held for any players who were unable to come to terms on a new contract agreement with their team, these hearings can determine whether a player ends up making 70 percent or 100 percent of their contract demands.

Observers often worry that arbitration hearings will affect the relationship between players and their team, although the players are quick to disagree.

"Look, this is a business thing," said one arbitration-eligible player who asked to speak anonymously. "I am doing what I can to make as much money as I can and the owner is doing the same. I don't take it personally."

One wonders if that player will feel the same if he loses his arbitration decision.
