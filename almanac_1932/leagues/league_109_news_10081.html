Hamilton Blanks Rebels, Zwick Wins
Thursday, August 9th, 1928
The Hamilton Blue Sox shut out the Albany Rebels 2-0 today behind the strong pitching of Totchtawan Zwick. Zwick surrendered only 5 hits in recording the complete-game victory, and struck out 4.

Following the last out, Zwick appeared to celebrate a little too boisterously for Albany manager Grayson St-Louis, who could be seen scolding the famously outspoken right-hander as the clubs left the field. Zwick's own manager then intervened with some words for his counterpart.

The Rebels skipper would not comment on the exchange, but at the Blue Sox press conference, Zwick confirmed that it was regarding the celebration.

"He basically told me I was being a lousy winner," Zwick said.

"And I told him he was being a lousy loser," Hamilton manager Nelson Méthot added.

Up to now Zwick has compiled an ERA of 2.82 with a 7-4 mark over 17 starts. He has 137 strikeouts in 124.1 innings and has held opponents to a .218 batting average.
