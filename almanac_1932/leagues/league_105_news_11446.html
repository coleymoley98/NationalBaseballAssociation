Player of the Week Honor to Detroit Pitcher
Monday, September 2nd, 1929
Punkorn McKinzie has been around National Baseball Association long enough to know that you don't win Player of the Week awards by being lucky. The Detroit Lions hurler used his well-honed skill to put up superlative numbers last week against National League hitters to take home the prize.

McKinzie won 3 while striking out 7 in 19 innings. He also logged an ERA of 0.47 and held opposing hitters to a .179 average.

McKinzie has fashioned a 13-8 mark for the season, 2.55 ERA and thrown 190.1 innings in 22 starts. Opposing clubs are batting .243 against him.
