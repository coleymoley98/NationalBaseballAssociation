Legends Heading to Adams Cup
Friday, August 1st, 1924
The Canton Legends are riding high after winning the Division Finals and eliminating the Hamilton Jr. Steelers by a series score of 3-1.

The final blow came tonight at Jr. Steelers Ballpark, when Canton secured its spot in the Adams Cup with a 7-5 victory.

"It feels good to put them away because we really didn't want to let them crawl back into this series," said right fielder Grayson Kimmel, who was named series MVP. "A team like that, you need to bury them when you get the chance."

Kimmel certainly helped his team's cause, as he hit .471 with a .500 on-base percentage in the series. He also chipped in 1 home runs, drove in 2 RBI and scored 5 runs.

The Canton Legends don't know who they will face in the next round. They will play the winner of the Fort Wayne Pioneers - Morgantown Mountaineers series. That series is tied at 2 games apiece. The Adams Cup schedule will be announced after the Division Finals finishes.
