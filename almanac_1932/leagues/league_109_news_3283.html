Redcoats End Mujahideen's Streak
Thursday, August 11th, 1932
Muneer Mujahideen, center fielder for the Brampton Blue Sox, gave it a good try, but his time chasing history came to an end when he went hitless in 3 tries against the Providence Redcoats today. Not only did the Redcoats break his 21-game hitting streak, they also beat Brampton by the score of 7-2.

"Now that the streak has ended, I can admit I'll breathe a bit easier the next time I step up to bat," Mujahideen said, speaking with the media after the game. "It was tough because I put a little pressure on myself. Now that it's over, I will definitely be a little bit looser at the plate."

This year Mujahideen is hitting .350, produced 59 RBIs and 5 home runs, scored 68 times and gotten on base at a .383 rate.
