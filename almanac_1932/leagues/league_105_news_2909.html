Dollar Hittin' on All Cylinders
Wednesday, June 29th, 1932
Edwin Dollar punished the Minnesota Saints today, leading the Cardinaux to a 7-1 win. In doing so, Dollar hit for the cycle, tagging the Saints for 4 hits in 4 at-bats.

A player hitting a single, a double, a triple and a home run in the same game is one of the more unusual feats in professional baseball and typically happens only a couple times per season.

"Well, I didn't expect that to happen," the St. Louis catcher said after the game. "I got some great pitches and I hit their mistakes."

Dollar also had 3 RBI and 3 runs scored in the game at St. Louis Grounds.

Edwin Dollar hit a solo-shot off Prayad Adeleye in the 2nd, tripled in the 4th, singled in the 6th and hit a two-run double in the 7th.

So far this year Dollar is batting .324 with 1 home run, 9 RBIs and 9 runs scored.
