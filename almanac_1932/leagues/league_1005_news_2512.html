Jones Selected CJBT's Best Player
Friday, April 8th, 1932
Duane Jones of the Team Columbia proved that you can step out of the shadows and onto the stage if you've got the talent and the drive to hone your skills. Hard work, long hours and a tireless resolve paid off for him today in the form of the Canadian Junior Baseball Championship Most Valuable Player Award.

Jones hit for a .518 average while collecting 44 hits, 5 doubles, no triples, 6 home runs, 34 RBIs and scoring 36 times.

He received 9 first place votes out of a possible 18. Kelsey Martinka of the Team Dakota finished second in voting with 2 first place votes, while Rod Gibb of the Team Erie finished third with 4 first place votes.
