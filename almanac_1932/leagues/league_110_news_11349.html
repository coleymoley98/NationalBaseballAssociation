Martel Rewarded with 1929 CBL Most Valuable Player Award
Friday, August 16th, 1929
The future looks extremely bright for Virgile Martel. At the age of 17, many players are still trying to find their way in Central Baseball League.

Martel has not only found his way, he's already established himself as one of the game's brightest stars, today being named the winner of the Central Baseball League Most Valuable Player Award.

"I didn't expect to hit .363 this year, that's for sure," the third baseman said.

The Minneapolis Jr. Saints star also put up a .434 on-base percentage this year, with 53 hits, 15 doubles, 1 triple, 6 home runs, 24 RBIs and 35 runs scored.

He received 8 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Woody Choe of the Winnipeg Jr. Thunder finished second in voting, while Manus Collard of the Adams Mounties finished third.
