Bélanger Collects Cycle
Monday, August 25th, 1930
Nicolas Bélanger did everything he could for the Toronto Braves today -- literally.

The 31-year-old shortstop hit a single, a double, a triple and a home run for the Braves, going for the cycle and helping them get past the St. Louis Cardinaux, 6-3, at St. Louis Grounds.

"Yeah, I was some kind of hitter in this game," Bélanger reflected later. "It was like all I had to do was close my eyes and swing the bat."

In a game headed for the record books, Bélanger banged out 4 hits in 5 at-bats and totaled 1 RBI and 4 runs scored. Speaking with the press about the feat, he attributed it to "a lot of luck."

"A triple is so hard to hit," he said. "To just decide to get one in a game when you need it for a cycle, it's impossible. Usually something weird has to happen, the ball kicking past an outfielder or somebody falling down. There's a ton of luck in hitting for a cycle."

Nicolas Bélanger doubled in the 1st, tripled in the 3rd, grounded out in the 5th, hit a solo-shot off Jacob McGowan in the 7th and singled in the 9th.

This season Bélanger is batting at a .394 clip with 1 home run and 3 RBIs.
