Skipper Pope Cut by Jr. Coyotes
Friday, August 5th, 1927
All is not well in Saskatoon.

Kerry Pope's tenure as the Jr. Coyotes skipper might end up being remembered more for a memorable exit than for anything he accomplished on the baseball diamond.

Pope was fired as the manager of the Saskatoon Jr. Coyotes today and didn't take the news particularly well. Upon hearing of his termination, Pope immediately went to the owner's office, where he laid into the Jr. Coyotes primary decision-maker with an expletive-filled tirade behind closed doors.

"He called me most every name in the book. It wasn't pretty," said Saskatoon owner The owner of the Saskatoon Jr. Coyotes. "I'd say Kerry was simply reacting in the heat of the moment. Overall though, I would have preferred a classier exit."

After his verbal ambush of the team owner, Pope stormed out of the stadium and was not immediately available for comment.

Club officials have scheduled a meeting tomorrow to appoint an interim manager and begin the search for Pope's replacement.
