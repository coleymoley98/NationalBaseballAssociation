Week's Best Player: St. Louis's MacCartney
Monday, June 20th, 1932
The premier performer in the National League this week was Grey MacCartney of the St. Louis Cardinaux.

The hot-hitting 26-year-old right fielder put up a .481 average and .500 on-base percentage, piling up 13 hits in 27 at-bats, 1 home run, 6 RBIs and 5 runs scored.

That kind of hitting got him the NL Player of the Week honors.

Currently MacCartney is batting .321 with 51 hits and 4 home runs. He has knocked in 26 runs and scored 23 times.
