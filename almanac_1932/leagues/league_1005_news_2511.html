Great Season For Gibb, Wins CJBT Best Pitcher
Friday, April 8th, 1932
The votes are in for the 1932 Canadian Junior Baseball Championship Best Pitcher. It has been bestowed on Rod Gibb of the Team Erie.

The 22-year-old Erie ace finished the year with a 2-0 mark, logged 5 starts, threw 28.2 innings, yielded 34 hits, struck out 10 and held the opposition to a .298 batting average.

He received 9 first place votes out of a possible 18. Lester Hutchens of the Team New England finished second in voting with 8 first place votes, while J.P. Prokop of the Team Columbia finished third.
