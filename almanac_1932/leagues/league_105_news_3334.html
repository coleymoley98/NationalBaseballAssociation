Landis Records Shutout Against Rebels
Thursday, August 18th, 1932
The Toronto Blue Sox shut out the New York Rebels 4-0 today behind the strong pitching of Sakunchai Landis. Landis surrendered only 3 hits in recording the complete-game victory, and struck out 5.

Following the last out, Landis appeared to celebrate a little too boisterously for New York manager William Davison, who could be seen scolding the famously outspoken right-hander as the clubs left the field. Landis's own manager then intervened with some words for his counterpart.

The Rebels skipper would not comment on the exchange, but at the Blue Sox press conference, Landis confirmed that it was regarding the celebration.

"He basically told me I was being a lousy winner," Landis said.

"And I told him he was being a lousy loser," Toronto manager Jabari Perrault added.

Landis has fashioned a 10-8 mark for the season, 4.90 ERA and thrown 156 innings in 21 starts. Opposing clubs are batting .277 against him.
