Mr. Perfect: Lawadin Tosses Rare Gem
Monday, June 24th, 1929
Over the course of baseball history, some moments stand a little taller than others. One of those moments happened today at Ballpark of Drummondville.

Drummondville right-hander Seng Lawadin threw a perfect game at as the Voltigeurs beat the Pawtucket Red Sox, 4-0. In his post-game comments, Lawadin told reporters that it was something he had never dreamed of.

"As a kid, you always think of pitching to win the final game of a championship," Lawadin said. "You never think of pitching a perfect game, because it just seems unattainable. I'll remember this day for the rest of my life."

By the end of the game, Lawadin had rung up 16 Pawtucket hitters on strikeouts, and did not allow a single baserunner. The fans gave Lawadin a standing ovation as he left the mound, knowing they had witnessed one of the rarest events in the sport.

In 6 starts this season, Lawadin has picked up 2 wins while suffering 2 losses. He has pitched 42 innings, allowed 30 hits, struck out 44 and posted a 3.00 ERA.
