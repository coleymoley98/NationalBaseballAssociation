NL Gives #1 Pitcher Award
Wednesday, October 10th, 1923
The Quebec Nordiques have a rising superstar or maybe even a budding Hall-of-Famer in the making.
It's Daniel Ibarra, this year's National League Pitcher of the Year Award selection. This blossoming hurler wowed fans, peers and sportswriters with a fabulous performance.

Ibarra compiled a 13-12 record in 26 starts, tallied 113 strikeouts in 224.1 innings and recorded a 2.85 ERA. Other clubs managed only a .235 batting average against him.

He received 7 first place votes out of a possible 16. Eric Woodmore of the Minnesota Saints finished second in voting with 5 first place votes, while Thai Edwards of the St. Louis Cardinaux finished third with 4 first place votes.
