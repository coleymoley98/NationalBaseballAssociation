Top Player Named by Eastern Baseball League
Friday, August 22nd, 1924
Sang Sunanda took Eastern Baseball League by storm this year, and he did it all at the age of 18.

For his spectacular campaign, the North Bay Battalion star has been named the 1924 Eastern Baseball League EBL Player of the Year recipient.

"To be considered amongst the best in the league is overwhelming," said the third baseman.

On the season he compiled a .360 batting average to go with a .429 on-base percentage. The native of Gervais, The United Provinces also added 8 doubles, 3 triples, 16 home runs, 42 RBIs and 43 runs scored.

He received 10 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Kyle Nadeau of the Morgantown Mountaineers finished second in voting, while Adam Klein of the Morgantown Mountaineers finished third.
