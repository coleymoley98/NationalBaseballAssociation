Coude Takes Division Finals
Tuesday, August 3rd, 1926
Coude center fielder Thomas Drolet couldn't hide his reaction when asked about being named MVP of the Division Finals.

"It's a nice honor," he said, failing to suppress a wide smile. The trophy was presented to Drolet moments after his club eliminated the St. John's Mariners to advance to the Laurier Cup.

Coude won the final game by a score of 13-1 at T-Mobile Park and took the series 4-2.

"We won the series and that's the main thing," Drolet said. "They were a tough team and we didn't want to take anything for granted."

For the series, Drolet hit .450 with a .593 on-base percentage. He had 2 home runs, drove in 5 RBI and scored 6 runs.

"He was tough to stop and that's a big reason they're there and we're here," said a dejected St. John's manager Joey Bumpers.

The Coude Voyageurs don't know who they will face in the next round. They will play the winner of the Hartford Whalers - Quebec City Jr. Nordiques series. That series is tied at 3 games apiece. The Laurier Cup schedule will be announced after the Division Finals finishes.
