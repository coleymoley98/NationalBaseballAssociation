Trottier Cracks 5 Hits
Saturday, May 12th, 1928
Charles Trottier knows as well as anyone that in baseball, the biggest contracts go to the biggest hitters. At League Park today, he made his case to be considered among that group.

The 28-year-old left fielder hammered 5 hits in 5 chances, though the remarkable effort came even as his Wolfeburg Celtics fell, 9-6, to the Chicagou White Sox.

"We made some bad pitches and he took advantage of it," White Sox manager Eric Scism told BNN sportswriters after the game. "Thankfully, we were able to do enough elsewhere that Trottier wasn't the difference."

Charles Trottier hit an RBI double in the 2nd, singled in the 4th, hit a two-run double in the 5th, doubled in the 8th and singled in the 9th.

This year Trottier is batting .450 with no home runs. He has scored 3 runs and driven in 7.
