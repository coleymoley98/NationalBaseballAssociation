Gilliard Wins EL Player of the Week Trophy
Monday, August 17th, 1925
It can be difficult for a young player to step out of the shadows and assert himself in the Eastern League, but last week Samuel Gilliard of the Providence Redcoats reminded everyone just why he's here as he threw up numbers that made him an easy selection for Player of the Week. Gilliard rocked opposing pitching for a .348 average (8-for-23), 4 home runs and 9 RBIs to come away with the award.

So far in 1925 Gilliard is batting .292 with 11 home runs, 39 RBIs and 31 runs scored. He has 54 hits in 185 at-bats and a .333 OBP.
