Canadian League Defeats National League 7-6 in Tight All-Star Game
Tuesday, July 20th, 1926
All-Star New York Rebels' closer Mathieu Gagné had a night to remember.

Not only did he take the field with the game's best players, he was also a huge factor in the 7-6 Canadian League victory over the National League All-Stars.

"We had a meeting before the game, and I was struck by the talent in the room. Old stars, young stars, it's a game I'll never forget," Gagné said. "I talked to a few of my friends from back home, and they told me to go out and win the game for them. I'm sure they'll be thrilled."

Since coming into the league, Gagné has chalked up 42 saves. In 177 games, he has registered a 26-22 won-lost record with 82 strikeouts in 283 innings and compiled a 3.63 ERA.
