Postseason Bid Ends for Morgantown
Tuesday, July 19th, 1927
Nathan Daniels said it's going to be "an awfully long offseason" for the Morgantown Mountaineers after they missed the Eastern Baseball League playoffs. The club came close, but ultimately ran out of games to secure a postseason spot and were eliminated from contention today.

Morgantown last appeared in the postseason in 1925. This is their 2nd consecutive year missing the playoffs, and in total they have not advanced past the regular season 29 times in their 33-year history. In the last 10 seasons, the Mountaineers have missed the playoffs 6 times. Morgantown last won the Adams Cup in 1905.
