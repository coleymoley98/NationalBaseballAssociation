Paquette's Treatment Not Going Well, Suffers Setback
Sunday, May 31st, 1931
"It's worse than we thought." That was the news from the Indians clubhouse today as it was learned that second baseman Sripatana Paquette will be out for an added 5 days as a result of a recent injury. His treatment for back tightness will have to be extended. Initially it didn't seem the injury was a major concern, but the Ottawa second baseman told team trainers of some discomfort yesterday morning, and upon examination it was determined to be a more serious injury. "We'd rather take this thing slowly and make sure Sripatana gets back onto the field healthy," said the team's head trainer.

Thus far this season Paquette is hitting .463 with 2 home runs, 18 RBIs and 12 runs scored. He has 31 hits in 67 at-bats and has played in 16 games.
