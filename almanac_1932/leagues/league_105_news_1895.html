5-for-5 Game for Kanterman
Wednesday, July 29th, 1931
It was almost a perfect day for Leo Kanterman.

The Ottawa center fielder displayed a hot bat today, enjoying an outstanding 5-hit day, but something was missing: The Indians lost to the Boston Redcoats, 14-11. Kanterman told BNN reporters after the game that luck was "fickle."

Ottawa manager Frank Boon told the beat writers otherwise.

"There was no luck today," he said. "He got 5 hits because he has a solid approach to hitting and he put it all together today, and we lost because the rest of us didn't put it together so well."

Leo Kanterman singled in the 1st, doubled in the 3rd, doubled in the 5th, grounded out in the 6th, grounded out in the 8th, hit an RBI triple in the 9th and singled in the 11th.

So far this season, Kanterman has recorded a .424 batting average with 25 hits, no home runs, 11 RBIs, 12 runs scored and a .469 OBP.
