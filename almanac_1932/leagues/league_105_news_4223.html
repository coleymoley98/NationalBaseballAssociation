Dawkins Deals Redcoats 4-0 Shutout
Thursday, July 26th, 1923
Before the game, Dylan Dawkins said he liked his chances. His instincts proved to be right. In a superb outing at Fenway Park 1912, the Indians pitcher whitewashed the Boston Redcoats 4-0.

"I just had a feeling it was going to be my day," Dawkins said in the clubhouse afterward. "My control was awesome. I could throw my slider even when I was behind in the count."

The Ottawa starter held Boston scoreless, giving up 4 hits while registering no strikeouts and issuing 2 walks.

"We didn't have very many good at-bats," Redcoats manager Apirak MacDonald said in his postgame interview. "When that happens, you usually lose."

This year Dawkins has worked 108.1 innings with 22 strikeouts en route to a 4-10 record and 4.82 ERA.
