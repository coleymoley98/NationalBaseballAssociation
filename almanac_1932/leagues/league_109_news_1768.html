Saenz Pitches Tecumseh to Shutout Victory
Thursday, July 16th, 1931
All went well for Ray Saenz of the Tecumseh Braves as he pitched his team to a 5-0 shutout victory over the Albany Rebels, limiting them to 6 hits. In the postgame interview, both managers had glowing things to say about Saenz on the day.

"Ray's got a good selection of pitches he can throw at a guy," said Braves manager Calvert DeShon. "He changes speed, gives different looks to different hitters. When he's on his game, he's quite a handful."

In the contest, the Tecumseh left-hander rang up 7 and surrendered 1 walk.

"He didn't give us any second chances," said Rebels manager Daniel Harris. "It's a guessing game with him and today we were always guessing wrong."

In 10 starts this season, Saenz has picked up 5 wins while suffering 3 losses. He has pitched 72.1 innings, allowed 69 hits, struck out 56 and posted a 3.24 ERA.
