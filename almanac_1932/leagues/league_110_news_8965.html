Jeremy Doyle Chosen CBL's Premier Player
Thursday, August 18th, 1927
The manager of the Lincoln Pioneers said he thinks Jeremy Doyle is only going to get better as he gets older.

He may only be 17, but it will be hard to top the right fielder's 1927 campaign, as he has been named the winner of the Central Baseball League Most Valuable Player Award.

His winning campaign featured a .395 batting average and a .534 on-base percentage this year. In 49 games he piled up 62 hits, 18 doubles, 1 triple, 11 home runs, 38 RBIs and 44 runs scored.

He received 3 first place votes out of a possible 8. Phat Watitapun of the Winnipeg Jr. Thunder finished second in voting with 2 first place votes, while Martin Daves of the Lincoln Pioneers finished third with 1 first place vote.
