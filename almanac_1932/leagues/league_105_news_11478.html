MacCartney of St. Louis Earns NL Batting Crown
Monday, September 9th, 1929
He may only be 23 years old, but Grey MacCartney has already established himself as an elite National Baseball Association player.

The St. Louis Cardinaux star posted a .350 batting average this year, which won him the National League batting crown.

"I can't even believe it, to be honest," he told Baseball News Network.

His fantastic campaign consisted of 150 hits, 24 doubles, 11 triples and 6 home runs for the year. In 108 games MacCartney added 90 RBIs and scored 79 runs.
