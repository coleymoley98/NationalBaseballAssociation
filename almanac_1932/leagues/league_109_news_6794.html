McLain Wins EL Manager of the Year Trophy
Tuesday, September 22nd, 1925
Pittsburgh manager Virgil McLain caused quite a stir when he came to town. Noted for his feisty, no-nonsense attitude, McLain threw his all into the 1925 campaign, winning over critics and fans as he went. He also won a few ballgames, running up a 59-23 record, which was good enough to take the first place spot in the standings.

"These guys are grown men. They don't need psychobabble, they need to win ballgames," McLain snapped at his first press conference. While his approach was controversial, even for Pittsburgh, it netted the 48-year-old the Eastern League Manager of the Year trophy. In response to which he replied, "It ain't molecular physics, it's baseball," and began discussing potential free agents with the local media.
