5-for-5 Day for Laliberté
Sunday, July 13th, 1930
The 5th time was not the charm for Johnny Laliberté today.

The left fielder for the New Britain Yankees hit the milestone today at Juniors Ballpark, but it was the Hartford Whalers that salvaged a 12-8 victory. Laliberté finished with a 5-for-5 line at the plate, and it still wasn't enough.

"He was not an easy out, that's for sure," Hartford manager Louie Farris said to the press in the postgame interview. "I'm just glad we held everybody else in check and got the win."

Laliberté declined to speak to reporters at length following the game, saying only that "no loss is a good one."

Johnny Laliberté singled in the 1st, hit a two-run single in the 3rd, hit a solo-shot off Mongkut Stevely in the 5th, doubled in the 7th and singled in the 9th.

This year Laliberté has collected a .298 batting average to date with 45 hits in 151 at-bats, 28 RBIs, 10 home runs and scored 28 times. He has played in 40 games.
