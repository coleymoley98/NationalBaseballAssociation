Walsh Raps Sherbrooke for 5 Hits
Tuesday, July 5th, 1927
There was no storybook ending. Jake Walsh of the St. John's Mariners socked it to the visiting Sherbrooke Canadiens with a terrific 5-hit game today, but his team didn't get the win. The Mariners fell, 7-4, at T-Mobile Park.

"I don't want to sound ungrateful, but something is missing here," Walsh complained following the loss. "When you have a great game you should win the game, too."

He definitely had an unforgettable day in the batter's box, ripping out 5 hits in 5 at-bats. St. John's manager Joey Bumpers sympathized with his first baseman, telling BNN reporters after the game that "Jake played a whale of a game, but sometimes individual brilliance is not enough."

Jake Walsh doubled in the 1st, singled in the 3rd, hit a solo-shot off Antoine Toupin in the 6th, hit a solo-shot off Antoine Toupin in the 8th and hit an RBI single in the 9th.

This year Walsh is batting .246 with 4 home runs. He has scored 14 runs and driven in 24.
