1924 Best Player Selected In WBL
Tuesday, August 12th, 1924
At the age of 18, Taradin Boulay of the Boise Broncos could very well have his best years ahead of him.

It's a startling thought when you consider the year he had in 1924 was good enough to win the first baseman the Western Baseball League WBL Player of the Year.

"See the ball, hit the ball," he said. "That's my basic approach."

The top hitter put up a .382 batting average this year, as well as a .438 on-base percentage. He played 50 games and racked up 71 hits, 25 doubles, no triples, 8 home runs, 31 RBIs and 35 runs scored.

He received 8 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Kevin Harris of the Everett Silvertips finished second in voting, while Xavier Bissonnette of the Abbotsford Alpines finished third.
