Lévesque Goes 5-for-5
Tuesday, July 1st, 1930
A jubilant August Lévesque joined the postgame press conference at Alpines Ballpark today to offer some unusual words of encouragement -- for the Abbotsford Alpines pitching staff he had just vanquished.

"It may not have looked like it," said the Victoria Admirals catcher, "but I saw some awful good pitches today. If luck hadn't been on my side it might have turned out a lot different."

That would not have been the conclusion of anyone who saw his dominant 5-for-5 hitting performance, which led the Admirals to a 23-0 win.

"He made it look easy," said Erick Sethna, manager of the Alpines. "We never figured out what to do with him. Obviously."

August Lévesque hit a three-run double in the 1st, hit an RBI single in the 2nd, hit an RBI double in the 3rd, walked in the 4th, doubled in the 6th and hit a three-run home run off Chontawat Kanter in the 8th.

So far this year, Lévesque is batting .300 with 1 home run and 11 RBIs.
