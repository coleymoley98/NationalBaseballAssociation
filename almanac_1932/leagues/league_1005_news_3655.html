Whisler Caps Memorable Season with CJBC Most Valuable Player Award Honor
Sunday, April 1st, 1923
If he wasn't already a household name in Canadian Junior Baseball Championship, Corbin Whisler of the Team Erie certainly is now.

He has a bright future ahead of him after today being named the winner of the 1923 Canadian Junior Baseball Championship Most Valuable Player Award, at the age of only 20.

"I feel like I'm getting better all the time, so I'm looking forward to the years to come," said the ace, who this year put up a 2-0 record.

Overall, Whisler made 4 starts and pitched 25.1 innings, racking up 8 strikeouts with 1 walks and a 0.71 ERA.

He received 7 first place votes out of a possible 16. Adisak Chotiwecharak of the Team New York finished second in voting with 6 first place votes, while Pittayapat McElhaney of the Team Pennsylvania finished third with 1 first place vote.
