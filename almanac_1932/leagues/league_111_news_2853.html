Jr. Steelers Pomarès and Mountaineers Mullen Suspended for Fight
Wednesday, June 22nd, 1932
Yesterday at Jr. Steelers Ballpark Teerath Pomarès and Jaxson Mullen got into each other's space and then into each other's face. They began to grapple and fell to the ground. That's when it boiled over into a full-fledged bench-clearing tussle between the Hamilton Jr. Steelers and Morgantown Mountaineers. Reason finally prevailed as the umpires restored order. After much discussion, only the two major combatants were ejected from the game.

Today the league office notified the ballclubs that Pomarès will have to sit out 3 while Mullen would be given a 7-game suspension for their roles in the brawl.

Pomarès has 1 save this year. In 10 relief appearances he has tossed 17.1 innings, fanned 16, and given up 10 walks with a 4-0 record. The Hamilton reliever has recorded a 5.19 ERA and allowed the opposition to hit .281 when he is on the mound.

Mullen has taken the mound for 9 relief appearances this season, nailing down 2 saves while notching a 1-1 record with a 4.96 ERA. The Morgantown reliever has limited opposing batters to a .309 batting average.
