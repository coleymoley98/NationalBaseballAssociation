O'Neill Goes 5-for-5 Against Toronto
Friday, August 7th, 1931
It's not something you read about in the sports pages every day -- a 5-for-5 day. For many, it might come around only once in a career -- and for Luke O'Neill, it came around today.

The 24-year-old third baseman scattered hits all over SunTrust Park to pace the Chicagou Frontenacs to a 6-5 victory over the Toronto Braves.

Toronto skipper Aubrey Nagle told reporters in the press room that "O'Neill deserves a hat-tip."

"He hit some good pitches against us," he said. "In fact, according to our scouting reports, O'Neill hit several that he is not supposed to be able to hit."

Luke O'Neill singled in the 1st, singled in the 2nd, doubled in the 5th, singled in the 7th and doubled in the 9th.

In 65 games this season O'Neill is batting .300 with 77 hits and 3 home runs. He has scored 38 runs and driven in 45.
