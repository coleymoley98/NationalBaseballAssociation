1932 #1 Player Chosen By CBL
Thursday, August 18th, 1932
The manager of the St. Louis Jr. Cardinaux said he thinks Ned Perkins is only going to get better as he gets older.

He may only be 16, but it will be hard to top the first baseman's 1932 campaign, as he has been named the winner of the Central Baseball League Most Valuable Player Award.

His winning campaign featured a .161 batting average and a .158 on-base percentage this year. In 17 games he piled up 9 hits, 3 doubles, no triples, no home runs, 4 RBIs and 4 runs scored.

He received 8 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Jiranat Ballard of the St. Louis Jr. Cardinaux finished second in voting, while Naret Perron of the St. Louis Jr. Cardinaux finished third.
