NL Rookie of the Year Award Goes to Boulay
Tuesday, October 13th, 1925
The regular season is now history and it's awards time in the National League. This year's winner of the Rookie of the Year Award is 19-year-old Taradin Boulay of the Minnesota Saints.

The talented first baseman batted .388, played in 76 games and delivered 112 hits in 289 at-bats. His totals also included 11 home runs, 43 RBIs, 56 runs scored, 31 walks and a .450 OBP.

He received 16 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Zachary Beaulieu of the Cincinnati Reds finished second in voting, while Simon Hoagland of the Juneau Brasseurs finished third.
