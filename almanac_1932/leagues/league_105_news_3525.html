Ottawa Takes Division Series in Deciding Game
Wednesday, September 21st, 1932
Mark one up for the Ottawa Indians.

In the crucial final game, they put away the Wolfeburg Celtics 10-3 at Lincoln Park to win the Division Series, 3-2 and advance to the League Chamionship Series.

Although his team came up short, Wolfeburg's Elvis Sylvia was named the series MVP, with many of the league media feeling the series would have been over much sooner if not for his contributions.

"Sylvia did a lot to keep Wolfeburg in it, no doubt about that," said Indians manager Frank Boon.

The Ottawa Indians will play against the Chicagou White Sox in the next round. Chicagou won their series against the Toronto Blue Sox 3 games to 2. The League Chamionship Series schedule will be announced after the Division Series finishes.
