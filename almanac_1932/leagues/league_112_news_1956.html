Acadiens Advance to Laurier Cup
Monday, August 3rd, 1931
Anthony Michaud said he didn't even realize how well he had been playing in the Division Finals until he was shown his own statistics.

The Pointe-Sainte-Anne Acadiens left fielder put up a .409 average with 1 home runs, 5 RBI and 3 runs scored, enough to earn him series MVP honors. It all added up to his Acadiens advancing to the Laurier Cup, with a 4-2 series win over the Providence Bruins.

"I didn't really pay attention to the numbers or my stats at all as we went along," he said. "I'm just so focused on trying to help the team win, that's it."

The series victory had become official with a 6-2 triumph at Juniors Ballpark. Even if Michaud is not paying close attention to his stats, the rest of baseball certainly will be keeping an eye on him as the playoffs continue.

"I like to be under pressure and think I respond well to it," he said.

The Pointe-Sainte-Anne Acadiens will play against the Coude Voyageurs in the next round. Coude won their series against the Drummondville Voltigeurs 4 games to 1. The Laurier Cup schedule will be announced after the Division Finals finishes.
