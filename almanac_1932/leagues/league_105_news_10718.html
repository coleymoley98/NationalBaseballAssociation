5-Hit Blitz by Philadelphia's Baum
Tuesday, May 21st, 1929
There are moments in a player's career that they will not soon forget.

Jack Baum of the Philadelphia Liberty had one such moment at Baker Bowl 1910 today, when he went 5 for 6 at the plate. His hitting heroics weren't enough to pull out the victory, however, and Philadelphia fell to a 15-12 defeat to the New York Rebels.

"This was quite a day for me individually, but a little disheartening at the same time," the 25-year-old center fielder told reporters in the postgame interview. "It would have been very special if we had won the game."

Jack Baum singled in the 1st, singled in the 3rd, flied out in the 5th, tripled in the 7th, singled in the 9th, hit an RBI single in the 9th and walked in the 11th.

In 14 games this year Baum has posted a .321 average and put up these numbers: 1 home run, 10 RBIs and 11 runs scored.
