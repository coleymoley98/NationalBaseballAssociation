Indians End Wilder's Streak
Saturday, July 5th, 1930
It was a doubly miserable day today for Harry Wilder of the New York Rebels, as he saw his 23-game hitting streak end and his team lose 2-1 to the Ottawa Indians at Lincoln Park. The 24-year-old center fielder was held hitless in 3 at-bats by the Indians.

"Anyone on a 23-game hitting streak is going to be a tough out," Ottawa manager Frank Boon said later. "But we had a plan and we were careful about it, and got it done."

In 51 games this season Wilder has compiled a .306 batting average with 68 hits and 5 home runs. He has also scored 52 times and knocked in 27 runs.
