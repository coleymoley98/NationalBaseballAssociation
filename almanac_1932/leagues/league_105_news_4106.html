Kitsada Richardson Tames Detroit 6-3 for #100
Sunday, July 15th, 1923
What would you do after you had just won your 100th game? Run around Bennett Park doing cartwheels, jumping for joy and waving to the crowd? Well, maybe you and I would do that, but not Kitsada Richardson -- that's not his way.

After he notched number 100, the 28-year-old Minnesota hurler spoke to the press like he always has -- in a soft, quiet voice. He wasn't doing any cartwheels or even anything resembling a jump for joy. "Obviously it feels very good," Richardson commented. "To win this many games, you've got to have a lot of help."

Richardson got his milestone victory by beating the Detroit Lions 6-3. He held them to 6 hits in 8 innings and rung up 3 strikeouts with 5 walks.

After sharing his thoughts with the press, he went to the clubhouse. There he was greeted by the guys who had helped him get #100. His teammates drenched him and then gave him a rousing round of applause. That got to him -- for the first time today, Richardson's face broke into a big smile.

In his career Richardson has 100 victories and 95 defeats and recorded an ERA of 2.75.
