Behind Yuschak, Quebec Blanks St. Louis, 2-0
Thursday, August 2nd, 1923
The Quebec Nordiques shut out the St. Louis Cardinaux 2-0 today behind the strong pitching of Cooper Yuschak. Yuschak surrendered only 4 hits in recording the complete-game victory, and struck out 1.

Following the last out, Yuschak appeared to celebrate a little too boisterously for St. Louis manager Surabordee Yodyingyong, who could be seen scolding the famously outspoken left-hander as the clubs left the field. Yuschak's own manager then intervened with some words for his counterpart.

The Cardinaux skipper would not comment on the exchange, but at the Nordiques press conference, Yuschak confirmed that it was regarding the celebration.

"He basically told me I was being a lousy winner," Yuschak said.

"And I told him he was being a lousy loser," Quebec manager Eric Scism added.

In 16 starts this season, Yuschak has picked up 7 wins while suffering 7 losses. He has pitched 125 innings, allowed 121 hits, struck out 55 and posted a 3.38 ERA.
