Best Pitcher Honor Goes to Weets of Everett
Saturday, August 15th, 1931
This year's winner of the coveted Western Baseball League WBL Pitcher of the Year is Richard Weets of the Everett Silvertips.

During the 1931 campaign, Weets excelled with a 7-1 won-lost mark in 13 starts. In 72 innings he yielded 37 hits and 10 walks, while striking out 103 and compiling an impressive 1.50 ERA.

He received 8 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Suksayam Hardin of the Spokane Eagles finished second in voting, while Caleb Townsell of the Everett Silvertips finished third.
