5-Hit Blitz by O'Leary
Monday, March 2nd, 1931
Team second baseman Bones O'Leary went down swinging at New York Ballpark today, knocking out 5 hits even as his Dakota fell, 11-6.

"It was like I was hitting Little League pitching," O'Leary said after his 5-for-5 performance against the Team Pennsylvania. "No offense meant to Team, but it was like I was back in little league again -- I could see the ball that well."

After the game, his manager elaborated on the second baseman's improvements, saying O'Leary spent the offseason trying to shorten his swing and stay inside the ball.

"That's what I'm trying to do," O'Leary agreed. "Be quick to the ball and let it travel a little farther so I get a better look at it."

Bones O'Leary singled in the 1st, doubled in the 3rd, hit an RBI single in the 5th, doubled in the 7th and singled in the 9th.
