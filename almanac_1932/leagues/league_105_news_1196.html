Detroit Lions Ship Rowlinson to Toronto
Saturday, April 25th, 1931
You can't always get what you want, but sometimes you find a deal anyway. The Baseball News Network is reporting that Detroit and Toronto have made a swap with 25-year-old CF Shea Rowlinson, 22-year-old minor league RHP Roméo Boudreau and 19-year-old minor league SS Jackson Danielson being shipped to the Blue Sox and in return the Lions would get 28-year-old LHP D.J. Pham.

Detroit general manager Jeevan Bahrama said the two sides were close to a blockbuster trade before agreeing upon a smaller scale transaction.

"This is just a bit of housekeeping compared to what we wanted to do," he said. "I won't name names, but the potential deal we were close to making would have certainly grabbed some headlines."

A source inside the Detroit organization said the two sides might not be done dealing, and another transaction could be in the works.


Looking at his all-time stats, Rowlinson has a lifetime .251 average with 153 hits, 16 doubles, 6 triples, 8 home runs and 53 RBIs. To date he has scored 81 runs, walked 51 times and stolen 11 bases.

In his career stats, Pham has won 27 and lost 11, collecting 143 strikeouts and 105 walks in 351 innings with a 3.49 ERA. Opponents have hit .256 against him.
