Pittsburgh Snaps Lafon's Streak at 24
Monday, August 18th, 1930
Wolfeburg Celtics first baseman Élie Lafon finally met his Waterloo.

A 24-game hitting spree is now history after Lafon was unable to make anything happen in 4 at-bats today against the Pittsburgh Quakers. Fortunately for Lafon and the Celtics, the rest of his team had enough in their bats to win the game 7-4.

"We're a true team here," he said. "For 24 games I've been able to contribute, but I always believed these guys would take care of things if I couldn't some day."

This year Lafon is batting .379 with 6 home runs. He has scored 70 runs and driven in 74.
