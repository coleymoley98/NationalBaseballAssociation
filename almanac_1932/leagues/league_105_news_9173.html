MacNally Plays Way to Rookie of the Year Honor
Friday, October 14th, 1927
Aldo MacNally started his Canadian League career in style, capping it off with the Rookie of the Year Award for 1927.

The first baseman for the Philadelphia Liberty played in 66 games, hitting .412 with 94 hits. He totaled 5 home runs, 51 RBIs and 43 runs scored.

He received 10 first place votes out of a possible 16. Weerasak Morillon of the Chicagou White Sox finished second in voting with 6 first place votes, while Elvis Sylvia of the Wolfeburg Celtics finished third.
