Rowsell Tags Seahawks for 5 Hits
Sunday, July 12th, 1931
After a 5-hit effort to lead Boise's 13-4 victory over the Ranier Seahawks, catcher Todd Rowsell told BNN beat writers it had been "a special day."

"It really doesn't matter whether it's sandlot ball, high school or college, two hits in a game is nice," he said. "Three or four is better, but five or more is out of sight. And to do that at the Western Baseball League-level is really something special."

The 18-year-old player earned his accolades today at Ballpark of Ranier with a spectacular 5-for-5 game against the Seahawks.

"His swing is getting there," said Broncos skipper Rudy Seguro, who spoke glowingly of Rowsell after the game. "We want him to be quick with his swing, short and compact. We want him to be patient and go the other way when he can. Todd did that to perfection today."

Todd Rowsell singled in the 2nd, hit a two-run double in the 3rd, singled in the 5th, singled in the 7th and singled in the 8th.

So far this year Rowsell has hit .348 with no home runs, 8 RBIs and 9 runs scored.
