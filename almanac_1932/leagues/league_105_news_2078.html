Blue Sox Whitewash Steelers, Nuregesan Winner
Sunday, August 23rd, 1931
The Hamilton Steelers manager was philosophical in the clubhouse following a shutout loss to the Toronto Blue Sox. The Steelers were overpowered by Toronto starter Vihaan Nuregesan, who hurled his club to victory, 6-0.

"It was just one of those games -- shake it off and do better next time," Kris Bruinsma said.

Over 9 innings, Nuregesan blanked Hamilton on 4 hits with 4 walks and 1 strikeout.

"I kept them off balance. I could tell a lot of them were late on fastballs because they were fouling them off. Once you see that, it sets them up for other pitches," Nuregesan said.

"I set them up, where if they chased one breaking ball, they were going to chase another one."

Up to now Nuregesan has recorded a record of 9-10. In 17 starts he has fanned 35 batters in 144.1 innings with a 4.18 ERA.
