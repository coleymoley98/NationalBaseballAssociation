Acadiens Advance to Laurier Cup
Tuesday, August 4th, 1925
L.J. Legros wore out opposing pitchers en route to being named Division Finals MVP.

The star's Pointe-Sainte-Anne Acadiens defeated the Providence Bruins by a series score of 4-2 to advance to the Laurier Cup.

Pointe-Sainte-Anne won the final game by a 7-2 count in front of a raucous crowd at Juniors Ballpark.

Legros hit .409 with a .458 on-base percentage in the series, while also adding 0 home runs, driving in 1 RBI and scoring 2 runs.

"Just trying to do my job, that's all there is too it," said Legros.

The Pointe-Sainte-Anne Acadiens don't know who they will face in the next round. They will play the winner of the Montreal Jr. Royales - Quebec City Jr. Nordiques series. That series is tied at 3 games apiece. The Laurier Cup schedule will be announced after the Division Finals finishes.
