Liberty Chill Monquet's Hot Streak
Tuesday, July 5th, 1932
The Portland Liberty got more than the 7-5 victory at Ballpark of Hamilton today -- they also put an end to the 21-game hitting streak of Brampton Blue Sox second baseman J.F. Monquet by holding him without a hit in 2 at-bats.

After the game, Monquet seemed more concerned with his team taking the loss than with the demise of his streak.

"I had some great looks at some pitches that I should have hit," he said. "I missed some that I shouldn't have swung at. All in all, I wasn't much help with the bat today."

This season Monquet is swinging the bat at a .400 pace. He has hit 6 home runs and scored 21 times, while driving in 21. His on-base percentage is .440 and he has played in 22 games.
