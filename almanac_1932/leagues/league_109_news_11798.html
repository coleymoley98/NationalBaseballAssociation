EL Announces #1 Player Award
Friday, September 19th, 1930
He may have less experience than most of his Eastern League peers, but that didn't slow Niki Dubois down at all in 1930.

At the age of 19, the Tecumseh third baseman has been named the winner of the Eastern League Most Valuable Player Award.

He had a .377 batting average and a .415 on-base percentage this year. In 59 games he piled up 87 hits, 12 doubles, 2 triples, 13 home runs, 46 RBIs and 34 runs scored.

"I'm blown away by the season I was able to put together," he said. "I'm looking forward to trying to do it all again next year."

He received 2 first place votes out of a possible 10. Reilly Becker of the Providence Redcoats finished second in voting with 2 first place votes, while Amélio Lozano of the Kingston Indians finished third with 2 first place votes.
