Monday, August 18th, 1930
The manager of the Morgantown Mountaineers said he thinks Mark Madgewick is only going to get better as he gets older.

He may only be 16, but it will be hard to top the left fielder's 1930 campaign, as he has been named the winner of the Eastern Baseball League EBL Player of the Year.

His winning campaign featured a .402 batting average and a .452 on-base percentage this year. In 50 games he piled up 80 hits, 19 doubles, 3 triples, 4 home runs, 37 RBIs and 46 runs scored.

He received 10 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Thanathep Seecheun of the Hamilton Jr. Steelers finished second in voting, while Ethan Vignal of the Buffalo Rapids finished third.
