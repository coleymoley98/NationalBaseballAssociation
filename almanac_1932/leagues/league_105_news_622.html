Frontenacs Get No Resistance from Braves in 9-0 Beat-Down
Friday, July 4th, 1930
Charles-Olivier King subdued the Toronto Braves, 9-0, pitching a 6-hit gem for the Chicagou Frontenacs at Wrigley Field 1928.

"He was kind of teasing them with his off-speed stuff, and his fastball was moving everywhere," Chicagou skipper Archie Cooper said afterward.

King registered 12 strikeouts and issued 1 walk in his superb performance.

"We just didn't swing the bats very well. Or else he just pitched good, whichever way you want to look at it," said Toronto manager Peerapong Owens after the defeat.

So far this season King has 7 wins and 4 losses and a 3.75 ERA.
