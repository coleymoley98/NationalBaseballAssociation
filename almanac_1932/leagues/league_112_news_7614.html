Mariners Win Tight Atlantic Race
Sunday, July 25th, 1926
They may have only won the Atlantic by an incredibly slim margin, but St. John's Mariners starting pitcher Giorgi Lallier said the outcome was "never in doubt" for his team.

"We felt like this is our destiny and we weren't going to be stopped," he said. "Whether you win the division by a couple games or 50 games, all that matters is that you win it. We knew we could do it."

That confidence will serve the club well as St. John's vies for its 1st Laurier Cup win, after posting the 2nd Atlantic championship in team history.
