McGowan's Great Year Nets Pitcher of the Year Award
Thursday, September 23rd, 1926
What does it take to win the Pitcher of the Year Award?

Just ask Jacob McGowan of the Pittsburgh Liberty -- the latest Eastern League Pitcher of the Year Award winner.

"My dad always told me to dream big and dream always," he said. "It's not like I shoot for the Pitcher of the Year Award," he said. "I have big aspirations for what I want to do in this game and what I want to achieve in a long career."

The Liberty pitching ace made some of his dreams come true this year, winning 7 games and losing only 4. In 14 starts, McGowan registered a 1.99 ERA and limited other clubs to a .196 batting average. He fanned 115 in 104 innings and issued 31 walks.

He received 8 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Andrew Patterson of the Providence Redcoats finished second in voting, while Steven Masson of the Trois-Rivieres Royales finished third.
