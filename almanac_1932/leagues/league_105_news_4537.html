Rebels on to Riel Cup
Thursday, September 20th, 1923
The New York Rebels are one step closer to winning a Riel Cup, and the season came to a close for the Chicagou White Sox.

New York won the League Championship Series 4-2, with a 6-5 victory in the deciding game at Comiskey Park 1910 today.

New York left fielder Charles Trottier was named the MVP of the series, after hitting .560 with a .607 on-base percentage. He had 2 homers, drove in 12 RBI and scored 7 runs.

"I just hope I can keep it up in the Riel Cup," he said.

The New York Rebels will play against the Quebec Nordiques in the next round. Quebec won their series against the St. Louis Cardinaux 4 games to 0. The Riel Cup schedule will be announced after the League Championship Series finishes.
