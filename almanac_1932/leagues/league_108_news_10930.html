Denzil Stalling Wins WL Trophy
Monday, June 24th, 1929
So far in his young career, Denzil Stalling has kept a low profile, but after his performance at the plate last week, he attracted the notice of the rest of the Western League in a big way as he was named the Player of the Week today for his efforts.

The 21-year-old Portland Thorns first baseman hit at a torrid .552 clip as he collected 16 hits in 29 at-bats, no home runs, 3 RBIs and 6 runs scored.

In his 1929 season totals, Stalling has no home runs, .380 BA, .424 OBP, 10 RBIs and 20 runs scored.
