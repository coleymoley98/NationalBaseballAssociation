Landis at His Best, Blanks Baltimore
Tuesday, September 8th, 1931
The Toronto Blue Sox shut out the Baltimore Robins 1-0 today behind the strong pitching of Sakunchai Landis. Landis surrendered only 7 hits in recording the complete-game victory, and struck out 3.

Following the last out, Landis appeared to celebrate a little too boisterously for Baltimore manager Eldin Gabriel, who could be seen scolding the famously outspoken right-hander as the clubs left the field. Landis's own manager then intervened with some words for his counterpart.

The Robins skipper would not comment on the exchange, but at the Blue Sox press conference, Landis confirmed that it was regarding the celebration.

"He basically told me I was being a lousy winner," Landis said.

"And I told him he was being a lousy loser," Toronto manager Jabari Perrault added.

Thus far this season Landis has posted a 9-9 record, started 22 games, struck out 70 batters in 172 innings and compiled a 4.19 ERA.
