Red-Hot Bat: Tienphosuwan Collects Cycle
Thursday, July 24th, 1924
It was a special day for Supphakorn Tienphosuwan at Liberty Ballpark -- the Pittsburgh Liberty second baseman hit for the cycle, getting every type of hit possible.

The 21-year-old Tienphosuwan racked up 1 single, 1 double, 1 triple and 1 home run to get his name in the history books. It was all for naught, however, as the Liberty didn't win the ballgame, dropping the decision to the Sherbrooke Nordiques, 10-8.

"He hit everything we threw," said Sherbrooke manager Malik Hill. "There was just no getting him out today."

For the game, he was 4-for-5 with 2 RBI and 2 runs scored.

"I'm glad for Supphakorn," Pittsburgh manager Virgil McLain said. "It's a once-in-a-lifetime thing and a nice feather in his cap. Would've been better if we could have won the game for him, too."

Speaking to the press after the game, Tienphosuwan said the key for his record-setting day was "just getting some good pitches to hit."

Supphakorn Tienphosuwan singled in the 1st, doubled in the 3rd, tripled in the 6th, grounded out in the 7th and hit a two-run home run off Basil Betts in the 9th.

Currently Tienphosuwan has 2 home runs and a .286 batting average.
