5-for-6 Day for Lepage
Tuesday, June 7th, 1932
There aren't many who could be pro ballplayers and there are even fewer who could put up 5 hits in a day.

A.J. Lepage is one of those rare few, as he went 5-for-6 against the Pittsburgh Quakers at Ballpark of Pittsburgh today. The monster effort from the 26-year-old first baseman helped the Ottawa Indians take the win, 16-4.

"We made some bad pitches and he took advantage of it," said Quakers manager Pierre Boutin. "That's the game, though. Don't make mistakes and you'll win ballgames. Make mistakes and, well, you saw what happened today."

A.J. Lepage doubled in the 2nd, singled in the 3rd, hit an RBI double in the 4th, singled in the 6th, flied out in the 8th and hit an RBI single in the 9th.

In this current campaign Lepage is hitting at a .393 clip with 1 home run and 7 RBIs.
