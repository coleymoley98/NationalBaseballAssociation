Rankin Rips 3 Homers Against Falcons
Thursday, July 31st, 1930
Winnipeg Thunder right fielder Timothy Rankin put on a dazzling display of power today at Thunder Stadium against the Omaha Falcons. In the 14-12 Thunder victory Rankin penned his name into the record books, slamming a trio of home runs in one of baseball's finest single-day efforts.

"He can hit them out anywhere, right, center, left, it doesn't matter," Omaha manager Kiatisuk Sanders said. "He's super strong and stays on the ball so well."

Along with his three home runs, the 20-year-old had 4 hits in 7 at-bats, 7 RBIs and 4 runs scored.

"Life will always throw you curves, just keep fouling them off," Rankin said after the game, when asked about his hitting philosophy. "The right pitch will come, and when it does, be prepared to run the bases. Or trot around them, like I did today."

Timothy Rankin hit an RBI single in the 1st, flied out in the 2nd, flied out in the 5th, struck out in the 7th, hit a three-run home run off German González in the 8th, hit a solo-shot off Edwin Beeler in the 10th and hit a two-run home run off Edwin Beeler in the 11th.

So far this season Rankin is hitting .217 with 4 home runs, 19 RBIs and 19 runs scored. He has 20 hits in 92 at-bats and a .318 OBP.
