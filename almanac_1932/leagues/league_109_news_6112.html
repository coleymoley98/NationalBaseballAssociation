Boutin Goes 5-for-5
Thursday, June 4th, 1925
After a 5-hit effort to lead Albany's 4-2 victory over the Pittsburgh Liberty, right fielder Bently Boutin told BNN beat writers it had been "a special day."

"It really doesn't matter whether it's sandlot ball, high school or college, two hits in a game is nice," he said. "Three or four is better, but five or more is out of sight. And to do that at the Eastern League-level is really something special."

The 21-year-old player earned his accolades today at Liberty Ballpark with a spectacular 5-for-5 game against the Liberty.

"His swing is getting there," said Rebels skipper Phaitoon Cohen, who spoke glowingly of Boutin after the game. "We want him to be quick with his swing, short and compact. We want him to be patient and go the other way when he can. Bently did that to perfection today."

Bently Boutin singled in the 1st, doubled in the 4th, hit an RBI single in the 6th, doubled in the 7th and doubled in the 9th.
