NL Reliever of the Year Award Winner Named
Sunday, October 7th, 1923
The National League Reliever of the Year Award for 1923 has been bestowed on St. Louis Cardinaux relief specialist Hervé Brunet.

The 29-year-old bullpen ace, out of Toronto, The United Provinces, nailed down the award with 12 saves in 50 relief appearances, 37 strikeouts in 74.1 innings and a record of 9 wins and no losses to go with his 2.91 ERA.

He received 9 first place votes out of a possible 16. Alan Tonnessen of the Chicagou Frontenacs finished second in voting with 7 first place votes, while Albie Edmunds of the Juneau Brasseurs finished third.
