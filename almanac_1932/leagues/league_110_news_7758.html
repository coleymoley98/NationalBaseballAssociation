Great Glove Award Winners in CBL Announced
Sunday, August 15th, 1926
Good glovemen take pride in their defensive skills. They know they make a difference in baseball. Even on days when they don't get a hit, they feel they can make a difference in the field -- and they do. The Central Baseball League announced its Great Glove Award honorees for 1926. This year's recipients are:

Pitcher
Guillaume Beaulieu (Saskatoon Jr. Coyotes)

Catcher
Edwin Dollar (Lincoln Pioneers)

First Baseman
Gunner Nalley (Davenport Rivermen)

Second Baseman
Tristan Houle (Saskatoon Jr. Coyotes)

Third Baseman
Alex Martel (Omaha Jr. Falcons)

Shortstop
Julian Tombrello (St. Louis Jr. Cardinaux)

Left Fielder
Spike Vogel (Davenport Rivermen)

Center Fielder
Walter Faulk (Omaha Jr. Falcons)

Right Fielder
Charlie Salmon (Minneapolis Jr. Saints)
