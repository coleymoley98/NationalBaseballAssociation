WBL Awards WBL Player of the Year, Hutchinson Gets Prize
Saturday, August 18th, 1928
He may have less experience than most of his Western Baseball League peers, but that didn't slow Randolph Hutchinson down at all in 1928.

At the age of 17, the Ranier third baseman has been named the winner of the Western Baseball League WBL Player of the Year.

He had a .325 batting average and a .423 on-base percentage this year. In 50 games he piled up 64 hits, 11 doubles, 2 triples, 9 home runs, 37 RBIs and 50 runs scored.

"I'm blown away by the season I was able to put together," he said. "I'm looking forward to trying to do it all again next year."

He received 8 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Komkrit Laliberté of the Ranier Seahawks finished second in voting, while Jake Gango of the Ranier Seahawks finished third.
