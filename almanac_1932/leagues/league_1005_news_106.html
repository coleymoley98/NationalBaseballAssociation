5-for-5 Day for Swisher
Sunday, March 16th, 1930
It's not something you read about in the sports pages every day -- a 5-for-5 day. For many, it might come around only once in a career -- and for Harald Swisher, it came around today.

The 18-year-old second baseman scattered hits all over Tokyo Memorial Coliseum to pace the Team New York to a 14-5 victory over the Team Illinois.

Team skipper Luke Kallstrom told reporters in the press room that "Swisher deserves a hat-tip."

"He hit some good pitches against us," he said. "In fact, according to our scouting reports, Swisher hit several that he is not supposed to be able to hit."

Harald Swisher hit an RBI single in the 1st, hit an RBI single in the 2nd, singled in the 4th, singled in the 6th and hit an RBI triple in the 8th.
