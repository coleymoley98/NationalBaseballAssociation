NL Announces Pitcher of the Year Award
Saturday, October 15th, 1927
Eric Woodmore had a terrific 1927 season in the National League. The Chicagou pitching stalwart received the most votes and has been proclaimed this year's Pitcher of the Year Award winner.

The Frontenacs ace starred with an impressive 17-7 record. In 24 starts, he gave up 194 base hits and struck out 79 in 211.2 innings with a 2.85 ERA. Opposing teams hit .245 against him.

He received 16 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Kitsada Richardson of the Minnesota Saints finished second in voting, while Ray O'Gorman of the Montreal Royales finished third.
