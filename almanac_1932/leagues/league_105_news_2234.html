Detroit Takes Division Series in Deciding Game
Tuesday, September 22nd, 1931
The joy and relief on the faces of the Detroit Lions players was obvious as they celebrated their Division Series win over the Chicagou Frontenacs.

"In a series like this, the smallest thing can make the difference in how it turns out," said Detroit third baseman Arnaud Boudreau, shortly after the 6-0 win that clinched the series 3-2. "We're happy we did what it takes to get it done."

The team is now off to the League Chamionship Series.

Boudreau won series MVP honors on the strength of his .625 batting average during the series. He also had a .640 on-base percentage and 0 home runs, drove in 4 RBI and scored 5 runs.

The Detroit Lions will play against the Montreal Royales in the next round. Montreal won their series against the Juneau Brasseurs 3 games to 1. The League Chamionship Series schedule will be announced after the Division Series finishes.
