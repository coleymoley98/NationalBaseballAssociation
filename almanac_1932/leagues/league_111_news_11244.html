Jr. Blue Sox Heading to Adams Cup
Wednesday, July 31st, 1929
Joseph Walton put the Toronto Jr. Blue Sox on his shoulders as they downed the Juneau Jr. Brasseurs in the Division Finals.

Toronto won the deciding game by a 4-0 count at Juniors Ballpark and wrapped up the series 3-1.

Walton played a key role in the victory, as he was named series MVP for his offensive output, which included a .375 batting average with a .412 on-base percentage, 1 home runs, 3 RBI and 2 runs scored.

"I told myself it was time to step it up and help the team win and I'm glad I was able to do that," he said.

The Toronto Jr. Blue Sox will play against the Hamilton Jr. Steelers in the next round. Hamilton won their series against the Fort Wayne Pioneers 3 games to 0. The Adams Cup schedule will be announced after the Division Finals finishes.
