Apisit Hopson Chosen CBL's Top Player
Sunday, August 19th, 1923
He may have less experience than most of his Central Baseball League peers, but that didn't slow Apisit Hopson down at all in 1923.

At the age of 16, the Adams center fielder has been named the winner of the Central Baseball League Most Valuable Player Award.

He had a .328 batting average and a .391 on-base percentage this year. In 50 games he piled up 63 hits, 16 doubles, no triples, 11 home runs, 44 RBIs and 35 runs scored.

"I'm blown away by the season I was able to put together," he said. "I'm looking forward to trying to do it all again next year."

He received 2 first place votes out of a possible 8. Cyril Cork of the Adams Mounties finished second in voting with 1 first place vote, while Weerasak Morillon of the Winnipeg Jr. Thunder finished third with 2 first place votes.
