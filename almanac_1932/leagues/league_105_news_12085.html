Tison NL's Top Player for 1932
Saturday, October 22nd, 1932
The manager of the Quebec Nordiques said he thinks Doug Tison is only going to get better as he gets older.

He may only be 22, but it will be hard to top the center fielder's 1932 campaign, as he has been named the winner of the National League Most Valuable Player Award.

His winning campaign featured a .329 batting average and a .386 on-base percentage this year. In 108 games he piled up 140 hits, 24 doubles, 6 triples, 22 home runs, 75 RBIs and 75 runs scored.

He received 14 first place votes out of a possible 20. Larry Millette of the Montreal Royales finished second in voting with 6 first place votes, while Eric Woodmore of the Chicagou Frontenacs finished third.
