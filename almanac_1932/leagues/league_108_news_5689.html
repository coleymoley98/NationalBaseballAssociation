WL Hands Out Reliever of the Year Award
Thursday, September 18th, 1924
Seattle Rainiers reliever Sathorn Thompson said he loves to come into the game when the outcome is in jeopardy.

He certainly did a good job of impacting his team's outcomes this season, as he has been named the winner of the 1924 Reliever of the Year Award for the Western League.

"There's no better feeling than running in from the bullpen with the game on the line and your heart pumping," Thompson said.

On the season, the Alton, The United Provinces native had 2 saves in 23 relief appearances, 28 strikeouts in 48 innings and a record of 1 win and 2 losses to go with his 4.50 ERA.

He received 5 first place votes out of a possible 8. Oliver Settle of the Seattle Rainiers finished second in voting with 3 first place votes, while Kan Lheureux of the Edmonton Eskimos finished third.
