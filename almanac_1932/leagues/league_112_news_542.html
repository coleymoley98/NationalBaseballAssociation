Bearden Shows Off, Hits for Cycle
Saturday, June 28th, 1930
It was a quite a day for Clement Bearden at Field of the Juniors.

He hit for the cycle, belting out every kind of hit there is -- a single, double, triple and home run. The Champlain second baseman accomplished the rare feat during his team's 11-2 victory over the Sherbrooke Canadiens.

"I never even thought about hitting for the cycle and what do you know, I did it," he told BNN reporters at the postgame press conference. "The cycle really doesn't matter to me, it's the win that's important."

Bearden went 4-for-4 in the game with 5 RBI and 2 runs scored.

Clement Bearden singled in the 1st, hit a two-run double in the 2nd, hit an RBI triple in the 4th and hit a two-run home run off Christian Leclerc in the 6th.

For the season Bearden has produced 29 hits in 100 at-bats for a .290 average with 2 home runs and gotten on base at a .324 clip.
