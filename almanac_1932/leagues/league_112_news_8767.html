Cunningham Hit String Snapped at 20
Wednesday, July 20th, 1927
Sol Cunningham saw his 20-game hitting streak wither and die today during the Drummondville Voltigeurs win over Providence at Ballpark of Drummondville. The 16-year-old second baseman was held hitless in 4 at-bats by the Providence Bruins, but his teammates produced enough offense to take the game, 6-2.

"Am I disappointed about the hit streak? Maybe, just a little bit," a seemingly upbeat Cunningham told reporters in the clubhouse. "But really, how could you be unhappy after you hit in 20 straight games? I'm just glad it lasted as long as it did. Life is good."

This season Cunningham is batting .290 with 9 home runs. His totals include 28 RBIs and 31 runs scored.
