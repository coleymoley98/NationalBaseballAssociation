Wranglers Take Title
Thursday, September 10th, 1925
It was a hard-fought battle for both teams to make it this far. But as the saying goes, "to the victors go the spoils." For the victorious Calgary Wranglers, that meant the Western League World Series trophy.

The Wranglers took a 10-3 victory over the Omaha Falcons at Calgary Grounds, winning their 2nd championship after taking the series 4-3.

"We've got grit," said Wranglers right fielder Draven McEachin, who was named MVP of the series. "We know how to win when we have to."

The new titleholders completed the regular season with a first-place finish in the and a 50-32 record.
