Bruins Heading to Laurier Cup
Saturday, August 4th, 1923
The Providence Bruins are one step closer to winning a Laurier Cup, and the season came to a close for the Drummondville Voltigeurs.

Providence won the Division Finals 4-2, with a 7-4 victory in the deciding game at Ballpark of Drummondville today.

Providence left fielder Noël Angers was named the MVP of the series, after hitting .750 with a .750 on-base percentage. He had 1 homers, drove in 4 RBI and scored 2 runs.

"I just hope I can keep it up in the Laurier Cup," he said.

The Providence Bruins don't know who they will face in the next round. They will play the winner of the Quincy Gunners - St. John's Mariners series. That series is tied at 3 games apiece. The Laurier Cup schedule will be announced after the Division Finals finishes.
