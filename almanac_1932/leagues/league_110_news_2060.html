Rattanapon Dostie Named Premier CBL Player
Tuesday, August 18th, 1931
At the age of 17, Rattanapon Dostie of the Minneapolis Jr. Saints could very well have his best years ahead of him.

It's a startling thought when you consider the year he had in 1931 was good enough to win the center fielder the Central Baseball League Most Valuable Player Award.

"See the ball, hit the ball," he said. "That's my basic approach."

The top hitter put up a .403 batting average this year, as well as a .456 on-base percentage. He played 49 games and racked up 79 hits, 21 doubles, 1 triple, 9 home runs, 40 RBIs and 38 runs scored.

He received 4 first place votes out of a possible 8. Elvis Guégan of the Minneapolis Jr. Saints finished second in voting with 4 first place votes, while Mo Foreman of the Winnipeg Jr. Thunder finished third.
