Injury Forces Kamai to Call It A Career
Thursday, September 15th, 1932
The Philadelphia Liberty have announced the retirement of starting pitcher Tadanori Kamai. The news, while disappointing, does not come as a complete surprise. Kamai has been out of action with a partially torn ulnar collateral ligament. He recently said that it had put his baseball future "seriously in doubt." All doubt is now removed: he has chosen to retire from the sport. The 35-year-old Kamai will have more to say about his decision when he meets with the media later this afternoon.

Kamai has gone 5-11 this season with an ERA of 4.83 and 41 strikeouts in 128.2 innings pitched. Other clubs are hitting .310 against him.
