Haas Guns Down Reds
Friday, August 23rd, 1929
The Juneau Brasseurs shut out the Cincinnati Reds 2-0 today behind the strong pitching of Kevin Haas. Haas surrendered only 3 hits in recording the complete-game victory, and struck out 2.

Following the last out, Haas appeared to celebrate a little too boisterously for Cincinnati manager Hoot Sinkus, who could be seen scolding the famously outspoken left-hander as the clubs left the field. Haas's own manager then intervened with some words for his counterpart.

The Reds skipper would not comment on the exchange, but at the Brasseurs press conference, Haas confirmed that it was regarding the celebration.

"He basically told me I was being a lousy winner," Haas said.

"And I told him he was being a lousy loser," Juneau manager Joshua Barry added.

For the year Haas has a 7-5 record with a 3.36 ERA in 15 starts.
