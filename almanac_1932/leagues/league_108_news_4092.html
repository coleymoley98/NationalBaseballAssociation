Portland Blanked by Whisler
Thursday, July 12th, 1923
Corbin Whisler was in a mood to talk following the Vancouver Giants contest against the Portland Thorns, expounding to BNN reporters on his pitching philosophy. He had good reason to be excitable, having tossed a complete game shutout of the Thorns, 2-0.

"There are three important elements in pitching success: location, location, and location," Whisler said. "I was hitting my spots time and time again."

The right-hander blanked Portland on a 7-hitter, allowing 5 walks and racking up 6 strikeouts.

So far this year Whisler has a 6-3 record with a 3.01 ERA.
