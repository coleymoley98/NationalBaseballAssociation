Paulson Dominates in 15-2 Win over Canadian League All-Stars
Tuesday, July 16th, 1929
All-Star Detroit Lions' second baseman Paul Paulson had a night to remember.

Not only did he take the field with the game's best players, he was also a huge factor in the 15-2 National League victory over the Canadian League All-Stars.

"We had a meeting before the game, and I was struck by the talent in the room. Old stars, young stars, it's a game I'll never forget," Paulson said. "I talked to a few of my friends from back home, and they told me to go out and win the game for them. I'm sure they'll be thrilled."

Paulson is hitting .341 lifetime with 156 hits, 29 doubles, 8 triples, 4 home runs and 74 RBIs to date. He has scored 59 runs, walked 45 times and has a .398 OBP.
