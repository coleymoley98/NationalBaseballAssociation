Admirals Win Tight Race
Tuesday, July 28th, 1925
It's a good day to be a fan of the Victoria Admirals, as the club has won the - by the slimmest of margins.

"It makes it that much sweeter to win the thing by only a game," said starting pitcher Albus Brushett. "It easily could have gone the other way, but we're all happy that wasn't the case."

Admirals players stayed in the clubhouse late into the night celebrating the victory, which was earned on the strength of the team's 34-17 record. It is the 1st title in Admirals history.

Victoria will now be vying for its 1st Western Baseball League championship.
