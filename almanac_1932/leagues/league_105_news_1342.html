May's NL Top Pitcher is Reds Tremblay
Monday, June 1st, 1931
Capping off a whirlwind month in which he went 4-0 with a 1.76 ERA, André Tremblay was selected as May's National League Pitcher of the Month today. Reporters caught up with the elated Cincinnati starter as he left the clubhouse.

"I know it's difficult for pitchers -- when you have a bad start or two, people start wondering if you've lost it. Then you ride stretches like this one for all you can. You find it easier to come to the park every day because know you're contributing to the club, not just waiting for your turn in the rotation to make up for your last start."

André Tremblay definitely contributed to the club. He had 5 starts, throwing 41 innings, striking out 22, and holding the opposition to a .205 average.
