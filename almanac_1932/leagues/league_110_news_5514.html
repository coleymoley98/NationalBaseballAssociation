CBL Best Skipper, Jones of Adams
Sunday, August 17th, 1924
Adams manager Joseph Jones was selected as the 1924 Central Baseball League Manager of the Year today.

He was somewhat of a dark horse next to some of the more flashy managers up for the award. Jones, who prefers that everyone call him "Cap" or "Skip," tries to shy away from the limelight in a media-driven world.

"It's really unfair to the guys that busted their humps all year long to put all the focus on me now. They showed up early, put in extra time in the cage, worked on their fielding, consulted with our coaching staff and really made 1924 a highlight in my career."

In fairness to this year's Manager of the Year, Jones certainly played an important part in the Mounties success as he guided them to a first place finish in the and a 32-18 record.
