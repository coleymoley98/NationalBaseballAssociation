Carpentier Gives Kelowna Goose Eggs
Thursday, July 11th, 1929
The Kelowna Apple Sox manager was philosophical in the clubhouse following a shutout loss to the Abbotsford Alpines. The Apple Sox were overpowered by Abbotsford starter Raphael Carpentier, who hurled his club to victory, 4-0.

"It was just one of those games -- shake it off and do better next time," Jesse Daniels said.

Over 9 innings, Carpentier blanked Kelowna on 2 hits with 1 walk and 12 strikeouts.

"I kept them off balance. I could tell a lot of them were late on fastballs because they were fouling them off. Once you see that, it sets them up for other pitches," Carpentier said.

"I set them up, where if they chased one breaking ball, they were going to chase another one."

So far this season Carpentier is batting .333 with 1 home run, 3 RBIs and 4 runs scored. He has 9 hits in 27 at-bats, 3 walks and a .424 OBP. Carpentier has played in 14 games.
