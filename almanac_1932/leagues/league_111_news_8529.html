Camp Outstanding, No-Hits Black Sox
Friday, June 24th, 1927
Morgantown Mountaineers starter Enzo Camp came up big today at Juniors Ballpark with an exceptional 3-0 outing against the Gallant Black Sox. Not only did he whip them, but Camp held them hitless, too.

"My arm was a bit sore in warmups," confessed Camp. "Sometimes you go out there and you're not feeling all that hot. You get into the game and all of a sudden in the seventh inning you look up and see the scoreboard and think to yourself, 'Oh, my goodness, I have a no-hitter!' You're so focused on what you're doing, it just happens."

In the course of the no-hitter, Camp collected 8 strikeouts and surrendered 7 walks -- a day good enough to get his name in the history books.

This season Camp has registered 2 wins and 3 losses in 5 starts with a 3.53 ERA.
