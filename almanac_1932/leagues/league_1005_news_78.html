Mujahideen 5-for-6 for Quebec
Wednesday, March 5th, 1930
After a 5-hit effort to lead Team's 14-9 victory over the Team Saskatchewan, center fielder Muneer Mujahideen told BNN beat writers it had been "a special day."

"It really doesn't matter whether it's sandlot ball, high school or college, two hits in a game is nice," he said. "Three or four is better, but five or more is out of sight. And to do that at the Canadian Junior Baseball Championship-level is really something special."

The 18-year-old player earned his accolades today at Tokyo Memorial Coliseum with a spectacular 5-for-6 game against the Saskatchewan.

"His swing is getting there," said Quebec skipper Daniel Harris, who spoke glowingly of Mujahideen after the game. "We want him to be quick with his swing, short and compact. We want him to be patient and go the other way when he can. Muneer did that to perfection today."

Muneer Mujahideen hit an RBI double in the 1st, singled in the 3rd, singled in the 4th, hit an RBI single in the 5th, hit an RBI double in the 7th and flied out in the 8th.
