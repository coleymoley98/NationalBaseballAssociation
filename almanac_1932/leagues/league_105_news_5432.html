Farmer Blisters St. Louis
Saturday, August 2nd, 1924
The Minnesota Saints stopped the St. Louis Cardinaux by a score of 7-5 at St. Louis Grounds today, with the lion's share of the credit going to first baseman Carter Farmer. The 21-year-old had a career day, collecting 5 hits.

"Every batter wants to have a quality at-bat every time he steps up to the plate," Farmer said later. "He wants to hit the ball hard."

Farmer did just that in his 5 at-bats, much to the chagrin of the Cardinaux.

"Not much that I can say," offered St. Louis manager Surabordee Yodyingyong. "He just hammered the heck out of us."

Carter Farmer singled in the 1st, hit an RBI triple in the 3rd, hit an RBI single in the 5th, singled in the 7th and singled in the 9th.

Farmer is currently hitting at a .307 pace with 2 home runs and 30 RBIs.
