Mongeau Triumphs in Pitcher of the Year Award Voting
Wednesday, October 10th, 1923
It was as good as money in the bank when Louis Mongeau took the hill for New York. He paid his club back with interest this season with a superb 18-9 mark, collecting the Canadian League Pitcher of the Year Award for 1923.

The Rebels star delighted the hometown fans with a fine 2.87 ERA and 64 strikeouts in 235.1 innings. Mongeau surrendered 223 hits and 56 walks and limited opposing batters to a .253 batting average.

He received 16 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Daran Stallings of the Chicagou White Sox finished second in voting, while T.J. Andrews of the Boston Redcoats finished third.
