Martel Selected 1930 CBL Most Valuable Player Award
Saturday, August 16th, 1930
Virgile Martel took Central Baseball League by storm this year, and he did it all at the age of 18.

For his spectacular campaign, the Minneapolis Jr. Saints star has been named the 1930 Central Baseball League Most Valuable Player Award recipient.

"To be considered amongst the best in the league is overwhelming," said the third baseman.

On the season he compiled a .338 batting average to go with a .403 on-base percentage. The native of Lincoln, The United Provinces also added 11 doubles, 1 triple, 1 home run, 16 RBIs and 26 runs scored.

He received 8 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Chevy Dodson of the St. Louis Jr. Cardinaux finished second in voting, while Elvis Guégan of the Minneapolis Jr. Saints finished third.
