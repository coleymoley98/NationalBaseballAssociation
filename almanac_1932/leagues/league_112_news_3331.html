1932 ABL ABL Pitcher of the Year Chosen
Thursday, August 18th, 1932
A sparkling 6-1 mark for the year just about says it all -- that's what Supete Edwards of the Pointe-Sainte-Anne Acadiens put up this season. That's why the Atlantic Baseball League rated him the #1 hurler in the league and named him the 1932 ABL Pitcher of the Year winner.

He held opposing clubs to a .174 batting average and compiled a 1.26 ERA. Edwards stood out with 80 strikeouts while giving up 40 hits and 6 walks in 64.1 innings.

He received 10 first place votes out of a possible 14. Jay Bouchard of the Pawtucket Red Sox finished second in voting with 2 first place votes, while Andrew Bordieu of the Hartford Whalers finished third with 2 first place votes.
