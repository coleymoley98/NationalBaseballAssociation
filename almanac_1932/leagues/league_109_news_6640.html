League Suspends Müller and Phaneuf for Brawl
Monday, August 17th, 1925
Yesterday at Liberty Ballpark Jackie Müller and Etienne Phaneuf got into each other's space and then into each other's face. They began to grapple and fell to the ground. That's when it boiled over into a full-fledged bench-clearing tussle between the Pittsburgh Liberty and Harrisburg Robins. Reason finally prevailed as the umpires restored order. After much discussion, only the two major combatants were ejected from the game.

Today the league office notified the ballclubs that Müller will have to sit out 4 while Phaneuf would be given a 6-game suspension for their roles in the brawl.



In 13 starts this year Phaneuf has fashioned a 7-1 record and a 2.08 ERA.
