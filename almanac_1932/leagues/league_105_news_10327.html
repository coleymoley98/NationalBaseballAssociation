Cincinnati Reliever Cream of the NL Rookie Crop
Saturday, October 6th, 1928
The National League Rookie of the Year Award has been given to relief specialist Myles Rushing of Cincinnati for his fine work on the mound.

In 53 relief appearances he rang up a 4-4 won-lost mark and a 2.50 ERA. In 72 innings the Reds rookie fanned 41 and walked 23.

He received 10 first place votes out of a possible 16. Runrot Savitree of the Cincinnati Reds finished second in voting with 2 first place votes, while Bunnawat Phomee of the Montreal Royales finished third with 3 first place votes.
