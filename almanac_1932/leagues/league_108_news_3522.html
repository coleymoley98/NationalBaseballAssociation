Arquette Garners WL Top Skipper Award
Wednesday, September 21st, 1932
Victoria manager Michel Arquette caused quite a stir when he came to town. Noted for his feisty, no-nonsense attitude, Arquette threw his all into the 1932 campaign, winning over critics and fans as he went. He also won a few ballgames, running up a 47-36 record, which was good enough to take the third place spot in the standings.

"These guys are grown men. They don't need psychobabble, they need to win ballgames," Arquette snapped at his first press conference. While his approach was controversial, even for Victoria, it netted the 44-year-old the Western League Manager of the Year trophy. In response to which he replied, "It ain't molecular physics, it's baseball," and began discussing potential free agents with the local media.
