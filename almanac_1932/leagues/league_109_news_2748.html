Streak Finally Over, Tillery Held Hitless
Friday, June 10th, 1932
Maybe it really is taboo to talk about a hitting streak before it's over. Hours after speaking to the press about his 20-game hitting streak, Providence left fielder Deon Tillery went hitless in 5 at-bats today at Providence Grounds.

The sudden hitting drought came as the Redcoats took the 12-9 win over the Harrisburg Robins.

"Nope, it's not magic," Tillery said in his postgame press conference. "Nobody jinxed me. Streaks don't last forever. I've heard about it every day for weeks now. My roomie told me to get a hit before every game and wished me luck. I just ran out of hits."

Presently Tillery is hitting .256 with 2 home runs and 5 RBIs. He has played in 10 games and scored 3 times.
