Coker Has Hot Hand, Bangs 5 hits
Sunday, July 29th, 1928
It was a high-five day for Bryan Coker, at least for a while.

The Juneau second baseman got plenty of them from his teammates when he reached the five-hit milestone during today's game against the St. Louis Cardinaux, but they were in short supply at the end, as St. Louis took a 5-4 win. Coker finished with an impressive 5-for-6 line, but the Brasseurs couldn't hold off the Cardinaux.

"Bryan was hitting everything," said Brasseurs manager Jake Downing. "It's too bad the other guys couldn't rise to the occasion."

Bryan Coker doubled in the 1st, hit a solo-shot off Pairote Blakey in the 3rd, doubled in the 4th, singled in the 7th, doubled in the 9th and struck out in the 11th.

So far in 1928 Coker is batting .286 with 3 home runs, 19 RBIs and 42 runs scored. He has 76 hits in 266 at-bats and a .394 OBP.
