Wintringham Makes History, Wins Triple Crown
Monday, September 9th, 1929
David Wintringham of the Ottawa Indians pushed aside the competition to collect one of baseball's rarest prizes, the Triple Crown. He excelled with a .379 batting average, 35 home runs and 107 RBIs, making 1929 a remarkable year for him.

"I'm just glad to be playing at Lincoln Park," Wintringham told BNN, "The fans really come alive when you're up."

Fans and foes alike were awed by his mythical performance. You don't see a Triple Crown winner very often. This is the 7th time it ever happened in NBA history.
