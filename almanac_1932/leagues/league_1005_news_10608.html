Wilhelm Touches All the Bases, Hits for Cycle
Saturday, March 30th, 1929
Raymond Wilhelm punished the Team Pennsylvania today, leading the Oregon to a 9-8 win. In doing so, Wilhelm hit for the cycle, tagging the Pennsylvania for 5 hits in 5 at-bats.

A player hitting a single, a double, a triple and a home run in the same game is one of the more unusual feats in professional baseball and typically happens only a couple times per season.

"Well, I didn't expect that to happen," the Team second baseman said after the game. "I got some great pitches and I hit their mistakes."

Wilhelm also had 5 RBI and 3 runs scored in the game at Blue Sox Ballpark.

Raymond Wilhelm singled in the 1st, hit a solo-shot off Phassakorn Chakrapan in the 3rd, hit an RBI double in the 5th, hit an RBI single in the 6th, hit a two-run triple in the 7th and walked in the 9th.

Thus far this year Wilhelm has hit .392 with 3 home runs and 15 RBIs.
