Rufolo Socks it to Indians, Cracks 5 Hits
Wednesday, May 15th, 1929
There was no storybook ending. Edge Rufolo of the Baltimore Robins socked it to the visiting Ottawa Indians with a terrific 5-hit game today, but his team didn't get the win. The Robins fell, 9-5, at Sportsmans Park 1911.

"I don't want to sound ungrateful, but something is missing here," Rufolo complained following the loss. "When you have a great game you should win the game, too."

He definitely had an unforgettable day in the batter's box, ripping out 5 hits in 5 at-bats. Baltimore manager Eldin Gabriel sympathized with his center fielder, telling BNN reporters after the game that "Edge played a whale of a game, but sometimes individual brilliance is not enough."

Edge Rufolo hit an RBI single in the 1st, doubled in the 3rd, singled in the 5th, singled in the 7th and singled in the 9th.

So far this year in 9 games Rufolo is hitting .441 with 1 home run, 7 RBIs and 9 runs scored.
