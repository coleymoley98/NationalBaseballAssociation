Free-for-All Costs Faulk and O'Faherty 4-Game Suspensions
Thursday, June 18th, 1925
You would have thought you were at a hockey game yesterday at Omaha Grounds. A free-for-all broke out when Walter Faulk of Omaha and Marshall O'Faherty of Saskatoon rushed one another on the field and had to be restrained by umpires and teammates. Few blows landed, but it was enough for them to be ejected from the game.

Today both teams received notification from the league office that the two players would be suspended for 4 games.

So far this year Faulk has 3 home runs and a .413 batting average.

O'Faherty has taken the mound for 6 relief appearances this season, nailing down no saves while notching a 0-0 record with a 14.34 ERA. The Saskatoon reliever has limited opposing batters to a .354 batting average.
