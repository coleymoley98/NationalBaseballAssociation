Carper Grabs CL Reliever of the Year Award
Tuesday, October 18th, 1932
When Andy Carper jogged in from the bullpen this season, opposing teams knew they were going to have a tough time putting up runs.

The Hamilton Steelers closer turned in a dominant campaign and has been named the Canadian League Reliever of the Year Award winner for 1932.

His award-winning season saw the bullpen ace get 15 saves in 47 relief appearances, 20 strikeouts in 89 innings and only 37 walks. He had a record of 6 wins and 6 losses to go with his 3.34 ERA.

"I hope I can pitch like this every year," he said.

He received 20 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Israel Hunt of the Toronto Blue Sox finished second in voting, while Crawford Jones of the New York Rebels finished third.
