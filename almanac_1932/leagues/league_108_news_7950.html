1926 #1 Player Chosen By WL
Tuesday, September 21st, 1926
The manager of the Omaha Falcons said he thinks Russ Schneider is only going to get better as he gets older.

He may only be 20, but it will be hard to top the first baseman's 1926 campaign, as he has been named the winner of the Western League Most Valuable Player Award.

His winning campaign featured a .393 batting average and a .495 on-base percentage this year. In 64 games he piled up 95 hits, 13 doubles, no triples, 18 home runs, 79 RBIs and 61 runs scored.

He received 7 first place votes out of a possible 8. Kyle Sulley of the Saskatoon Coyotes finished second in voting with 1 first place vote, while Weerasak Morillon of the Vancouver Giants finished third.
