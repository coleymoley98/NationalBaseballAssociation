ABL's Premier Player: McNeely of Montreal
Thursday, August 25th, 1927
He may have less experience than most of his Atlantic Baseball League peers, but that didn't slow Jakkapan McNeely down at all in 1927.

At the age of 16, the Montreal first baseman has been named the winner of the Atlantic Baseball League ABL Player of the Year.

He had a .349 batting average and a .400 on-base percentage this year. In 49 games he piled up 66 hits, 18 doubles, 2 triples, 8 home runs, 44 RBIs and 28 runs scored.

"I'm blown away by the season I was able to put together," he said. "I'm looking forward to trying to do it all again next year."

He received 14 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Somporn Lalancette of the Pointe-Sainte-Anne Acadiens finished second in voting, while Liam Labbé of the Sherbrooke Canadiens finished third.
