Boulay Dominates in 3-1 Win over Canadian League All-Stars
Tuesday, July 17th, 1928
National Baseball Association put its best players on the field today, and the game certainly showed that.

In the annual Midseason Classic, the National League came out over the Canadian League by a final score of 3-1. Leading the way for the National League All-Stars was first baseman Taradin Boulay.

"I go out there and do my best every game, All-Star game or not," the star said after his great show, going 1-for-3 in the contest.

Boulay has 438 career hits with a .347 batting average and 38 home runs.
