Barbe Scorches Blue Sox for 3 HR
Wednesday, June 23rd, 1926
Fans seated in the bleacher section at Ballpark of Hamilton were taking cover all day long as Sylvain Barbe zeroed in on them, rattling three long blasts off the seats in a display of Herculean power.

The big game from the Providence second baseman helped the Redcoats take the victory, 12-7, over the Hamilton Blue Sox.

"I told myself not to press on the last one," he told reporters in the postgame press conference. "I knew I couldn't force it -- it has to be a nice easy swing."

For the game, he collected 3 hits in 4 at-bats, drove in 6 and scored 3 runs.

Sylvain Barbe grounded out in the 1st, hit a solo-shot off Tadamasa Moriguchi in the 3rd, walked in the 4th, hit a two-run home run off Totchtawan Zwick in the 6th, hit a three-run home run off Kent Lanser in the 7th and walked in the 9th.

Posting a .250 batting average to date, Barbe has 3 home runs and 10 RBIs.
