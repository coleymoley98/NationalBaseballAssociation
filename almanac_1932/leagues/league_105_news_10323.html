Best NL Reliever Named
Thursday, October 4th, 1928
At the young age of only 24, Jack Huba has been named the National League Reliever of the Year Award winner for 1928.

The future appears bright for the Montreal Royales relief ace, who captured the award with 10 saves in 36 relief appearances, 31 strikeouts in 44 innings and a record of 6-2 to go with his 1.02 ERA.

He received 16 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Alan Tonnessen of the Chicagou Frontenacs finished second in voting, while Luke Reppenhagen of the Cincinnati Reds finished third.
