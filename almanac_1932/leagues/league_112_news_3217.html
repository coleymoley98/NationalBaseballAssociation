Sherbrooke Takes Division Finals
Monday, August 1st, 1932
The Sherbrooke Canadiens said they felt the Division Finals with the Pawtucket Red Sox would go the distance.

But it didn't end up being that difficult, as Sherbrooke advanced to the Laurier Cup with a 2-1 score, to clinch the series 4-1 at Field of the Juniors.

"Whether it goes to the final game or not, we faced a good team and got the job done. It's a big momentum boost for us," said Jeb Carbello, who was named series MVP. "We're all feeling good about our chances to win the Laurier Cup."

For the series, Carbello hit .353 with a .450 on-base percentage. He had 1 home runs, drove in 3 RBI and scored 1 runs.

The Sherbrooke Canadiens will play against the Coude Voyageurs in the next round. Coude won their series against the Pointe-Sainte-Anne Acadiens 4 games to 1. The Laurier Cup schedule will be announced after the Division Finals finishes.
