Rainiers Pilot Woodhall Picked as WL's Best
Monday, September 23rd, 1929
A baseball manager's success usually depends on the talent of his club, but in the case of Sebastian Woodhall, he seems to have taken the Seattle Rainiers to a higher level and gotten his just due. He was named this season's Manager of the Year in the Western League for leading the team to a very rewarding season.

"Everyone on the team put in a lot of hard work this season," he told reporters. "It's always nice when it's acknowledged in some way."

Woodhall led Seattle to a first place finish in the . The Rainiers ended up with a 50-32 record.
