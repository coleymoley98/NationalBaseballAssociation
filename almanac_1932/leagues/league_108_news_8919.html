Haas Razor-Sharp, Shuts Out Seattle
Tuesday, August 9th, 1927
The Edmonton Eskimos shut out the Seattle Rainiers 7-0 today behind the strong pitching of Kevin Haas. Haas surrendered only 3 hits in recording the complete-game victory, and struck out 6.

Following the last out, Haas appeared to celebrate a little too boisterously for Seattle manager Sebastian Woodhall, who could be seen scolding the famously outspoken left-hander as the clubs left the field. Haas's own manager then intervened with some words for his counterpart.

The Rainiers skipper would not comment on the exchange, but at the Eskimos press conference, Haas confirmed that it was regarding the celebration.

"He basically told me I was being a lousy winner," Haas said.

"And I told him he was being a lousy loser," Edmonton manager Nattaporn Lamontagne added.

Haas has made 6 starts so far this year and put up a 2-4 won-lost record with a 3.83 ERA. In 51.2 innings he has allowed 41 hits, walked 20 and struck out 33.
