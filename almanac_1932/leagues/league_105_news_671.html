McGregor Touches All Bases, Hits for Cycle
Wednesday, July 9th, 1930
Clark McGregor accomplished one of the rarer feats in baseball today when he hit for the cycle against the Toronto Blue Sox in the 11-4 New York Rebels win at Griffith Stadium 1911.

Afterward, McGregor acknowledged that his accomplishment was special, but said he was more interested in helping the Rebels win.

"Maybe one day I'll look back on this with more meaning, but right now I just want to go out tomorrow and put some good swings on the ball and help my club win another game," McGregor said.

On his historic day, he went 4-for-4 with 4 RBI and 3 runs scored -- and also got a spot in the history books.

Clark McGregor hit a solo-shot off Jamison Wise in the 1st, singled in the 3rd, hit an RBI double in the 4th, hit a two-run triple in the 6th and walked in the 8th.

Currently McGregor has 14 home runs and a .421 batting average.
