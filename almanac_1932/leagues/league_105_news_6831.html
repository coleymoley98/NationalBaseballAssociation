Brunet Wins Award for CL Dominance
Sunday, October 11th, 1925
The manager of the Toronto Blue Sox said he always had confidence handing the ball to Hervé Brunet out of the bullpen.

The 31-year-old was a dominant performer all year for Toronto and his efforts have been rewarded with the Canadian League Reliever of the Year Award for 1925.

The bullpen ace had an impressive 3.08 ERA this season, with 10 saves in 30 relief appearances. He fanned 16 batters in 38 innings and sported a record of 4 wins and 2 losses.

He received 16 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Prentice Swain of the Philadelphia Liberty finished second in voting, while Dwight Lynch of the Wolfeburg Celtics finished third.
