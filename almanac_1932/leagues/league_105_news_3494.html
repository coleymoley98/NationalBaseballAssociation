Toronto Narrowly Wins Canadian League West
Wednesday, September 14th, 1932
The Toronto Blue Sox are champions of the Canadian League West, but it certainly didn't come easily.

"We got pushed right to the brink - we were pressured, but didn't fold," said starting pitcher Sakunchai Landis. "Over a long season it's crazy to have a division win by a single game, but we're grateful we came out on the good end of it."

The team's 64-49 has clinched the division crown, which is the franchise's 7th. The team will now be looking to capture the 4th National Baseball Association championship in franchise history.
