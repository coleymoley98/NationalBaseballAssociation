Bad News for Flamand -- Career Likely Over
Thursday, July 18th, 1929
"The writing was on the wall," said Milan Flamand, the Drummondville closer, as he announced his retirement from baseball. The 18-year-old Flamand had been sidelined indefinitely with a torn labrum. From the start, the prognosis was not good. Flamand had a good idea he'd have to call it quits. "As much as I love this sport, I knew I'd have to give it up sooner or later."

Looking at his pitching stats so far this season, Flamand has 28 relief appearances, pitched 40.1 innings and fashioned a 5-2 record with a 2.68 ERA. He has recorded 4 saves, struck out 31 and allowed opposing clubs to hit .257 when he is on the hill.
