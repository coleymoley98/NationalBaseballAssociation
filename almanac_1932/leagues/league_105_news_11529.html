Riel Cup to Royales
Sunday, September 29th, 1929
It's party time in Montreal. The confetti is flying and residents are smiling.

The Montreal Royales have reached the mountaintop, capturing the 1929 Riel Cup today. They defeated the Wolfeburg Celtics 13-7 at Montreal Grounds to officially wrap up the series 4-3.

Asked how it feels to be champion, right fielder William Byrd was to the point.

"It feels great, like a million bucks. I'm so happy right now, not just for me but for all the guys," he said. "We put a lot of work into this and deserve to be champions."

The team is planning a victory parade and fan appreciation day later in the week. Montreal finished the regular season 71-41 and claimed first place in the National League.
