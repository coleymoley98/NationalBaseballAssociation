Robins End Pelletier's Streak
Thursday, May 23rd, 1929
The Baltimore Robins did something to Jason Pelletier no team had done for the last 23 games -- they stopped him, ending his consecutive-game hitting streak. Unfortunately, they couldn't do the same to the Redcoats, who came away with the 9-5 victory at Sportsmans Park 1911.

"Hey, I knew it wasn't going to last forever," the 25-year-old right fielder said of the demise of his streak. "It was a good ride while it lasted."

Currently Pelletier has 2 home runs and a .339 batting average.
