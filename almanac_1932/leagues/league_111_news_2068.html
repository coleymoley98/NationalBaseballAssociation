Great Year Gets Ettrick EBL EBL Player of the Year
Thursday, August 20th, 1931
He may have less experience than most of his Eastern Baseball League peers, but that didn't slow Earnest Ettrick down at all in 1931.

At the age of 18, the Hamilton first baseman has been named the winner of the Eastern Baseball League EBL Player of the Year.

He had a .365 batting average and a .446 on-base percentage this year. In 45 games he piled up 57 hits, 9 doubles, 4 triples, 12 home runs, 41 RBIs and 34 runs scored.

"I'm blown away by the season I was able to put together," he said. "I'm looking forward to trying to do it all again next year."

He received 10 first place votes, as a unanimous winner. Pierre-Thomas Caron of the Toronto Jr. Blue Sox finished second in voting, while Ken LeForce of the Fort Wayne Pioneers finished third.
