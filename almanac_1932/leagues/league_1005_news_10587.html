Columbia Roussillon Smacks 5 Hits
Monday, March 25th, 1929
Dusit Roussillon was at the head of a Team hit parade today at Blue Sox Ballpark. The second baseman went to the plate 6 times and came away with 5 hits, sparking the Columbia to a 24-9 victory over the Team Iowa.

"I told the guys in the dugout, just be thinking about hitting the ball hard somewhere," the sweet-swinging Roussillon told reporters after the game. "Seems like it worked out well for us today."

Iowa skipper Gary Meadows said later that the club gave up "too much plate."

"Not the way we planned it," he said. "Every hitter in the league will rip you when you do that."

Dusit Roussillon grounded out in the 1st, singled in the 2nd, singled in the 4th, singled in the 6th, singled in the 7th, walked in the 8th and hit a two-run single in the 8th.
