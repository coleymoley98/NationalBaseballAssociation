Dionne Beats Liberty for 100th Win
Saturday, May 25th, 1929
His goal at twenty was to win twenty games one year, pitch until he was thirty, win the league pennant and to make some money. Well, Maurice Dionne did something even better -- the 36-year-old Baltimore Robins star has now won 100 games in his career. That put him in a very select group of pitchers.

He joined that exclusive club today at Sportsmans Park 1911 by whipping the Philadelphia Liberty 4-2, holding them to 7 hits in 5.2 innings while chalking up 1 strikeout and 4 walks.

In the postgame interview, the modest Dionne said, "It's special to win 100 games. You don't do it by yourself. You do it with good stuff and a good bullpen. You are only as good as the guys behind you. Years from now I might look back on it and give myself a little pat on the back."

Lifetime Dionne has a won-lost record of 100 victories and 102 losses with 674 strikeouts in 1819.1 innings and a 3.56 ERA.
