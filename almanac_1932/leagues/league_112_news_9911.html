Postseason Bid Ends for Providence
Thursday, July 19th, 1928
It won't be a season to remember for the Providence Bruins, as the club has officially missed out on the playoffs. The Bruins were mathematically eliminated from qualification today.

"With any luck, in a couple years we will look back on the 1928 season as an important stepping stone to a title," said Providence starting pitcher Woody MacIntosh.

The Bruins have missed the playoffs 31 times in their 34 years as a Atlantic Baseball League franchise. This playoff miss is the club's 3rd in a row, with their last postseason appearance coming in 1924. In the last 10 seasons, the Bruins have missed the playoffs 7 times. Providence has won the Laurier Cup 5 times in their history.
