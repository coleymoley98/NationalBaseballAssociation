Voyageurs Win Division Finals
Monday, August 1st, 1932
The championship aspirations of the Coude Voyageurs are still alive and well, while the Pointe-Sainte-Anne Acadiens are left to wait until next year.

Coude won the clinching game of the Division Finals 6-2 over Pointe-Sainte-Anne at Coude Grounds, to win the series 4-1.

"You feel like a little kid again, celebrating something like this," said right fielder Elijah Drummond, who paced the Coude offense and was named series MVP. "We take everything real seriously when we're on the field, but after a series is over you can finally relax a little bit."

Drummond claimed the MVP by racking up 6 hits for a .353 average, with a .389 on-base percentage. He had 2 home runs, 6 RBI and scored 3 runs.

The Coude Voyageurs will play against the Sherbrooke Canadiens in the next round. Sherbrooke won their series against the Pawtucket Red Sox 4 games to 1. The Laurier Cup schedule will be announced after the Division Finals finishes.
