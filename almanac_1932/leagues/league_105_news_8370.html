Tahera Socks it to Blue Sox, Cracks 5 Hits
Friday, June 3rd, 1927
A jubilant Aimar Tahera joined the postgame press conference at Yankee Stadium 1923 today to offer some unusual words of encouragement -- for the Toronto Blue Sox pitching staff he had just vanquished.

"It may not have looked like it," said the New York Rebels left fielder, "but I saw some awful good pitches today. If luck hadn't been on my side it might have turned out a lot different."

That would not have been the conclusion of anyone who saw his dominant 5-for-5 hitting performance, which led the Rebels to a 11-7 win.

"He made it look easy," said Jabari Perrault, manager of the Blue Sox. "We never figured out what to do with him. Obviously."

Aimar Tahera hit an RBI single in the 1st, singled in the 3rd, hit a solo-shot off Dylan Sharp in the 5th, doubled in the 7th and singled in the 8th.

So far this season Tahera is hitting .422 with 1 home run.
