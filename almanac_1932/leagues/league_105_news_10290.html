Royales Triumph in 1928 Riel Cup
Sunday, September 23rd, 1928
With a 3-1 win over the Boston Redcoats tonight at Fenway Park 1912, the Montreal Royales wrapped up their 6th title in team history.

Montreal left little doubt who was the better club. "Team" was the word of the day as the Royales celebrated the culmination of their banner year in the victors clubhouse following the game.

"Hitting, pitching, fielding; those are all things you need to win, but most importantly you need a team, a team that works together toward the same collective goal," said Montreal manager Hervé Morel. "Our players felt that way all year and they played that way. When one guy had an off day, 24 others stepped up their game and filled the void."

A jovial mood filled the air as Montreal celebrated into the night. The city's mayor said parade plans were underway and a "huge celebration" in the works.

"We're going to celebrate our team, our championship Montreal Royales," he said.

The champions completed the regular season with a first-place finish in the National League and a 74-38 record.
