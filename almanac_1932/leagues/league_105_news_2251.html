Boston Takes League Chamionship Series
Friday, October 2nd, 1931
The League Chamionship Series didn't live up to the billing from those who thought it would be an all-out battle to the last game.

But the Boston Redcoats are no doubt pleased by the results, as they have knocked out the Toronto Blue Sox 4-2 to advance to the Riel Cup.

"It doesn't matter how you win it, as long as you win it and get yourself moving forward," said Redcoats catcher Danusorn Lavallée. "I know a lot of people thought this was going to be a really tight series the whole way through, but we're glad it didn't go right down to the wire."

Boston won the final game by a score of 4-1 at Fenway Park 1912.

Lavallée was named series MVP after hitting .364 with a .391 on-base percentage. He also had 1 home runs, drove in 4 RBI and scored 1 runs.

The Boston Redcoats will play against the Montreal Royales in the next round. Montreal won their series against the Detroit Lions 4 games to 2. The Riel Cup schedule will be announced after the League Chamionship Series finishes.
