News Gets Worse for Omaha, Warburton
Saturday, July 10th, 1926
"The writing was on the wall," said Mark Warburton, the Omaha reliever, as he announced his retirement from baseball. The 16-year-old Warburton had been sidelined indefinitely with a partially torn ulnar collateral ligament. From the start, the prognosis was not good. Warburton had a good idea he'd have to call it quits. "As much as I love this sport, I knew I'd have to give it up sooner or later."

To date this year Warburton has fashioned a 2.16 ERA over 16.2 innings with a 1-1 record. He has made 11 relief appearances with 12 strikeouts and 3 saves.
