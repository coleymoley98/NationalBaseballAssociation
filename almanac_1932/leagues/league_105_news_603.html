Mahaffey Dominates in 10-4 Win over National League All-Stars
Tuesday, July 1st, 1930
There's a whole lot of pride on the line when the best baseball players throughout National Baseball Association gather for the all-star game.

It was no different this year, as the Canadian League bested the National League by a score of 10-4, in a spirited contest played at Citi Field.

"It might be a little lower intensity than the regular season or playoffs, but everyone in that clubhouse still wants to win this game," said Josh Mahaffey of the Canadian League, who was named the game MVP.

Mahaffey has played in 157 games with a .316 career batting average and 139 hits. He also has scored 79 runs and driven in 74 runs with 8 home runs.
