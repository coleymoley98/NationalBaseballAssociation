It was a sensational day at the plate for Juneau Brasseurs first baseman William Wood, but a tough one for the team, as the Brasseurs dropped the contest 6-5 to the Philadelphia Mets.

Even with the loss, Wood was pure misery for the Philadelphia pitching staff. He left them shell-shocked after going 5-for-5 in an explosive display of hitting.

"It's always good to get a bunch of hits in a game, but you also want to win," said Wood, who is the 21-year old first baseman .

"Wood really hit the ball hard and when you do that, you've got a good chance to do some damage," Mets manager William Dunning told BNN reporters after the game. "I feel like we threw him some good pitches, but he just put some good swings on the ball."
