Wilder Goes 5-for-7
Tuesday, May 5th, 1931
It was a big day with the bat for Harry Wilder of the New York Rebels at Yankee Stadium 1923 as he bashed out hit after hit after hit to defeat the Ottawa Indians, 33-8.

After the game he said, "Guys, it's just one game in a long season. I hit it hard and found some holes today. 5 hits in 7 tries. But my hits are not the big thing in the game. The victory is the important thing. That's the story."

At the end of the game Wilder had collected 5 hits in 7 at-bats and a small mention in the record book. And the Rebels had won. Harry Wilder doubled in the 1st, hit an RBI double in the 2nd, grounded out in the 3rd, hit a two-run triple in the 3rd, walked in the 4th, grounded out in the 6th, hit a two-run home run off Zach Gaumer in the 7th and hit an RBI single in the 8th.
