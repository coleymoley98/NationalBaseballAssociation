Saucier Spins No-Hitter Against Adams
Friday, July 15th, 1927
In a dominant outing, Étienne Saucier of the Minneapolis Jr. Saints totally silenced the bats of the Adams Mounties, finishing with a no-hitter.

In the Jr. Saints' 4-0 victory, Saucier faced only 32 batters, surrendered no hits, had 10 strikeouts and 4 walks.

"Honestly, I didn't even know the situation was what it was until the seventh inning," Minneapolis manager Prasarn Heemkhao said. "I happened to look up there to see how many hits we had gotten, and I happened to notice that they hadn't gotten any."

Commenting on the defeat, losing manager Timothy Wilkins told reporters in the clubhouse that he had a "pretty simple" analysis of the game.

"The Jr. Saints had all the hits and all the runs, so they won," he snapped. "But yeah, Saucier pitched a heck of a ballgame."

His record this season is 1-3 in 8 starts and Saucier has a 2.33 ERA.
