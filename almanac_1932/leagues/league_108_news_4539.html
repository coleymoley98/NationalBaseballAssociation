Cole Welter Chosen WL's #1 Player
Friday, September 21st, 1923
Cole Welter took Western League by storm this year, and he did it all at the age of 19.

For his spectacular campaign, the Vancouver Giants star has been named the 1923 Western League Most Valuable Player Award recipient.

"To be considered amongst the best in the league is overwhelming," said the third baseman.

On the season he compiled a .311 batting average to go with a .399 on-base percentage. The native of Calgary, The United Provinces also added 14 doubles, 10 triples, 29 home runs, 106 RBIs and 79 runs scored.

He received 4 first place votes out of a possible 8. Beckham Dinsmore of the Winnipeg Thunder finished second in voting with 2 first place votes, while Jules Caouette of the Winnipeg Thunder finished third with 1 first place vote.
