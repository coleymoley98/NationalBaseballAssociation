Baltimore Trades Roca for Boston's Sexauer
Thursday, January 7th, 1932
After prolonged negotiations Baltimore agreed to ship 36-year-old RHP Steve Roca and 19-year-old minor league 2B Pattarapol Beard to Boston for 28-year-old LF Seamus Sexauer. The deal is awaiting confirmation from the league office but neither team expects any hang-ups.

"It took longer than expected," said one GM, "but we knew that the deal was going to happen."

Baltimore will retain a portion of Roca's remaining contract.

Career-to-date, Roca has a record of 208 wins and 153 losses and a 3.42 ERA.

Sexauer has 682 career hits with a .329 batting average and 56 home runs.
