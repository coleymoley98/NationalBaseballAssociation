Red-Hot Bat: Michaud Collects Cycle
Friday, June 5th, 1931
It was a quite a day for Anthony Michaud at Field of the Acadiens.

He hit for the cycle, belting out every kind of hit there is -- a single, double, triple and home run. The Pointe-Sainte-Anne left fielder accomplished the rare feat during his team's 7-2 victory over the Champlain Coureurs.

"I never even thought about hitting for the cycle and what do you know, I did it," he told BNN reporters at the postgame press conference. "The cycle really doesn't matter to me, it's the win that's important."

Michaud went 4-for-4 in the game with 4 RBI and 2 runs scored.

Anthony Michaud hit an RBI single in the 1st, doubled in the 4th, hit a two-run triple in the 5th and hit a solo-shot off Ronan Clancey in the 7th.

This season Michaud is batting at a .571 clip with 2 home runs and 9 RBIs.
